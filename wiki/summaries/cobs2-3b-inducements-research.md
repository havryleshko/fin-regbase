# COBS 2.3B — Inducements and Research (Research Unbundling)

## Application — COBS 2.3B.1R

COBS 2.3B applies to a firm carrying on MiFID, equivalent third country or optional exemption business **(COBS 2.3B.1R)**.

## Purpose — COBS 2.3B.2G

COBS 2.3B serves two distinct purposes **(COBS 2.3B.2G)**:

1. **Relieving the inducements ban for advisory/PM firms:** A firm providing independent/restricted advice or portfolio management to UK retail clients (subject to the absolute ban in COBS 2.3A.15R) or to overseas retail/professional clients (subject to the transfer-back obligation in COBS 2.3A.16R) is otherwise prohibited from receiving third-party research. Compliance with COBS 2.3B allows such a firm to receive third-party research without breaching those prohibitions.

2. **Removing research from the inducements assessment for other firms:** For investment firms not subject to COBS 2.3A.15R or 2.3A.16R, compliance with COBS 2.3B means research acquired in accordance with this section does not constitute an inducement under COBS 2.3A. No quality enhancement assessment under COBS 2.3A.8R is needed.

---

## Three Permissible Payment Routes — COBS 2.3B.3R

Third-party research will not be an inducement under COBS 2.3A.5R, 2.3A.15R or 2.3A.16R if received in return for **one of the following three payment routes**:

| Route | Mechanism | Conditions |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **(1) Direct payment** | Firm pays from its own resources | No additional conditions beyond being from firm's own resources |
| **(2) Research payment account (RPA)** | Separate account controlled by firm, funded by specific research charges to clients | Must meet COBS 2.3B.4R |
| **(3) Joint payments** | Combined payment for research and execution services | Must meet COBS 2.3B.25R–2.3B.33G |

**(COBS 2.3B.3R)** [Note: MiFID Delegated Directive article 13(1)(a) and (b)]

---

## Research Payment Accounts (Route 2)

### Operating Conditions — COBS 2.3B.4R

The RPA must meet four conditions:

**Condition 1 — Funding:** The RPA must be funded **only** by a specific research charge to clients. That charge must:
- (a) be based only on a research budget set by the firm for the amount needed for third-party research in respect of investment services rendered to clients; and
- **(b) not be linked to the volume or value of transactions executed on behalf of clients.**

This is the core de-bundling requirement — any link between research charges and execution volumes or values contaminates the RPA and negates the exemption from COBS 2.3A.

**Condition 2 — Budget management:** The firm must set and regularly assess a research budget as an internal administrative measure, and must agree the research charge with its clients. The budget must comply with COBS 2.3B.7R (ceiling and no internal research), COBS 2.3B.8R(2) (advance notice of increases), and COBS 2.3B.11R (governance).

**Condition 3 — Firm responsibility:** The firm must be **fully responsible** for the RPA.

**Condition 4 — Quality assessment:** The firm must **regularly assess the quality** of research purchased, based on robust quality criteria, and its ability to contribute to better investment decisions for the clients who pay the research charge.

**(COBS 2.3B.4R)** [Note: MiFID Delegated Directive article 13(1)(b)(i)–(iv) and (2)(a) and (b)]

### Client Information — COBS 2.3B.5R

A firm using an RPA must provide to clients:
1. **Before provision of investment or ancillary service:** Information about the budgeted amount for research and the estimated research charge for each of them.
2. **Annually:** Total costs each client has incurred for third-party research.

The annual information should also inform clients that they are entitled to request the on-request disclosure in COBS 2.3B.20R(1) **(COBS 2.3B.6G)**.

### Budget Ceiling and Internal Research — COBS 2.3B.7R

- The total amount of research charges collected from clients must **not exceed** the research budget.
- The research budget and RPA must **not be used to fund research generated internally** by the firm itself.

**(COBS 2.3B.7R)**

### Client Agreement and Surplus — COBS 2.3B.8R

The firm must agree with clients, in the investment management agreement or general terms of business:
- (a) the research charge as budgeted; and
- (b) the frequency with which the specific research charge will be deducted from the client's resources over the year.

The firm **must not increase** its research budget unless it has provided, in advance, clear information to relevant clients about the intended increase **(COBS 2.3B.8R(2))**.

**Surplus at period end — COBS 2.3B.8R(3):** If there is a surplus in the RPA at the end of a period, the firm must either:
- (a) rebate those funds to relevant clients; or
- (b) offset against the research budget and research charge for the following period.

The firm must take reasonable steps to maintain a **fair allocation of costs between clients** when calculating rebates or offsets **(COBS 2.3B.8R(4))**.

### Governance and Audit Trail — COBS 2.3B.11R

For the purposes of COBS 2.3B.4R(2), the firm must ensure:
1. The research budget is managed **solely by the firm** based on reasonable assessment of need for third-party research.
2. Budget allocation is subject to **appropriate controls and senior management oversight** to ensure it is managed and used in the best interests of clients.
3. Controls include a **clear audit trail** of:
   - (a) payments made to research providers; and
   - (b) how amounts paid were determined with reference to: (i) the quality criteria required by COBS 2.3B.4R(4); and (ii) the firm's written policy under COBS 2.3B.12R.

**(COBS 2.3B.11R)**

### Written Policy — COBS 2.3B.12R

A firm using an RPA must establish a **written policy** setting out how the firm will:
- (a) comply with all elements of COBS 2.3B.4R(4) — the quality criteria; and
- (b) address the extent to which research purchased may benefit clients' portfolios (including by reference to investment strategies applicable to various portfolio types) and the firm's approach to fair cost allocation.

The firm must **provide the policy to its clients** **(COBS 2.3B.12R(2))**.

### Research Charges Collected Alongside Commission — COBS 2.3B.16R

If a firm collects the research charge alongside a transaction commission (rather than as a separate deduction), the firm must still:
- Indicate a **separately identifiable** research charge; and
- Ensure the arrangements comply with COBS 2.3B.4R and COBS 2.3B.5R.

**(COBS 2.3B.16R)** A research charge funded by client charges must not be linked to volume or value of other services, and must not be used to cover anything other than research — such as execution charges **(COBS 2.3B.17G)**.

### Delegation of RPA Administration — COBS 2.3B.18R

A firm may delegate administration of the RPA to a third party, provided the arrangement facilitates payments to research providers in the firm's name, without undue delay, and in accordance with the firm's instruction **(COBS 2.3B.18R)**.

When relying on a third-party administrator, the firm should consider whether arrangements ensure **(COBS 2.3B.19G)**:
- (a) Client research charges flow into a separate RPA budget as cleared funds within **30 days** of deduction from the client's account;
- (b) The firm retains **sole, full and absolute discretion** over the account and all payments/rebates;
- (c) RPA monies are ring-fenced from the third party's own assets (or held on deposit for the firm if the administrator is a bank); and
- (d) The third party has **no right of access or recourse** to the RPA for its own benefit — not to offset other fees owed by the firm, not as collateral.

The firm **remains fully responsible** for discharging all COBS 2.3B obligations regardless of delegation **(COBS 2.3B.19G(2))**.

### On-Request Disclosure — COBS 2.3B.20R

Where a firm operates an RPA, it must provide on request to clients a summary of:
- (a) the providers paid from the account;
- (b) the total amount they were paid over a defined period;
- (c) the benefits and services received by the firm; and
- (d) how the total amount spent compares to the budget set for that period, noting any rebate or carry-over if residual funds remain.

The firm must also be able to provide this information to the **FCA on request** for all research payment accounts **(COBS 2.3B.20R(2))**.

## What Is Not Research — COBS 2.3B.23G

The FCA does not regard the following as research, meaning they cannot be paid from an RPA or through joint payments:

1. Post-trade analytics
2. Price feeds or historical price data that has not been analysed or manipulated to present the firm with meaningful conclusions
3. Services relating to valuation or performance measurement of portfolios
4. Seminar fees
5. Corporate access services
6. Subscriptions for publications
7. Travel, accommodation or entertainment costs
8. Order and execution management systems
9. Membership fees to professional associations
10. Direct money payments
11. Administration of a research payment account
12. Administration of: (a) an account for joint payments for research and execution services; or (b) a research provider payment allocation structure

**(COBS 2.3B.23G)**

Note: some of the above (particularly corporate access services under £200m market cap at COBS 2.3A.19R(5)(k)) may qualify as acceptable minor non-monetary benefits under COBS 2.3A.19R or COBS 2.3A.22G and can be received without breaching the inducements rules — but they cannot be paid from an RPA **(COBS 2.3B.22G)**.

## Research Must Not Be an Execution Factor — COBS 2.3B.29R

**Research services must not be treated as an execution factor** under COBS 11.2A.2R **(COBS 2.3B.29R)**.

This is a hard Rule. It prevents firms from re-bundling research and execution through the back door — specifically, from routing client orders to a particular execution venue partly on the basis that that venue provides research services. Doing so would circumvent the entire unbundling framework by treating research provision as a reason to prefer a particular execution venue, defeating the volume/value de-linkage requirement.

A firm must not enter into any arrangements for receipt and payment of research (whether under Routes 1, 2, or 3) that would compromise its ability to meet its best execution obligations under COBS 11.2A **(COBS 2.3B.24G)**.

## Joint Payments for Research and Execution Services (Route 3)

### Conditions — COBS 2.3B.25R

A firm using joint payments for research and execution services must meet eight conditions:

1. **Written policy** describing the firm's approach to joint payments and how it will ensure compliance with all conditions; and how governance, decision-making and controls operate for third-party research purchased using joint payments, including how these are maintained separately from trade execution.

2. **Methodology** for calculating and identifying research costs separately within total charges for joint payments.

3. **Research provider payment allocation structure** for allocating payments between: (a) providers that supply both research and execution services; and (b) research providers not engaged in execution services and not in a financial services group including an investment firm offering execution or brokerage.

4. **Full firm responsibility** for: (a) administration of accounts for purchasing research from joint payments; (b) ensuring operation does not interfere with compliance obligations; (c) ensuring timely payments to research providers.

5. **Annual research budget** based on expected amount needed for third-party research in respect of investment services, and **not linked to expected volumes or values of transactions** executed on behalf of clients. Must be set at least annually and at an aggregation level appropriate to the firm's investment processes, products, services and clients.

6. **Fair cost allocation** between clients.

7. **Periodic assessment** (at least annually): assess value, quality and use of research and its contribution to investment decisions; ensure research charges are reasonable compared to comparable services.

8. **Client disclosure** of items in COBS 2.3B.30R.

**(COBS 2.3B.25R)**

### Budget Overrun Policy — COBS 2.3B.26R

If research charges to clients **exceed the budget** or the budget is **increased**, the firm's written policy must set out:
1. The relevant actions to be taken in such circumstances; and
2. The information to be disclosed to clients.

**(COBS 2.3B.26R)**

### Permitted Use of Joint Payment Funds — COBS 2.3B.21R

RPA funds and the separately identifiable research element of joint payments may **only** be used to pay for research or to pay rebates to clients. No other use is permitted **(COBS 2.3B.21R)**.

### Delegation — COBS 2.3B.28R

If a firm delegates administration of the research provider payment allocation structure or joint payments research account, it retains responsibility for compliance. The firm must ensure reconciliation and reporting are undertaken with appropriate frequency; and must continue to monitor and manage risks from unspent surplus amounts and research provider concentrations **(COBS 2.3B.28R)**.

---

## Joint Payments Disclosure — COBS 2.3B.30R–2.3B.31R

### What Must Be Disclosed — COBS 2.3B.30R

For the purposes of COBS 2.3B.25R(8), the firm must disclose to relevant clients:

1. The firm's use of joint payments for research, including how combined with other permitted payments under COBS 2.3B.3R.
2. The key features of the firm's joint payments policy (or the policy itself), having regard to clients' information needs — must be clear, fair and not misleading.
3. **Expected annual research costs** to the client — provided as ex-ante disclosure as part of costs and charges, based on either: (a) budget-setting and cost allocation procedures; or (b) actual costs for prior annual periods.
4. The most significant items at appropriate aggregation level: (a) benefits and services received from research providers measured by total amounts paid; and (b) types of research providers from which services are purchased.
5. **Total costs incurred** by the client — ex-post annual total payments for research using joint payments, provided as part of costs and charges disclosures, **separately identifying joint payment research charges**.
6. Where relevant, budget overrun disclosures under COBS 2.3B.26R(2).

### When Disclosures Must Be Made — COBS 2.3B.31R

| Disclosure | Timing |
|------------|--------|
| Items (1)–(4) | Before providing investment or ancillary service; and thereafter on request and at least annually |
| Item (5) | As part of costs and charges disclosures, separately identifying joint payment research charges |
| Item (6) | As soon as reasonably practicable, and in any case in the firm's next periodic disclosure to clients on costs and charges |

**(COBS 2.3B.31R)**

## Key Points for Agents

- **Three routes, not one:** Research can only be received outside the COBS 2.3A.5R prohibition via: (1) direct payment from own resources; (2) RPA meeting COBS 2.3B.4R; or (3) joint payments for research and execution services meeting COBS 2.3B.25R–2.3B.33G. There is no fourth route. Receiving research through any other mechanism — including arrangements that look like RPAs but do not meet all four conditions — remains an inducement **(COBS 2.3B.3R)**.
- **The volume/value de-linkage is the core RPA condition (COBS 2.3B.4R(1)(b)).** Any link between the research charge to clients and transaction volumes or values rendered on behalf of those clients contaminates the RPA. This includes indirect links — a research charge that fluctuates in proportion to AUM where AUM drives transaction activity is suspect.
- **RPA surplus must be rebated or carried forward — it cannot be retained (COBS 2.3B.8R(3)).** A firm that accumulates RPA surpluses and does not rebate or offset them is in breach. This has audit significance — the FCA's review of RPA surplus treatment is a standard supervisory test.
- **12-item negative list (COBS 2.3B.23G) is exhaustive for RPA/joint payment purposes.** None of the 12 named categories can be paid from an RPA or joint payment mechanism. Note: some (e.g. corporate access for sub-£200m companies) can still be received as acceptable minor NMBs under COBS 2.3A.19R — the NMB route and the RPA route are separate.
- **Research is not an execution factor — hard Rule (COBS 2.3B.29R).** A firm must not treat research provision as a reason to prefer a particular execution venue under COBS 11.2A.2R. Breach of this Rule simultaneously infringes the best execution framework and the unbundling framework.
- **Written policy is mandatory for both RPA and joint payments (COBS 2.3B.12R; 2.3B.25R(1)).** The written policy must be provided to clients. An internal policy not disclosed to clients does not satisfy the requirement. The policy must address quality criteria compliance and fair cost allocation — both elements are hard Rule requirements, not guidance.
- **Delegation does not transfer responsibility (COBS 2.3B.18R; 2.3B.28R).** Whether the firm delegates RPA administration or joint payment allocation to a third party, it remains fully responsible for discharging all COBS 2.3B obligations. The FCA can look through the delegate structure to hold the firm accountable.

# SYSC 4 — General Organisational Requirements

## Overview

SYSC 4 sets out the FCA's foundational governance and organisational requirements for authorised firms. It operates at two levels:

1. **SYSC 4.1.1R — the baseline rule** for all firms: a principles-based governance obligation covering organisational structure, risk management processes, internal controls, and information system safeguards
2. **SYSC 4.1.1-AR — the common platform rule**: a more prescriptive set of six operational requirements for common platform firms (MiFID investment firms, management companies, full-scope UK AIFMs)

SYSC 4 also covers persons who effectively direct the business (SYSC 4.2), senior personnel responsibility (SYSC 4.3), the management body (SYSC 4.3A, common platform firms), and apportionment of responsibilities (SYSC 4.4, non-common platform firms).

Note: SYSC 4.7 (whistleblowing provisions) and SYSC 4 Annex 1 (Responsibilities Map for enhanced SMCR firms) have been moved — to SYSC 24.3.9G and SYSC 25 Annex 1G respectively.

## SYSC 4.1 — General Requirements

### The Baseline Governance Rule (SYSC 4.1.1R — All Firms)

A firm must have **robust governance arrangements**, which include **(SYSC 4.1.1R)**:
- A **clear organisational structure** with well-defined, transparent and consistent lines of responsibility
- **Effective processes** to identify, manage, monitor and report the risks it is or might be exposed to
- **Internal control mechanisms**, including sound administrative and accounting procedures
- **Effective control and safeguard arrangements** for information processing systems

This rule applies to all FCA-authorised firms. For firms that are not common platform firms, it operates as a principles-based obligation; the FCA expects compliance calibrated to the nature, scale and complexity of the business **(SYSC 4.1.2A G)**.

### Common Platform Firm Organisational Requirements (SYSC 4.1.1-AR)

A common platform firm must, taking into account the nature, scale and complexity of its business and the nature and range of its designated investment business, comply with six organisational requirements **(SYSC 4.1.1-AR)**:

1. **(a) Documented organisational structure** — establish, implement and maintain decision-making procedures and an organisational structure which clearly and in a documented manner specifies reporting lines and allocates functions and responsibilities
2. **(b) Internal control mechanisms** — adequate mechanisms designed to secure compliance with decisions and procedures at all levels of the firm
3. **(c) Internal reporting and communication** — effective internal reporting and communication of information at all relevant levels of the firm
4. **(d) Adequate and orderly records** — of its business and internal organisation
5. **(2) Information security** — systems and procedures adequate to safeguard the security, integrity and confidentiality of information, taking into account the nature of the information in question
6. **(3) Business continuity policy** — an adequate BCP aimed at ensuring, in case of interruption to its systems and procedures, the preservation of essential data and functions and the timely resumption of designated investment business
7. **(4) Accounting policies** — that enable the firm to deliver accurate financial reports to the FCA in a timely manner
8. **(5) Regular monitoring** — monitor and, on a regular basis, evaluate the adequacy and effectiveness of its systems, internal control mechanisms and arrangements, and take appropriate measures to address any deficiencies

### Information Security (Common Platform Firms) (SYSC 4.1.1R(3))

A common platform firm must maintain sound security mechanisms for communications, including **(SYSC 4.1.1R(3))**:
- (a) guaranteeing the security and authentication of the means of transfer of information
- (b) minimising the risk of data corruption and unauthorised access
- (c) preventing information leakage

### Business Continuity Policy Contents (SYSC 4.1.8G)

A firm's BCP should include six elements:
1. Resource requirements (people, systems, assets) and arrangements for obtaining them
2. Recovery priorities for the firm's operations
3. Communication arrangements for internal and external parties (including the FCA, clients, and the press)
4. Escalation and invocation plans
5. Processes to validate the integrity of information affected by the disruption
6. Regular testing of the BCP

### P2P Platform Failure Arrangements (SYSC 4.1.8AR / 4.1.8DB)

An operator of an electronic system in relation to lending (P2P platform) must **(SYSC 4.1.8AR)**:
- Have arrangements in place to ensure that P2P agreements it facilitates will have a reasonable likelihood of being managed and administered, in accordance with contract terms, if it ceases to manage and administer those agreements
- These arrangements must not prefer any particular customers or class of customers

The firm must also maintain a **P2P resolution manual** — a live document containing information to assist in resolving the firm's business of management and administration of P2P agreements in the event of insolvency **(SYSC 4.1.8DB)**. Minimum content:
1. How the firm conducts the business (critical staff, premises, IT systems, record-keeping, bank accounts, external persons, legal documentation, group structure)
2. Steps needed to implement the platform failure arrangements
3. Contract terms relied on to ensure continued management
4. How the firm's systems can produce ongoing P2P disclosure data (COBS 18.12.31R)

The P2P resolution manual must be immediately available to an insolvency practitioner and to the FCA on request **(SYSC 4.1.8DC R)**. P2P operators must notify the FCA in writing before ceasing to manage P2P agreements, implementing platform failure arrangements, or implementing analogous arrangements **(SYSC 4.1.8DAG)**. P2P platforms must not accept full ownership transfer of client money relating to P2P agreements **(SYSC 4.1.8ER)**.

## SYSC 4.2 — Persons Who Effectively Direct the Business

### The Fit and Proper Standard (SYSC 4.2.1R)

The **senior personnel** of a common platform firm, a management company, a full-scope UK AIFM, or the UK branch of a non-UK bank must be of **sufficiently good repute** and **sufficiently experienced** as to ensure the sound and prudent management of the firm **(SYSC 4.2.1R)**. Other firms should take account of this rule as if it were guidance **(SYSC 4.2.1AG)**.

### Minimum Two Persons (SYSC 4.2.2R)

A common platform firm, a management company, a full-scope UK AIFM, and the UK branch of a non-UK bank must ensure that its management is undertaken by **at least two persons** meeting the requirements of SYSC 4.2.1R **(SYSC 4.2.2R)**.

### The Two Independent Minds Test (SYSC 4.2.4G)

At least two independent minds should be applied to the formulation and implementation of the firm's policies **(SYSC 4.2.4G)**. Where a firm nominates just two individuals:
- The FCA will **not** regard them as both effectively directing the business where one makes decisions relating to only a few aspects of the business
- Both should play a part in the decision-making process on all significant decisions
- Both should demonstrate the qualities and application to influence strategy, day-to-day policy and its implementation
- Where a **single individual is particularly dominant**, this will raise doubts about whether SYSC 4.2.2R is met **(SYSC 4.2.5G)**

This is a substance test, not a title test. Day-to-day involvement in execution is not required, but involvement in strategy and general direction — and knowledge of and influence on how strategy is implemented — is required.

### Alternative Arrangements for Single-Person Firms (SYSC 4.2.6R)

If a common platform firm (other than a credit institution or AIFM investment firm) is a natural person or a legal person managed by a single natural person, it must have alternative arrangements in place ensuring:
- Sound and prudent management of the firm
- Adequate consideration of the interests of clients and the integrity of the market
The natural persons concerned must be of sufficiently good repute, possess sufficient knowledge, skills and experience, and commit sufficient time to perform their duties.

## SYSC 4.3 — Responsibility of Senior Personnel

### Allocation Obligation (SYSC 4.3.1R)

When allocating functions internally, a firm must ensure that **senior management** and, where appropriate, the **supervisory function** are responsible for ensuring that the firm complies with its obligations under the regulatory system. In particular, senior management must assess and periodically review the effectiveness of policies, arrangements and procedures and take appropriate measures to address deficiencies **(SYSC 4.3.1R)**.

### Written Reports to Senior Management (SYSC 4.3.2R / SYSC 4.3.2-AR)

For management companies and P2P operators: senior personnel must receive on a frequent basis, and at least annually, written reports on compliance, risk, and internal audit matters — including whether appropriate remedial measures have been taken in the event of deficiencies **(SYSC 4.3.2R)**.

For common platform firms: senior management must receive the same at least annually, with the supervisory function also receiving written reports on the same matters **(SYSC 4.3.2-AR)**. Senior management must also keep up-to-date records of the allocation of significant functions and who is responsible for overseeing and maintaining the firm's organisational requirements **(SYSC 4.3.2-AR(3)(b))**.

## SYSC 4.3A — Management Body and Nomination Committee

### The Seven-Function Management Body Mandate (SYSC 4.3A.1R)

A common platform firm must ensure that the **management body** defines, oversees and is accountable for the implementation of governance arrangements. The management body must **(SYSC 4.3A.1R)**:

1. Have overall responsibility for the firm
2. Approve and oversee implementation of the firm's strategic objectives, risk strategy and internal governance
3. Ensure the integrity of the firm's accounting and financial reporting systems, including financial and operational controls and compliance with the regulatory system
4. Oversee the process of disclosure and communications
5. Have responsibility for providing effective oversight of senior management
6. Monitor and periodically assess the adequacy of: (a) the firm's strategic objectives; (b) the effectiveness of the firm's governance arrangements; (c) the adequacy of policies relating to client services — and take appropriate steps to address deficiencies
7. Have adequate access to information and documents needed to oversee and monitor management decision-making

Additionally, the management body must define, approve and oversee: (1) the firm's organisation (skills/knowledge/expertise/resources/procedures/arrangements); (2) a policy on services/activities/products/operations in accordance with the firm's risk tolerance and client characteristics; and (3) a remuneration policy for persons involved in service provision, designed to encourage responsible business conduct, fair client treatment and conflict avoidance **(SYSC 4.3A.1AR)**.

### Chair/CEO Separation — Hard Rule (SYSC 4.3A.2R)

A common platform firm must ensure that the **chairman of the management body does not simultaneously exercise the PRA's Chief Executive function (SMF1) or the chief executive function** within the same firm **(SYSC 4.3A.2R)**. A firm may apply to the FCA for a waiver of this rule under s.138A FSMA **(SYSC 4.3A.2AG)**. This is an unconditional hard Rule — not a "should" or "as if guidance."

### Management Body Member Requirements (SYSC 4.3A.3R)

Members of the management body must **(SYSC 4.3A.3R)**:
1. Be of sufficiently good repute
2. Possess sufficient knowledge, skills and experience to perform their duties
3. Possess adequate collective knowledge, skills and experience to understand the firm's activities, including the main risks
4. Reflect an adequately broad range of experiences
5. Commit sufficient time to perform their functions
6. Act with honesty, integrity and independence of mind to effectively assess and challenge the decisions of senior management and to effectively oversee and monitor management decision-making

The firm must devote adequate human and financial resources to the induction and training of management body members **(SYSC 4.3A.4R)**. Members must not hold more directorships than is appropriate taking into account individual circumstances and the nature, scale and complexity of the firm's activities **(SYSC 4.3A.5R)**. For significant SYSC firms, additional directorship caps apply **(SYSC 4.3A.6R)** — broadly: one executive directorship + two non-executive directorships, or four non-executive directorships.

### Nomination Committee (SYSC 4.3A.8R–10R)

A significant SYSC firm that is a common platform firm must establish a **nomination committee** composed of management body members who do not perform any executive function **(SYSC 4.3A.8R)**. The nomination committee must carry out eight functions including: promoting diversity policy; identifying and recommending candidates for management body vacancies (evaluating knowledge/skills/diversity/experience balance); preparing role descriptions and assessing time commitment; setting a target for underrepresented gender representation; and annually assessing the structure, size, composition and performance of the management body **(SYSC 4.3A.9R)**.

Common platform firms without a nomination committee must still put in place a diversity policy and engage a broad set of qualities when recruiting management body members **(SYSC 4.3A.10R)**.

## SYSC 4.4 — Apportionment of Responsibilities

### Application

SYSC 4.4 does **not** apply to common platform firms — those firms are subject to the management body rules in SYSC 4.3A instead **(SYSC 4.4.2G)**. SYSC 4.4 applies to all other SMCR firms (including limited scope firms, core SMCR firms, and authorised professional firms for their non-mainstream activities).

### The Apportionment Obligation (SYSC 4.4.3R)

A firm must take reasonable care to **maintain a clear and appropriate apportionment of significant responsibilities** among its directors and senior managers in such a way that:
1. It is clear who has which of those responsibilities; and
2. The business and affairs of the firm can be adequately monitored and controlled by the directors, relevant senior managers and governing body of the firm

### Two Mandatory Allocated Functions (SYSC 4.4.5R)

A firm must allocate to one or more individuals, in accordance with the table below, both:
1. **The apportionment function** — dealing with the apportionment of significant responsibilities (SYSC 4.4.3R)
2. **The oversight function** — overseeing the establishment and maintenance of systems and controls (SYSC 4.1.1R)

| Firm type | Column 2: Must be allocated to | Column 3: Optional additional |
|-----------|-------------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Body corporate, member of a group | Firm's chief executive (all jointly, if multiple); or a director/senior manager responsible for overall management of the group or a group division | Firm's and group's directors and senior managers |
| EEA SMCR firm | Not applicable (Column 2) | Directors and senior managers |
| Any other firm | Firm's chief executive (all jointly, if multiple) | Firm's and group's directors and senior managers |

Column 2 allocations are mandatory. Column 3 allocations are compulsory only where there is no Column 2 allocation available, but are otherwise additional and optional. The FCA considers allocation to one or two individuals appropriate for most firms **(SYSC 4.4.6G Q3)**.

Key interpretive points from the SYSC 4.4.6G FAQ:
- A dominant individual with the title of managing director or managing partner qualifies as "chief executive" **(Q5)**
- Where there are multiple chief executives, all must be allocated the functions jointly **(Q6)**
- For group firms without their own chief executive, allocation may be to a group director/senior manager of equivalent or greater seniority than the firm's CEO — such individuals may still need FSMA s.59 approval **(Q7)**
- For overseas SMCR firms: the "chief executive" is the person responsible for the conduct of the firm's UK business — this may be the UK establishment manager or the global CEO **(Q11)**

## Key Points for Agents

- **SYSC 4.1.1R is the baseline governance Rule for all firms** — clear org structure, transparent/consistent lines of responsibility, effective risk identification/management/monitoring/reporting, internal controls, sound admin/accounting procedures, information system safeguards. This is not guidance; it is a hard Rule.
- **Common platform firms have six additional documented organisational requirements (SYSC 4.1.1-AR)** — documented org structure, internal controls, internal reporting/communication, orderly records, information security, and BCP. Plus mandatory monitoring and evaluation of the adequacy of these arrangements.
- **At least two persons must effectively direct the business, and two independent minds must be applied to all significant policies (SYSC 4.2.2R/4.2.4G).** A single dominant individual in a two-person management team raises doubts about compliance regardless of titles.
- **For common platform firms, the chair of the management body cannot simultaneously be the CEO (SYSC 4.3A.2R) — hard Rule, waivable only by FCA application.** The management body has seven mandatory functions including annual assessment of governance effectiveness and client service policy adequacy.
- **For non-common platform SMCR firms, SYSC 4.4 requires explicit allocation of two functions (apportionment and systems/controls oversight) to named individuals (SYSC 4.4.5R).** For a body corporate in a group, this must go to the chief executive or a group director/senior manager of equivalent or greater seniority. This is the non-Enhanced-firm equivalent of the SM&CR Responsibilities Map.

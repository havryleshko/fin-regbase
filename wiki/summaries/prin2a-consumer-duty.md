# PRIN 2A — The Consumer Duty

## Overview

PRIN 2A sets out the detailed requirements that together constitute the Consumer Duty. The Duty applies to firms within scope of Principle 12 ("A firm must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers") and consists of three cross-cutting rules and four outcome-specific rules. PRIN 2A also contains governance, distribution chain, and business-acquisition obligations.

The territorial and activity scope of Principle 12 — and therefore PRIN 2A — is set entirely by PRIN 3. See **[[prin3-rules-about-application]]**.

---

## PRIN 2A.1 Application

### Who Is Covered

PRIN 2A applies to every firm to which Principle 12 applies **(PRIN 2A.1.1R)**. Application is determined by PRIN 3:
- **Activity scope** (PRIN 3.2.6R–3.2.8R): Principle 12 applies only in connection with regulated activities addressed by rules in COBS, ICOBS, MCOB, BCOBS, CMCOB, FPCOB, PROD, or CONC.
- **Territorial scope** (PRIN 3.3.1R): Principle 12 applies where retail customers are *located in the UK*.
- **Distribution chains** (PRIN 3.2.7R): in a chain, P12 applies only to the extent the firm determines or materially influences retail customer outcomes.

### Who Is a Retail Customer

A "retail customer" for PRIN 2A purposes means a person who is **(PRIN 2A.1.2R)**:
- A "retail client" as defined in the Glossary, or
- A person whose habitual residence is in the UK and who is not acting in the course of a business or profession (for activities outside MiFID scope).

Guarantors and indemnifiers under regulated credit, consumer hire, or P2P agreements who are not themselves the borrower or hirer are also treated as retail customers for PRIN 2A purposes **(PRIN 3.4.3AR)** (see [[prin3-rules-about-application]]).

### Closed Products

PRIN 2A applies to closed products and services — that is, products or services that are no longer sold or renewed but where the firm continues to have ongoing dealings with retail customers — subject to proportionality adjustments at PRIN 2A.10 **(PRIN 2A.1.6R)**. See section on Closed Products below.

---

## PRIN 2A.2 The Cross-Cutting Rules

### The Three Rules

PRIN 2A.2 sets three cross-cutting obligations that apply across all activities within scope of P12 **(PRIN 2A.2.1R–2A.2.3R)**:

1. **Act in good faith**: A firm must act in good faith toward retail customers.
2. **Avoid foreseeable harm**: A firm must avoid causing foreseeable harm to retail customers.
3. **Enable financial objectives**: A firm must enable and support retail customers to pursue their financial objectives.

These rules apply independently of the four outcome rules and govern the firm's overall conduct.

### Meaning of "Act in Good Faith" (PRIN 2A.2.1R)

Acting in good faith means behaving honestly and openly in dealings with retail customers. It includes **(PRIN 2A.2.5G–2A.2.7G)**:
- Not pursuing the firm's own interests at the expense of retail customers.
- Not exploiting customers' behavioural biases, emotions, or circumstances against their interests.
- Being transparent about products, services, and fees.

A firm that designs products or communications to exploit customer inertia, complexity, or emotions fails the good faith standard even if technically complying with specific disclosure rules.

### Meaning of "Avoid Foreseeable Harm" (PRIN 2A.2.2R)

"Foreseeable harm" includes harm the firm could reasonably have anticipated at the time it took the action, even if it did not actually foresee it **(PRIN 2A.2.8G–2A.2.10G)**. The obligation requires firms to:
- Identify the potential for harm in product design, distribution, and servicing.
- Assess whether features, communications, or processes create foreseeable harm.
- Take pre-emptive steps rather than waiting for harm to materialise.

A firm must identify harm not only to its direct customers but also to end retail customers further down the distribution chain where the firm's actions influence those outcomes.

### Meaning of "Enable Financial Objectives" (PRIN 2A.2.3R)

A firm must design its products, services, and communications so that retail customers can realistically pursue their financial objectives — not merely make products theoretically available **(PRIN 2A.2.11G–2A.2.13G)**. This includes not designing processes that create barriers to switching, claiming, or cancelling.

### Cross-Cutting Rules Exhaust Principle 12

**PRIN 2A.2.26R** is a hard rule of structural importance:

> A firm that complies with the three cross-cutting rules and the four outcome rules is to be taken to have complied with Principle 12.

This means PRIN 2A.2–2A.6 together *exhaust* what Principle 12 requires. There is no residual Principle 12 obligation beyond those rules. Compliance assessment should focus on the specific PRIN 2A rules — not on Principle 12 text in the abstract.

## PRIN 2A.3 Products and Services Outcome

### The Rule

A firm must ensure that its products and services are designed to meet the needs, characteristics and objectives of customers in the target market, and are only distributed to that target market **(PRIN 2A.3.1R)**.

### Key Requirements

- **Target market identification**: Firms must identify a target market and ensure the product or service is appropriate for it **(PRIN 2A.3.2G)**.
- **Product testing**: Firms must test that products deliver the intended outcomes before and after launch **(PRIN 2A.3.3G)**.
- **Distribution strategy**: Firms must specify a distribution strategy that targets the right customers and excludes the wrong ones **(PRIN 2A.3.4G)**.
- **Ongoing review**: Products must be kept under review; firms must act where products are no longer meeting the needs of the target market **(PRIN 2A.3.5G)**.

Manufacturers and distributors have complementary but distinct obligations. Manufacturers set the target market and distribution strategy; distributors must act within them and report back **(PRIN 2A.3.6G)**. Detailed rules on product governance are in PROD.

---

## PRIN 2A.4 Price and Value Outcome

### The Rule

A firm must offer a product or service where the price represents fair value to retail customers in the target market **(PRIN 2A.4.1R)**.

### Fair Value Assessment

Fair value is not merely a price benchmark — it requires assessing the overall bundle of benefits and costs from the retail customer's perspective **(PRIN 2A.4.3G–2A.4.6G)**:
- The nature, quality and durability of the product or service.
- Whether the product or service meets the needs and objectives of the target market.
- The costs to the retail customer (direct and indirect).
- The quality of related services (e.g. claims handling in insurance, customer support).

**Cross-subsidy scrutiny**: A firm must not exploit retail customers who remain in existing products to fund lower prices for new customers, where the effect is that existing customers receive poor value **(PRIN 2A.4.7G)**.

**Third-party costs**: Where a firm pays or receives commissions or other payments from third parties, it must consider whether those arrangements distort the fair value delivered to retail customers **(PRIN 2A.4.9G)**.

---

## PRIN 2A.5 Consumer Understanding Outcome

### The Rule

A firm must ensure that its communications equip retail customers to make informed decisions **(PRIN 2A.5.1R)**. Communications must be:
- Timely — delivered at the right point in the customer journey.
- Clear — not obscured by complexity, length, or technical language.
- Tailored — where possible, to the circumstances and characteristics of the recipient.

### Behavioural Risks

Firms must not use communications that exploit customer biases or vulnerabilities — for example, sludge practices (excessive friction), urgency prompts, or pre-ticked boxes **(PRIN 2A.5.4G)**.

### Monitoring

Firms must test communications on retail customers or representatives of the target market to verify that they are understood **(PRIN 2A.5.5G)**. Testing is not aspirational; it is expected as part of the design process.

---

## PRIN 2A.6 Consumer Support Outcome

### The Rule

A firm must provide retail customers with support that meets their needs throughout the product or service lifecycle **(PRIN 2A.6.1R)**.

### Key Requirements

- Support must be as easy to access as sales or marketing channels **(PRIN 2A.6.3G)**.
- Firms must not use processes that create unreasonable friction — e.g. requiring customers to navigate multiple stages to make a claim or cancel a product **(PRIN 2A.6.4G)**.
- Support for vulnerable customers must be appropriate to their circumstances **(PRIN 2A.6.5G)**.
- Firms must monitor whether support is adequate by reference to customer outcomes, not just internal service standards **(PRIN 2A.6.6G)**.

---

## PRIN 2A.7 Monitoring and Assessment

### The Rule

A firm must monitor and regularly assess whether it is achieving the outcomes required by the Consumer Duty **(PRIN 2A.7.1R)**.

### What Monitoring Must Cover

Firms must **(PRIN 2A.7.2G–2A.7.5G)**:
- Collect and analyse data on outcomes across the four outcome areas.
- Identify root causes of poor outcomes, not just their incidence.
- Be able to demonstrate that outcomes are consistent across customer groups — including vulnerable customers and those with protected characteristics.
- Have clear escalation processes so that poor outcomes trigger management action.

The FCA expects firms to move from process-based to outcomes-based MI. Metrics such as complaints volumes and call resolution times are inputs, not evidence of good outcomes.

---

## PRIN 2A.8 Governance

### Annual Governing Body Review (Hard Rule)

**PRIN 2A.8.4R** is a hard rule:

> At least once a year, a firm's governing body must review and approve an assessment of whether the firm is delivering good outcomes for retail customers. The assessment must:
> 1. Cover all four outcome areas.
> 2. Identify any areas where outcomes are not meeting the required standard.
> 3. Set out the actions the governing body has agreed to take to improve outcomes.

The assessment must be formally approved — not merely noted. The governing body has personal accountability for the conclusion and the remediation plan.

### Consumer Duty Champion

The FCA expects each firm's governing body to appoint a Consumer Duty Champion — a non-executive director responsible for ensuring board-level scrutiny **(PRIN 2A.8.6G)**. This is guidance (G), not a hard rule.

### Management Information Requirements

Firms must provide the governing body with sufficient MI to discharge its oversight responsibilities. The FCA expects MI to include:
- Data on customer outcomes across the four outcome areas.
- Complaints data disaggregated by product, channel, and customer segment.
- Fair value assessment results.
- Results of communications testing **(PRIN 2A.8.3G)**.

---

## PRIN 2A.9 Distribution Chains

### The Scope of Obligations in a Chain

Where products or services are distributed through intermediaries, all firms in the distribution chain have Consumer Duty obligations — but only to the extent that they determine or materially influence retail customer outcomes (PRIN 3.2.7R). Manufacturers set the terms that limit distributor conduct; distributors implement and interact with retail customers directly.

### Information Sharing in the Chain

**PRIN 2A.9.4R** (hard rule): A firm must share information with other firms in the distribution chain that those firms reasonably need to comply with their own PRIN 2A obligations.

This is a positive duty — firms cannot cite commercial confidentiality to withhold information another firm needs to comply. Refusal to share required information is itself a PRIN 2A.9.4R breach.

### Monitoring Distributors

**PRIN 2A.9.6R** (hard rule): A manufacturer must obtain information from distributors to enable it to assess whether distribution is consistent with its target market determination, distribution strategy, and fair value assessment.

Manufacturers cannot disclaim responsibility for distributor conduct by pointing to contractual obligations. They must actively monitor outcomes.

### FCA Notification Duty (Hard Rule)

**PRIN 2A.9.17R** is a hard rule with no carve-outs:

> A firm in a distribution chain **must** notify the FCA if it becomes aware, or has reasonable grounds to believe, that another firm in that chain is or may not be complying with Principle 12 or PRIN 2A.

This obligation applies regardless of whether the non-compliant firm is the manufacturer, distributor, or any other intermediary. Awareness of another firm's potential non-compliance creates an immediate reporting obligation. This rule operates in parallel with — and does not substitute for — PRIN 11 (relations with regulators).

### Limiting Distributor Conduct

Where a manufacturer has set requirements on distributors (e.g. target market restrictions, price floors), distributors **must** comply with those requirements **(PRIN 2A.9.14R)**. A distributor that distributes outside the target market or overrides a pricing restriction breaches both PRIN 2A.9 and its own product governance obligations under PROD.

## PRIN 2A.10 Closed Products

### Application to Closed Business

The Consumer Duty applies to closed products and services — products and services no longer on sale to new customers — where the firm continues to provide services or have dealings with retail customers **(PRIN 2A.10.1R)**.

### Proportionality for Closed Books

What is required of firms with closed books is determined by the nature of the ongoing relationship. Key considerations **(PRIN 2A.10.3G)**:
- Whether the firm can materially change the product's features.
- Whether the firm retains control over pricing or terms.
- The ongoing harm potential from the product's features.

A firm cannot argue that a closed product is exempt merely because it cannot be varied — if retail customers continue to be harmed by its features, the Duty requires the firm to take whatever steps are available to it (e.g. informing customers, facilitating switching, initiating variation where possible).

---

## PRIN 2A.11 Purchase of Business Books

### Due Diligence Obligations (Hard Rule)

**PRIN 2A.11.4R**: When a firm acquires a portfolio of existing retail customer relationships from another firm, it must undertake due diligence before or at the time of purchase to understand:
1. The **vulnerability profile** of the retail customers in the portfolio.
2. The **outcome of product approval** for the products in the portfolio (i.e. whether products were properly approved under PROD, or equivalent).
3. The **fair value basis** on which the products were designed and priced.

Failure to conduct this due diligence is a hard-rule breach at the moment of acquisition, regardless of what is subsequently discovered about the portfolio.

### Post-Acquisition Obligations

Following acquisition, the purchasing firm steps into the shoes of the previous provider and assumes full Consumer Duty obligations for the acquired retail customers **(PRIN 2A.11.5G)**. Knowledge gaps identified in due diligence must be addressed promptly — a purchasing firm cannot rely on the absence of historical records to delay addressing poor outcomes.

---

## PRIN 2A.12 Retail Customer Relationships

The Consumer Duty governs the firm's overall relationship with retail customers — not merely individual transactions or products **(PRIN 2A.12.1G)**. This means:
- Firms must consider cumulative impacts on customers, not only the impact of individual products or communications.
- A pattern of individually compliant actions that cumulatively produce poor outcomes may still breach the Duty.
- The relationship lens is particularly significant in ongoing services such as investment management, insurance, and credit.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- **PRIN 2A.2.26R**: Compliance with the three cross-cutting rules (PRIN 2A.2.1R–2A.2.3R) and the four outcome rules (PRIN 2A.3–2A.6) *exhausts* what Principle 12 requires. There is no residual P12 obligation beyond these specific rules.
- **PRIN 2A.8.4R (hard rule)**: The governing body must at least annually review and approve a consumer outcomes assessment covering all four outcomes, identify gaps, and formally agree remediation. Approval must be recorded — noting is insufficient.
- **PRIN 2A.9.17R (hard rule)**: Any firm in a distribution chain that becomes aware that another firm in the chain may not be complying with P12/PRIN 2A *must* notify the FCA. No threshold, no materiality qualifier, no chain-confidentiality carve-out.
- **PRIN 2A.9.4R (hard rule)**: Firms must share information across the distribution chain that other firms need to comply with PRIN 2A. Commercial confidentiality does not override this obligation.
- **PRIN 2A.11.4R (hard rule)**: On any acquisition of a retail customer book, the purchasing firm must conduct due diligence into the vulnerability profile, product approval outcomes, and fair value basis of the acquired portfolio. This is a pre- or at-acquisition obligation.
- Closed products are in scope. A closed book is not an exemption — it narrows the options available to the firm but does not reduce the obligation to achieve good outcomes with those options.
- The Duty governs the cumulative relationship with retail customers, not just individual transactions. Individually compliant actions that produce poor aggregate outcomes may still breach the Duty (PRIN 2A.12.1G).

# COBS 9 — Suitability (non-MiFID and non-insurance-based investment products)

## Scope

COBS 9 applies to a firm which **(COBS 9.1.1R)**:
- Makes a **personal recommendation** to a retail client in relation to a **designated investment**
- **Manages investments** of a retail client of the firm
- Manages the assets of an occupational pension scheme, stakeholder pension scheme, or personal pension scheme (other than in relation to its MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business, or insurance-based investment products)

**COBS 9A** applies instead for: insurance-based investment products; and a firm's MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business involving investment advice or portfolio management.

**COBS 9B exclusion:** The rules in COBS 9 do not apply to a firm providing a targeted support service within the scope of COBS 9B **(COBS 9.1.1BR)**.

**P2P exclusion:** COBS 9 does not apply to a firm managing investments when it takes a decision to trade relating to a P2P agreement — the regulated activity of managing investments does not extend to assets that are P2P agreements **(COBS 9.1.3AG)**.

## Core Suitability Obligation

### The Three-Dimension Test (COBS 9.2.1R)

A firm must **(COBS 9.2.1R)**:
1. Take reasonable steps to ensure that a personal recommendation, or a decision to trade, is **suitable for its client**; and
2. Ensure that any life policy proposed is consistent with the client's insurance demands and needs.

When making the personal recommendation or managing investments, the firm must obtain the necessary information regarding the client's:
- **(a) Knowledge and experience** in the investment field relevant to the specific type of designated investment or service
- **(b) Financial situation**
- **(c) Investment objectives**

so as to enable the firm to make a recommendation suitable for the client.

### What Information Must Cover (COBS 9.2.2R)

The firm must obtain sufficient information to have a reasonable basis for believing that the specific transaction:
- **(a)** meets the client's investment objectives
- **(b)** is such that the client is financially able to bear any related investment risks consistent with those objectives
- **(c)** is such that the client has the necessary experience and knowledge to understand the risks involved

Investment objectives information must include, where relevant: time horizon, risk preferences, risk profile, and the purposes of the investment **(COBS 9.2.2R(2))**.

Financial situation information must include, where relevant: source and extent of regular income, assets (liquid, investments, real property), and regular financial commitments **(COBS 9.2.2R(3))**.

Knowledge and experience information includes: types of service, transaction and designated investment the client is familiar with; nature, volume, frequency of past transactions; level of education, profession, or relevant former profession **(COBS 9.2.3R)**.

### Prohibition on Discouraging Disclosure (COBS 9.2.4R)

A firm **must not** encourage a client not to provide information for the purposes of its suitability assessment **(COBS 9.2.4R)**. This is directly relevant to streamlined or robo-advice models that design friction-reduction into their onboarding flows.

### Reliance on Client Information (COBS 9.2.5R)

A firm is entitled to rely on information provided by its clients unless it is **aware** that the information is **manifestly out of date, inaccurate, or incomplete** **(COBS 9.2.5R)**. The duty is to use reasonable skill in assessment — not to independently verify every client-stated fact.

### No Information = No Recommendation (COBS 9.2.6R)

If a firm **does not obtain the necessary information** to assess suitability, it **must not** make a personal recommendation to the client or take a decision to trade for the client **(COBS 9.2.6R)**. This is a hard prohibition — there is no discretion. Where a client declines to provide sufficient information, the firm must decline to advise (though the client may still ask the firm to execute on a non-advised basis, subject to the appropriateness rules in COBS 10 and the insistent client regime in COBS 9.5A).

## Guidance on Assessing Suitability

### Transaction-Level vs. Portfolio-Level Suitability (COBS 9.3.1G–9.3.2G)

A transaction may be unsuitable because of the risks of the designated investment involved, the type of transaction, the characteristics of the order, or the frequency of trading **(COBS 9.3.1G)**.

**Churning (COBS 9.3.2G):** A series of transactions that are each suitable when viewed in isolation may be **unsuitable** if the recommendation or decisions to trade are made at a frequency that is not in the best interests of the client. Portfolio-level suitability is a distinct obligation from individual transaction suitability — a firm must have regard to the client's agreed investment strategy in determining transaction frequency.

### Investments Subject to Retail Distribution Restrictions (COBS 9.3.5G)

Where an investment is subject to a restriction on retail distribution (e.g. non-mass market investments under s.238 of the Act, restricted mass market investments, contingent convertibles/CoCo funds), a firm should be satisfied that an exemption is available before recommending that investment to a retail client. A personal recommendation to invest will generally incorporate a financial promotion **(COBS 9.3.5G)**.

For discretionary managers: where a restriction on promotion applies, purchasing a restricted investment on behalf of a retail client should be supported by detailed and robust justification of the suitability assessment **(COBS 9.3.5G(3)(b))**.

## Suitability Reports

### When a Suitability Report Is Required (COBS 9.4.1R)

A firm must provide a suitability report to a retail client if the firm makes a personal recommendation to the client and the client:

1. Acquires a holding in, or sells all or part of a holding in: (a) a regulated collective investment scheme; (b) an investment trust where shares are to be acquired through an investment trust savings scheme; (c) an investment trust where shares are to be held within an ISA promoted as the means for investing in specific investment trusts; OR
2. Buys, sells, surrenders, converts or cancels rights under, or suspends contributions to, a personal pension scheme or stakeholder pension scheme; OR
3. Elects to make income withdrawals, an uncrystallised funds pension lump sum payment, or purchase a short-term annuity; OR
4. Enters into a pension opt-out.

A suitability report is also required for personal recommendations in relation to life policies **(COBS 9.4.2R)**.

For pension transfers or pension conversions, the firm must additionally provide a **one-page summary** at the front of the suitability report **(COBS 9.4.2AR)**.

### When a Suitability Report Is NOT Required (COBS 9.4.3R)

The obligation does not apply: (1) where the firm acting as investment manager makes a recommendation relating to a regulated collective investment scheme; (2) where the client is habitually resident outside the UK and not present in the UK at the time of acknowledging consent; (4) where the recommendation is to increase a regular premium to an existing contract; (5) where the recommendation is to invest additional single premiums to an existing packaged product to which a single premium has previously been paid.

### Timing (COBS 9.4.4R)

The suitability report must be provided:
- **Life policy:** before the contract is concluded
- **Personal pension scheme / stakeholder pension scheme** (not a life policy, where cancellation rights apply): no later than **14 days** after the contract is concluded
- **Pension transfer or pension conversion:** in good time **before** the transaction is effected
- **Any other case:** when or as soon as possible after the transaction is effected or executed

### Mandatory Content (COBS 9.4.7R)

The suitability report must, at least:
1. **Specify** the client's demands and needs, on the basis of information obtained from the client
2. **Explain** why the firm has concluded that the recommended transaction is suitable for the client, having regard to information provided by the client
3. **Explain any possible disadvantages** of the transaction for the client
4. For a life policy: include a personalised recommendation explaining why a particular life policy would best meet the client's demands and needs

Details must be modulated according to the complexity of the transaction or contract and the type of client **(COBS 9.4.8R)**.

### Pension Transfer One-Page Summary (COBS 9.4.11R)

For pension transfer or pension conversion recommendations, the one-page summary at the front of the suitability report must include: a summary of the recommendation; whether it is abridged or full pension transfer advice; ongoing charges information (in cash terms, alongside the ceding arrangement charges and any default arrangement charges); risks associated with the transfer; and an invitation to the client to confirm they understand those risks.

## Record Keeping

### Retention Periods (COBS 9.5.2R)

A firm must retain its suitability records for a minimum of:

| Recommendation type | Minimum retention period |
|--------------------|-------------------------|
| Pension transfer, pension conversion, pension opt-out, or FSAVC | **Indefinitely** |
| Life policy, personal pension scheme, stakeholder pension scheme, or benefits in a defined contribution occupational pension scheme | **5 years** |
| Any other case | **3 years** |

**No retention required** if the client does not proceed with the recommendation **(COBS 9.5.3R)**.

## Insistent Clients (COBS 9.5A)

### Who Is an Insistent Client?

A client should be considered an insistent client where **(COBS 9.5A.2G)**:
1. The firm has given the client a personal recommendation
2. The client decides to enter into a transaction which is **different** from that recommended by the firm
3. The client wishes the firm to **facilitate** that transaction

### What the Firm Must Communicate (COBS 9.5A.3G)

Where a firm proceeds to execute a transaction for an insistent client which is not in accordance with the firm's personal recommendation, the firm should communicate to the insistent client, clearly, fairly and not misleadingly:
- **(a)** That the firm has not recommended the transaction and it will not be in accordance with the firm's personal recommendation
- **(b)** The reasons why the transaction will not be in accordance with the personal recommendation
- **(c)** The risks of the transaction proposed by the insistent client
- **(d)** The reasons why the firm did not recommend that transaction to the client

### Acknowledgement Requirement (COBS 9.5A.4G)

The firm should obtain from the insistent client an acknowledgement that:
- The transaction is not in accordance with the firm's personal recommendation
- The transaction is being carried out at the request of the client

Where possible, the acknowledgement should be in the **client's own words**.

Where the firm gives a further personal recommendation in relation to the insistent client's proposed transaction, it should make clear that this is distinct from the original recommendation and does not affect its conclusions **(COBS 9.5A.5G)**.

### Record Keeping for Insistent Client Transactions (COBS 9.5A.6G)

The firm should retain a record of:
- The advice and transaction process followed, including all communications with the client
- The acknowledgement from the client

These records are subject to the retention periods in COBS 9.5 and SYSC 9.1.

## Basic Advice on Stakeholder Products (COBS 9.6)

A firm may choose to give **basic advice** on a stakeholder product to a retail client instead of the full COBS 9 suitability assessment **(COBS 9.1.2R)**. Basic advice is a simplified, scripted process:

- The firm may only advise from within a defined range of stakeholder products **(COBS 9.6.9R)**
- Basic advice uses pre-scripted questions to assess the client's answers
- The firm must not recommend a product outside its defined range, recommend fund choice within a product, or recommend contributions above ISA limits **(COBS 9.6.10R)**
- At first contact, initial disclosure information (COBS 9 Annex 1) must be provided in a durable medium **(COBS 9.6.5R)**
- Remuneration structures must not incentivise representatives to give unsuitable basic advice **(COBS 9.6.18R)**
- Records must be retained for at least 5 years from the date of the basic advice **(COBS 9.6.19R)**

## Key Points for Agents

- **COBS 9 scope**: applies to personal recommendations to retail clients in relation to designated investments, and to discretionary investment management of retail clients. Does NOT apply to MiFID/insurance-based investment products (those fall under COBS 9A), or to targeted support services (COBS 9B). **(COBS 9.1.1R)**
- **Three dimensions of suitability (COBS 9.2.1R–9.2.2R):** knowledge and experience; financial situation (ability to bear risk); investment objectives (risk tolerance, time horizon, purpose). All three must be assessed. File evidence of all three is required — inadequate documentation is itself a breach regardless of whether the advice was suitable.
- **No information = no recommendation (COBS 9.2.6R).** If the firm cannot obtain necessary information, it must not make a personal recommendation or take a decision to trade. No carve-out, no discretion. A firm must not encourage clients not to provide information (COBS 9.2.4R). The firm may rely on client-provided information unless manifestly wrong (COBS 9.2.5R).
- **Churning (COBS 9.3.2G):** Individually suitable transactions can collectively be unsuitable if traded at a frequency not in the client's best interests. Portfolio-level suitability is a distinct obligation.
- **Suitability report mandatory content (COBS 9.4.7R):** must specify client demands/needs; explain why the recommendation is suitable having regard to client information; explain possible disadvantages. For pension transfers, a one-page summary at the front is additionally required. Timing: before conclusion for life policies; by day 14 for pension schemes without cancellation rights; before execution for pension transfers; as soon as possible after execution in other cases.
- **Record retention tiers (COBS 9.5.2R): pension transfer/conversion/opt-out/FSAVC → indefinitely; life policy/personal pension/stakeholder pension/DC occupational → 5 years; all other → 3 years.** No retention needed if the client does not proceed.
- **Insistent client regime (COBS 9.5A):** Where a client insists on a transaction different from the firm's recommendation, the firm must communicate: the transaction is not recommended; why; the risks; why the firm didn't recommend it. Obtain a client acknowledgement, preferably in the client's own words. Record the full advice and transaction process. This is guidance (G), not a hard rule, but departure from it creates significant FOS risk.

# CASS 6 — Custody Rules

## Scope and Application

CASS 6 applies to a firm that:

- Holds or controls safe custody assets in the course of **MiFID business** **(CASS 6.1.1R(1A))**;
- Holds or controls safe custody assets in the course of **safeguarding and administering investments** that is not MiFID business **(CASS 6.1.1R(1B))**;
- Acts as **trustee or depositary of an AIF** and holds or controls safe custody assets belonging to the AIF **(CASS 6.1.1R(1C))**;
- Acts as **trustee or depositary of a UCITS** and holds or controls safe custody assets belonging to the UCITS **(CASS 6.1.1R(1D))**; or
- Has entered into a **TTCA** with a client in respect of assets that would otherwise be subject to CASS 6 **(CASS 6.1.1R(1E))** — see below.

**Key definitions:**
- **Safe custody assets** = financial instruments and other designated investments held for clients (broader than safe custody investments, which are specifically MiFID financial instruments).
- **Safe custody investments** = a subset of safe custody assets: MiFID financial instruments held in the course of MiFID business.

### Exemptions from CASS 6

| Exemption | Rule | Condition |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Personal investment firms, trustee/bare trustee firms | CASS 6.1.2R | Firm does not hold the assets in the course of investment business |
| Arrangers and introducers | CASS 6.1.5R | Firm merely introduces or arranges but does not hold or control the assets |
| Delivery-versus-payment transactions | CASS 6.1.12R | Settlement in one business day; DvP conditions met |

## Title Transfer Collateral Arrangements (TTCAs)

### Retail client ban — absolute Rule

**CASS 6.1.6R:** A firm must not enter into a TTCA with a **retail client** in relation to assets that would otherwise be safe custody assets. This prohibition is absolute — it cannot be dis-applied by consent or disclosure.

### Non-retail conditions

For non-retail clients, a TTCA is permitted only if the firm has:
1. **Documented the arrangement** — the TTCA must be clearly set out in the agreement with the client;
2. **Assessed proportionality** — the firm must assess whether the TTCA is proportionate in the context of the client relationship; and
3. **Disclosed risks** — the firm must inform the client of the risks arising from the use of a TTCA, including the loss of ownership rights and exposure to the firm's insolvency.

Where a TTCA exists, CASS 6.1.1R(1E) still applies — the firm must comply with CASS 6 in respect of the TTCA assets to the extent required by the rules.

## Section 6.2 — Holding of Safe Custody Assets

### Core Protection Obligation

**CASS 6.2.1R:** A firm must make adequate arrangements to **safeguard clients' ownership rights**, in particular in the event of the firm's insolvency, and to **prevent the use of a client's safe custody assets on the firm's own account** except with the client's express consent.

**CASS 6.2.2R:** A firm must maintain adequate organisational arrangements to minimise the risk of the loss or diminution of clients' safe custody assets, or of rights in connection with those assets, as a result of misuse, fraud, poor administration, inadequate record-keeping, or negligence.

### Registration

**CASS 6.2.3R:** A firm must, as soon as practicable, register or record legal title to a client's safe custody asset in the name of:
- The **client**;
- A **nominee company** controlled by the firm (where the firm has evidenced that this is in the client's best interests); or
- A **third party** (where the firm has evidenced that this is in the client's best interests).

A firm may register safe custody assets in its own name only where: (a) the nature of the safe custody asset or of the investment service requires it; and (b) the assets are otherwise clearly identifiable as belonging to clients and separately identified from the firm's own assets in the firm's records.

**CASS 6.2.5R:** Where a firm holds its own assets in the same accounts or records as client assets, the firm must ensure that those assets are separately identifiable from the client assets at all times.

### Unclaimed Safe Custody Assets

**CASS 6.2.10R:** Where a safe custody asset has been held by a firm for a client for a period of at least **12 years** without any movement, and the firm has been unable to trace the client despite taking reasonable steps, the firm may:

1. Liquidate the asset and pay the proceeds to a **registered charity** of its choice; and
2. Cease to treat the proceeds as client assets.

**However**, the firm must retain an **unconditional undertaking** to pay an amount equal to the value of the proceeds to any person who subsequently establishes a valid claim to those assets. This obligation is perpetual — it does not expire when the charity payment is made.

**CASS 6.2.14R / 6.2.15R:** The firm must retain records relating to unclaimed assets **indefinitely** (no five-year cap applies).

**CASS 6.2.16G:** All costs associated with divesting unclaimed assets — including carrying out the steps in 6.2.10R(4) and the cost of any insurance — must be paid from the **firm's own funds**, not from client assets.

## Section 6.3 — Depositing Safe Custody Assets with Third Parties

### Third-Party Due Diligence Obligation

**CASS 6.3.1R:** A firm may deposit safe custody assets held for its clients with a third party, but **only if** it exercises **all due skill, care and diligence** in the:
- Selection and appointment of the third party; and
- Periodic review of the third party and of the arrangements for holding and safekeeping.

When conducting selection, appointment, and review, the firm must take into account **(CASS 6.3.1R(3))**:
- (a) The expertise and market reputation of the third party; and
- (b) Any legal requirements related to the holding of those safe custody assets that could adversely affect clients' rights.

**CASS 6.3.2G** — additional factors to consider in discharge of 6.3.1R include: the third party's performance; safeguarding arrangements; market practices that could adversely affect client rights; industry standard assurance reports (e.g. AAF 01/06); capital and financial resources; credit-worthiness; other activities (including affiliated companies); and regulatory permissions.

### Record-Keeping for Third-Party Selection

**CASS 6.3.2A R:**
1. A firm must make a **record of the grounds** on which it was satisfied as to the appropriateness of its selection and appointment of a third party. The record must be made on the date of selection/appointment and retained until **five years after the firm ceases to use that third party**.
2. A firm must make a **record of each periodic review**, including the firm's considerations and conclusions. The record must be retained until five years after the firm ceases to use that third party.

### Jurisdiction Restriction

**CASS 6.3.4R(1):** A firm must only deposit safe custody assets with a third party in a **jurisdiction that specifically regulates and supervises the safekeeping of safe custody assets** for the account of another person, and where the third party is subject to such regulation.

**CASS 6.3.4R(2):** A firm must **not** deposit safe custody assets held on behalf of a client with a third party in a **third country** which does not regulate such safekeeping, **unless**:
- (a) The nature of the safe custody assets or of the investment services connected with those assets **requires** them to be deposited in that third country; or
- (b) The safe custody assets are held on behalf of a **professional client** and that client **requests in writing** that they be deposited with a third party in that third country.

**CASS 6.3.4R(4):** These jurisdiction requirements also apply when the third party has delegated any of its holding and safekeeping functions to another third party (chain of custody).

### Written Third-Party Custody Agreement

**CASS 6.3.4A R:** A firm must have entered into a **written agreement** with any third party with whom it deposits clients' safe custody assets. The agreement must, at a minimum:
1. Set out the binding terms of the arrangement between the firm and the third party;
2. Be in force for the duration of the arrangement; and
3. Clearly set out the custody service(s) the third party is contracted to provide.

**CASS 6.3.4B G** sets out additional terms that should be addressed where relevant, including: title indicators on the third party's books; segregation of client assets from the firm's assets and the third party's own assets; registration arrangements; withdrawal restrictions; instruction procedures; entitlement claim procedures; and liability provisions for fraud/default/negligence of the third party.

### Identifiability at Third Party

**CASS 6.3.4A-1R:** A firm must take the necessary steps to ensure that any client's safe custody assets deposited with a third party are **identifiable separately** from both the firm's own applicable assets and the third party's own applicable assets — by means of differently titled accounts on the books of the third party or other equivalent measures achieving the same level of protection.

### Security Interests and Liens — Prohibition

**CASS 6.3.6A R(1):** A firm must **not grant any security interest, lien or right of set-off** to another person over clients' safe custody assets in order to recover debts, **unless**:
- (a) Those debts relate to:
  - (i) One or more of the firm's clients; or
  - (ii) The provision of services by that other person to one or more of the firm's clients; or
- (b) To the extent those debts relate to anything else, then:
  - (i) The security interest, lien or right of set-off is **required by applicable law in a third country** in which the safe custody assets are held;
  - (ii) The firm **discloses** information to the client so that the client is informed of the associated risks; **and**
  - (iii) The firm has taken reasonable steps to determine that holding those safe custody assets subject to the security interest, lien or right of set-off is **in the best interests of the firm's clients**.

**CASS 6.3.6A R(2):** Where security interests, liens or rights of set-off are granted or the firm has been informed they are granted, these must be **recorded in client contracts** and the firm's own books and records to make the ownership status of safe custody assets clear, including in the event of insolvency.

**CASS 6.3.9R:** CASS 6.3.6A R does not permit a firm to agree to a third party having any recourse or right against client money in a client bank account or client transaction account (as defined in CASS 7 Annex 2R, 3R, 4R).

## Section 6.4 — Use of Safe Custody Assets

### SFT Consent Requirement — Hard Rule

**CASS 6.4.1R(1):** A firm must **not** enter into arrangements for **securities financing transactions** (SFTs) in respect of safe custody assets held by it on behalf of a client, or otherwise use such safe custody assets for its own account or the account of any other person or client, **unless**:
- (a) The client has given **express prior consent** to the use of the safe custody assets **on specified terms**; and
- (b) The use of the client's safe custody assets is **restricted to the specified terms** to which the client consents.

**CASS 6.4.1R(2) — omnibus accounts:** A firm must not use safe custody assets held in an omnibus account at a third party unless, in addition to the conditions in (1):
- (a) Each client whose assets are held together has given express prior consent; or
- (b) The firm has systems and controls that ensure only assets of clients who have given express prior consent are used.

**CASS 6.4.1R(3) — consent formalities:** Express prior consent must be **clearly evidenced in writing** and the **signature of the client or an equivalent alternative means of affirmative execution** is required. A consent buried in general terms and conditions without separate acknowledgement is unlikely to be sufficient.

**CASS 6.4.1A G:** The FCA expects firms entering into arrangements under CASS 6.4.1R with **retail clients** to only enter into SFTs and **not otherwise use retail clients' safe custody assets**. Use beyond SFTs for retail clients would contradict the FCA's expectations under the client's best interests rule.

### Collateral Obligations for SFTs

**CASS 6.4.2G:** For SFTs involving retail clients, the FCA expects that:
1. The firm ensures that **relevant collateral** is provided by the borrower in favour of the client;
2. The **current realisable value** of the safe custody asset and the relevant collateral is **monitored daily**; and
3. The firm provides relevant collateral to **make up any shortfall** where the collateral value falls below the safe custody asset value, unless otherwise agreed in writing by the client.

**CASS 6.4.2A R:** A firm must adopt specific arrangements for all clients to ensure that the borrower of client safe custody assets provides **appropriate collateral** and that the firm **monitors the continued appropriateness** of such collateral, taking the necessary steps to maintain the balance with the value of the client safe custody assets.

### Record-Keeping for SFT Use

**CASS 6.4.3R:** Where a firm uses safe custody assets as permitted under CASS 6.4, the firm's records must include:
- Details of the **client** on whose instructions the use was effected; and
- The **number of safe custody assets used** belonging to each client who has given consent.

This enables correct allocation of any loss in the event that not all clients gave consent.

## Section 6.6 — Records, Accounts and Reconciliations

### Core Record-Keeping Obligations

**CASS 6.6.2R:** A firm must keep such records and accounts as are necessary to enable it at any time and **without delay** to distinguish safe custody assets held for one client from those held for any other client, and from the firm's own applicable assets.

**CASS 6.6.3R:** Records must be maintained in a way that ensures their **accuracy and correspondence** to the safe custody assets held for clients, and must be capable of use as an **audit trail**.

**CASS 6.6.4R:** A firm must maintain a **client-specific safe custody asset record** — a record separately maintained for each client showing the safe custody assets held for that client.

**CASS 6.6.6R:** A firm must keep a copy of every executed client agreement that includes the firm's right to use safe custody assets for its own account (CASS 6.4.1R agreements) — including, in the case of a prime brokerage agreement, the disclosure annex referred to in CASS 9.3.1R.

**CASS 6.6.7R — General retention period:** Unless otherwise stated, records made under CASS 6 must be retained for **five years** from the later of (a) the date the record was created and (b) if modified since creation, the date of the most recent modification.

### Three-Tier Reconciliation Regime

CASS 6.6 establishes three separate and mandatory reconciliation processes. Each has its own purpose, methodology, and hard frequency cap.

#### Tier 1 — Internal Custody Record Check

**CASS 6.6.11R:** A firm must perform an internal custody record check:
- **(1)(a)** As regularly as is necessary but **without allowing more than one month** to pass between each check; and
- **(1)(b)** As soon as reasonably practicable after the date to which the check relates.

**CASS 6.6.13R:** A firm must perform the internal custody record check using **either** the *internal custody reconciliation method* **or** the *internal system evaluation method*. It must **not** use a combination of these methods.

**CASS 6.6.14R:** A firm must only use its **own internal records** (depot and client-specific ledgers, accounting records) to perform an internal custody record check. Third-party statements or records cannot be used for this purpose **(CASS 6.6.15G)**.

**Internal custody reconciliation method (CASS 6.6.16R–6.6.17R):** Available only if the firm separately maintains both an aggregate safe custody asset record and a client-specific safe custody asset record, and these records are capable of being compared. The check requires a comparison between the two as at the date of the check.

**Internal system evaluation method (CASS 6.6.19R):** Available to any firm, including those that cannot use the reconciliation method. Requires: (a) a process that evaluates the completeness and accuracy of the firm's internal records and whether systems and controls correctly identify and resolve all discrepancies; and (b) running the evaluation on the date of each check and promptly investigating and resolving any causes of discrepancies found.

**After failure (CASS 6.6.10A R / 6.6.46A R):** Normal frequency rules do not apply following a firm's failure — see Section 6.6.46A R below.

#### Tier 2 — Physical Asset Reconciliation

**CASS 6.6.22R:** A firm that holds **physical safe custody assets** must perform a physical asset reconciliation for all such assets:
- As regularly as is necessary but **without allowing more than six months** to pass between each reconciliation; and
- As soon as reasonably practicable after the date to which it relates.

**CASS 6.6.24R:** A physical asset reconciliation requires: (a) a count of all physical safe custody assets held as at the reconciliation date; and (b) a comparison of that count against what the firm's internal records state as being in the firm's possession at the same date.

**CASS 6.6.25R:** The reconciliation must use either the **total count method** (single-stage comprehensive count) or the **rolling stock method** (two or more stages covering different stock lines, all completed within the six-month cap).

**CASS 6.6.30R:** A firm wishing to use the rolling stock method must first **establish and document in writing** its reasons for concluding that its reconciliation approach adequately mitigates the risk of manipulation or falsification. This documentation must be retained for at least five years after ceasing to use the rolling stock method.

#### Tier 3 — External Custody Reconciliation

**CASS 6.6.34R:** A firm must conduct, **on a regular basis**, reconciliations between its internal records of safe custody assets held for clients and those of **any third parties by whom those safe custody assets are held**.

**CASS 6.6.37R:** A firm must conduct external custody reconciliations:
- As regularly as is necessary but **allowing no more than one month** to pass between each external custody reconciliation; and
- As soon as reasonably practicable after the date to which the reconciliation relates.

**CASS 6.6.35R:** The third parties whose records a firm must reconcile against include:
1. Third parties with which the firm has deposited clients' safe custody assets;
2. Where the firm has not deposited an asset with a third party: (a) the third party responsible for registration of legal title, or (b) a person acting as operator of a relevant overseas USR (uncertificated securities register) in specified circumstances.

**CASS 6.6.42G:** External custody reconciliations must be performed for each safe custody asset, except physical safe custody assets. A reconciliation of transactions involving safe custody assets (i.e., a transaction-based check) does not satisfy the external reconciliation requirement — it must be a reconciliation of the assets themselves.

**CASS 6.6.39G:** Best practice where a central securities depositary can provide daily holdings information is to conduct external custody reconciliations each **business day** in respect of those assets.

### Determining Reconciliation Frequency

**CASS 6.6.44R:** When determining how frequently to conduct each of the three types of reconciliation, the firm must have regard to:
1. The **frequency, number and value** of transactions in respect of clients' safe custody assets; and
2. The **risks to which clients' safe custody assets are exposed** — including the nature, volume and complexity of the firm's business and where and with whom the assets are held.

**CASS 6.6.45R:** A firm must make and retain records sufficient to explain its decisions on frequency. Records must be retained **indefinitely** (unless superseded by a subsequent decision, in which case the earlier record need only be retained for a further five years).

**CASS 6.6.46R:** A firm must review the frequency of its reconciliation activities **at least annually** to ensure continued compliance, and must record the date and actions taken in each review.

### Reconciliations After Failure

**CASS 6.6.46A R:** Following a firm's **failure** (i.e. insolvency or other failure event), the firm must:
- Perform an internal custody record check and a physical asset reconciliation relating to the time of its failure **as soon as reasonably practicable after its failure**;
- Perform an external custody reconciliation relating to the time of its failure as soon as reasonably practicable (if records are unavailable, use the next available records);
- Perform further internal custody record checks and physical asset reconciliations **as regularly as necessary** to maintain compliance with records accuracy obligations; and
- Perform further external custody reconciliations on a regular basis.

### Independence of Person Performing Reconciliations

**CASS 6.6.47G:** Whenever possible, a firm should ensure that checks and reconciliations are carried out by a **person independent** of the production or maintenance of the records being checked and/or reconciled.

### Resolution of Discrepancies

**CASS 6.6.48G:** A discrepancy is not considered **resolved** until it is fully investigated and corrected, and any associated shortfall is made good by the firm ensuring it is holding each of the safe custody assets it ought to be holding for each client, and that its own records and the records of any relevant third party accurately correspond to that position.

**CASS 6.6.49R:** When a firm identifies a discrepancy as a result of a reconciliation, the firm must:
1. **Promptly investigate** the reason for the discrepancy and resolve it **without undue delay**; and
2. Take appropriate steps under CASS 6.6.54R for the treatment of any **shortfalls** until the discrepancy is resolved.

**CASS 6.6.50R:** When a discrepancy is identified outside the reconciliation processes, the firm must take all reasonable steps to investigate and resolve it, and apply CASS 6.6.54R for shortfall treatment.

Items in suspense or error accounts **(CASS 6.6.52G)** and liquidated stocks no longer recorded by third parties **(CASS 6.6.53G)** fall within the scope of discrepancies requiring investigation.

### Treatment of Shortfalls

**CASS 6.6.54R:** Where a firm identifies a discrepancy that reveals a **shortfall** which it has not yet resolved, until the discrepancy is resolved the firm must do **one** of the following:

**(a)** Appropriate a sufficient number of its own **applicable assets** to cover the value of the shortfall and hold them for the relevant clients under the custody rules, such that those assets (or the proceeds of their liquidation) will be available for distribution in the event of the firm's failure — and keep a record including: description of shortfall, affected clients, assets appropriated (updated on resolution); or

**(b)** Appropriate a sufficient amount of its own **money** to cover the value of the shortfall, hold it for the relevant clients as client money under CASS 7, and keep a corresponding record; or

**(c)** A combination of (a) and (b) sufficient in aggregate to cover the shortfall value.

**CASS 6.6.54R(3):** Where the firm concludes (even tentatively) that another person is responsible for the discrepancy, the firm must still take all reasonable steps to resolve the situation without undue delay, and must consider whether it would be appropriate to **notify the affected client** of the situation.

**CASS 6.6.56G:** The value of a shortfall is determined by the **previous day's closing market valuation** or the most recently available valuation. Where the firm is taking shortfall coverage measures, it must review the value as regularly as necessary and adjust the coverage to reflect any change in shortfall value.

### FCA Notification Obligation

**CASS 6.6.57R:** A firm must **inform the FCA in writing without delay** if:
1. Its internal records and accounts of safe custody assets are materially out of date, materially inaccurate, or invalid — such that the firm is no longer able to comply with CASS 6.6.2R–6.6.4R;
2. (For AIF trustee/depositary) The firm has not complied with, or is materially unable to comply with, CASS 6.6.2R or the relevant AIFMD level 2 regulation safekeeping obligations;
3. (For UCITS trustee/depositary) The firm has not complied with, or is materially unable to comply with, CASS 6.6.2R or the relevant UCITS level 2 regulation safekeeping obligations;
4. It will be unable, or materially fails, to take the steps required under CASS 6.6.54R for the treatment of shortfalls;
5. It will be unable, or materially fails, to conduct an internal custody record check in compliance with CASS 6.6.11R–6.6.19R;
6. It will be unable, or materially fails, to conduct a physical asset reconciliation in compliance with CASS 6.6.22R–6.6.30R; or
7. It will be unable, or materially fails, to conduct an external custody reconciliation in compliance with CASS 6.6.34R–6.6.37R.

### Annual Audit of Compliance

**CASS 6.6.58G:** The auditor of the firm must confirm in the report submitted to the FCA under **SUP 3.10** (Duties of auditors: notification and report on client assets) that the firm has maintained systems adequate to enable it to comply with the custody rules.

## Section 6.7 — Treatment of Custody Assets After a Failure

### Application

**CASS 6.7.1R:** This section applies to a firm following its **failure** (insolvency or other failure event under the IBSA Regulations).

### Disposal of Safe Custody Assets

**CASS 6.7.2R:** Before taking any steps to **dispose** of a safe custody asset, a failed firm must:
- (a) Attempt to **return the asset to the relevant client** or transfer it to another person for safekeeping on behalf of the client in accordance with CASS 6.7.8R; and
- (b) Take reasonable steps to **notify the client** of the firm's proposed course of action for disposing of the asset.

Exceptions: the return attempt is not required where the client has confirmed it disclaims all interests in the asset **(CASS 6.7.2R(2))**; notification is not required where the firm is able to return or transfer the asset, or where the client has disclaimed **(CASS 6.7.2R(3))**.

**CASS 6.7.4E — Reasonable steps for notification:** A firm is expected to:
1. Determine the correct contact details for the client (using all available means including internal records, public records, mortality screening, credit reference agencies, tracing agents);
2. Write to the client at its last known address informing it of the intended disposal and inviting a claim;
3. If no response within 28 days, attempt to communicate again via a different channel; and
4. For professional clients, use the same procedure and repeat again if no response after the second communication.

**CASS 6.7.7R:** A firm must make a **record** of any safe custody asset disposed of under CASS 6.7.2R, including: the asset, the value of consideration received, the client's name and contact details, and the efforts made to determine contact details. This record must be kept **indefinitely**.

### Transfers to Another Firm

**CASS 6.7.8R:** Where a failed firm (Firm A) is able to transfer a safe custody asset to another person (Firm B) for safekeeping on behalf of the client rather than returning it directly, Firm A may do so only if, in advance of the transfer, it has obtained a **contractual undertaking** from Firm B that:
- (a) Firm B will return the asset to the client at the client's request; and
- (b) Firm B will **notify the client within 14 days** of the transfer of:
  - (i) The applicable regulatory regime under which the safe custody asset will be held by Firm B;
  - (ii) Either any relevant compensation scheme limits that may apply to Firm B's handling of the asset, or the fact that Firm B does not participate in a relevant compensation scheme; and
  - (iii) Where applicable, that the client has the option to have the asset returned to it by Firm B.

## Key Points for Agents

- **TTCAs are absolutely prohibited with retail clients (CASS 6.1.6R).** For non-retail clients, the arrangement must be documented, proportionality assessed, and risks disclosed. There is no waiver or exemption from the retail ban.
- **SFTs using client safe custody assets require express written consent with affirmative execution (CASS 6.4.1R).** The consent must be clearly evidenced in writing and separately signed or executed. For retail clients, only SFTs are a permitted use — no other use of retail client assets is expected by the FCA.
- **Security interests, liens and rights of set-off over client safe custody assets are prohibited unless conditions are met (CASS 6.3.6A R).** The permitted cases are narrow: debts relating to the firm's own clients, or a mandatory third-country law requirement (with disclosure and best interests assessment). A custodian cannot pledge client assets to cover its own debts.
- **Third-party deposit requires a regulated jurisdiction (CASS 6.3.4R).** Deposit with a third party in a jurisdiction that does not specifically regulate safekeeping is prohibited except in narrow circumstances (nature requires it; or professional client written request). This restriction also applies through chains of custody.
- **Three separate mandatory reconciliation processes with hard frequency caps (CASS 6.6):**
  - Internal custody record checks: **max 1 month** (CASS 6.6.11R)
  - External custody reconciliations: **max 1 month** (CASS 6.6.37R)
  - Physical asset reconciliations: **max 6 months** (CASS 6.6.22R)
- **Shortfall coverage from own assets is mandatory and immediate (CASS 6.6.54R).** When a reconciliation reveals a shortfall, the firm must appropriate its own assets (or money held as client money under CASS 7) to cover the shortfall value, held for affected clients, until the discrepancy is resolved. The firm cannot leave clients exposed while investigating.
- **FCA must be notified without delay if the firm cannot maintain records or cannot conduct any of the three reconciliation processes (CASS 6.6.57R).** Six separate notification triggers; each is independent.
- **Unclaimed assets after 12 years may be divested to charity, but the firm must retain a perpetual unconditional undertaking to repay any valid claimant (CASS 6.2.10R).** All divestment costs come from the firm's own funds.

# CONC 7 — Arrears, Default and Recovery (including Repossessions)

## What This Document Is

CONC 7 sets out how firms must treat customers who are in or approaching arrears or in default. It applies to consumer credit lenders, consumer hiring firms, P2P platform operators (in relation to borrowers), and debt collectors **(CONC 7.1.1R)**. CONC 7.17–7.19 apply only to P2P platform operators.

The chapter contains 19 sections covering: arrears policies, forbearance standards, debt collection conduct, CPA rules, interest and charge controls, contact rules, mental capacity obligations, disputed and statute barred debts, lender responsibilities in debt assignment, data accuracy, and P2P-specific statutory notice requirements.

**The compliance baseline (CONC 7.3.2G):** When dealing with customers in or approaching arrears, firms must pay due regard to Principle 12 (Consumer Duty) and PRIN 2A, or Principle 6 (Customers' interests). CONC 7 obligations are layered on top of those high-level principles.

---

## CONC 7.2 — Arrears and Default Policies

A firm must establish and implement **clear, effective and appropriate policies and procedures** for **(CONC 7.2.1R)**:
1. Dealing with customers in or approaching arrears or in default.
2. The fair and appropriate treatment of customers who the firm understands or reasonably suspects to be vulnerable.

Policies must be reviewed for effectiveness at appropriate intervals **(CONC 7.2.4R)**. The review must consider the full extent of support provided to customers — not only individual interactions in isolation **(CONC 7.2.5G)**. Reference guidance: FG21/1 (vulnerable customers) and MALG "Good Practice Awareness Guidelines for Consumers with Mental Health Problems and Debt" **(CONC 7.2.2A G, 7.2.3G)**.

---

## CONC 7.3 — Forbearance and Customer Treatment

### The Core Obligation

**CONC 7.3.4R** is a hard rule: a firm **must** treat customers in or approaching arrears or in default with **forbearance and due consideration**. When determining appropriate forbearance, the firm must take into account the individual circumstances of the customer of which it is or should be aware **(CONC 7.3.4B R)**.

A firm should regard a customer as **approaching arrears** as soon as the customer indicates they are at risk of not meeting one or more repayments when due **(CONC 7.3.4A G)**.

### Examples of Forbearance (CONC 7.3.5G)

The following are guidance examples — the list is not exhaustive **(CONC 7.3.5A G)**:
1. Suspending, reducing, waiving or cancelling further interest or charges where a customer provides evidence of financial difficulties and is unable to meet repayments or can only make token payments.
2. Allowing deferment of arrears where immediate payment would push repayments to an unsustainable level, or where deferment would not make the repayment term unreasonably excessive.
3. Accepting no payments, reduced payments, or token payments for a reasonable period from a customer who demonstrates that meeting existing debts would mean failing to meet priority debts or essential living expenses.
4. Agreeing a repayment arrangement that allows the customer a reasonable period to repay.
5. Transferring the debt to an alternative credit agreement (refinancing) to reduce the debt over a reasonable period without adversely affecting the customer's financial situation.
6. In relation to pawn agreements: extending the redemption period or suspending notice of intention to sell.

### Interest and Charges During Active Repayment Arrangements

**CONC 7.3.5G R** (a hard rule): Where a firm has put in place a repayment arrangement as a forbearance measure, **and** the customer is meeting the terms of that arrangement, the firm **must** reduce, waive or cancel any further interest or charges **to the extent necessary to ensure the level of debt under the arrangement does not rise** for the period of that arrangement.

This is not discretionary — once a forbearance arrangement is in place and honoured by the customer, the firm cannot allow the debt to grow through continued interest accrual. The extent of the waiver may vary as the customer's capacity changes **(CONC 7.3.5H G)**.

The firm must take reasonable steps to ensure that forbearance provided remains appropriate **(CONC 7.3.5I R)**.

### Income and Expenditure Assessment

When a firm assesses income and expenditure in the arrears context, it must do so **objectively** **(CONC 7.3.5D R)**. The assessment should be informed by sufficiently detailed information; the firm may have regard to spending guidelines in the Standard Financial Statement **(CONC 7.3.5E G)**. A repayment arrangement is unlikely to be sustainable if it means the customer cannot meet priority debts (mortgage, rent, council tax, food, utilities) and essential living expenses **(CONC 7.3.5C G)**.

The firm should make the income and expenditure record available to the customer so they can share it with other lenders and debt providers **(CONC 7.3.7A G(4))**.

### Prohibited Conduct

| Rule | Prohibition |
|---|---|
| CONC 7.3.9R | Must not operate a policy of refusing to negotiate with a customer developing a repayment plan |
| CONC 7.3.10R(1) | Must not pressurise customer to pay in one single or very few payments in unreasonably large amounts where it would adversely impact their financial circumstances |
| CONC 7.3.10R(2) | Must not pressurise to pay within an unreasonably short period |
| CONC 7.3.10R(3) | Must not pressurise to raise funds by selling property, borrowing, or increasing existing borrowing |
| CONC 7.3.14R(1) | Must not take disproportionate action against a customer in arrears or default |
| CONC 7.3.14R(2) | Must not apply to court for an order for sale or submit a bankruptcy petition without first having fully explored more proportionate options |
| CONC 7.3.17R | Must not repossess a customer's home, goods or vehicles other than as a last resort, having explored all other possible options |
| CONC 7.3.17A R | Must not commence or continue repossession action where a forbearance arrangement is in place, as long as the customer is meeting its terms |
| CONC 7.3.18R | Must not threaten court action (including charging orders) to pressurise customers in arrears to pay more than they can reasonably afford |

### Debt Counsellor Suspension Rule

A firm **must** suspend active pursuit of debt recovery for a reasonable period where the customer informs the firm that a debt counsellor or other person acting on the customer's behalf is developing a repayment plan **(CONC 7.3.11R)**.

"Reasonable period" is generally **30 days** where there is evidence of genuine intention to develop a plan; extendable to a further **30 days** where there is reasonable evidence of progress **(CONC 7.3.12G)**.

### Referral to Debt Advice

Where appropriate, a firm should inform customers of free and impartial money guidance and debt advice available from not-for-profit debt advice bodies, and refer the customer **(CONC 7.3.7A G)**. A firm must not accept a reasonable instalment offer without at least referring it to the lender or owner if it has no authority to accept **(CONC 7.5.4R)**.

---

## CONC 7.4 — Information on Status of Debts

A firm **must** provide the customer (or a person acting on their behalf) with information on the amount of arrears and the balance owing **(CONC 7.4.1R)**.

Where a lender decides to stop pursuing a customer for a debt, and the debt continues to exist, the lender **must** ensure the customer understands in clear terms that the debt continues to exist and that a purchaser may pursue it **(CONC 7.4.2R)**.

---

## CONC 7.5 — Pursuing and Recovering Repayments

Key prohibitions **(CONC 7.5.2R–7.5.5R)**:
- Must not pursue an individual the firm knows or believes might not be the borrower or hirer.
- Must not ignore a customer's claim that a debt has been settled or is disputed, and must not continue demands without clear justification.
- A firm acting for a lender/owner must refer a reasonable instalment offer to the lender/owner for acceptance if it has no authority to accept directly.
- Payments received must be passed to the lender/owner within five working days **(CONC 7.5.6G)**.

Assignment notification: failure to comply with CONC 6.5.2R (notice to customer on assignment) is taken into account by the FCA in deciding whether to take action against the firm **(CONC 7.5.1G)**.

---

## CONC 7.6 — Exercise of Continuous Payment Authority (CPA)

See **[[conc-cpa-rules]]** for full detail. Summary:

- **Pre-condition (CONC 7.6.1R):** CPA may only be exercised if it has been explained to the customer and is in accordance with the credit or P2P agreement terms.
- **Proportionality obligation (CONC 7.6.3R):** Must exercise reasonably, proportionately and not excessively; must show appropriate forbearance if the customer is or may be in financial difficulties.
- **Financial difficulty suspension (CONC 7.6.7R):** Must not exercise CPA if the customer provides reasonable evidence of being in financial difficulties and cannot afford to repay, or if the firm otherwise becomes aware.
- **Two-strike rule for high-cost short-term credit (CONC 7.6.12R):** After two refused CPA attempts under the same agreement, the firm cannot make a further attempt to collect sums due under that agreement for HCSTC.
- **Cancellation (CONC 7.6.16R, 7.6.17R):** Must not discourage cancellation of CPA. Must cease exercising CPA once notified it has been cancelled.

## CONC 7.7 — Application of Interest and Charges

| Rule | Obligation |
|---|---|
| CONC 7.7.2R | Must not claim costs of recovering a debt without a contractual right to do so |
| CONC 7.7.3R | Must not cause a customer to believe they are legally liable to pay costs where no such obligation exists |
| CONC 7.7.5R | Must not impose charges higher than necessary to cover the firm's reasonable costs |

Note: The obligation to reduce or freeze interest during an active repayment arrangement is in CONC 7.3.5G R (not in CONC 7.7). CONC 7.7 addresses the separate question of what charges may lawfully be imposed at all.

---

## CONC 7.8 — Jurisdictional Requirements

A firm must account for differences in law and court procedure when dealing with a customer resident in a different jurisdiction **(CONC 7.8.1R)**. This applies, for example, where a firm's place of business is in England and the customer is in Scotland **(CONC 7.8.2G)**. A firm must not commence or threaten to commence proceedings in the wrong jurisdiction **(CONC 7.8.3R)**.

---

## CONC 7.9 — Contact with Customers

### Identification Requirements

Any person contacting a customer on the firm's behalf **must** explain: who they work for, their role or relationship with the firm, and the purpose of the contact **(CONC 7.9.1R)**.

A firm must not make a statement that may induce the customer to contact the firm under a misapprehension **(CONC 7.9.2R)**. A calling card left at a customer's address that implies a missed delivery is an example of a misleading communication **(CONC 7.9.3G)**.

### Conduct Prohibitions

| Rule | Prohibition |
|---|---|
| CONC 7.9.4R | Must not contact at unreasonable times; must pay due regard to reasonable customer requests about when and how they may be contacted |
| CONC 7.9.5R | Must not require customer to make contact on a premium rate telephone number |
| CONC 7.9.6R | Must not unfairly disclose or threaten to disclose debt information to a third party |
| CONC 7.9.7R | Must not act publicly embarrassingly to the customer; must take reasonable steps to ensure third parties do not become aware the customer is being pursued for a debt |
| CONC 7.9.10R | Must not disclose details of a debt without first establishing the individual is (or acts for) the borrower or hirer |

### Debt Collection Visits

A firm must ensure that visitors: clearly explain the purpose and intended outcome of the proposed visit; give adequate notice of date and time at a reasonable time of day **(CONC 7.9.12R)**. Visitors **must not** **(CONC 7.9.14R)**:
- Act in a threatening manner.
- Visit when they know or suspect the customer may be particularly vulnerable.
- Visit at an inappropriate location unless the customer has expressly consented.
- Enter a customer's property without consent or a court order.
- Refuse to leave when the customer is unduly distressed or may lack mental capacity.
- Refuse to leave when reasonably asked.
- Visit or threaten to visit when the debt is disputed or deadlocked.

---

## CONC 7.10 — Customers with Mental Capacity Limitations

A firm **must suspend** the active pursuit of recovery of a debt where **(CONC 7.10.1R)**:
1. The firm has been notified that the customer might not have mental capacity to make relevant financial decisions, or
2. The firm understands or ought reasonably to be aware that the customer lacks mental capacity.

The firm should allow a reasonable period for evidence of the impact of any mental capacity limitation **(CONC 7.10.2G)**. Recovery may be pursued through a responsible third party acting on the customer's behalf (e.g. under a lasting power of attorney) where the customer has given prior consent **(CONC 7.10.3G)**.

Firms must note CONC 7.2.1R which requires written policies for the appropriate treatment of vulnerable customers **(CONC 7.10.4G)**. See **[[conc-debt-recovery-treatment]]** and **[[vulnerable-customers]]** for detail.

---

## CONC 7.11 — Disclosures Relating to Authority or Status

A firm **must not** **(CONC 7.11.1R–7.11.9R)**:
- Misrepresent its authority or legal position regarding the debt or recovery process.
- Use official-looking documents designed to mislead a customer as to the firm's status.
- Falsely claim to be a member of or accredited by a trade body.
- Suggest that action can or will be taken when legally it cannot.
- Suggest or state that court proceedings will be initiated when the outstanding debt is too low for such proceedings.
- Suggest a right of entry will be exercised without a court order.
- State that goods will be repossessed when they are protected goods without specific court authorisation.
- Threaten proceedings for a warrant of execution or attachment of earnings before obtaining a court judgment.

---

## CONC 7.12 — Lenders' Responsibilities in Relation to Debt

This section applies only to consumer credit lenders (and firms that would be consumer credit lenders but for article 60C(4A) RAO) **(CONC 7.12.1R)**.

A lender **must not** **(CONC 7.12.2R)**:
- Refuse to deal with a not-for-profit debt advice body, debt counsellor, debt adjuster, or other person acting on behalf of the customer, unless objectively justifiable.
- Refuse to accept a payment tendered by a customer or a person acting on their behalf (unless the credit agreement requires payments to a specific third party).
- Refuse to deal with a customer developing a repayment plan or a third party developing a debt management plan, without justification.
- Directly contact a customer when a person is acting on the customer's behalf, without the customer's consent, unless objectively justifiable.
- Operate a policy of only freezing interest where the lender has an existing arrangement with a specific intermediary.
- Return or refuse a repayment merely because it is tendered by a debt management firm.

**Post-assignment responsibility:** The lender remains responsible under CONC 1.2.2R for ensuring employees and agents comply with CONC. Where a debt collector acts as agent or on behalf of a lender, this rule applies directly to the lender in respect of the collector's conduct **(CONC 7.12.3G(1))**.

---

## CONC 7.13 — Data Accuracy and Outsourced Activities

A firm **must** **(CONC 7.13.2R, 7.13.4R)**:
- Maintain accurate and adequate data on debt and repayment history, to avoid pursuing the wrong individual or pursuing for an incorrect amount.
- Before pursuing a customer, take reasonable steps to verify the accuracy and adequacy of available data.

Information the firm holds about a customer that must be made available to persons involved in recovery on its behalf includes: whether the customer is in financial difficulties, whether they are particularly vulnerable, whether the debt is disputed, whether a repayment plan or forbearance is in place, and whether a representative is acting **(CONC 7.13.5G)**.

Where a firm outsources debt recovery, it must exercise due care in selecting third parties **(CONC 7.13.9G)**, take reasonable steps to avoid multiple simultaneous approaches to the customer by different parties **(CONC 7.13.10G)**, and ensure third parties have any necessary Part 4A permissions **(CONC 7.13.13R)**.

---

## CONC 7.14 — Settlements, Disputed and Deadlocked Debt

### Disputed Debt

A firm **must suspend** any steps it or its agent takes in recovery of a debt where the customer disputes the debt on valid grounds or what may be valid grounds **(CONC 7.14.1R)**. Valid grounds include: the individual is not the true borrower, the debt does not exist, or the amount is incorrect **(CONC 7.14.2G)**.

Where there is a dispute as to identity or amount, the burden is on the **firm** (not the customer) to establish that the customer is the correct person and the amount is correct **(CONC 7.14.4R)**.

### Settlement

A firm **must give due consideration** to a reasonable offer of repayment **(CONC 7.14.8R)**. If it rejects the offer, the response must include a clear explanation of the reason **(CONC 7.14.9R)**. A firm must not respond to a rejected offer by intimidating the customer into increasing it **(CONC 7.14.10R)**.

If a firm accepts a settlement offer, it must communicate formally and unequivocally that the payment has been accepted as settlement of the customer's liability **(CONC 7.14.14R)**.

## CONC 7.15 — Statute Barred Debts

See **[[conc-debt-recovery-treatment]]** for full detail. Summary:

**Limitation periods (CONC 7.15.1G):**
- England, Wales, Northern Ireland: 6 years (Limitation Act 1980).
- Scotland: 5 years (Prescription and Limitation (Scotland) Act 1973). In Scotland, the debt **ceases to exist** once barred if no claim made and not acknowledged during the period **(CONC 7.15.3G)**.

**Hard rules:**
- **CONC 7.15.4R:** Must not attempt to recover a statute barred debt in England/Wales/NI if the lender has not been in contact with the customer during the limitation period.
- **CONC 7.15.6R:** Must not mislead a customer about their rights and obligations.
- **CONC 7.15.8R:** Must not continue demanding payment after the customer states they will not pay because the debt is statute barred.
- **CONC 7.15.9R:** Must identify statute barred debts for prospective purchasers.
- **CONC 7.15.10R:** Must not initiate legal proceedings where the lender is aware of a valid complaint being considered by the [[disp1-treating-complainants-fairly|Financial Ombudsman Service]].

---

## CONC 7.16 — Data Sharing

A firm **must not** pass a customer's details to third parties (including lead generators, debt management firms, lenders, owners, debt collectors or credit brokers) unless it is appropriate to do so **(CONC 7.16.1R)**.

---

## CONC 7.17–7.19 — P2P Arrears and Default Notices

These sections apply to P2P platform operators. They prescribe the timing and content of statutory notices:

**CONC 7.17 — Fixed-sum credit arrears notice:** Where the borrower has missed at least two payments totalling no less than the last two required payments, the platform operator must give a notice within 14 days of the conditions being satisfied, with follow-up notices every six months while the shortfall persists **(CONC 7.17.3R–7.17.4R)**. The notice must accompany the FCA's current arrears information sheet (section 86A CCA), with P2P-specific modifications **(CONC 7.17.5R)**.

**CONC 7.18 — Running-account credit arrears notice:** Triggered when two consecutive repayments have been missed. Notice required next time the firm sends a statement to the borrower **(CONC 7.18.2R–7.18.3R)**.

**CONC 7.19 — Default sum notice:** Within 35 days of a default sum becoming payable by the borrower under a P2P agreement, the firm must give notice with prescribed content **(CONC 7.19.4R)**. Applies to agreements above £50, with aggregation rules where concurrent P2P agreements exist **(CONC 7.19.2R)**.

Firms must not charge a fee for preparing or issuing these notices.

## Key Points for Agents

- CONC 7 requires written policies for arrears and vulnerable customer treatment — a governing-body-level obligation (CONC 7.2.1R). This is not a process obligation; it is a structured policy requirement.
- Once a forbearance arrangement is in place and the customer is meeting it, the firm **must** freeze, reduce or waive interest and charges sufficiently so the total debt does not rise during the arrangement (CONC 7.3.5G R). This is a hard rule, not guidance.
- Debt pursuit **must be suspended** for at least 30 days when a customer informs the firm a debt counsellor is developing a repayment plan (CONC 7.3.11R, 7.3.12G).
- Debt pursuit **must be suspended** when the customer disputes the debt on valid or potentially valid grounds — the burden of proof is on the firm (CONC 7.14.1R, 7.14.4R).
- Debt pursuit **must be suspended** where the firm is aware or ought to be aware the customer may lack mental capacity (CONC 7.10.1R).
- Firms must not pursue a statute barred debt in England/Wales/NI without prior contact during the limitation period (CONC 7.15.4R). In Scotland, the debt itself ceases to exist once barred.
- Lenders remain responsible for the conduct of debt collectors acting as their agents — the CONC 1.2.2R employee/agent obligation applies in full (CONC 7.12.3G).
- CPA exercise for high-cost short-term credit is subject to a two-strike limit. For all credit types, CPA must be exercised proportionately and suspended if financial difficulty is evidenced (CONC 7.6).

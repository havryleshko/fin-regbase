# CASS 10 — CASS Resolution Pack (Sourcebook Reference)

## What CASS 10 Does

CASS 10 requires firms that hold safe custody assets (CASS 6) or client money (CASS 7) to maintain a **CASS resolution pack** — a defined set of documents that can be retrieved on demand within 48 hours to support an orderly return of assets and money to clients in the event of the firm's failure.

The resolution pack serves three audiences **(CASS 10.1.2G)**:
1. An **insolvency practitioner** appointed in respect of the firm — to achieve a timely return of client money and safe custody assets to clients;
2. The **Bank of England** — in connection with the firm's or another firm's resolution under the Banking Act; and
3. The **FCA**.

CASS 10 is a **preparedness obligation** — it is not triggered by failure but must be maintained at all times. The pack must exist in a state from which it can be retrieved and acted upon immediately.

---

## Section 10.1 — Application, Purpose, and General Provisions

### Application

CASS 10 applies to a firm when it **(CASS 10.1.1R)**:
- Holds financial instruments, is safeguarding and administering investments, or is acting as AIF/UCITS trustee or depositary in accordance with CASS 6; or
- Holds client money in accordance with CASS 7.

It does not apply to firms that merely arrange safeguarding and administration without holding assets, or small AIFMs carrying on excluded custody activities that amount only to arranging **(CASS 10.1.1R(2))**.

### The Maintenance Obligation

A firm must maintain and be able to retrieve a CASS resolution pack at **all times** when CASS 10.1.1R applies **(CASS 10.1.3R; 10.1.4G)**. The pack must be kept current — not assembled at the point of failure. Documents within the pack will change from time to time (for example, reconciliations must be included and are performed daily or monthly), and the firm must maintain these components on an ongoing basis **(CASS 10.1.5G)**.

### The 48-Hour Retrieval Obligation

For each document in the resolution pack, the firm must **(CASS 10.1.7R)**:
- Put in place adequate arrangements to ensure that an **insolvency officer** (administrator, receiver, trustee, liquidator, or analogous officer) appointed in respect of the firm or any material part of its property is able to retrieve each document **as soon as practicable and in any event within 48 hours** of that officer's appointment; and
- Ensure the firm itself is able to retrieve each document within 48 hours where it has decided to do so, or where the **FCA or Bank of England requests** it.

The 48-hour period is a hard outer limit — the expectation is that the most critical documents should be retrievable immediately **(CASS 10.1.9E(1))**: specifically the master retrieval document, the institutions document, any acknowledgement letters, and the most recent reconciliations.

Where the firm relies on systems for the provision of resolution pack documents, it must have arrangements to ensure those systems remain **operational and accessible after insolvency** **(CASS 10.1.9E(2))**. Failure to meet these conditions may be relied upon as tending to establish contravention of the retrieval obligation.

### Group Reliance

A firm may satisfy a CASS 10.2.1R content requirement by relying on a group member's resolution pack, provided **(CASS 10.1.6R)**:
1. The group member is subject to the same rule under CASS 10; and
2. The firm is still able to meet the 48-hour retrieval obligation for that document.

Where documents are held by group members under this rule, the firm must have **adequate inter-group arrangements** in place to ensure delivery within the 48-hour window **(CASS 10.1.8R)**.

### Ongoing Accuracy — 5 Business Day Correction Deadline

The firm must review the resolution pack on an ongoing basis to ensure it remains accurate **(CASS 10.1.11R)**. Any change of circumstances that renders a document in CASS 10.2.1R **inaccurate in any material respect** must be corrected promptly and **in any event within 5 business days** of the change of circumstances arising **(CASS 10.1.11R(2))**. An example of a triggering change: a new or replaced institution identified in CASS 10.2.1R(2) **(CASS 10.1.12G)**.

Documents may be held in electronic form provided the firm can still meet the retrieval and accuracy obligations **(CASS 10.1.13G)**.

### Governance — CASS Oversight Officer Annual Report

The individual allocated responsibility for **CASS operational oversight** under CASS 1A.3.1R, CASS 1A.3.1A R, or CASS 1A.3.1C R (as applicable) must report **at least annually** to the firm's governing body on compliance with CASS 10 **(CASS 10.1.14R)**. CASS oversight function holders are reminded that their responsibilities include compliance with CASS 10 **(CASS 10.1.15G)**.

### FCA Notification — Immediate

If a firm is unable to comply with, or has not complied with, the CASS 10 maintenance obligation **(CASS 10.1.3R)**, it must **notify the FCA in writing immediately (CASS 10.1.16R)**. This is a standalone notification obligation — separate from and in addition to the six-trigger regimes under CASS 6.6.57R (custody) and CASS 7.15.33R (client money).

## Section 10.2 — Core Content Requirements

### The Nine Mandatory Items (CASS 10.2.1R)

A firm must include within its CASS resolution pack all of the following:

| Item | Content | Rule |
|------|---------|------|
| (1) Master document | Information sufficient to retrieve every other document in the pack | 10.2.1R(1) |
| (2) Institutions document | Identifies every institution the firm has appointed (directly or through agents) to hold client money (CASS 7.13.3R / 7.14.2R) or safe custody assets (CASS 6.3.1R); must include full name, postal and email address, telephone number, and **all account numbers** | 10.2.1R(2); 10.2.3R |
| (3) Agents document | Identifies each appointed representative, tied agent, field representative, or other agent that receives client money or safe custody assets in its capacity as the firm's agent | 10.2.1R(3) |
| (4) Key individuals document | Identifies each senior manager and director critical or important to CASS 6 / CASS 7 operational functions, **and** the individual to whom CASS operational oversight has been allocated | 10.2.1R(4); 10.2.2G |
| (5) Executed agreements and acknowledgement letters | Copies of all executed agreements (including side letters) with each institution in (2), and any acknowledgement letters under CASS 7.18 | 10.2.1R(5) |
| (6) Group and third-party map | Identifies each group member involved in CASS 6/7 operations (including nominee companies, with entity type, jurisdiction, and description of operational functions) and each third party used for CASS 6/7 functions | 10.2.1R(6) |
| (7) Third-party agreements | Copies of all executed agreements with each third party identified in (6)(b) | 10.2.1R(7) |
| (8) Third-party access/transfer document | Where the firm relies on a third party that **controls** client money or safe custody assets, a document describing how to gain access to that information and how to effect a transfer of those assets | 10.2.1R(8) |
| (9) Procedures manual | A copy of the firm's manual recording its procedures for the management, recording, and transfer of client money and safe custody assets | 10.2.1R(9) |

Item (8) is particularly significant for outsourced custody and outsourced client money arrangements: the firm must be able to demonstrate that a third party controlling assets on its behalf can be accessed and the assets transferred — not merely that the firm knows who holds them.

## Section 10.3 — Existing Records Forming Part of the Pack

CASS 10.3.1R specifies which records required by other CASS rules must also be included within the resolution pack (as applicable). These records are already mandated by their source rules — CASS 10 does not change those underlying requirements **(CASS 10.3.2G)**; it simply designates them as components of the pack.

The designated existing records are:

| Record | Source rule | Subject matter |
|--------|------------|----------------|
| Third-party selection appropriateness | CASS 6.3.2AR | Safe custody assets — due diligence on third-party custodian |
| Third-party rights records | CASS 6.3.6AR | Rights held over client safe custody assets |
| Use of safe custody assets | CASS 6.4.3R | Firm's use of safe custody assets (SFT consent etc.) |
| Custody asset holdings per client | CASS 6.6.2R; 6.6.3R | Safe custody assets held for each client |
| Client agreements — right to use | CASS 6.6.6R | Client agreements permitting firm to use safe custody assets |
| Reconciliation records | CASS 6.6.8R | Internal custody record checks, physical reconciliations, external custody reconciliations |
| SYSC compliance policies | SYSC 6.1.1R(2)(a) | Policies and procedures for carrying out record checks and reconciliations |
| Client bank account policy | CASS 7.13.14BR | Policy for use of client bank accounts under CASS 7.13.13R(3A)(b) |
| Client money holdings per client | CASS 7.15.2R; 7.15.3R; 7.15.5R | Client money held for each client |
| Reconciliation outcome records | CASS 7.15.7R | Internal and external client money reconciliation outcomes |
| Client categorisation records | COBS 3.8.2R(2)(a); (2)(c) | Client categorisation |
| Client agreements | COBS 8.1.4R or COBS 8A.1.9R | Retail and professional client agreements |

## Key Points for Agents

- **CASS 10 is a live preparedness obligation, not a failure-event trigger (CASS 10.1.3R; 10.1.4G).** The pack must be maintained at all times — not assembled on insolvency. It serves insolvency practitioners, the Bank of England (resolution), and the FCA.
- **48-hour hard retrieval deadline from insolvency officer appointment or FCA/Bank of England request (CASS 10.1.7R).** Systems on which the pack depends must remain operational and accessible post-insolvency. The most critical items (master document, institutions document, acknowledgement letters, most recent reconciliations) should be immediately retrievable **(CASS 10.1.9E)**.
- **Nine mandatory content items, including a third-party access/transfer document (CASS 10.2.1R).** Item (8) — the document describing how to access and transfer assets held by but controlled by a third party — is required wherever the firm outsources custody or client money holding. It is not sufficient merely to identify the third party.
- **5 business day correction deadline on any material inaccuracy (CASS 10.1.11R).** The pack must be reviewed on an ongoing basis. A change of institution triggers the correction obligation.
- **Standalone FCA notification obligation on inability to maintain the pack (CASS 10.1.16R).** This is separate from the CASS 6 (6.6.57R) and CASS 7 (7.15.33R) notification regimes. Failing to maintain the pack is itself an immediately notifiable breach.
- **CASS oversight officer must report annually to governing body on CASS 10 compliance (CASS 10.1.14R).** This integrates CASS 10 into the SM&CR governance structure.
- **Group reliance is permitted but requires inter-group arrangements and preserved 48-hour capability (CASS 10.1.6R; 10.1.8R).** A group member's pack can satisfy the firm's content requirement, but only if retrieval within 48 hours remains achievable.

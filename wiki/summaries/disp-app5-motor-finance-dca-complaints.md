# DISP App 5 — Motor Finance DCA and Non-DCA Complaint Handling Rules

DISP App 5 modifies the standard DISP complaint handling rules for motor finance commission complaints. It applies alongside the rest of DISP — all other DISP rules continue to apply unless specifically modified by this appendix. **(DISP App 5.1.1G(3))**

The appendix was introduced in response to the FCA's motor finance discretionary commission arrangement review, which prompted a surge in complaints. It pauses firm response deadlines, extends FOS referral windows, imposes mandatory consumer communications obligations, and requires long-form record retention.

---

## DISP App 5.1 — Complaint Definitions

### Relevant Motor Finance DCA Complaint (DISP App 5.1.2R)

All four conditions must be satisfied:

1. The subject matter relates, in whole or part, to a **regulated credit agreement entered into before 28 January 2021**;
2. The agreement, in whole or part, **financed the purchase of a motor vehicle**, or a motor vehicle was bailed or hired under it;
3. There were **arrangements between the lender and a credit broker** that included a **discretionary commission arrangement (DCA)**; AND
4. The respondent **received the complaint** in the period **17 November 2023 – 31 May 2026**, OR **sent a final response** in the period **12 July 2023 – 26 July 2026**.

**Extension of definition (DISP App 5.1.2R(2)):** Arrangements in condition (3) include any arrangement which, if entered into on or after 28 January 2021, would have constituted a DCA — capturing pre-CONC-prohibition arrangements that are equivalent in substance. This ensures the appendix covers OFT-licensed arrangements that predate FCA regulation.

### Motor Finance Non-DCA Complaint — Two Definitions

The non-DCA complaint definition changed on 5 December 2025:

**26 October 2024 – 4 December 2025 (DISP App 5.1.3AR):** A complaint where: (1) the subject matter relates to a **regulated credit agreement or regulated consumer hire agreement**; (2) the agreement financed/bailed/hired a motor vehicle; (3) there were arrangements between the lender/owner and a credit broker providing for payment of **any commission, fee, or other remuneration** (not a DCA); (4) it is not a relevant motor finance DCA complaint; AND (5) the respondent received the complaint 26 October 2024 – 4 December 2025, or sent a final response 21 June 2024 – 29 January 2026.

**5 December 2025 onwards (DISP App 5.1.3BR):** Same as above but **regulated consumer hire agreements are excluded** — only regulated credit agreements qualify. Consumer hire agreement complaints received on or after 5 December 2025 fall back under the standard DISP 1.6.2R 8-week rule. **(DISP App 5.1.3CR)**

## DISP App 5.2 — Modified Complaint Handling Rules

### The 8-Week Pause (DISP App 5.2.1R / 5.2.1BR / 5.2.1CR)

The standard 8-week final response deadline (DISP 1.6.2R) is modified so that **time does not run** during the following periods:

| Complaint type | Pause period |
|---------------|-------------|
| DCA complaint | **11 January 2024 – 31 May 2026** |
| Non-DCA complaint (regulated credit agreement) | **20 December 2024 – 31 May 2026** |
| Non-DCA complaint (consumer hire agreement) | **Received 26 Oct 2024 – 4 Dec 2025**: pause ran until 4 December 2025; time resumed 5 December 2025 |

The pause also applies to the DISP 2.8.1R(2) and DISP 2.8.1R(4)(a) 8-week thresholds — meaning the FOS gateway does not open during the pause. The **3-year complaints record retention period (DISP 1.9.1R(2))** is similarly paused for the same windows.

> **Modification to wiki:** The account of the 8-week rule in [[complaints-handling-framework]] and [[disp1-treating-complainants-fairly]] is correct for standard complaints but does not reflect this pause. For motor finance DCA and non-DCA complaints, the 8-week clock does not run during the pause periods above.

### Extended FOS Referral Window (DISP App 5.2.2R / 5.2.2BR / 5.2.2C)

The standard DISP 2.8.2R 6-month FOS referral window is modified for motor finance complaints. The applicable limit depends on when the final response was sent:

**For DCA complaints:**

| Final response sent | Modified time limit |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| 12 July 2023 – 29 April 2025 | FOS cannot consider if referred **on or after 30 July 2026** (fixed deadline) |
| 30 April 2025 – 29 January 2026 | FOS cannot consider if referred more than **15 months** after final response |
| On or after 30 January 2026 | Standard **6 months** (DISP 2.8.2R(1)) applies |

**For non-DCA complaints:**

| Final response sent | Modified time limit |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| 21 June 2024 – 29 April 2025 | FOS cannot consider if referred **on or after 30 July 2026** |
| 30 April 2025 – 29 January 2026 | FOS cannot consider if referred more than **15 months** after final response |
| On or after 30 January 2026 | Standard **6 months** applies |

> **Modification to wiki:** The account of the 6-month FOS referral limit in [[disp2-fos-jurisdiction]] and [[fos-eligible-complainants]] is correct for standard complaints but does not reflect this extension. Motor finance respondents sending final responses during the extended period must use the modified time limits above.

### Consumer Communication Obligations

**Published consumer awareness information (DISP App 5.2.4R):** Respondents must update any information published under DISP 1.2.1R(1) (consumer awareness obligations) to inform consumers of the pause to time limits and direct them to **fca.org.uk/carfinance**. This obligation applies until 23:59 on 26 July 2026.

**Acknowledgement of receipt — DCA complaints (DISP App 5.2.5AR / 5.2.5CAR):** When acknowledging a DCA complaint received in scope, respondents must inform the complainant in writing of the pause and direct them to fca.org.uk/carfinance.

**Acknowledgement of receipt — non-DCA complaints (DISP App 5.2.5D / 5.2.5E):** Same obligation for non-DCA complaints received in the relevant windows.

### Mandatory Final Response Wording — Extended Period (DISP App 5.2.11R / 5.2.14R)

When sending a final response to a DCA or non-DCA complaint during the extended period (**26 September 2024 – 29 January 2026** for DCA; **21 June 2024 – 29 January 2026** for non-DCA), the respondent must:

- **(a)** Inform the complainant that the FOS referral time limit has been extended under DISP App 5.2.2R;
- **(b)** State the specific date by which the complainant must refer to the FOS;
- **(c)** Explain that the 6-month time limit in the FOS standard explanatory leaflet **does not apply**;
- **(d)** Direct the complainant to fca.org.uk/carfinance.

The standard DISP 1 Annex 3R wording is substituted so that:
- References to **"within six months of the date of this letter"** → **"within fifteen months of the date of this letter"**
- The reference to **"is usually six months"** → **"is, in this case, fifteen months"**

**From 30 January 2026 (DISP App 5.2.12R / 5.2.15R):** Standard DISP 2.8.2R(1) 6-month limit applies. Final responses must revert to standard Annex 3R wording and direct complainants to fca.org.uk/carfinance explaining why the 6-month limit applies.

## DISP App 5.3 — Record Retention to 11 April 2031

### DCA-Related Records (DISP App 5.3.1R)

**Lenders and credit brokers** must retain and preserve records:
- Relating to any regulated credit agreement entered into **before 28 January 2021** that meets the vehicle and DCA conditions in DISP App 5.1.2R(1)(b) and (c);
- That are or could be relevant to the handling of existing or future complaints or civil claims relating to DCAs;
- **Regardless of whether a relevant motor finance DCA complaint has been made**;
- For the period **11 January 2024 – 11 April 2031**.

### Non-DCA-Related Records (DISP App 5.3.1AR)

**Lenders, owners, and credit brokers** must retain and preserve records:
- Relating to any regulated credit agreement or regulated consumer hire agreement that financed/bailed/hired a motor vehicle;
- Where they are or could be relevant to complaints or civil claims relating to payment of any commission, fee, or other remuneration to a credit broker;
- **Regardless of whether a complaint has been made**;
- For the period **20 December 2024 – 11 April 2031**.

### What Counts as Relevant Records (DISP App 5.3.2E)

Records that are relevant include: (1) the credit or hire agreement itself; (2) records of commission/remuneration arrangements; (3) records of all commissions, fees, or other payments made to the credit broker; (4) customer files and records, including any agreement setting out services offered and any transactions and payments; (5) all communications with the customer.

## Key Points for Agents

- DISP App 5 creates two modified complaint categories: DCA complaints (pre-28 Jan 2021 agreements with actual DCA) and non-DCA complaints (any commission arrangement, motor vehicle, within the relevant date windows). Both have distinct definitions and different pause/extension dates.
- The 8-week final response deadline does **not run** during the FCA-mandated pauses: 11 Jan 2024 – 31 May 2026 (DCA); 20 Dec 2024 – 31 May 2026 (non-DCA credit). The 3-year complaints records retention period is also paused.
- The standard 6-month FOS referral window is extended to **15 months** (or a fixed 30 July 2026 deadline for earlier responses) for final responses sent during the extended period. It reverts to 6 months for final responses sent on or after 30 January 2026.
- Final response letters sent during the extended period must substitute "fifteen months" for "six months" in all Annex 3R wording and must direct complainants to fca.org.uk/carfinance.
- Lenders and credit brokers must retain all in-scope motor finance records until **11 April 2031** — regardless of whether a complaint has been received.

# FCA PS22/9 — A New Consumer Duty: Feedback to CP21/36 and Final Rules

## What This Document Is

PS22/9 is the FCA's policy statement setting out the **final rules and guidance for the Consumer Duty**, published July 2022. It responds to consultation paper CP21/36 (December 2021), summarises the 151 responses received, explains the changes made, and introduces the made rules (Appendix 1). The accompanying non-Handbook guidance is FG22/5, published simultaneously. **(PS22/9 §1.1)**

The Duty was required by the Financial Services Act 2021, which mandated the FCA to consult on whether to make rules providing that authorised firms owe a duty of care to consumers. **(PS22/9 §1.12–§1.13)**

## What the Duty Introduces

Three layers of rule: **(PS22/9 §1.15)**

1. **Consumer Principle (Principle 12):** Firms must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers. Replaces Principles 6 and 7 in scope.
2. **Three cross-cutting rules:** Act in good faith; avoid causing foreseeable harm; enable and support retail customers to pursue their financial objectives.
3. **Four outcome rules:** Products and services; price and value; consumer understanding; consumer support.

## Scope

The Duty applies to all FCA-authorised firms in the distribution chain that can **determine or materially influence** retail customer outcomes — including manufacturers with no direct customer relationship. **(PS22/9 §2.10)** Each firm is liable only for its own actions. **(PS22/9 §2.16)**

Applies to: retail customers as defined by sectoral sourcebooks, including certain SMEs, and beneficiaries of trust-based pension schemes where an FCA-authorised firm provides services. **(PS22/9 §2.3–§2.4)**

Does **not** apply to: purely wholesale products marketed and approved exclusively for non-retail distribution; activities where a Handbook or legislative exclusion already applies; Bounce Back Loans at origination. **(PS22/9 §2.20, §2.39)**

## Implementation Timeline

| Phase | Date | Scope |
|-------|------|-------|
| Phase 1 | 31 July 2023 | All new and existing open products/services |
| Phase 2 | 31 July 2024 | Closed products/services (no longer on sale) |

**(PS22/9 §1.57, §12.6)**

Boards were required to sign off implementation plans by end of October 2022. **(PS22/9 §12.9)**

## Application to Existing and Closed Products

The Duty applies on a **forward-looking basis** only — it does not require firms to assess whether past actions breached it. **(PS22/9 §3.1)** However, firms must assess whether existing and closed products continue to meet the Duty going forward, including on fair value. If a product is found to offer poor value, firms must act but are **never required to amend vested contractual rights**. **(PS22/9 §3.14)**

## Governance Requirements

- Boards must produce an **annual report** assessing customer outcomes. **(PS22/9 §13.12)**
- The Duty must be reflected in firms' strategies, governance, leadership and people policies including pay and performance management. **(PS22/9 §13.13)**
- A board-level **Consumer Duty champion** is expected. **(PS22/9 §13.13)**
- Under SM&CR, a new Individual Conduct Rule 6 requires individuals to act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers. **(PS22/9 §13.16–§13.22)**

## No Private Right of Action

The FCA chose **not to attach a private right of action** to any part of the Duty at introduction. Consumer redress routes are the Financial Ombudsman Service and FCA enforcement under FSMA s.384 (restitution). **(PS22/9 §11.1, §11.10)**

## Relationship to Existing Rules

- Principles 6 and 7 are **disapplied** where Principle 12 applies; they continue to apply to wholesale and out-of-scope business. **(PS22/9 §4.12)**
- Existing TCF outcomes remain relevant but firms should focus on the Duty where it applies. **(PS22/9 §4.15)**
- Firms complying with existing PROD, COLL 6/8/15, and PROD 4/7 fair value rules meet the corresponding Duty outcomes. **(PS22/9 §7.12)**

## Key Points for Agents

- The Duty introduces a single apex principle (Principle 12) replacing TCF — "good outcomes" is the operative standard, not "fair treatment."
- All four outcomes must be met: products/services; price/value; consumer understanding; consumer support. Meeting three is not sufficient.
- The Duty runs through the entire distribution chain; each firm is responsible only for its own contribution to customer outcomes.
- The Duty applies to closed books from 31 July 2024 on a forward-looking basis; vested contractual rights cannot be forced to change.
- No private right of action exists — enforcement is via FOS or FCA regulatory action.

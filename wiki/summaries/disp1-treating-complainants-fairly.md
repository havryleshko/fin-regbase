# DISP 1 — Treating Complainants Fairly

## What DISP 1 Is

DISP 1 sets the FCA's rules on how respondents must handle complaints from eligible complainants. It covers internal complaint handling procedures, timelines for resolution, root cause analysis obligations, complaints record keeping, complaints reporting to the FCA, and public data publication requirements.

DISP 1 applies to **respondents** — a defined term that includes FCA-authorised firms, EEA firms operating in the UK, payment service providers (PSPs), electronic money institutions (EMIs), and other entities the FCA supervises. The detailed scope is in DISP 1.1.

---

## Scope — DISP 1.1

### Who DISP 1 Applies To

DISP 1 applies to respondents in relation to complaints from **eligible complainants**. The definition of eligible complainant is in DISP 2.7 and covers retail customers, micro-enterprises, charities and trustees below specified thresholds, and small businesses in specified circumstances.

Two specific scope extensions are notable:

**PSPs and EMIs not otherwise FSMA-authorised** *(DISP 1.1.10ACR)*: The complaints handling rules and the complaints time limit rules apply to payment service providers, e-money issuers, and registered account information service providers even where they are not FSMA-authorised firms and are not otherwise caught by DISP 1.1.1R. This means an Authorised PI or Authorised EMI that does not hold an FSMA permission is still subject to DISP 1 complaints handling obligations.

**Motor finance DCA complaints** *(DISP 1.1.10MR)*: Complaints about discretionary commission arrangements in relation to motor finance credit agreements are subject to DISP 1 App 5 rather than the standard DISP 1 rules. DISP 1 App 5 sets out modified timelines and procedures for this category.

### Outsourcing Does Not Transfer Responsibility

Where a respondent outsources any function related to complaint handling, the respondent remains responsible for compliance with DISP 1. *(DISP 1.1.4R)* You may outsource the function; you cannot outsource the obligation.

---

## MiFID Complaints — DISP 1.1A

The MiFID complaints requirements in DISP 1.1A impose additional obligations where a complaint is a "MiFID complaint" (relating to MiFID business). The critical scope point is that **MiFID complaints requirements apply to all clients, including Eligible Counterparties** — not just eligible complainants under the standard DISP definition. *(DISP 1.1A.3R)*

This means that for investment firms carrying on MiFID business, the complaints handling obligations arising from DISP 1.1A extend beyond the retail/eligible-complainant population to ECPs. This is one of the few areas of the FCA Handbook where obligations run to ECPs in a retail-protection context.

---

## Consumer Awareness Rules — DISP 1.2

Respondents must publish information about their internal complaint handling procedures in a clear, comprehensible, and easily accessible way. *(DISP 1.2.1R)* This includes:

- Summary details of internal procedures (at or before point of sale for in-person, on request otherwise; in writing on first written communication with the customer)
- That if the complaint is unresolved, the complainant may be entitled to refer it to the Financial Ombudsman Service
- For PSPs and EMIs: equivalent disclosures to all payment service users, not only eligible complainants

Not-for-profit debt advice bodies have a modified approach to when oral vs. written delivery is required. *(DISP 1.2.2G)*

---

## Complaints Handling Rules — DISP 1.3

### Core Handling Obligation

Every respondent must establish, implement, and maintain effective and transparent procedures for the reasonable and prompt handling of complaints. *(DISP 1.3.1R)* Complaints must be capable of being made free of charge. *(DISP 1.3.1AR)*

**Call charges rule** *(DISP 1.3.1AAR)*: Where a respondent operates a telephone line for complaints, the complainant must not be bound to pay more than the basic rate. Prohibited: 09 numbers, 084 numbers, 0871/0872/0873, 0870. Permitted: geographic (01/02/03), 0800/0808, standard mobile (07) where the firm ordinarily uses mobile numbers.

### Root Cause Analysis — Hard Rule

A respondent **must** put in place appropriate management controls and take reasonable steps to ensure that in handling complaints it identifies and remedies any recurring or systemic problems. *(DISP 1.3.3R)* This includes:

1. Analysing the causes of individual complaints to identify root causes common to types of complaint
2. **Considering whether such root causes may also affect other processes or products, including those not directly complained of**
3. Correcting, where reasonable to do so, such root causes

The extension to unaffected products and processes — point (2) — is operationally significant. A systemic root cause discovered in one product line triggers an obligation to assess all other products and processes where the same cause may operate, even if no customer has complained about them. Management information systems must be designed to enable this analysis. *(DISP 1.3.3BG)*

Separately, respondents should apply lessons learned from FOS determinations to future complaint handling. *(DISP 1.3.2AG)*

### Proactive Redress Obligation (Systemic Problems)

Where a respondent identifies recurring or systemic problems — whether from its complaints or otherwise — it should consider whether customers who have **not complained** may have suffered detriment. *(DISP 1.3.6G)* If so, the respondent should assess the scope and severity of potential detriment and consider whether a proactive redress or remediation exercise is appropriate, including contacting customers who have not complained. This is guidance (G), but non-compliance with it would be taken into account by the FCA when assessing the respondent's compliance with Principle 6 (Customers' interests).

### Named Responsible Individual

A firm **must** appoint an individual to have responsibility for oversight of the firm's compliance with DISP 1. *(DISP 1.3.7R)* The individual appointed must be carrying out an **FCA governing function** at the firm or in the same group. Where no such individual exists at the firm or in the group, the firm must appoint a person of appropriate seniority — and a person approved to perform the **limited scope function** satisfies this condition.

This creates a named personal accountability link for DISP 1 compliance. The firm need not notify the FCA or FOS of the individual's name proactively but must provide it promptly on request.

---

## Complaints Resolution Rules — DISP 1.4

Once a complaint has been received, the respondent must:

1. Investigate competently, diligently, and impartially
2. Assess fairly, consistently, and promptly: the subject matter; whether to uphold; what remedial action or redress is appropriate; and whether another respondent may be wholly or jointly responsible
3. Offer redress or remedial action where appropriate
4. Explain the assessment promptly and in a fair, clear, and not misleading way
5. Comply promptly with any accepted offer of redress

*(DISP 1.4.1R)*

Respondents should aim to resolve complaints at the earliest possible opportunity. *(DISP 1.4.3G)* Where a complaint is referred to the FOS, the respondent must cooperate fully and comply promptly with any settlements or awards made by the FOS. *(DISP 1.4.4R)*

---

## Complaints Resolved by Close of Third Business Day — DISP 1.5

### The 3-Day Track

Where a complaint is resolved by close of business on the **third business day** following the day of receipt, the following rules do **not** apply:

- The complaints time limit rules (DISP 1.6)
- The complaints forwarding rules (DISP 1.7)

*(DISP 1.5.1R)*

The complaints resolution rules (DISP 1.4) **do** continue to apply to 3-day complaints. *(DISP 1.5.2G)*

### When a Complaint Is Resolved

A complaint is resolved under DISP 1.5 where the complainant has **indicated acceptance** of a response from the respondent. Neither the response nor the acceptance needs to be in writing. *(DISP 1.5.2AR)*

### Summary Resolution Communication

Where the respondent considers a complaint to be resolved under DISP 1.5, it must promptly send the complainant a **summary resolution communication** — a written communication that:

1. Acknowledges that a complaint has been made and states the respondent considers it resolved
2. Informs the complainant that if dissatisfied they may refer the complaint to the FOS
3. Indicates whether the respondent consents to waive the DISP 2 time limits for FOS referral (using prescribed wording from DISP 1 Annex 3R)
4. Provides the FOS website address
5. Refers to further information available on the FOS website

*(DISP 1.5.4R)*

The summary resolution communication is meaningfully lighter than a final response under DISP 1.6.2R. It does not need to set out reasons for the resolution.

---

## Complaints Time Limit Rules — DISP 1.6

### Initial Acknowledgement

On receipt, a respondent must promptly send a written acknowledgement confirming receipt and stating that the complaint is being dealt with. *(DISP 1.6.1R)*

### Standard 8-Week Deadline

Subject to the EMD/PSD exception below, by the end of **eight weeks** from receipt the respondent must send either:

**(a) A final response**, which:
- Accepts the complaint and offers redress or remedial action, **or**
- Offers redress or remedial action without accepting, **or**
- Rejects the complaint with reasons
- And which encloses the FOS standard explanatory leaflet, provides the FOS website, informs the complainant of their right to refer to the FOS, and indicates whether the respondent consents to waive DISP 2 time limits

**(b) A written response** that explains why it cannot yet provide a final response, indicates when it expects to do so, and informs the complainant they may now refer to the FOS.

*(DISP 1.6.2R)*

### EMD and PSD Complaints — Shorter Deadlines

Where a complaint is an **EMD complaint** or a **PSD complaint** (relating to electronic money or payment services), the respondent must:

- Send a final response within **15 business days** of receipt, **or**
- In exceptional circumstances beyond the respondent's control, send a holding response by day 15 explaining the delay and specifying the deadline, then a final response by the end of **35 business days**

*(DISP 1.6.2AR)*

EMD/PSD final responses must be on paper or another durable medium agreed with the complainant. Where only part of a complaint is an EMD/PSD complaint, that part must be handled on the shorter timeline (DISP 1.6.2BR), though the respondent may choose to treat the whole complaint under DISP 1.6.2AR *(DISP 1.6.2CR)*.

### Written Acceptance Displaces the 8-Week Rule

DISP 1.6.2R does not apply where the complainant has already indicated **in writing** acceptance of the respondent's response, provided that response contained the required FOS information. *(DISP 1.6.4R)* This provision allows complaints closed by mutual agreement before eight weeks to be treated as closed without a formal final response being required.

---

## Complaints Forwarding Rules — DISP 1.7

A respondent that has reasonable grounds to believe another respondent may be solely or jointly responsible for the subject of a complaint may forward the complaint (or the relevant part) in writing to that other respondent, provided it:

1. Does so promptly
2. Informs the complainant in a final response why the complaint has been forwarded and provides the other respondent's contact details
3. Where jointly responsible, complies with DISP 1 obligations itself in respect of the part it has not forwarded

*(DISP 1.7.1R)*

A forwarded complaint is treated as made directly to the receiving respondent, from the date the forwarded complaint was received. *(DISP 1.7.2R–1.7.3G)*

Forwarded complaints are excluded from the forwarding firm's complaints report (DISP 1.10.1AR), but where only part is forwarded or joint responsibility exists, both respondents must report the complaint.

---

## Complaints Time Barring Rule — DISP 1.8

Where a complaint is received that is outside the time limits for FOS referral (see DISP 2.8), the respondent **may** reject it without considering the merits. However, if it does so, it must explain this in a final response complying with DISP 1.6.2R or 1.6.2AR. *(DISP 1.8.1R)*

---

## Complaints Record Rule — DISP 1.9

Every firm, payment service provider, and e-money issuer must keep a record of each complaint received and the measures taken for its resolution, retaining that record for:

- **5 years** for complaints relating to UCITS collective portfolio management services
- **3 years** for all other complaints

from the date the complaint was received. *(DISP 1.9.1R)*

---

## Complaints Reporting Rules — DISP 1.10

### Standard Reporting — Twice Yearly

Unless the reduced frequency exception applies, a firm must provide the FCA with a **complete complaints report twice a year**, covering complaints received from eligible complainants. *(DISP 1.10.1R(1))*

The report format is **DISP 1 Annex 1R** (or DISP 1 Annex 1ABR for claims management complaints; DISP 1 Annex 1ACR for funeral plan complaints).

**Reduced frequency (annual)** applies to: firms with only credit-related regulated activities or P2P platform permission with revenue ≤ £5m/year; firms with only regulated claims management activities; firms with only regulated funeral plan activities. *(DISP 1.10.1R(2))*

### Reporting Periods and Submission

Standard reporting periods are the six months immediately following, and immediately preceding, the firm's accounting reference date. *(DISP 1.10.4R)*

Reports must be submitted within **30 business days** of the end of the relevant period via the FCA Complaints Reporting System or the FCA website. *(DISP 1.10.5R)*

Late submission triggers an administrative fee under SUP 16.3.14R. *(DISP 1.10.6AR)*

### Definition of Closed Complaint

A complaint is **closed** when: (a) the firm has sent a final response, **or** (b) the complainant has indicated in writing acceptance of an earlier response under DISP 1.6.4R. *(DISP 1.10.7R)*

### Contact Point Notification

At the time of authorisation, a firm must provide the FCA with details of a single contact point for complainants. Changes must be notified to the FCA at the latest in the firm's next complaints report. *(DISP 1.10.9R)*

---

## Complaints Data Publication Rules — DISP 1.10A

Firms that report **500 or more complaints** in a reporting period must publish a summary of the complaints data contained in that report (the complaints data summary) in the format set out in DISP 1 Annex 1BR. *(DISP 1.10A.1R)*

Firms with only credit-related or P2P permission with ≤ £5m revenue, or only CMC or funeral plan permissions, have a higher publication threshold of **1,000 or more complaints**.

Publication deadlines: by **31 August** for reporting periods ending between 1 January and 30 June; by **28 February** of the following year for reporting periods ending between 1 July and 31 December. *(DISP 1.10A.3R)*

Firms must confirm publication to the FCA by email to complaintsdatasummary@fca.org.uk immediately after publishing. *(DISP 1.10A.4R)*

---

## Payment Services and Electronic Money Complaints Reporting — DISP 1.10B

### Separate Regime

DISP 1.10B creates a **separate and distinct complaints reporting regime** for:

- Credit institutions providing payment services or issuing electronic money
- Electronic money institutions
- Payment institutions
- Registered account information service providers (RAISPs)

These respondents must submit an **annual** complaints report to the FCA in **DISP 1 Annex 1AD** format. *(DISP 1.10B.1)*

### Broader Scope Than Eligible Complainants

A critical distinction: unlike the standard DISP 1 reporting rules (which cover complaints from eligible complainants only), the DISP 1.10B reporting directions apply **in addition to complaints from payment service users that are not eligible complainants**. *(DISP 1.10B.2G)*

This means a PI or EMI must report ALL payment services and e-money complaints, including those from business customers outside the eligible complainant definition. The Annex 1AA guidance notes explain the service groupings and contextualisation metrics required.

### Reporting Periods and Submission

The DISP 1.10B reporting period is annual, ending on the respondent's accounting reference date (or 31 December where the respondent has no accounting reference date). Reports are due within **30 business days** of the end of the period. *(DISP 1.10B.9–1.10B.10)*

### Data Publication by FCA

Unlike DISP 1.10A, the complaints data publication rules do not directly apply to DISP 1.10B reports. However, the Annex 1AD form asks for the respondent's consent to the FCA publishing the data. The FCA may publish aggregated and anonymised data, and respondent-level data where consent is given. *(DISP 1.10B.14G)*

---

## Key Points for Agents

- DISP 1 applies to FCA-authorised firms and also directly to PSPs, EMIs, and RAISPs that are not otherwise FSMA-authorised (DISP 1.1.10ACR). Outsourcing a complaints function does not transfer responsibility.
- **Standard complaint deadline: 8 weeks** (final response or holding response). **EMD/PSD complaint deadline: 15 business days** (final response) or 35 business days in exceptional circumstances (DISP 1.6.2R, 1.6.2AR). These are hard rules.
- **3-day track**: Complaints resolved by close of business on day 3 are exempt from the time limit rules and forwarding rules. A summary resolution communication is required (lighter than a final response — no need to set out reasons). Acceptance need not be in writing. (DISP 1.5)
- **Root cause analysis is a mandatory rule** (DISP 1.3.3R): firms must identify root causes of recurring/systemic problems and assess whether those causes affect other products and processes not directly complained of. This can trigger proactive remediation of customers who have not complained.
- **Named SMF-level individual required** (DISP 1.3.7R): a firm must appoint an individual carrying out an FCA governing function (or limited scope function) to have personal oversight responsibility for DISP 1 compliance.
- **MiFID complaints rules extend to ECPs** (DISP 1.1A.3R): for investment firms, the MiFID complaints requirements apply to all clients including Eligible Counterparties — not only to eligible complainants.
- **DISP 1.10B is a separate annual reporting regime for PIs/EMIs** (DISP 1.10B.1/2G) in Annex 1AD format, covering ALL payment services complaints including those from non-eligible-complainant business customers — broader scope than the standard DISP 1.10 regime.
- Records must be kept for 3 years (5 years for UCITS) from receipt (DISP 1.9.1R). Publication of complaints data summaries is mandatory for firms reporting 500+ complaints (DISP 1.10A.1R).

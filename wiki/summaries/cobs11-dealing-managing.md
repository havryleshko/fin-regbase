# COBS 11 — Dealing and Managing

## Overview

COBS 11 covers four distinct areas: (1) best execution obligations (three parallel tracks by firm type); (2) client order handling; (3) client limit orders; and (4) personal account dealing. Several sections have been deleted post-MiFID II implementation — notably COBS 11.5 (old record keeping), COBS 11.6 (use of dealing commission), and COBS 11.8 (telephone/electronic communications recording, now in SYSC). COBS 11 Annex 1 (RTS 28 annual best execution reports) has also been deleted.

---

## COBS 11.1 Application

**General application (COBS 11.1.1R):** The chapter applies to a firm.

**Personal account dealing (COBS 11.1.4R):** The personal account dealing sections apply to the *designated investment business* of a firm in relation to activities carried on from an establishment in the United Kingdom.

**Best execution — spread-bets (COBS 11.1.6R):** The MiFID best execution section (COBS 11.2A) does **not** apply to a firm when executing orders, placing orders with other entities for execution, or transmitting orders to other entities for execution, in relation to a spread-bet which is not a financial instrument and where the firm has not made a personal recommendation in relation to that spread-bet.

**Best execution — CIS operators' own units (COBS 11.1.7R):** The best execution sections (COBS 11.2 and COBS 11.2B, as applicable) do **not** apply to a firm when, acting as operator of a regulated collective investment scheme, it purchases or sells units in that scheme.

## COBS 11.2 Best Execution for AIFMs and Residual CIS Operators

### Application

COBS 11.2 applies to small authorised UK AIFMs and residual CIS operators (COBS 18.5.2R), and to full-scope UK AIFMs (COBS 18.5A.3R), with modifications for the latter **(COBS 11.2-7G)**. It does not apply where the fund documents state best execution does not apply and no current or former investor is a retail client **(COBS 11.2-6G)**.

### Core Obligation

**COBS 11.2.1R:** A firm must take **all reasonable steps** to obtain, when executing orders, the best possible result for its clients, taking into account the execution factors.

### Best Execution Criteria

**COBS 11.2.6R:** When executing a client order, a firm must take into account the following criteria for determining the relative importance of the execution factors:
1. The characteristics of the client, including their categorisation as retail or professional
2. The characteristics of the client order
3. The characteristics of the financial instruments subject to that order
4. The characteristics of the execution venues to which that order can be directed

### Role of Price — Retail Client Total Consideration Rule

**COBS 11.2.7R:** Where a firm executes an order on behalf of a retail client, the best possible result must be determined in terms of **total consideration** — the price of the financial instrument plus all execution costs (execution venue fees, clearing, settlement fees, any other third-party fees paid in the course of the order).

**COBS 11.2.8G:** Speed, likelihood of execution and settlement, order size and nature, market impact, and any other implicit transaction costs may be given precedence over the immediate price and cost consideration — but **only insofar as they are instrumental in delivering the best possible result in terms of total consideration**. This is a constraint, not a free weighting choice.

### Order Execution Policy

**COBS 11.2.14R:** A firm must establish and implement effective arrangements for complying with the best execution obligation, including an order execution policy.

**COBS 11.2.15R:** The policy must include, for each class of financial instruments, information on the execution venues where the firm executes client orders, the factors affecting choice of venue, and must at least include those venues that enable the firm to obtain the best possible result on a consistent basis.

**COBS 11.2.19R:** Whenever there is a specific instruction from the client, the firm must execute following that instruction. Doing so satisfies best execution for that aspect only — the firm remains obligated for all parts of the order not covered by the specific instruction **(COBS 11.2.20G)**.

**COBS 11.2.21G:** A firm must not induce a client to instruct it to execute in a particular way when the firm ought reasonably to know that instruction is likely to prevent it from obtaining the best possible result for that client.

**COBS 11.2.22R / 11.2.23R:** A firm must provide appropriate information to clients on its order execution policy. For retail clients, this must be provided in good time before service commencement and must include: (a) account of relative importance of execution factors or the process; (b) list of execution venues on which the firm places significant reliance; (c) clear and prominent warning that specific client instructions may prevent best execution.

**COBS 11.2.24R:** Where the policy provides for execution outside a regulated market or MTF, the firm must in particular inform clients of this possibility.

**COBS 11.2.25R / 11.2.26R:** A firm must obtain the prior consent of its clients to the execution policy; and prior **express** consent before executing any order outside a regulated market or MTF (either as a general agreement or per individual transaction).

**COBS 11.2.27R:** A firm must monitor the effectiveness of its order execution arrangements and policy and notify clients of any **material changes** to those arrangements.

**COBS 11.2.28R:** A firm must review its execution policy and arrangements **at least annually**, and whenever a material change occurs that affects the firm's ability to obtain the best possible result on a consistent basis.

**COBS 11.2.29R:** A firm must be able to demonstrate to its clients, at their request, that it has executed their orders in accordance with its execution policy.

**COBS 11.2.12R:** A firm must not structure or charge its commissions in a way that **discriminates unfairly between execution venues**.

### Portfolio Managers, Receivers and Transmitters

**COBS 11.2.30R / 11.2.31R:** A firm providing portfolio management services or reception and transmission of orders must act in accordance with the best interests of its clients when placing orders with other entities for execution. This requires taking all reasonable steps to obtain the best possible result, establishing and implementing a policy for this purpose, monitoring on a regular basis, and reviewing the policy at least annually **(COBS 11.2.32R)**.

## COBS 11.2A Best Execution — MiFID Provisions

### Application

COBS 11.2A applies to MiFID investment firms. It does **not** apply to UCITS management companies (see COBS 11.2B) or AIFMs (see COBS 11.2). Where a firm providing portfolio management or RTO services also executes the orders received, it must comply with COBS 11.2A.8R and COBS 11.2A.25R **(COBS 11.2A.1R(5))**.

### Core Obligation — Higher Standard

**COBS 11.2A.2R:** A firm must take **all sufficient steps** to obtain, when executing orders, the best possible results for its clients taking into account the execution factors. [Note: MiFID article 27(1)]

The execution factors are: **price, costs, speed, likelihood of execution and settlement, size, nature**, or any other consideration relevant to the execution of an order **(COBS 11.2A.2R(2))**.

The "all sufficient steps" standard is **higher** than the "all reasonable steps" standard in COBS 11.2. This mirrors the deliberate MiFID II uplift.

### Back-to-Back / Matched Principal Dealing

**COBS 11.2A.5G:** Executing orders from different clients on a **matched principal basis** (back-to-back trading) is regarded as acting as principal and is subject to best execution obligations in relation to both the execution of orders on behalf of clients and dealing on own account.

### Best Execution Criteria

**COBS 11.2A.8R(1):** When executing client orders, a firm must take into account the following criteria for determining the relative importance of the execution factors:
- (a) characteristics of the client, including their retail/professional categorisation
- (b) characteristics of the client order, including where it involves a securities financing transaction
- (c) characteristics of the financial instruments subject to that order
- (d) characteristics of the execution venues to which that order can be directed

**COBS 11.2A.8R(2):** A firm satisfies its MiFID best execution obligation to the extent that it executes an order or a specific aspect of an order following specific instructions from the client relating to the order or specific aspect.

**COBS 11.2A.8R(3):** A firm must not structure or charge its commissions in a way as to discriminate unfairly between execution venues.

**COBS 11.2A.8R(4):** A firm must, when executing orders or taking decisions to deal in **OTC products including bespoke products**, check the fairness of the price proposed to the client, by **gathering market data used in the estimation of the price of such product and, where possible, by comparing with similar or comparable products**. This is a hard Rule.

### Role of Price — Retail Client Total Consideration Rule

**COBS 11.2A.9R:** Where a firm executes an order on behalf of a retail client, the best possible result must be determined in terms of **total consideration** — the price of the financial instrument plus all execution costs. [Note: MiFID article 27(1)]

**COBS 11.2A.11G:** Speed, likelihood of execution and settlement, size and nature, market impact, and other implicit transaction costs may be given precedence only insofar as they are instrumental in delivering the best possible result in terms of total consideration to the retail client.

### Prohibition on Receiving Remuneration for Routing — PFOF Anchor Rule

**COBS 11.2A.19R:** A firm must **not receive any remuneration, discount or non-monetary benefit for routing client orders to a particular trading venue or execution venue** which would infringe the requirements on conflicts of interests (as set out in SYSC 10) or inducements as set out in COBS 2.3A (for MiFID business) and COBS 2.3B/COBS 2.3C (for MiFID, equivalent third country or optional exemption business). [Note: MiFID article 27(2)]

This is the **primary anchor rule for Payment for Order Flow** — see [[payment-for-order-flow]] and [[fca-pfof-supervisory-report-2019]] for operational detail on how the FCA applies this rule.

### Order Execution Policy

**COBS 11.2A.20R:** A firm must establish and implement effective arrangements, including an order execution policy. [Note: MiFID article 27(4)]

**COBS 11.2A.21R:** The policy must include, for each class of financial instruments, information on the different execution venues and the factors affecting choice. [Note: MiFID article 27(5)]

**COBS 11.2A.23R:** A firm must obtain the prior consent of its clients to the execution policy. [Note: MiFID article 27(5)]

**COBS 11.2A.24R:** Where the policy provides for execution outside a trading venue, the firm must inform clients and obtain **express prior consent** before executing outside a trading venue (either as a general agreement or per individual transaction). [Note: MiFID article 27(5)]

**COBS 11.2A.25R:** A firm must review its order execution policy and arrangements:
- (a) at least on an **annual basis**; and
- (b) upon occurrence of a **material change** that affects the firm's ability to continue to obtain the best possible result on a consistent basis.

The execution policy information must be customised depending on the class of financial instrument and type of service. For retail clients, the policy must include **(COBS 11.2A.25R(3)–(9))**:
- (a) Account of the relative importance assigned to execution factors, or the process by which the firm determines this
- (b) List of execution venues on which significant reliance is placed, specifying which venues are used for retail client orders, professional client orders, and securities financing transactions per instrument class
- (c) List of factors used to select an execution venue (including qualitative factors such as clearing schemes, circuit breakers, scheduled actions) and the relative importance of each
- (d) How execution factors of price, costs, speed, and likelihood of execution are considered
- (e) Where applicable, information that orders may be executed outside a trading venue, including consequences (e.g. counterparty risk) and statement that additional information is available on request
- (f) Clear and prominent warning that specific client instructions may prevent best execution for covered elements
- (g) Summary of the selection process for venues, execution strategies employed, procedures for analysing execution quality, and how the firm monitors and verifies best possible results

**COBS 11.2A.25R(4):** Where a firm applies different fees depending on the execution venue, it must explain these differences in sufficient detail for the client to understand the advantages and disadvantages of each venue choice.

**COBS 11.2A.25R(6):** A firm must only receive third-party payments that comply with COBS 2.3A and must inform clients about inducements it may receive from execution venues, specifying the fees it charges to all counterparties involved in the transaction.

**COBS 11.2A.31R:** A firm must monitor the effectiveness of its order execution arrangements and execution policy to identify and, where appropriate, correct any deficiencies; must assess on a regular basis whether execution venues included in the policy continue to provide the best possible result; must notify clients of any material changes. [Note: MiFID article 27(7)]

**COBS 11.2A.32R:** A firm must be able to demonstrate to its clients, at their request, that it has executed their orders in accordance with its execution policy; and must be able to demonstrate to the FCA, at its request, its compliance with COBS 11.2A.2R and related provisions. [Note: MiFID article 27(8)]

### Specific Instructions — Partial Carve-Out

**COBS 11.2A.12R:** Whenever there is a specific instruction from a client, a firm must execute the order following the specific instruction. [Note: MiFID article 27(1)]

**COBS 11.2A.13G:** Following a specific instruction satisfies best execution obligations **only in respect of the part or aspect covered**. The fact that a client has given specific instructions covering one part of an order should not release the firm from its best execution obligations in respect of **any other parts or aspects not covered**.

**COBS 11.2A.14G:** A firm must not induce a client to instruct it to execute in a particular way when the firm ought reasonably to know that instruction is likely to prevent it from obtaining the best possible result for that client.

### Duty of Portfolio Managers, Receivers and Transmitters

**COBS 11.2A.34R:** A firm providing portfolio management services must comply with the client's best interests rule when placing orders with other persons for execution. A firm providing reception and transmission of orders must comply with the client's best interests rule when transmitting client orders to other persons for execution. Compliance requires: (1) taking all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result; (2) establishing and implementing a policy identifying execution entities by instrument class; (3) providing information to clients; (4) monitoring on a regular basis; (5) reviewing the policy at least annually.

## COBS 11.2B Best Execution for UCITS Management Companies

### Application

COBS 11.2B applies to a UCITS management company when carrying on scheme management activity, in accordance with COBS 18.5B.2R **(COBS 11.2B.1G)**. References to "scheme" in this section are references to a UCITS scheme.

### Core Obligation

**COBS 11.2B.4R:** A management company must act in the **best interests of each scheme it manages** when executing decisions to deal on behalf of the scheme. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 25(1)]

**COBS 11.2B.5R:** A management company must take all sufficient steps to obtain, when executing decisions to deal, the best possible result for each scheme it manages, taking into account: (1) price; (2) costs; (3) speed; (4) likelihood of execution; (5) likelihood of settlement; (6) order size and nature; and (7) any other consideration relevant to the execution of the decision. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 25(2)]

### Best Execution Criteria — Scheme-Specific

**COBS 11.2B.7R:** A management company must determine the relative importance of the execution factors taking into account:
1. The objectives, investment policy, and risks specific to the scheme (as indicated in its prospectus or instrument constituting the fund)
2. The characteristics of the order, including where it involves a securities financing transaction
3. The characteristics of the financial instruments subject to the order
4. The characteristics of the execution venues

This scheme-level weighting (criterion 1) is the key structural difference from the COBS 11.2A framework, which weights by client characteristics.

### OTC Price Fairness Check

**COBS 11.2B.13R:** When executing orders or taking decisions to deal in OTC products including bespoke products, the management company must check the fairness of the price proposed to the scheme, by gathering market data used to estimate the price and, where possible, by comparing with similar or comparable products.

### Remuneration Prohibition

**COBS 11.2B.10R:** A management company must not receive any remuneration, discount, or non-monetary benefit for routing orders to a particular trading venue or execution venue which would infringe the requirements on conflicts of interest (SYSC 10) or inducements (COBS 2.3 and COBS 18 Annex 1).

### Order Execution Policy

**COBS 11.2B.17R:** A management company must establish and implement effective arrangements, including an order execution policy, to take all sufficient steps to obtain the best possible result for each scheme it manages — both when executing orders on behalf of the scheme and when placing orders with other entities for execution.

**COBS 11.2B.18R:** The policy must include, for each type of financial instrument, information on the different execution venues and must at least include those venues that enable the management company to obtain the best possible result on a consistent basis.

**COBS 11.2B.21R:** The policy must identify, for each type of financial instrument, the entities with which orders are placed or to which the management company transmits orders for execution. Those entities must have execution arrangements that enable the management company to comply with this section.

**COBS 11.2B.26R:** A management company of an ICVC that is a UCITS scheme structured as an investment company must obtain the **prior consent of the ICVC or investment company** to the execution policy.

**COBS 11.2B.27R / 11.2B.28R:** A management company must monitor the effectiveness of its arrangements and policy on a regular basis; must review at least annually and whenever a material change occurs.

**COBS 11.2B.23R:** A management company must be able to demonstrate that it has executed or placed orders on behalf of each scheme it manages in accordance with its execution policy. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, articles 25(5) and 26(4)]

### Information to Unitholders

**COBS 11.2B.31R–11.2B.33R:** A management company must make available appropriate information on its execution policy and any material changes to that policy, to unitholders of each scheme. The information must be customised by financial instrument type and must include: (1) account of the relative importance of execution factors or the process; (2) list of execution venues relied upon significantly, specifying which are used for each type of financial instrument and SFT; (3) information about the management company and entities chosen for execution; (4) list of factors used to select venues including qualitative factors; (5) how execution factors are considered; (6) where applicable, information about execution outside a trading venue and consequences; (7) summary of selection process, execution strategies, quality analysis procedures, and verification of best possible results.

**COBS 11.2B.34R:** This information must be made available in a durable medium, via a website (where that does not constitute a durable medium and website conditions are satisfied), or in the prospectus of the scheme.

**COBS 11.2B.37R:** Upon reasonable request from a unitholder or potential unitholder, a management company must provide information about entities where orders are transmitted or placed for execution.

## COBS 11.3 Client Order Handling

### General Principles

**COBS 11.3.1R:** A firm (other than a UCITS management company providing collective portfolio management services) that is authorised to execute orders on behalf of clients must implement procedures and arrangements which provide for the **prompt, fair and expeditious execution of client orders**, relative to other orders or the trading interests of the firm. These procedures must allow for the execution of otherwise comparable orders in accordance with the time of their reception. [Note: MiFID article 28(1)]

A UCITS management company providing collective portfolio management services must establish and implement procedures providing for the prompt, fair and expeditious execution of portfolio transactions on behalf of the UCITS scheme.

### Carrying Out Client Orders

**COBS 11.3.2AR:** A firm must, when carrying out client orders:
1. Ensure orders executed on behalf of clients are **promptly and accurately recorded and allocated**
2. Carry out otherwise comparable client orders **sequentially and promptly** unless the characteristics of the order or prevailing market conditions make this impracticable, or the interests of the client require otherwise
3. **Inform a retail client** about any material difficulty relevant to the proper carrying out of orders promptly upon becoming aware of the difficulty

### Settlement

**COBS 11.3.4AR:** Where a firm is responsible for overseeing or arranging the settlement of an executed order, it must take all reasonable steps to ensure that any client financial instruments or client money received in settlement are promptly and correctly delivered to the account of the appropriate client.

### No Misuse of Pending Client Order Information

**COBS 11.3.5AR:** A firm must not misuse information relating to pending client orders, and must take all reasonable steps to prevent the misuse of such information by any of its relevant persons.

**COBS 11.3.6G:** Any use by a firm of information relating to a pending client order in order to deal on own account in the financial instruments to which the client order relates, or in related financial instruments, should be considered a misuse of that information.

### Aggregation of Orders

**COBS 11.3.7AR:** A firm must not carry out a client order or a transaction for own account **in aggregation with another client order** unless the following three conditions are met:
1. It is **unlikely** that the aggregation of orders and transactions will work overall to the **disadvantage** of any client whose orders are to be aggregated
2. It is **disclosed** to each client whose order is to be aggregated that the effect of aggregation may work to their disadvantage in relation to a particular order
3. The firm has established and effectively implemented an **order allocation policy** which provides for the fair allocation of aggregated orders, including how volume and price determine allocations and the treatment of partial executions

**COBS 11.3.7BR:** A management company must ensure that the order allocation policy referred to in COBS 11.3.7AR(3) is in sufficiently precise terms.

### Partial Execution of Aggregated Orders

**COBS 11.3.8AR:** Where a firm aggregates an order with one or more other client orders and the aggregated order is partially executed, it must allocate the related trades **in accordance with its order allocation policy**.

### Aggregation with Own Account Transactions

**COBS 11.3.9AR:** A firm which has aggregated transactions for own account with one or more client orders must not allocate the related trades in a way that is detrimental to a client.

**COBS 11.3.10AR:** Where a firm aggregates a client order with a transaction for own account and the aggregated order is **partially executed**, it must allocate the related trades to the **client in priority to the firm**. Exception: where the firm is able to demonstrate on reasonable grounds that without the combination it would not have been able to carry out the order on such advantageous terms, or at all, it may then allocate the transaction for own account proportionally, in accordance with the order allocation policy.

**COBS 11.3.11AR:** The order allocation policy must include procedures to prevent the reallocation of own account transactions, in a way detrimental to the client, which are executed in combination with client orders.

### Record Keeping

**COBS 11.3.15R:** A firm subject to COBS 11.3.2AR to COBS 11.3.13G must retain the records it is required to make under those rules.

## COBS 11.4 Client Limit Orders

**COBS 11.4.1R:** Unless a client expressly instructs otherwise, a firm must, in the case of a client limit order in respect of shares admitted to trading on a regulated market or traded on a trading venue which is **not immediately executed** under prevailing market conditions, take measures to facilitate the **earliest possible execution of that order by making the limit order public immediately** in a manner which is easily accessible to other market participants. [Note: MiFID article 28(2)]

**COBS 11.4.2G:** In respect of transactions executed between eligible counterparties, the obligation to disclose client limit orders should only apply where the counterparty is explicitly sending a limit order to a firm for its execution.

**COBS 11.4.3AR:** A client limit order is considered made available to the public when the investment firm has submitted the order for execution to a regulated market or an MTF, or the order has been published by a person authorised to provide data reporting services under the DRS Regulations, and can be easily executed as soon as market conditions allow.

**COBS 11.4.4AG:** Firms may comply with COBS 11.4.1R by **transmitting the client limit order to a trading venue**.

**COBS 11.4.5R:** The obligation to make a limit order public is **disapplied** in respect of transactions that are large in scale compared with normal market size, as determined under article 4 of MiFIR.

## COBS 11.5A Record Keeping: Client Orders and Transactions

COBS 11.5 (old record keeping) has been deleted. COBS 11.5A is the live record keeping section.

**Scope (COBS 11.5A.1R):** This section does not apply to corporate finance business carried on by a firm which is not a MiFID investment firm.

### Initial Orders

**COBS 11.5A.2R:** A firm must, in relation to every initial order received from a client and every initial decision to deal taken, **immediately record and keep** at the disposal of the FCA at least the details set out in **COBS 11.5A.4R** to the extent applicable. The 16 minimum fields include: client name and designation; relevant person name; Trader ID; Algo ID; B/S indicator; instrument identification; unit price and notation; price; price multiplier; currencies (1 and 2); initial quantity and notation; validity period; order type; any other conditions/instructions; date and exact time of receipt (measured per MiFID RTS 25 methodology).

### Transaction and Order Processing Records

**COBS 11.5A.3R:** A firm must, immediately after receiving a client order or making a decision to deal, record and keep at the disposal of the FCA at least the details set out in **COBS 11.5A.5R**. The 40 minimum fields include all of the above plus: transaction reference number; Order ID; order ID assigned by trading venue; unique aggregated order identification (format "aggregated_X"); segment MIC code of the trading venue; name of person to whom order was transmitted; seller and buyer designation; trading capacity; Trader/Algo ID for execution; B/S indicator; instrument identification; ultimate underlying; put/call identifier; strike price; upfront payment; delivery type; option style; maturity date; price; price multiplier; currencies; remaining/modified/executed quantities; precise date and exact timestamps for: submission, trading venue events, and messages to/from other investment firms (all per MiFID RTS 25); each placed order's sequence of events; short selling flag; SSR exemption flag; waiver flag.

## COBS 11.7 Personal Account Dealing (Non-MiFID)

**Application (COBS 11.7.-1R):** This section does **not** apply to a firm in relation to MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business (which is governed by COBS 11.7A).

### Core Obligation

**COBS 11.7.1R:** A firm that conducts designated investment business must establish, implement and maintain adequate arrangements aimed at preventing relevant persons who are involved in activities that may give rise to a conflict of interest, or who have access to inside information or to other confidential information relating to clients or transactions, from:
1. Entering into a personal transaction that: (a) is prohibited under the Market Abuse Regulation; (b) involves misuse or improper disclosure of confidential information; or (c) conflicts or is likely to conflict with an obligation of the firm to a customer under the regulatory system or MiFID or the UCITS Directive
2. Advising or procuring any other person to enter into such a transaction (other than in the proper course of employment)
3. Disclosing any information or opinion to any other person if the relevant person knows or reasonably ought to know that as a result of the disclosure that other person will or would be likely to enter into such a transaction or advise another to do so

[Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 13(1)]

### Implementation Requirements

**COBS 11.7.4R:** The arrangements must in particular ensure that:
1. Each relevant person covered is aware of the restrictions on personal transactions and the measures established in connection with personal transactions and disclosure
2. The firm is informed promptly of any personal transaction entered into by a relevant person (by notification or by other identifying procedures); or in the case of outsourcing, the service provider maintains a record of personal transactions by any relevant person and provides that information to the firm promptly on request
3. A record is kept of the personal transaction notified to or identified by the firm, including any authorisation or prohibition in connection with it

### Disapplications

**COBS 11.7.5R:** This section does not apply to:
1. Personal transactions under a discretionary portfolio management service where there is no prior communication between the portfolio manager and the relevant person (or other person for whose account the transaction is executed)
2. Personal transactions in units or shares in collective undertakings that comply with UCITS conditions or are subject to EEA-State equivalent supervision, where the relevant person and any other person for whose account the transactions are effected are not involved in the management of that undertaking
3. Personal transactions in life policies

## COBS 11.7A Personal Account Dealing — MiFID Business

**Application (COBS 11.7A.1R):** This chapter applies to a firm in relation to its MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business.

**COBS 11.7A.3R:** A firm that conducts designated investment business must establish appropriate rules governing personal transactions by **managers, employees, and tied agents**. [Note: MiFID article 16(2)]

### Requirements

**COBS 11.7A.5R:** A firm must establish, implement and maintain adequate arrangements to prevent relevant persons from:
1. (para 2) Entering into a personal transaction that meets at least one of three criteria: (a) prohibited under the Market Abuse Regulation; (b) involves the misuse or improper disclosure of confidential information; (c) conflicts or is likely to conflict with an obligation of the firm under UK law on markets in financial instruments
2. (para 3) Advising or recommending any other person (other than in the proper course of employment) to enter into a transaction in financial instruments which, if it were a personal transaction of the relevant person, would be covered by (1) or a relevant provision
3. (para 4) Subject to MAR Article 10(1), disclosing information or opinions where the relevant person knows or reasonably ought to know that the recipient will or would be likely to enter into such a transaction or advise another to do so

**COBS 11.7A.5R(5):** The arrangements must ensure: (a) relevant persons are aware of restrictions and measures; (b) the firm is informed promptly of personal transactions entered into by relevant persons; (c) a record is kept of transactions notified or identified, including any authorisation or prohibition.

**COBS 11.7A.5R(5A):** In the case of outsourcing, the firm must ensure the service provider maintains a record of personal transactions by relevant persons and provides that information to the firm promptly on request.

### Disapplications

**COBS 11.7A.2R(3):** The requirements in COBS 11.7A.5R do not apply in respect of:
- (a) Personal transactions under a discretionary portfolio management service where there is no prior communication between the portfolio manager and the relevant person (or other person for whose account the transaction is executed)
- (b) Personal transactions in units or shares in UCITS or AIFs subject to supervision under UK law requiring equivalent risk spreading, where the relevant person and any other person for whose account the transactions are effected are not involved in the management of that undertaking

## Key Points for Agents

- **Three parallel best execution tracks with different standards:** COBS 11.2 (AIFMs/residual CIS operators) requires "all reasonable steps"; COBS 11.2A (MiFID firms) requires "all sufficient steps" (a deliberately higher MiFID II bar); COBS 11.2B (UCITS management companies) applies "all sufficient steps" at scheme level, weighted by scheme investment policy and objectives.
- **Retail client total consideration rule applies to all three tracks:** For retail clients, best result = price + execution costs (total consideration). Speed, likelihood, size etc. can only take precedence insofar as they are instrumental in delivering best total consideration — this is a constraint, not a free weighting override **(COBS 11.2.7R; 11.2A.9R; see also 11.2B.5R)**.
- **OTC price fairness check is a hard Rule for MiFID firms and UCITS ManCos:** When executing OTC products (including bespoke), firms must gather market data to check price fairness and, where possible, compare with similar/comparable products **(COBS 11.2A.8R(4); COBS 11.2B.13R)**.
- **PFOF prohibition anchor rule:** COBS 11.2A.19R prohibits a MiFID firm from receiving any remuneration, discount or non-monetary benefit for routing client orders to a venue in a way that infringes SYSC 10 conflicts rules or COBS 2.3A inducements rules. This is the primary sourcebook anchor for PFOF prohibition.
- **Specific client instructions — partial carve-out only:** Following a specific client instruction satisfies best execution for that aspect only. The firm remains obligated for all other parts of the order not covered by the instruction. Firms must not induce clients to give instructions they know will prevent best execution **(COBS 11.2A.12R–11.2A.14G)**.
- **Client order handling — three sequential execution obligations:** (1) prompt and accurate recording/allocation; (2) sequential execution for comparable orders unless impracticable; (3) retail client notification of material difficulties. Aggregation with own account requires prior disclosure, order allocation policy, and client priority on partial executions **(COBS 11.3.2AR; COBS 11.3.7AR; COBS 11.3.10AR)**.
- **Unexecuted limit orders must be made public immediately** for shares admitted to trading on a regulated market or trading venue, unless the client instructs otherwise or the order is large in scale (MiFIR article 4 exemption) **(COBS 11.4.1R)**.
- **Record keeping — 16 fields for initial orders, 40 fields for transaction processing:** MiFID RTS 25 timestamps required; immediate recording obligation **(COBS 11.5A.2R–11.5A.5R)**.

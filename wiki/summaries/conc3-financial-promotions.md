# CONC 3 — Financial Promotions and Communications with Customers

## Overview

CONC 3 sets out the rules and guidance governing financial promotions and communications made in connection with consumer credit, consumer hire, and debt activities. It imposes sector-specific obligations that sit alongside the Financial Promotions Order (FPO) and s.21 FSMA regime — not instead of them.

The chapter applies to: firms communicating financial promotions to persons in the UK; firms approving financial promotions for communication by unauthorised persons; and, in some sections, firms communicating with existing customers.

---

## 3.1 — Application

CONC 3 applies to financial promotions for consumer credit, consumer hire, debt counselling, debt adjusting, and certain information services **(CONC 3.1.1R)**. It does not apply to qualifying credit (mortgages etc. governed by MCOB), business credit, or promotions not targeted at customers in the UK.

**Three exclusions of note:**

- **Excluded communications** (as defined in COBS/FPO): fall outside CONC 3 **(CONC 3.1.3R)**.
- **Business promotions:** promotions for credit solely in connection with a business are excluded **(CONC 3.1.5G)**.
- **Image advertising:** a promotion that contains only the firm's name, logo, contact details, and a brief description of the firm's activities is excluded from the representative example and APR rules — but remains subject to CONC 3.3.1R (clear, fair and not misleading), APR rules if any cost is mentioned, and the restricted expressions rules **(CONC 3.1.12G)**.

The jurisdictional rule for CONC 3 differs from the rest of CONC: CONC 3 applies to any promotion **communicated to a person** in the UK — the trigger is the recipient's location, not the customer's habitual residence **(CONC 3.1.6G)**.

---

## 3.2 — General Guidance: "Prominent" and Approver Liability

**Prominent** means that a requirement would draw the attention of an average customer **(CONC 3.2.1G)**. This is the standard applied wherever CONC 3 requires information to be prominent — including risk warnings, representative examples, and APR disclosures.

Firms that approve financial promotions under s.21 FSMA and s.55NA FCA permission must comply with the full CONC 3 requirements. The approver takes on responsibility for the communication meeting CONC standards — not just the CFNM standard but also all specific mandatory content rules **(CONC 3.2.3G)**.

---

## 3.3 — Clear, Fair and Not Misleading

The CFNM standard in CONC 3.3.1R is the foundational rule for all financial promotions in CONC scope. For full treatment see **[[conc-financial-promotions-cfnm]]**.

**Five elements** — a financial promotion must be:

1. Clearly identifiable as a financial promotion
2. Accurate and not emphasising potential benefits while downplaying risks
3. Balanced — risks and consequences are fairly and prominently shown
4. Sufficient for the target audience to understand the key features and risks
5. Not designed to disguise, diminish, or conceal material information

**(CONC 3.3.1R)**

**Additional rules:**

- **Comparisons (3.3.1R(1B)):** Must be fair, balanced, and meaningful.
- **No credit regardless:** A promotion must not state or imply that credit is available regardless of financial status, income, or commitments **(CONC 3.3.3R)**.
- **BNPL promotional rate (3.3.11AR):** A promotion for a credit agreement that includes a promotional interest rate must not omit information about material risks — in particular, that interest will be charged if the balance is not cleared by the end of the promotional period.
- **Overdraft must say "borrowing" (3.3.12R):** A promotion for an overdraft — or any non-business credit — must use the word "borrowing" where the promotion refers to money available or any similar concept.
- **Unfair practice examples (3.3.10G):** Nine named examples of promotions that are likely to contravene CONC 3.3.1R, including: implying speed or ease of credit without proper risk context; creating false urgency; exploiting vulnerability; omitting mandatory cost disclosures.

---

## 3.4 — HCSTC Mandatory Risk Warning

Every financial promotion for high-cost short-term credit (HCSTC) — including payday loans — must carry the following **verbatim warning** **(CONC 3.4.1R)**:

> **Warning: Late repayment can cause you serious money problems. For help, go to moneyhelper.org.uk**

The warning must be **prominent**. The MoneyHelper logo is an approved alternative to the verbatim text. There is no option to paraphrase or abbreviate. The requirement applies to all media — digital, print, outdoor, broadcast.

---

## 3.5 — Promotions for Unsecured Credit: Representative Example and APR

For full treatment of the representative example and APR trigger rules, see **[[conc-representative-example-apr]]**.

**When a representative example is required:**

If a financial promotion includes any indication of the rate of interest or the cost of credit, it must include a **representative example** and the firm's postal address **(CONC 3.5.3R)**.

**Eight mandatory elements of the representative example (CONC 3.5.5R):**

1. Rate of interest (stating whether fixed or variable)
2. Any other charges included in the total charge for credit
3. Total amount of credit
4. Representative APR
5. Cash price and amount of any advance payment (purchase promotions)
6. Duration
7. Total amount payable
8. Amount of each repayment (where available)

All eight elements must be given **equal prominence**, be clearly labelled "representative example", and the example must be given **no less prominence** than any other cost information or any incentive to apply.

**Three APR triggers (CONC 3.5.7R):** Even without a full example, a representative APR must be stated alone where the promotion:

1. Implies that credit is restricted to or only available to certain applicants
2. Makes a favourable comparison with another product or provider
3. Offers an incentive to apply — including gifts, discounts, cashback, loyalty points, or by emphasising speed or ease of application

**APR formatting (CONC 3.5.9R):** Must be shown as "%APR". Variable rate products: "variable". Representative figure: "representative". Written overdraft promotions must include the statement "How does our overdraft compare?" followed by the prescribed comparison table.

**Compulsory ancillary services (CONC 3.5.10R):** Where a product requires a compulsory ancillary service (e.g. mandatory insurance), the promotion must include a clear, concise and prominent statement to that effect, presented alongside the representative APR.

**Security statement (CONC 3.5.11R):** Where credit is or may be secured, the promotion must state that security is or may be required and specify its nature.

**Restricted expressions — unsecured (CONC 3.5.12R):** Certain expressions are prohibited unless the literal condition is satisfied:

| Expression | Permitted only when |
|---|---|
| "overdraft" | The agreement is a genuine overdraft |
| "interest free" | Total amount payable ≤ cash price |
| "no deposit" | No advance payment is required |
| "gift" / "present" | No repayment condition attached |
| "weekly equivalent" | Repayments are actually made weekly |

## 3.6 — Promotions for Credit Secured on Land

### Mandatory Verbatim Warnings

Three scenarios each require specific verbatim prominence warnings **(CONC 3.6.5R)**:

**Standard (all secured promotions):**
> *"YOUR HOME MAY BE REPOSSESSED IF YOU DO NOT KEEP UP REPAYMENTS ON A MORTGAGE OR ANY OTHER DEBT SECURED ON IT."*

**Debt consolidation:**
> *"THINK CAREFULLY BEFORE SECURING OTHER DEBTS AGAINST YOUR HOME. YOUR HOME MAY BE REPOSSESSED IF YOU DO NOT KEEP UP REPAYMENTS ON A MORTGAGE OR ANY OTHER DEBT SECURED ON IT."*

**Interest roll-up / equity release (where no/deferred repayments):**
> *"CHECK THAT THIS MORTGAGE WILL MEET YOUR NEEDS IF YOU WANT TO MOVE OR SELL YOUR HOME OR YOU WANT YOUR FAMILY TO INHERIT IT. IF YOU ARE IN ANY DOUBT, SEEK INDEPENDENT ADVICE."*

**Foreign currency (where applicable):**
> *"CHANGES IN THE EXCHANGE RATE MAY INCREASE THE STERLING EQUIVALENT OF YOUR DEBT."*

These warnings must be given **greater prominence** than any rate of charge (other than the typical APR) and any incentive in the promotion **(CONC 3.6.5R)**.

### Additional Restricted Expressions — Secured (CONC 3.6.8R)

On top of the unsecured restricted expressions, secured promotions also prohibit:

| Expression | Permitted only when |
|---|---|
| "loan guaranteed" / "pre-approved" | Lending is truly unconditional on any underwriting |
| "no credit checks" | No credit check is in fact carried out |

## 3.7 — Credit Broker Promotions

Credit brokers have three specific disclosure obligations in promotions **(CONC 3.7)**:

1. **Extent of powers:** Must indicate in any promotion intended for individuals the extent and basis of their powers and the extent of any independence **(CONC 3.7.3R)**.
2. **Legal name:** Must specify the firm's full legal FSR-registered name, not merely the trading name **(CONC 3.7.5R)**.
3. **Broker identification:** Must prominently state it is a credit broker, not a lender, where the firm is not also the lender **(CONC 3.7.7R)**. A dual-role firm promoting its broker activities must state it is not currently promoting its lender activities.

---

## 3.7A — P2P Promotions

P2P platform operators promoting lending opportunities to prospective lenders must meet equivalent transparency obligations to credit brokers — disclosing the nature of their role, the basis of the platform, and the legal identity of the operator **(CONC 3.7A.2R)**.

---

## 3.8 — Lender Unfair Practices

Three specific lender prohibitions in financial promotions **(CONC 3.8.2R)**:

1. **Pre-completed credit amounts:** A lender must not communicate a promotion where the amount of credit has been pre-completed by the lender and is not based on a creditworthiness assessment of the customer.
2. **Equity-only lending implication:** Must not imply that lending is solely or primarily dependent on the value of the customer's property or equity, rather than the customer's ability to repay.
3. **Known unsuitable credit:** Must not promote credit to a customer where the firm knows or has reason to believe that the credit is unsuitable for that customer's financial circumstances.

## 3.9 — Debt Counsellor and Debt Adjuster Promotions

### Mandatory 16-Item Disclosure (CONC 3.9.3R)

Every promotion by a debt counsellor or debt adjuster must disclose all of the following:

| # | Required item |
|---|---|
| 1 | Services offered |
| 2 | Any business associates and the basis of the association |
| 3 | Fee level and how it is calculated |
| 4 | Whether any third-party services form part of what is offered |
| 5 | Whether the customer may complain to FOS and how |
| 6 | Whether the firm is profit-seeking |
| 7 | Whether the firm receives payment from the customer (vs. from creditors) |
| 8 | That free and impartial advice is available from not-for-profit bodies (with signposting) |
| 9 | Options for dealing with debts (including advantages and disadvantages of each) |
| 10 | That the firm's advice may impact the customer's credit rating |
| 11 | That an IVA, PTD (Scotland), or DRO entry will be recorded on a public register |
| 12 | Insolvency risks — specifically five named risks: (a) loss of home; (b) loss of certain assets; (c) credit rating impact; (d) public notice; (e) restrictions on financial activities |
| 13 | That alternative options may be available |
| 14 | The name of the compensation scheme the firm belongs to |
| 15–16 | Whether the firm acts in any capacity other than debt counsellor/adjuster; and if so, the nature of that other activity |

### Five Prohibitions (CONC 3.9.5R)

A debt counsellor or adjuster must not:

1. Claim to be free, impartial, or independent if it is not
2. Claim to be a charity or not-for-profit body if it is not
3. Promote a claims management service as if it were a debt management service
4. Guarantee a favourable outcome with a lender or creditor
5. Direct customers to premium rate telephone numbers in ways that are unfair

### Look-Alike Website and SEO Prohibition (CONC 3.9.7R)

A firm must not:
- Operate a website designed to appear to be from a not-for-profit or free debt advice service when it is in fact a commercial firm
- Use SEO techniques, paid search, or other digital tools to attract customers who are searching for free or not-for-profit debt advice into visiting a commercial debt management site

## 3.10 — Promotions Not in Writing (Off-Premises)

A promotion made orally outside trade premises must **(CONC 3.10.2R)**:
- Be made only at an appropriate time
- Clearly identify the person and the firm making the promotion at the outset
- State the purpose of the communication at the outset

**Criminal offence — canvassing (CONC 3.10.3G):** Under CCA s.49, it is a **criminal offence** to canvass regulated credit agreements (borrower-lender type) off trade premises unless the visit or call was made pursuant to a prior signed written request from the customer **specifically for the purpose of discussing further borrowing**. Blanket or standing permissions ("umbrella requests") — such as a standing authority granted as part of an existing agreement — do not meet the statutory test. A valid request requires a positive, specific act by the borrower. Note: this is a CCA offence, not merely an FCA breach **(CONC 3.10.3G)**.

---

## 3.11 — Cannot Approve Interactive Promotions

A firm **must not** approve a financial promotion that is to be made in the course of:
- A personal visit
- A telephone conversation, or
- Any other interactive dialogue

**(CONC 3.11.2R)**

This is absolute — there is no authorisation route for real-time interactive financial promotions. The rationale is that an approver cannot assess compliance with CONC 3 requirements for a promotion whose content will be determined dynamically in real-time interaction.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- CONC 3.3.1R five-element CFNM standard: identifiable; accurate; balanced/risks fairly shown; sufficient for target audience; no disguise/concealment of material information. Applies to all CONC financial promotions.
- HCSTC: verbatim risk warning mandatory — "Warning: Late repayment can cause you serious money problems. For help, go to moneyhelper.org.uk" — must be prominent. No paraphrase permitted.
- Representative example: triggered whenever cost of credit or interest rate is mentioned; 8 elements, all equal prominence, labelled "representative example." Three additional triggers require APR alone (restricted audience implication; favourable comparison; incentive to apply including speed/ease of application).
- Five restricted expressions for unsecured (3.5.12R): "overdraft", "interest free", "no deposit", "gift/present", "weekly equivalent" — each permitted only when the literal product condition is met. Secured adds: "loan guaranteed", "pre-approved", "no credit checks" (3.6.8R).
- Land-secured: three separate verbatim warnings (standard; debt consolidation; equity release) — must have greater prominence than any rate other than typical APR, and any incentive.
- Debt counsellors: 16-item mandatory disclosure in every promotion; look-alike website and SEO manipulation for free-advice seekers prohibited; premium rate referrals prohibited.
- CCA s.49 criminal offence: canvassing borrower-lender agreements off trade premises without a signed, specific, customer-initiated request. Umbrella standing permissions are invalid.
- Cannot approve: firms cannot approve financial promotions for personal visits, telephone conversations, or other interactive real-time dialogues (3.11.2R).

# PROD 3 — Product Governance: MiFID

PROD 3 implements MiFID II product governance obligations for firms that manufacture and/or distribute financial instruments. It has two sections: **PROD 3.2** (manufacturer obligations) and **PROD 3.3** (distributor obligations). References to financial instruments include structured deposits **(PROD 3.1.1R)**.

---

## PROD 3.1 — General and Proportionality

**(PROD 3.1.2R)** A firm must comply with PROD 3 in a way that is appropriate and proportionate, taking into account:
1. The nature of the financial instrument or investment service
2. The target market for the financial instrument

**(PROD 3.1.3G)** A plain vanilla listed bond issued by an ESCC issuer or ESCC subsidiary is an example of an instrument where compliance with the rules could be relatively simple on an execution-only basis, as such instruments are compatible with the needs of the mass retail market.

---

## PROD 3.2 — Manufacturer Obligations

### General Obligations (PROD 3.2.1R)

A manufacturer must:
1. Ensure that financial instruments are designed to meet the needs of an identified target market of end clients within the relevant category of clients
2. Ensure the distribution strategy is compatible with the identified target market
3. Take reasonable steps to ensure the financial instrument is distributed to the identified target market

Target market considerations must permeate all aspects of product development and distribution, including selection of appropriate distribution channels and ensuring promotions are accompanied by sufficient and correct information **(PROD 3.2.2G)**.

### Product Approval Process (PROD 3.2.3R–3.2.4R)

**(PROD 3.2.3R)** A manufacturer must maintain, operate and review a process for the approval of:
1. Each financial instrument
2. Significant adaptations of existing financial instruments

in each case **before** they are marketed or distributed to clients.

**(PROD 3.2.4R)** For each financial instrument, the product approval process must:
1. Specify an identified target market of end clients within the relevant category of clients
2. Ensure that all relevant risks to the identified target market are assessed
3. Ensure that the intended distribution strategy is consistent with the identified target market

### Target Market (PROD 3.2.8R–3.2.11R)

**(PROD 3.2.8R)** Manufacturers must identify the potential target market for each financial instrument at a **sufficiently granular level** and must:
1. Specify the type or types of client for whose needs, characteristics and objectives the financial instrument is compatible — the **positive target market**
2. Identify any group or groups of client for whose needs, characteristics and objectives the financial instrument is **not** compatible — the **negative target market**

Both positive and negative target markets are mandatory. An assessment without a negative target market is incomplete.

**(PROD 3.2.9G)** The required level of granularity depends on the financial instrument. Simpler instruments (e.g., plain vanilla bonds) require less detail; complex instruments (e.g., bail-inable securities, less common instruments) require more detail.

**(PROD 3.2.10R)** Manufacturers must determine for each financial instrument whether it meets the identified needs, characteristics and objectives of the target market by examining:
1. Whether the financial instrument's **risk/reward profile is consistent** with the target market
2. Whether the design of the financial instrument is driven by **features that benefit the client** and **not** by a business model which relies on **poor client outcomes** to be profitable

The second limb (2) is the **business model integrity test**: a financial instrument designed primarily to generate revenue from client detriment (e.g., through excessive charges, features that generate revenue when clients take unfavourable outcomes, or a structure that profits from client error) fails PROD 3.2.10R regardless of how the target market is defined.

**(PROD 3.2.11R)** For financial instruments distributed through other firms, manufacturers must determine the needs and characteristics of clients for whom the product is compatible based on:
1. Their theoretical knowledge of, and past experience with, the financial instrument or similar instruments
2. The financial markets
3. The needs, characteristics and objectives of potential end clients

### Product Scenario Analysis (PROD 3.2.12R–3.2.13R)

**(PROD 3.2.12R)** Manufacturers must undertake scenario analysis to assess:
1. The risks of poor outcomes for end clients posed by the financial instrument
2. In which circumstances those poor outcomes may occur

**(PROD 3.2.13R)** The scenario analysis must assess the financial instrument under **negative conditions** covering what would happen if:
1. The market environment deteriorated
2. The manufacturer or a third party involved in manufacturing or the functioning of the financial instrument experiences financial difficulties or counterparty risk materialises
3. The financial instrument fails to become commercially viable
4. Demand is much higher than anticipated, putting a strain on the firm's resources and/or the market of the underlying financial instrument

This is **instrument-level** scenario testing — it assesses how the specific product performs under stress, not whether the firm as a whole survives. See the 2021 multi-firm review at [[mifid-ii-product-governance-review-2021.md]] for named failure modes.

### Charging Structure Assessment (PROD 3.2.14R)

**(PROD 3.2.14R)** Manufacturers must consider the charging structure proposed for each financial instrument, including examination of:
1. Whether the financial instrument's costs and charges are **compatible with the needs, objectives and characteristics** of the target market
2. Whether the charges **undermine the financial instrument's return expectations** — for example, where costs or charges equal, exceed or remove almost all the expected tax advantages linked to a financial instrument
3. Whether the charging structure of the financial instrument is **appropriately transparent** for the target market, such that it does not disguise charges or is too complex to understand

### Market Integrity (PROD 3.2.15R)

**(PROD 3.2.15R)** Manufacturers must consider whether the financial instrument may represent a **threat to the orderly functioning, or to the stability, of financial markets** before deciding to proceed with the launch.

### Information Disclosure to Distributors (PROD 3.2.16R)

**(PROD 3.2.16R)** A manufacturer must make available to any distributor of that financial instrument:
1. All appropriate information on the financial instrument
2. All appropriate information on the product approval process
3. The identified target market of the financial instrument, **including information about the target market assessment undertaken**
4. Information about the appropriate channels for distribution of the financial instrument

This information must be at an adequate standard to enable distributors to understand and recommend or sell the financial instrument properly.

Where information is not intended for end client use, the manufacturer should make this clear **(PROD 3.2.17G)**.

### Review of Financial Instruments (PROD 3.2.19R–3.2.26R)

**(PROD 3.2.19R)** A manufacturer must regularly review the financial instruments it manufactures, taking into account any event that could materially affect the potential risk to the identified target market. For each financial instrument, this review must assess:
- (a) Whether the financial instrument remains consistent with the needs, characteristics and objectives of the identified target market
- (b) Whether the intended distribution strategy remains appropriate
- (c) Whether the financial instrument is being distributed to the target market
- (d) Whether the financial instrument **is reaching clients for whose needs, characteristics and objectives the financial instrument is not compatible** — i.e., whether it is leaking into the negative target market

**(PROD 3.2.20G)** To carry out these reviews, manufacturers should collect and analyse management information to detect patterns in distribution as compared with the planned target market.

**(PROD 3.2.21G)** When reviewing financial instruments, firms should communicate to the end client contractual "breakpoints" (such as the end of a long tie-in period) that may have a material impact and that the client cannot reasonably be expected to already know. Where the manufacturer does not know the identity of the end client, it should communicate breakpoints to the distributor.

**(PROD 3.2.22R)** Manufacturers must review financial instruments **prior to any further issue or re-launch** if they are aware of any event that could materially affect the potential risk to clients, and must identify crucial events that would affect the potential risk or return expectations of the financial instrument.

**(PROD 3.2.23G)** Crucial events include: (1) the crossing of a threshold that will affect the return profile; (2) the solvency of certain issuers whose securities and guarantees may impact performance.

**(PROD 3.2.24R)** When a crucial event occurs, the manufacturer must take appropriate action, which may include:
1. Providing relevant information on the event and its consequences to clients or distributors
2. Changing the product approval process
3. Stopping further issuance
4. Changing the financial instrument to avoid unfair contract terms
5. Reconsidering whether sales channels are appropriate
6. Contacting the distributor to discuss modification of the distribution process
7. Terminating the relationship with the distributor
8. Informing the FCA

**(PROD 3.2.25R–3.2.26R)** Manufacturers must review financial instruments at **regular intervals**, with the frequency determined based on relevant factors including the complexity or innovative nature of the investment strategies pursued.

### Conflicts of Interest in Product Design (PROD 3.2.27R–3.2.30R)

**(PROD 3.2.27R)** Manufacturers must establish, implement and maintain procedures to ensure that the manufacture of financial instruments complies with conflicts of interest requirements (SYSC 10.1.7R), including remuneration.

**(PROD 3.2.28R)** Manufacturers must ensure that the design of each financial instrument, including its features, does not:
1. Adversely affect end clients
2. Lead to problems with market integrity by enabling the firm to mitigate and/or dispose of its own risks or exposure to the underlying assets of the product where the firm already holds those underlying assets on its own account

**(PROD 3.2.29R)** Each time a financial instrument is manufactured, the manufacturer must analyse potential conflicts of interest.

**(PROD 3.2.30R)** In analysing potential conflicts of interest, manufacturers must assess whether the financial instrument creates a situation where end clients may be adversely affected if end clients take:
1. An exposure opposite to the one previously held by the manufacturer itself
2. An exposure opposite to the one the manufacturer wants to hold after the sale of the product

These are the "opposite position" conflict scenarios — they arise where the manufacturer uses product issuance to offload unwanted risk onto clients.

### Oversight, Training and Compliance Reports (PROD 3.2.31R–3.2.36R)

**(PROD 3.2.31R)** Manufacturers must ensure that their management bodies have effective control over their product governance process.

**(PROD 3.2.32R)** The development and periodic review of product governance arrangements must be monitored by the person allocated the **compliance oversight function** of a firm in order to detect any risk of failure by the manufacturer to comply with applicable provisions of PROD.

**(PROD 3.2.33R)** All relevant staff involved in the manufacturing of financial instruments must possess the **necessary expertise** to understand the characteristics and risks of the financial instruments they intend to manufacture.

**(PROD 3.2.35R)** Compliance reports to the management body must include information about the financial instruments that the firm has manufactured, including information on the distribution strategy.

**(PROD 3.2.36R)** Manufacturers must make compliance reports available to the FCA on request.

## PROD 3.3 — Distributor Obligations

### General Obligations (PROD 3.3.1R)

**(PROD 3.3.1R)** A distributor must:
1. Understand the financial instruments it distributes to clients
2. Assess the **compatibility** of the financial instruments with the needs of the clients to whom it distributes investment services, taking into account the manufacturer's identified target market of end clients
3. Ensure that financial instruments are distributed only when this is **in the best interests of the client** (COBS 2.1.1R)

### Obtaining Information from Manufacturers (PROD 3.3.3R–3.3.8R)

**(PROD 3.3.3R)** Distributors must obtain from manufacturers subject to PROD 3.2 information to gain the necessary understanding and knowledge of the financial instruments they intend to distribute in order to ensure they will be distributed in accordance with the needs, characteristics and objectives of the target market.

**(PROD 3.3.4G)** In ensuring sufficient understanding, distributors:
1. Should review materials provided by the manufacturer or earlier distributor
2. Should ask the manufacturer for additional information or training if necessary to understand the financial instrument adequately
3. Must not distribute the financial instrument if they do not understand it sufficiently
4. When providing information to a further distributor in a chain, should consider how that distributor will use the information

### Third Country Manufacturers (PROD 3.3.5R–3.3.7R)

**(PROD 3.3.5R)** Distributors must take all reasonable steps to comply with PROD 3.3 when distributing financial instruments manufactured by any firm to which PROD 3.2 does not apply (including third country investment firms). As part of this, distributors must put in place effective arrangements to obtain sufficient, adequate and reliable information from the manufacturer. This obligation applies to financial instruments sold on either the primary or secondary market.

**(PROD 3.3.6R)** The obligation to obtain adequate and reliable information applies proportionately, depending on: (1) the degree to which publicly available information is obtainable; and (2) the complexity of the financial instrument.

**(PROD 3.3.7R)** Where relevant information is not publicly available, distributors must take all reasonable steps to obtain it from the manufacturer or its agent.

### Distributor Target Market Identification (PROD 3.3.9R–3.3.14R)

**(PROD 3.3.9R)** Distributors must determine the target market for the respective financial instrument, **even if the target market was not defined by the manufacturer**. The distributor cannot rely on a manufacturer's failure to identify a target market as a basis for proceeding without one.

**(PROD 3.3.10R)** Distributors must identify the target market and their distribution strategy using:
1. The information obtained from manufacturers
2. Information they have on their own clients

**(PROD 3.3.14R)** Where a firm acts both as a manufacturer and a distributor, only **one target market assessment** is required.

### Distributor Product Governance Arrangements (PROD 3.3.15R–3.3.16R)

**(PROD 3.3.15R)** Distributors must have in place adequate product governance arrangements to ensure that:
- (a) The financial instruments and investment services they intend to distribute are **compatible with the needs, characteristics and objectives of the identified target market**
- (b) The intended distribution strategy is **consistent with the identified target market**

Additionally, distributors must:
- (2) Appropriately identify and assess the circumstances and needs of the clients they intend to focus on, to ensure their clients' interests are not compromised as a result of commercial or funding pressures
- (3) Identify any groups of end clients for whose needs, characteristics and objectives the financial instrument or investment service **is not compatible**

**(PROD 3.3.16R)** Distributors must **periodically review** their product governance arrangements under PROD 3.3.15R and take appropriate actions where necessary to ensure they remain robust and fit for purpose.

### Distributor Compliance Checklist (PROD 3.3.18R)

**(PROD 3.3.18R)** Distributors must have in place procedures and measures to ensure that when deciding the range of financial instruments and investment services to be distributed, and the target market, all applicable rules are complied with, including but not limited to:
1. **Disclosure** — COBS 4 and COBS 14.3A
2. **Suitability** — COBS 9A
3. **Appropriateness** — COBS 10A
4. **Inducements** — COBS 2.3A
5. **Conflicts of interest** — SYSC 10.1

This is the primary compliance gate for distributors — PROD 3.3 does not replace these obligations; it adds to them.

### Distributor Oversight and Training (PROD 3.3.20R–3.3.22R)

**(PROD 3.3.20R)** The development and periodic review of product governance arrangements must be monitored by the person allocated the compliance oversight function of a firm in order to detect any risk of failure by the distributor to comply with applicable provisions of PROD.

**(PROD 3.3.21R)** The management body of a distributor must have effective control over the firm's product governance process to determine:
1. The range of financial instruments the firm offers or recommends
2. The investment services provided to the respective target markets

**(PROD 3.3.22R)** All relevant staff must possess the necessary expertise to understand:
1. The characteristics and risks of the financial instruments the firm intends to distribute
2. The investment services provided by the firm
3. The needs, characteristics and objectives of the identified target market

### Distributor Compliance Reports (PROD 3.3.24R–3.3.25R)

**(PROD 3.3.24R)** Compliance reports to the management body must include information about the financial instruments distributed by the firm and the investment services provided.

**(PROD 3.3.25R)** A distributor shall make compliance reports available to the FCA on request.

### Post-Sale Review (PROD 3.3.26R–3.3.28R)

**(PROD 3.3.26R)** Distributors must **regularly review** the financial instruments they distribute and the investment services they provide, taking into account any event that could materially affect the potential risk to the identified target market.

**(PROD 3.3.27R)** In carrying out this review, distributors must assess at least:
1. Whether the financial instrument or investment service remains consistent with the needs, characteristics and objectives of the identified target market
2. Whether the intended distribution strategy remains appropriate

**(PROD 3.3.28R)** If a distributor becomes aware that it has wrongly identified the target market for a specific financial instrument, or that the financial instrument no longer meets the circumstances of the identified target market, it must take appropriate steps, including at least:
1. Reconsidering the target market
2. Updating its product governance arrangements

### Information Sharing Back to the Manufacturer (PROD 3.3.30R)

**(PROD 3.3.30R)** To support the reviews carried out by manufacturers under PROD 3.2.19R to 3.2.26R, a distributor must provide to the manufacturer of each financial instrument it distributes:
1. Information on sales
2. Where appropriate, information on the reviews carried out under PROD 3.3.26R to 3.3.28R

This is the statutory basis for the manufacturer–distributor information flow obligation. Commercial sensitivity does not excuse a distributor from providing this information.

**(PROD 3.3.31G)** Information on sales should include information on any sales made **outside the target market**.

### Chain Responsibilities (PROD 3.3.32R–3.3.33R)

**(PROD 3.3.32R)** A firm which distributes financial instruments or investment services to end clients is responsible for ensuring that the PROD 3.3 obligations are met in respect of any financial instrument or investment service it distributes. A firm which distributes to clients which are not end clients must also consider whether it is undertaking a manufacturing role — and if so, must also comply with PROD 3.2.

**(PROD 3.3.33R)** A distributor which distributes financial instruments to other distributors must:
1. Ensure that relevant product information is passed from the manufacturer to the final distributor in the chain
2. If the manufacturer requires information on product sales in order to comply with its obligations under PROD 3.2, enable them to obtain it

## Key Points for Agents

- **Manufacturer TM obligation is dual** (PROD 3.2.8R): both a positive target market (compatible clients) and a negative target market (incompatible clients) must be identified at a sufficiently granular level. An assessment without both is incomplete.
- **PROD 3.2.10R second limb** is a design integrity test: the financial instrument must not be designed to generate revenue from poor client outcomes. Risk/reward compatibility with the TM is necessary but not sufficient.
- **PROD 3.2.14R charging structure assessment** has three limbs: (1) charges compatible with TM needs/characteristics; (2) charges don't undermine return expectations (e.g., charges = expected tax advantages); (3) charging structure transparent and not complex to understand.
- **Distributor must independently determine its own TM** (PROD 3.3.9R) — even if the manufacturer has not defined one. A distributor cannot point to a manufacturer's omission as a defence.
- **PROD 3.3.18R** is the distributor's compliance checklist gate: COBS 4 (disclosure), COBS 9A (suitability), COBS 10A (appropriateness), COBS 2.3A (inducements), SYSC 10.1 (conflicts). PROD 3.3 is additive to, not a substitute for, these obligations.
- **Information sharing back to manufacturer** (PROD 3.3.30R) is a statutory obligation — distributors must provide sales data (including out-of-TM sales) to enable manufacturer review. Commercial sensitivity is not a defence.

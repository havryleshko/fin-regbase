# CONC 8 — Debt Advice (Sourcebook Reference)

## What CONC 8 Does

CONC 8 sets the conduct standards for firms providing **debt counselling**, **debt adjusting**, and **providing credit information services** — the three regulated activities that make up the debt advice sector. It applies to profit-seeking and not-for-profit bodies alike, and covers activities in relation to both regulated and unregulated credit and consumer hire agreements **(CONC 8.1.1R; 8.1.4G)**.

CONC 8 is structured around the customer relationship: what must be disclosed before a contract, how advice must be given, what a contract must and must not contain, how ongoing plans are managed, and what firms can and cannot charge.

---

## Application — CONC 8.1

### Scope

CONC 8 applies to every firm with respect to **(CONC 8.1.1R)**:
1. **Debt counselling** — advising debtors about the liquidation of debts
2. **Debt adjusting** — negotiating with creditors on behalf of debtors, or taking over repayment obligations
3. **Providing credit information services** — to the extent of the advice referred to in article 89A(2) of the Regulated Activities Order

CONC 8.10 (conduct of business: providing credit information services) applies separately to credit information services and to operating an electronic system in relation to lending in relation to activities similar to providing credit information services **(CONC 8.1.2G)**.

CONC 8 covers **all firms** with the requisite permissions — including not-for-profit bodies holding FCA permission by virtue of article 62 of the Regulated Activities Order. The obligations apply to profit-seeking and charitable/NFP bodies equally **(CONC 8.1.3G)**.

The activities of debt counselling and debt adjusting apply to credit agreements and consumer hire agreements **whether they are regulated or not** **(CONC 8.1.4G)**.

---

## Conduct Standards — CONC 8.2

### Overarching Principles

The Principles for Businesses (PRIN) apply in full to firms providing debt counselling, debt adjusting, and credit information services **(CONC 8.2.1G)**. In particular:

- **Principle 2** (due skill, care and diligence): Recommending a debt solution the firm knows or ought to know is **unaffordable** for the customer is likely to contravene Principle 2, Principle 6, and Principle 9 **(CONC 8.2.2G(2))**. Actively discouraging customers from considering alternative sources of debt counselling contravenes Principle 6 **(CONC 8.2.2G(3))**.
- **Principle 7** (fair communications): All communications must be clear, fair and not misleading **(CONC 3.3.1R — cross-reference)**.
- **DISP 1:** Firms are subject to the FCA's complaint handling rules **(CONC 8.2.3G)**.

### Mandatory MoneyHelper Signposting

A **debt management firm** must prominently include in its **first written or oral communication** with the customer a statement that free debt counselling, debt adjusting, and credit information services are available, and direct the customer to **MoneyHelper** **(CONC 8.2.4R(1))**. The firm must also include on its website a link to the MoneyHelper debt advice locator **(CONC 8.2.4R(2))**.

### Dealings With Lenders

Firm communications to lenders on behalf of customers must be **transparent** so as not to adversely affect the customer's interests **(CONC 8.2.5R)**.

Where entry into a debt solution means payments to lenders will not be made or will be retained by the firm, the firm must **notify lenders as soon as possible** of the reason payments are not being made and the period for which this will be the case **(CONC 8.2.6R)**.

### Vulnerable Customers

A firm must establish and implement **clear and effective policies and procedures** to identify particularly vulnerable customers and deal with them appropriately **(CONC 8.2.7R)**. Most customers seeking debt advice are financially distressed to some degree; some are particularly vulnerable because of their reduced ability to deal with lenders or debt collectors. Customers with mental health or mental capacity issues are specifically mentioned **(CONC 8.2.8G)**. See [[conc-mental-capacity-guidance]] and [[vulnerable-customers]].

## Pre-Contract Information and Advice — CONC 8.3

### Fourteen-Item Pre-Contract Disclosure (CONC 8.3.1R)

Before a customer enters into a contract (except where CCA disclosure regulations apply), the firm must provide in a **durable medium** the following information:

1. Nature of the firm's service (including the specific debt solution to be offered)
2. Duration of the contract
3. Total cost of the service (or formula/estimate where the total cannot be stated)
4. Any arrangement fee, periodic management fee, or administrative fee
5. Any fee or charge for cancellation
6. Any other costs likely to be incurred
7. Where fees are based on percentage, hourly rate, or other formula: how calculated
8. The elements of the service covered by the fees
9. Circumstances in which the customer may terminate and receive a refund, and any fees payable
10. Consequences for the customer's **credit rating** (including how long the matter will show on the credit file and that the customer may not be able to obtain credit in the future)
11. Any right to cancel: period, conditions, and amounts payable
12. How payments will be allocated to lenders and when payments will be made
13. The **period between receiving payments from the customer and making payments to lenders**, including when the first payment will be made
14. That compensation from the **compensation scheme** may be available if there is a shortfall in client money held for that customer

### Advice Quality Standard (CONC 8.3.2R)

A firm must ensure that all advice given and action taken by the firm (or agent or appointed representative):

1. **Has regard to the best interests of the customer**
2. **Is appropriate to the individual circumstances** of the customer
3. **Is based on a sufficiently full assessment** of the financial circumstances of the customer

Customers must receive sufficient information about the available options identified as suitable for their needs, and the reasons why available options are considered suitable and others unsuitable **(CONC 8.3.2R(2)–(3))**.

### Individual circumstances

Individual circumstances include: the customer's financial position; the country in the UK to whose laws and procedures the customer and lender are subject; and the customer's level of understanding **(CONC 8.3.3G)**.

### Full Financial Assessment Requirement (CONC 8.3.7R)

Before giving advice or any recommendation, a firm must carry out a **reasonable and reliable assessment** of:

1. The customer's **financial position** — income, capital, and expenditure **(8.3.7R(2)(a))**
2. The customer's **personal circumstances** — including reasons for financial difficulty, whether temporary or longer term, and whether the customer has entered a debt solution previously and (if it failed) why **(8.3.7R(2)(b))**
3. Any **other relevant factors** including known or reasonably foreseeable changes in the customer's circumstances (e.g. change in employment status) **(8.3.7R(2)(c))**

The firm must also:
- Provide the customer with a source of **impartial information** on the range of debt solutions available in the relevant country of the UK **(8.3.7R(1))**
- **Refer the customer** to an appropriate not-for-profit debt advice body where: the customer has problems requiring immediate attention the firm is unable or unwilling to address; or the customer does not have enough disposable income to pay the firm's fees **(8.3.7R(3))**
- Refer the customer to another debt advice provider where the firm is unable to provide appropriate advice or an appropriate debt solution **(8.3.7R(4))**
- Seek to ensure the customer understands the options and the implications and consequences of the recommended course of action **(8.3.7R(5))**

### Durable Medium Delivery (CONC 8.3.4R)

Advice must be provided in a durable medium — before or after entry into the contract — and must:
1. Make clear which debts will and will not be included in any debt solution
2. Make clear advantages, disadvantages, costs, and risks of each option
3. Warn the customer of consequences of failing to pay taxes, fines, child support, and debts whose non-payment could result in loss of access to essential goods/services, repossession, or eviction
4. Warn of consequences of not making repayments under credit or consumer hire agreements
5. Warn of consequences of ignoring lender correspondence
6. Warn that action to recover debts may be commenced and may involve further cost
7. Warn that a debt management plan or non-statutory repayment plan does not guarantee that current recovery action will be suspended
8. Where relevant, make clear the specific risks of the proposed debt solution (e.g. IVA failure = bankruptcy risk; remortgage risks; homeowners may need to release equity; restrictions on expenditure)
9. Take proper account of the individual needs of the customer

### Prohibited Incentive Structures (CONC 8.3.6G)

A firm should not unfairly incentivise debt advisers (employees, agents, or appointed representatives) to the extent that an incentive might lead the firm not to comply with CONC 8.3.2R. This is a direct conflict-of-interest rule on adviser remuneration design.

### Debt Packager Commission Prohibition (CONC 8.3.11R)

**A firm must not** (and must take all reasonable steps to ensure none of its associates or appointed representatives):
1. Enter into an agreement to receive
2. Solicit or accept; or
3. Seek to exercise, enforce, or rely on rights under an agreement to receive

any **commission, fee, or any other financial consideration**, directly or indirectly, from a **debt solution provider** in connection with the firm referring customers to a debt solution provider, or any other related services — **except as provided in CONC 8.3.14R** (statutory, DAS Scotland, and Insolvency Service carve-outs).

**Scope:** The prohibition applies to firms that do debt counselling but do not themselves provide debt solutions — i.e. "debt packagers" **(CONC 8.3.9R)**. It does not apply to not-for-profit debt advice bodies **(CONC 8.3.9R(1)(b))**.

A firm is treated as not itself providing debt solutions (and therefore in scope of the prohibition) where it provides debt solutions on a **single or occasional basis** or receives only an **insignificant amount** of its total annual revenue from providing debt solutions **(CONC 8.3.9R(2))**.

**Purpose:** The prohibition removes the conflict of interest between the firm's CONC obligations (acting in the customer's best interests) and the financial incentive to act in a way that generates referral fees from debt solution providers **(CONC 8.3.10G(2))**.

**Related services** covered by the prohibition include: recommending a debt solution provider; providing debt counselling to customers prior to referring them to a solution provider; and providing debt counselling to customers who were referred to the firm by a solution provider **(CONC 8.3.12R)**.

**Appointed representatives:** Where an AR would be subject to the prohibition if it were an authorised person, the principal must take all reasonable steps to ensure the AR complies with CONC 8.3.11R **(CONC 8.3.16R)**. This prevents avoidance by becoming an AR **(CONC 8.3.17G)**.

**Permitted payments (CONC 8.3.14R):**
- Payments made pursuant to an enactment
- Payments in administration of the Debt Arrangement Scheme (Scotland) by a money adviser
- Payments by an officer of the Insolvency Service or Accountant in Bankruptcy

## Debt Solution Contracts — CONC 8.4

### Written Contract Requirement (CONC 8.4.1R)

A firm must provide the customer with a **written contract** setting out its terms and conditions for provision of its services.

### Mandatory Contract Contents (CONC 8.4.2R)

The written contract must include: the nature of the service (and the specific debt solution); the duration; the total cost (or formula/estimate); the circumstances in which the customer may terminate and receive a refund and any applicable fees; and the duration and conditions for any cancellation right.

### Prohibited Contract Terms (CONC 8.4.3R)

A firm must not include in its contract with a customer:
1. A term requiring the customer to **sign a declaration** stating in any way that they understand the requirements of the contract
2. A term **restricting or prohibiting the customer from corresponding with a lender**
3. A term stating or implying the firm has **no liability** to the customer
4. A term stating or implying there are **no circumstances in which the customer is entitled to a refund**

A refund obligation exists where the firm fails to deliver the service or has not provided the service with reasonable care and skill **(CONC 8.4.4G)**.

## Financial Statements and Debt Repayment Offers — CONC 8.5

### Accuracy Obligation (CONC 8.5.1R)

A financial statement sent to a lender on behalf of a customer must:
1. Be **accurate and realistic** and present a sufficiently clear and complete account of income, expenditure, debts, and surplus income
2. State any fees or charges being made by the firm
3. Be sent only after obtaining the customer's **consent** and **confirmation of accuracy**
4. Be provided to lenders as soon as practicable after the customer confirms accuracy
5. Also be sent to the **customer**, together with any accompanying correspondence

### Sustainable Offers (CONC 8.5.3G)

Repayment offers must be **realistic and sustainable**: the offer should enable the customer to meet repayments in full from disposable income throughout the duration of the repayment proposal. Setting offers must account for priority obligations (taxes, fines, child support, and debts that could result in home repossession or loss of access to essential goods/services).

### Verification Obligation (CONC 8.5.4R)

Before submitting a financial statement, the firm must:
1. Take **reasonable steps to verify** the customer's identity, income, and outgoings
2. **Seek explanations** where the customer indicates expenditure that is particularly high or low
3. Where applicable, **notify the customer** that a particular lender will not deal with the firm

## Changes to Contractual Payments — CONC 8.6

### Advising to Stop Payments (CONC 8.6.1R)

Where a firm gives advice to a customer not to make a contractual repayment or to cancel a means of making such a repayment before any debt solution is agreed, the firm must be able to **demonstrate the advice is in the customer's best interests**. The firm must also advise the customer that if they adopt this advice, they should notify their lenders without delay.

### Consequence Warnings (CONC 8.6.2R)

Where the effect of advice (if adopted) is that contractual repayments will not be made or not be made in full, the firm must **warn the customer** of the actual or potential consequences of taking that course.

### Interest-Accruing Rate (CONC 8.6.3R)

A firm must only advise a customer to make repayments at a rate lower than that necessary to meet interest and charges accruing where this is in the customer's best interests. The FCA generally expects it to be in the customer's best interests to maintain regular payments to lenders, even if less than the full sum due **(CONC 8.6.4G)**.

### Course Correction Obligation (CONC 8.6.5R)

Where a firm has advised a customer not to make contractual repayments and it becomes clear this approach is not producing effects in the customer's best interests, the firm must advise the customer to enable corrective action. Where withdrawing from a DMP may be in the customer's best interests, the firm should advise the customer of this possibility **(CONC 8.6.6G)**.

## Charging — CONC 8.7

### Fee Structure Prohibition (CONC 8.7.2R)

A firm must ensure that the obligations of the customer in relation to the amount or timing of its fees or charges:
1. Do **not have the effect** that the customer pays all, or substantially all, of those fees in priority to making repayments to lenders under the DMP; and
2. Do **not undermine** the customer's ability to make significant repayments to lenders throughout the duration of the DMP, starting with the first month

**The practical test (CONC 8.7.3G):** An obligation likely contravenes (2) if the firm allocates more than **half of the sums received from the customer in any one-month period** to discharging its fees or charges at the start of the plan. Once an initial fee has been paid, or six months from the start of the plan have elapsed, the FCA expects a **reduction in the proportion** allocated to fees.

Charges must be **spread evenly** over the duration of the plan, proportionate to the repayments the customer makes **(CONC 8.7.3G(3)–(4))**.

### Commission and Conflict Disclosure (CONC 8.7.4R)

A firm must:
1. **Disclose** the existence of any commission or incentive payments between the firm and any third party before entering into a contract; and disclose the amount on request
2. **Send a revised financial statement** to lenders where the firm's fees or charges alter during an arrangement and would affect the amount available for lender distribution
3. **Refund or credit** the customer's account fees or charges where the firm identifies (or it is established) that advice was incorrect or not appropriate
4. Make an **appropriate refund** where the agreed service has not been provided or has not been provided to a reasonable standard of care

### Prohibited Charging Practices (CONC 8.7.6R)

A firm must not:
1. Without reasonable justification, **switch** a customer from one debt solution to another while making a further charge to the extent that work has already been carried out
2. Switch a customer to a different debt solution without **obtaining the customer's consent** after fully explaining the reason
3. **Take payment before** entering into a contract concerning a debt solution
4. **Request payment from the customer's payment account** unless the customer has specifically authorised this and has not cancelled that authorisation
5. **Accept payment by credit card** (excluding where the firm does not know and cannot be expected to know that the customer's account is in debit or would be taken into debit)
6. Impose **cancellation charges** that are unreasonable or disproportionate compared to actual costs of providing the service
7. Claim a fee or take payment not provided for in the agreement, or that is unfair under the Consumer Rights Act 2015 (or Unfair Terms in Consumer Contracts Regulations 1999 for pre-1 October 2015 contracts)
8. Charge an additional fee for further or revised advice where the firm identified that prior advice was incorrect or inappropriate
9. Request, suggest, or instruct customers seeking refunds to contact the firm on a **premium rate telephone number**

## Debt Management Plans — CONC 8.8

### Ongoing Obligations for DMP Firms (CONC 8.8.1R)

A firm operating a DMP must:

1. **Maintain contact** with the customer
2. **Regularly monitor and review** the customer's financial position and circumstances
3. **Adapt the plan** to take into account relevant changes in the customer's circumstances
4. **Inform the customer without delay** of the outcome of negotiations with lenders — in particular, where a lender has: refused to deal with the firm; returned payments; refused the debt repayment offer; or refused to freeze interest or charges
5. **Inform the customer** of any material developments in the customer/lender relationship
6. **Provide copies** of correspondence or documentation relating to material developments
7. Where making repayments on behalf of the customer: (a) monitor repayments for evidence of changed circumstances; (b) review, amend, or terminate the plan at the earlier of each anniversary or on becoming aware of material change; (c) inform the customer of the outcome of any reassessment
8. Provide an **annual statement** (and at plan start) covering: total debt balance with interest; all fees and charges; fee calculation narrative; estimated plan duration; total cost of service; monthly/periodic payments to lenders
9. Maintain **adequate records** of each DMP until the contract is completed or terminated
10. **Check the accuracy** of the customer's account details
11. Use reasonable endeavours **not to send inaccurate information** to lenders

**Material change evidence (CONC 8.8.2G):** Includes where a customer who has not previously missed DMP payments begins to miss them. The firm should discuss any plan changes with the customer at the earliest opportunity.

## Lead Generators — CONC 8.9

### Firm Responsibility for Lead Generator Conduct (CONC 8.9.1G)

The Principles (particularly Principles 6 and 7) apply to a firm's actions in dealing with a customer who has been referred through a **lead generator**. Where a firm acts on a sales lead and **knows or ought to know** that the lead generator is using misleading information, advice, or actions to obtain customer data, this is likely to amount to a breach of Principles 6 and 7 by the firm.

### Pre-Agreement Checks (CONC 8.9.2R)

Before agreeing to accept sales leads from a lead generator, the firm must take reasonable steps to ensure:
1. The lead generator's advice, website content, advertising, and commercial practices comply with applicable legal requirements (including the Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008)
2. The lead generator is **registered with the ICO** under data protection legislation
3. The lead generator has processes in place to comply with the **Privacy and Electronic Communications (EC Directive) Regulations 2003**

### Ongoing Obligations (CONC 8.9.4R)

A firm that has agreed to accept sales leads must take reasonable steps to ensure the lead generator:
1. Does not carry on debt counselling without appropriate authorisation (unless not required)
2. Where it does carry on debt counselling, has and maintains the relevant Part 4A permission
3. Does not claim to provide debt counselling or debt adjusting if it lacks permission
4. Complies with applicable consumer protection legislation
5. Makes the true nature of its services clear to customers
6. Makes clear to customers that their personal data will be passed to the firm for a fee (where applicable)
7. Makes clear any financial interest it has in passing the lead to the firm
8. Does not falsely claim to be a charitable, not-for-profit, or government body **(CONC 8.9.6G)**
9. Communicates consistently with, and promotes, services the firm is able to provide

**FCA traceability expectation (CONC 8.9.5G):** The FCA expects firms to be able to identify, on request, **all lead generators** from which they have received leads for debt counselling or debt adjusting, including the FCA authorisation number where applicable.

## Credit Information Services — CONC 8.10

### Application (CONC 8.10.1R)

CONC 8.10 applies to: firms providing credit information services in relation to an individual's financial standing; and firms operating an electronic system in relation to lending (P2P platforms) for borrowers under P2P agreements.

### Prohibited Representations (CONC 8.10.3R)

A firm must not:
1. Claim to be able to **remove negative but accurate information** from a customer's credit file, including adverse entries and court judgments
2. **Mislead a customer** about the length of time that negative information is held on the credit file or any official register
3. Claim that a **new credit file can be created** (e.g. by the customer changing address)

These prohibitions apply regardless of how the representation is framed: claims in communications, on websites, in advertising, or implied through the firm's service description **(CONC 8.10.4G)**.

## Key Points for Agents

- **CONC 8 applies to all firms providing debt counselling, debt adjusting, and credit information services — profit-seeking and not-for-profit alike (CONC 8.1.1R; 8.1.3G).** The activities apply to regulated and unregulated credit and consumer hire agreements (8.1.4G).
- **Debt management firms must signpost MoneyHelper in the first communication with the customer and on their website — this is a hard Rule with no exceptions (CONC 8.2.4R).**
- **Advice must be based on a full individual financial assessment (income, expenditure, personal circumstances, foreseeable changes) and must have regard to the customer's best interests (CONC 8.3.2R; 8.3.7R).** Recommending an unaffordable solution breaches Principles 2, 6, and 9.
- **The debt packager commission ban (CONC 8.3.11R) prohibits firms doing debt counselling but not providing solutions from receiving any commission, fee, or financial consideration from debt solution providers for referrals.** The prohibition extends to associates and appointed representatives. Becoming an AR does not avoid it (8.3.16R; 8.3.17G). Permitted carve-outs are narrow: statutory schemes, DAS Scotland, and Insolvency Service payments only (8.3.14R).
- **Four contract terms are absolutely prohibited (CONC 8.4.3R):** understanding declarations; lender correspondence restrictions; no-liability clauses; no-refund clauses.
- **Fee structures must not consume all or substantially all of customer payments in priority to lender repayments (CONC 8.7.2R).** The one-month 50% rule is the FCA's practical test (8.7.3G). Charges must be spread proportionately over the plan duration.
- **Nine charging practices are prohibited (CONC 8.7.6R)** including: unjustified solution-switching with additional charge; pre-contract payment taking; unauthorised payment account access; premium rate complaint lines for refund-seeking customers.
- **Active debt management plans carry eleven ongoing obligations (CONC 8.8.1R)** including: regular monitoring; plan adaptation; prompt lender-response notification; annual statements; accurate record-keeping. Annual plan review or on material change — whichever is earlier.
- **Firms are responsible for lead generator conduct where they knew or ought to have known of misconduct (CONC 8.9.1G).** Pre-agreement checks on marketing compliance, ICO registration, and PECR compliance are mandatory (8.9.2R). The FCA expects firms to be able to identify all lead generators on request (8.9.5G).
- **Credit information services firms must not claim to remove accurate negative entries, mislead about retention periods, or claim new credit files can be created (CONC 8.10.3R).** These are hard prohibitions — representations through any channel or medium are caught.

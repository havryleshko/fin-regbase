# FCA Guide for Solo-Regulated Firms — SM&CR

> **Source note**
>
> This summary covers **Part One only** (§§1–12, pp. 5–52) of the FCA guide (July 2019). Part Two (pp. 53–74) set out transitional arrangements for converting from the Approved Persons Regime (APR) and is not ingested — it has no current operational relevance. The SM&CR replaced the APR for solo-regulated firms on **9 December 2019**.

---

## §1–2: Scope — Three Tiers of Solo-Regulated Firms

SM&CR applies to **FSMA-authorised firms** supervised by the FCA under FSMA. Firms that hold only payment services or e-money authorisation and are not FSMA-authorised are **outside the scope of SM&CR entirely**.

| Tier | Who it applies to |
|------|-------------------|
| **Limited Scope** | Limited permission consumer credit firms; claims management companies that do not carry on any other FCA-regulated activity; oil market participants and energy market participants whose principal purpose is non-regulated activity; authorised professional firms whose only regulated activities are non-mainstream; insurance intermediaries whose principal business is not insurance intermediation and who only hold permission for non-investment insurance contracts |
| **Core** | The default tier — all FSMA-authorised solo-regulated firms not categorised as Limited Scope or Enhanced |
| **Enhanced** | The most complex firms: asset managers above AUM threshold, investment banks, corporate finance firms, platform operators, and others named in SYSC 23 Annex 1 |

Firms can opt up to Enhanced voluntarily via Form O (3-month compliance deadline from submission). Firms cannot cherry-pick: moving to a tier requires applying all rules for that tier. The transition from Core to Enhanced upon meeting the criteria carries a **1-year transition period**.

## §3: SM&CR Terminology

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **SMF (Senior Management Function)** | An FCA-controlled function requiring individual FCA approval before the person may perform it |
| **Senior Manager** | An individual approved to perform one or more SMFs |
| **SoR (Statement of Responsibilities)** | The document filed with the FCA setting out the Senior Manager's areas of responsibility |
| **PR (Prescribed Responsibility)** | A specific regulatory obligation defined in SYSC 24 that the firm must formally allocate to a named Senior Manager's SoR |
| **Duty of Responsibility** | FSMA s.66A personal liability mechanism — a Senior Manager may be held personally liable for a firm contravention in their area if they failed to take reasonable steps to prevent it |
| **Certification Function** | A function not requiring FCA approval but having significant potential impact on customers, firm, or market integrity — certified annually by the firm |
| **FIT** | FCA Fit and Proper test sourcebook — three elements: honesty/integrity/reputation; competence/capability; financial soundness |
| **Ancillary staff** | Employees whose work is unrelated to financial services activity (e.g. cleaners, caterers, security guards) — outside the scope of Conduct Rules |

## §4: Senior Managers Regime — Core Concepts

### FCA Approval and SMF Mechanics

Senior Managers must be FCA-approved before performing their SMF (FSMA s.59). Application is via Form A (new appointment) or Form E (internal transfer). An individual can hold multiple SMFs simultaneously — separate FCA approval is required for each function, but only one SoR is needed (covering all functions).

SMFs apply only if someone is actually performing the relevant role, or the SMF is a **Required Function** mandated by Handbook rules. The SM&CR does not require firms to change their governance structure or hire people to fill roles they do not otherwise have.

### Statements of Responsibilities (SoRs)

Each Senior Manager must maintain a current SoR describing: their SMF(s); areas of business for which they are responsible; and any PRs allocated to them. When PRs move from one SM to another, the relevant SoRs must be updated and submitted to the FCA using Form J. Giving a PR to a new SM does not require re-approval.

### Duty of Responsibility

Under FSMA s.66A, the FCA may take action against a Senior Manager where:
1. The firm has contravened a relevant requirement; and
2. The contravention occurred in the Senior Manager's area of responsibility

**Statutory defence:** A Senior Manager is not liable if they took such steps as it was reasonable to expect them to take to prevent the contravention. The burden of demonstrating this lies on the Senior Manager. The Duty applies per-SM to their own area — not to the whole firm.

### 12-Week Rule

A person may cover for a Senior Manager without FCA approval where the absence is temporary or reasonably unforeseen and the appointment is for less than **12 consecutive weeks**. *(SUP 10C.3.13R)*

### Outsourcing

Outsourcing an operational function does not transfer accountability for it. The Senior Manager responsible for that function retains full regulatory accountability regardless of who operationally delivers it.

### FCA Approval Conditions

The FCA may: approve a Senior Manager subject to conditions; grant time-limited approval only; or change, impose, or remove conditions on existing approvals. *(SUP 10C)*

### Overseas Senior Managers

The SMR has no territorial limitation. An individual performing an SMF is subject to it whether based in the UK or overseas.

## §5: Core SMFs and Prescribed Responsibilities

### Core Senior Management Functions (6)

| SMF | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| SMF1 – Chief Executive | Person(s) with responsibility, under the immediate authority of the governing body, for the conduct of the whole business (or relevant activities). The CEO's seniority does not allow the governing body to avoid allocating specific responsibilities to other SMs. |
| SMF3 – Executive Director | A director of the firm, other than a Non-Executive Director |
| SMF27 – Partner | A partner in the firm, other than a limited partner under the Limited Partnership Act 1907 |
| SMF9 – Chair | Person responsible for chairing and overseeing the performance of the governing body. **The only SMF that a Non-Executive Director can hold.** |
| SMF16 – Compliance Oversight | Person responsible for the compliance function and reporting to the governing body. **Required Function** — applies only if Handbook rules require the firm to have one. |
| SMF17 – MLRO | Person responsible for overseeing the firm's compliance with FCA rules on systems and controls against money laundering. **Required Function** — applies only if Handbook rules require the firm to have an MLRO. |

Non-Executive Directors (other than the Chair/SMF9) are **not** FCA-approved under the Core tier. They are, however, subject to Conduct Rules, FIT requirements, and regulatory reference rules.

### Core Prescribed Responsibilities (5)

All 5 must be allocated to named Senior Managers. Each PR should normally be held by one person; sharing requires justification and documentation.

| PR Ref. | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| (a) | Performance by the firm of its obligations under the SMR, including implementation and oversight |
| (b) | Performance by the firm of its obligations under the Certification Regime |
| (b-1) | Performance by the firm of its obligations in respect of notifications and training of the Conduct Rules |
| (d) | Responsibility for the firm's policies and procedures for countering the risk that the firm might be used to further financial crime |
| (z) | Responsibility for the firm's compliance with CASS *(if applicable)* |

AFMs additionally carry PR(za): value assessments, independent director representation, and acting in investors' best interests.

The PR allocation should go to whoever is **in practice the most senior person** for that issue — for example, if the MLRO reports to a more senior individual on financial crime matters, PR(d) should go to that more senior person, not automatically the MLRO.

| SMF | Description |
|-----|-------------|

## §7: Enhanced SMFs and PRs

### Enhanced Senior Management Functions (17)

All Core SMFs plus:

**Additional Governing Functions:**

| SMF | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| SMF7 – Group Entity Senior Manager | Person with significant influence on the management or conduct of the UK-regulated entity, employed by or an officer of another group entity |
| SMF10 – Chair of the Risk Committee | Chairs and oversees the risk management systems, policies and procedures committee |
| SMF11 – Chair of the Audit Committee | Chairs and oversees the internal audit system committee |
| SMF12 – Chair of the Remuneration Committee | Chairs and oversees remuneration policy design and implementation |
| SMF13 – Chair of the Nominations Committee | Chairs the nominations committee (where one exists) |
| SMF14 – Senior Independent Director | Person with particular responsibility for leading the assessment of the Chair's performance |
| SMF18 – Other Overall Responsibility | A senior executive with overall responsibility for an area of the firm's business who does not perform any other SMF. Gives firms flexibility to reflect governance diversity. |

**Additional Systems & Controls Functions:**

| SMF | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| SMF2 – Chief Finance Function | Person responsible for managing the firm's financial resources, including reporting to the governing body on financial affairs |
| SMF4 – Chief Risk Function | Person with overall responsibility for managing the firm's risk controls |
| SMF5 – Head of Internal Audit | Person responsible for managing the internal audit function and reporting to the governing body (or audit committee) |
| SMF24 – Chief Operations Function | Most senior person responsible for managing internal operations, including HR, systems and technology |

*Only NED chair roles (SMF9–13) and the Senior Independent Director (SMF14) are FCA-approved for NEDs. Other NEDs are not approved but remain subject to Conduct Rules, FIT, and regulatory references.*

*The Head of Legal is expressly excluded from SM approval (legal privilege grounds) but is included in the Certification Regime and subject to Conduct Rules.*

### Enhanced Prescribed Responsibilities (12)

All Core PRs plus:

| PR Ref. | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| (c) | Compliance with the rules relating to the firm's Responsibilities Map |
| (j) | Safeguarding and overseeing the independence and performance of the internal audit function (SYSC 6.2) |
| (j-3) | Where internal audit is outsourced: taking reasonable steps to ensure independence from external audit, including supervision and management of outsourced internal auditors |
| (k) | Safeguarding and overseeing the independence and performance of the compliance function (SYSC 6.1) |
| (l) | Safeguarding and overseeing the independence and performance of the risk function (SYSC 7.1.21R and SYSC 7.1.22R) |
| (s) | Managing the firm's internal stress-tests and ensuring accuracy and timeliness of information provided to the FCA for stress-testing purposes |
| (t) | Developing and maintaining the firm's business model |

PRs (j), (k), and (l) should be allocated, where possible, to a NED or a partner without management responsibilities — to preserve the independence of the function being overseen. If no suitable NED exists, a different SM must hold these PRs.

PRs **cannot** be allocated to the SMF18 (Other Overall Responsibility) holder, with the single exception of PR(z) for CASS compliance.

### Enhanced-Only Requirements

**Overall Responsibility (SYSC 25):**
Every activity, business area, and management function of the firm — including those carried out from a branch overseas — must have a Senior Manager with overall responsibility for it. This prevents accountability gaps. Having Overall Responsibility for an area means the SM: (i) has ultimate responsibility for managing or supervising that function; (ii) briefs and reports to the governing body about it; and (iii) puts matters for decision about it to the governing body. Overall Responsibility cannot be divided informally but can be shared (two SMs jointly accountable).

**Responsibilities Map (SYSC 25):**
Enhanced firms must prepare and maintain a single document setting out: how PRs have been allocated, who has Overall Responsibility for each activity/area/function, individuals' and committees' reporting lines, and how responsibilities are shared or divided. SYSC 25 Annex 1 provides a (non-mandatory) template. The Responsibilities Map allows the FCA to identify the accountable SM for any specific area and verify there are no gaps in SoRs.

**Handover Procedures (SYSC 25.9):**
Enhanced firms must take all reasonable steps to ensure a new Senior Manager has all the information and materials they could reasonably need to do the job effectively. One mechanism is a handover note prepared by the predecessor. Firms must have a documented handover policy and maintain adequate records of steps taken.

## §8: Branches

### EEA Branches
- SMF21 – EEA Branch Senior Manager
- SMF17 – MLRO
- No PRs apply

### Third Country Branches (TCBs)
SMFs: SMF19 (Head of Third Country Branch), SMF3 (Executive Director), SMF16 (Compliance Oversight), SMF17 (MLRO)

8 PRs apply to all TCBs: (a), (b), (b-1), (d), (z), plus:
- (aa) Responsibility for management of the firm's risk management processes in the UK
- (ff) Responsibility for the firm's compliance with the UK regulatory system applicable to the firm
- (ee) Responsibility for the escalation of correspondence from the PRA, FCA and other regulators to the governing body and/or management body of the firm, or where appropriate the parent undertaking or holding company

AFMs operating as Third Country Branches also receive PR(za).

## §9: Certification Regime

The Certification Regime covers specific functions that are not SMFs but can significantly impact customers, the firm, or market integrity. The **FCA does not approve** Certification Function holders — the **firm certifies** them, at least annually, confirming they are fit and proper to perform their function. This is a requirement under FSMA, set out in SYSC 27.

Certification Functions apply on a **legal entity basis** (the whole SM&CR applies on this basis). In very small firms there may be no Certification Function holders at all if the only relevant individuals are Senior Managers supported by ancillary staff.

### Certification Functions (8)

| Certification Function | Notes |
|------------------------|-------|
| Significant Management Function (CF29 equivalent) | Individuals with significant responsibility for a business unit — SYSC 27.7 |
| Proprietary traders | All proprietary traders are covered |
| CASS Operational Oversight (CF10a equivalent) | Oversight of operational effectiveness of CASS systems and controls |
| Functions subject to qualification requirements | Mortgage advisers, retail investment advisers, pension transfer specialists, and others in the Training and Competence Sourcebook |
| Client dealing functions | Expanded from APR CF30; covers individuals dealing with clients |
| Supervisors of Certification Function staff | Those who manage or supervise any of the above |
| Material Risk Takers | As defined under applicable remuneration codes |
| Algorithmic trading | Those involved in the creation or deployment of algorithmic trading systems |

The Certification Regime applies to **employees only** (including contractors and secondees under FSMA's broad definition of employee). It does not apply to Non-Executive Directors.

Certification Functions staff appear on the FCA's central **Financial Services Register** (PS19/7).

## §10: Fitness and Propriety and Regulatory References

### FIT Test — Three Elements

1. **Honesty, integrity and reputation** — past conduct, criminal records, regulatory findings
2. **Competence and capability** — qualifications, training, experience
3. **Financial soundness** — not based solely on wealth; concerns history of financial defaults/court judgements

All three elements must be assessed for Senior Managers on appointment and reviewed annually for Certification Function holders.

### Criminal Records Checks

| Population | Criminal Records Check |
|------------|------------------------|
| Senior Managers | **Mandatory** |
| Non-Executive Directors | **Mandatory** |
| Certification Function staff | Firm's discretion |
| Other conduct rule staff (non-SM, non-Certification) | Not required |

### Regulatory References

Firms must obtain regulatory references from previous employers covering the last **6 years** when hiring: Senior Managers; Certification Function staff; and non-approved NEDs.

Mandatory disclosures in a regulatory reference:
- Breaches of Conduct Rules
- Findings that the person is not fit and proper
- Disciplinary history relating to honesty, integrity, or competence
- Ongoing or concluded investigations by the FCA/PRA

**NDAs cannot prevent disclosures required under regulatory reference rules.** Where a person left before an investigation concluded but the firm would have concluded there was serious misconduct, there is **no time limit on disclosure** — the 6-year lookback does not apply.

Firms can and should request updated references where relevant information may have emerged since an initial hire.

## §11: Conduct Rules

### Individual Conduct Rules (all employees except ancillary staff)

1. You must act with integrity
2. You must act with due care, skill and diligence
3. You must be open and cooperative with the FCA, the PRA and other regulators
4. You must pay due regard to the interests of customers and treat them fairly
5. You must observe proper standards of market conduct

**Consumer Duty addition — Individual Conduct Rule 6:** Act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers where carrying out activities within scope of the Consumer Duty. *(PS22/9 §13.16–§13.22 — added post-publication of this guide)*

### Senior Manager Conduct Rules (SC1–SC4)

In addition to Individual Conduct Rules 1–5 (and 6), Senior Managers are subject to:

| Rule | Obligation |
|------|------------|
| SC1 | Take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the firm for which you are responsible is organised so that it can be controlled and monitored effectively |
| SC2 | Take reasonable steps to ensure that the business of the firm for which you are responsible complies with the relevant requirements and standards of the regulatory system |
| SC3 | Take reasonable steps to ensure that any delegation of your responsibilities is to an appropriate person and that you oversee the discharge of the delegated responsibility effectively |
| SC4 | Disclose appropriately any information of which the FCA or PRA would reasonably expect notice |

### Who Is Subject to Conduct Rules

- All employees **except ancillary staff** are subject to Individual Conduct Rules 1–5
- Senior Managers are additionally subject to SC1–SC4
- **Ancillary staff** = employees whose work is not related to financial services activity (cleaners, caterers, security guards, receptionists whose role is purely administrative)

### Conduct Rule Breach Reporting

| Breach type | Reporting obligation |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Senior Manager conduct rule breach | Notify the FCA within **7 business days** |
| All other conduct rule breaches | Report annually via **REP008** on GABRIEL |

## §12: Changing Tier

- **Entering the Enhanced tier** (because criteria are met): 1-year transition period to come into compliance
- **Voluntarily opting up to Enhanced**: submit Form O; firm has **3-month compliance deadline** from submission
- Firms cannot cherry-pick individual rules from different tiers

## Key Points for Agents

- SM&CR applies to FSMA-authorised firms only. **Payment institutions and e-money institutions** that are not FSMA-authorised are entirely outside its scope.
- Three tiers: Limited Scope / Core / Enhanced. Core is the default. Limited Scope has no PRs; Core has 5; Enhanced has 12.
- Senior Managers must be FCA-approved (FSMA s.59) before performing their function. The Duty of Responsibility (FSMA s.66A) creates personal liability for contraventions in their area unless they took reasonable preventative steps.
- Core PRs: (a) SMR, (b) Certification Regime, (b-1) Conduct Rules notifications/training, (d) financial crime, (z) CASS if applicable.
- Enhanced-only: Overall Responsibility (every activity/area/function must have a named SM), Responsibilities Map (SYSC 25 mandatory document), Handover Procedures (documented policy + records).
- Criminal records checks are mandatory for SMs and NEDs; firm's discretion for Certification staff.
- Regulatory references: 6-year lookback; NDAs cannot suppress required disclosures; no time limit where serious misconduct but person left before investigation concluded.
- SM conduct rule breaches: 7 business days to FCA. All others: annual REP008.

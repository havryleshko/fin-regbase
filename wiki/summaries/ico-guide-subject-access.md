# ICO Guide to Subject Access

## What This Document Is

This is the ICO's top-level overview guide to subject access requests (SARs) under Article 15 of the UK GDPR, clipped from ico.org.uk in April 2026. It provides the framework and key principles, but points to detailed sub-pages for most operational mechanics. Those sub-pages have not been ingested — where sub-page detail is needed, the primary source should be consulted.

## Coverage Note

This document is the primary source for:
- SAR recognition and scope
- Third-party SAR authority mechanics
- Child SAR competence framework
- The clarification pause mechanism
- What must be provided in a SAR response
- The enforced SAR criminal offence
- DPA 2018 Schedules 2/3 exemption categories

It points to, but does not contain, detailed guidance on: the mechanics of each exemption; identity verification detail; how to conduct a reasonable search; format and security of delivery; credit file, health, educational and social work special cases.

## What a SAR Is

The right of access (Art. 15 UK GDPR) gives individuals the right to obtain a copy of their personal data and supplementary information. It helps individuals understand how and why their data is being used and check the controller is processing it lawfully.

## Recognising a SAR

- Valid verbally or in writing, including via social media
- No specific form of words required; no need to cite legislation or direct to a named contact
- Any part of the organisation can receive a valid SAR
- The individual must be clearly asking for their own personal data — the trigger is substance, not form

## Third-Party SAR Mechanics

A third party (relative, friend, solicitor, representative) may make a SAR on behalf of an individual. SARs may also arrive via online portals acting as intermediaries.

**Before responding:** The controller must be satisfied the third party is entitled to act on behalf of the individual. The burden of providing evidence of authority sits with the third party, not the controller.

Financial services context: solicitors routinely make SARs on behalf of clients as a pre-litigation step. The authority verification obligation applies before any response — it is not an optional step.

## Child SAR Framework

Before responding to a SAR for information held about a child:

1. Assess whether the child is mature enough to understand their rights
2. If the child is competent and understands their rights: respond directly to the child
3. A parent or guardian may exercise rights on the child's behalf if: (a) the child authorises this; or (b) it is evident it is in the child's best interests
4. A competent child may also authorise someone other than a parent/guardian to act for them

## Responding — Timeline and Clarification

**Standard deadline:** Without undue delay and at the latest within 1 month of receipt.

**Extension:** Up to 2 further months (total: up to 3 months) for complex requests or where multiple requests have been received from the individual.

**Clarification pause:** Where the controller processes a large amount of information about an individual and it is not clear what the request relates to, the controller may ask the individual to specify the information or processing activities. The response clock is **paused** until clarification is received. However, any supplementary information that can be provided must still be supplied within 1 month.

This pause is distinct from the extension — it pauses the clock entirely rather than extending the deadline.

## What Must Be Provided

An individual is entitled to:

1. **A copy of their personal data** — the actual data the controller processes about them
2. **Supplementary information** — which largely corresponds with the information provided in a privacy notice (i.e., the Art. 13/14 mandatory content: purposes, legal basis, recipients, retention period, rights, etc.)

**Format:** Where the request was made electronically, the response must be in a commonly used electronic format unless the individual requests otherwise. The controller should consider the individual's ability to access the data in the format provided — good practice is to establish preferred format before fulfilling the request.

**Verbal response:** A verbal SAR response is permissible if identity has been confirmed by other means. A log must be kept of: the date of request; date of response; who provided the information; what information was provided.

**Remote access:** As an alternative to providing a copy, the individual may be allowed to access their data remotely and download it.

## Identity Verification

The controller must be satisfied it knows the identity of the requester (or the person on whose behalf the request is made). If unsure, ID can be requested. The response clock does not start until the requested identity information is received. The request for ID should be made promptly.

## Fee

Not usually permitted. A reasonable fee based on administrative costs may be charged only where the request is:
- Manifestly unfounded or excessive; or
- For additional copies of data already provided

## Reasonable Search

The controller must make reasonable efforts to find and retrieve the requested information. The obligation is not unlimited — searches that would be unreasonable or disproportionate to the importance of providing access are not required.

## Third-Party Data in SAR Responses

Where complying with a SAR would require disclosing information about another individual:

1. First consider whether compliance is possible without disclosing data that identifies the third party
2. If not possible: the controller is not required to disclose — unless the third party consents, or it is reasonable to comply without that consent
3. The controller must respond to the requester in all cases (even if declining to provide third-party data)
4. Must document the decision and reasons

## Refusal — Exemptions

The controller may refuse wholly or partly where an exemption applies, or where the request is manifestly unfounded or excessive.

### Manifestly Unfounded and Excessive

Same definitions as for other rights — see [[uk-gdpr-individual-rights-overview]] for the cross-cutting definitions.

**Procedure on refusal:** Inform the individual of: (a) the reasons; (b) the right to complain to the ICO; (c) the ability to seek enforcement through the courts.

## Enforced SAR — Criminal Offence

An enforced SAR occurs when someone **requires an individual to make a SAR** in order to obtain certain information about them — for example:
- Requiring a job applicant to SAR their criminal record as a condition of employment
- Requiring a prospective customer to SAR their health records before granting insurance

**Forcing an individual to make a SAR in such circumstances is a criminal offence under the DPA 2018.** The prohibition applies regardless of whether the underlying purpose (e.g., pre-employment vetting, underwriting) might otherwise be legitimate.

Financial services context: the prohibition is directly relevant to underwriting (health data), credit decisions, and employment screening. The correct route to obtaining such information is via the individual's direct consent and a formal disclosure mechanism — not by requiring a SAR.

## Special Cases

Special rules apply to SARs involving:
- **Unstructured manual records** — different search obligations
- **Credit files** — specific provisions under DPA 2018
- **Health data** — additional safeguards; may require clinical professional involvement
- **Educational data** — school record provisions
- **Social work data** — specific exemptions to protect vulnerable individuals

Detailed rules for each special case are in ICO sub-page guidance not yet ingested.

## Enforcement

Failure to comply with a SAR may result in:
- ICO regulatory action (including enforcement notices and fines) in accordance with the ICO's Regulatory Action Policy
- Court order requiring compliance
- Compensation claim by the requester

## Key Points for Agents

- A SAR is valid verbally, in writing, or via social media — no legal language or specific contact required.
- **Third-party SARs:** The third party must provide evidence of authority — burden is on them, not the controller.
- **Child SARs:** Competence-based — respond directly to the child if they understand their rights; parent can act only with child's authorisation or in child's best interests.
- **Clarification pause:** Where large volumes of data are held, the clock can be paused (not merely extended) while the controller seeks clarification of scope — but supplementary information must still be provided within 1 month.
- **What's provided:** Copy of personal data + supplementary information equivalent to Art. 13/14 mandatory privacy notice content.
- **Enforced SAR is a criminal offence** — requiring an individual to SAR their own criminal record, health data, or other personal data as a condition of employment or contract is unlawful.
- **DPA 2018 Schedules 2/3 exemptions** are SAR-specific and distinct from the general UK GDPR framework. The most financially relevant: crime/taxation, legal professional privilege, regulatory functions, management information, negotiations with the requester, confidential references.
- Refusal in all cases requires notification to the individual of reasons, ICO complaint right, and judicial remedy right.

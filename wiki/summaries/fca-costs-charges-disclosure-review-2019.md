# FCA Review on Disclosure of Costs by Asset Managers (February 2019)

## Overview

The FCA reviewed how asset managers calculate and disclose transaction costs and how effective overall cost disclosures are. The review ran two workstreams:

1. **Transaction cost calculation** — 16 asset managers (small, mid, large) reviewed for methodology compliance under PRIIPs and UCITS requirements
2. **Effectiveness and consistency of disclosures** — desk-based review of 26 product cost disclosures across KIIDs, PRIIPs KIDs, factsheets, and firm websites

Context: the review was triggered by the FCA's Asset Management Market Study finding of weak price competition, partly attributable to ineffective cost disclosure. MiFID II and the PRIIPs Regulation had both introduced new cost disclosure requirements in January 2018 — this review assessed early compliance.

Overall conclusion: most firms calculate transaction costs in accordance with the rules, but the FCA found calculation errors that cause costs to be understated, and disclosure inconsistencies that leave investors confused and potentially misled. Asset managers may be communicating with customers in a manner that is unfair, unclear, or misleading.

---

## Scope

Applies to:
- UCITS managers — Key Investor Information Documents (KIIDs)
- Operators of Non-UCITS Retail Schemes (NURS) — NURS-KII documents
- Fund managers producing PRIIPs Key Information Documents (KIDs)
- Disclosures in other marketing material subject to MiFID II

---

## Workstream 1 — Transaction Cost Calculation

Most firms calculate transaction costs correctly, but four failure modes were identified.

### Failure 1: Arrival Price Methodology Misapplied to Primary Issues

Some firms incorrectly apply the arrival price methodology when calculating transaction costs for primary issues (subscriptions to new issues). This treatment credits the investment product with a **negative transaction cost** on each primary issue subscription — an artificial reduction of the disclosed cost figure that misleads investors about the true cost of investing. The correct treatment per the ESMA PRIIPs Q&A is to assign no transaction cost (zero) to primary issues, not a negative figure.

### Failure 2: Anti-Dilution Levy Misuse

The anti-dilution levy exists to protect existing investors from the cost of others entering or exiting a fund (dilution). Some firms use it in a way that artificially reduces the stated transaction cost — in some cases, the levy applied exceeds the total explicit and implicit trading costs, resulting in a **negative net transaction cost figure**. This is a misapplication: the levy should reduce dilution harm, not produce a below-zero disclosure figure.

### Failure 3: Ineffective Oversight of Outsourced Calculation

Some asset managers outsource transaction cost analysis and calculation, in some cases to overseas jurisdictions. The FCA found instances of firms **failing to effectively oversee the outsourced activity**. Responsibility for compliance remains with the authorised firm regardless of outsourcing arrangement. Specific requirements apply to AFMs that outsource certain activities. **(SYSC)**

### Failure 4: UCITS Underlying Fund Costs Classified as "Other Ongoing Costs"

Some UCITS that invest in other collective investment schemes disclose the transaction costs of those underlying funds under "other ongoing costs" rather than as transaction costs. This is consistent with existing guidance but results in a **reported transaction cost figure of zero** for the fund itself. The FCA notes this as an explanation for why some UCITS products show zero transaction costs — it does not indicate zero cost, merely a disclosure classification that obscures the true figure.

## Workstream 2 — Effectiveness and Consistency of Disclosures

26 product disclosures reviewed across KIIDs, PRIIPs KIDs, factsheets, and websites. Many cost disclosures found to be unclear and failing to disclose all related costs and charges.

### Finding 1: Material Portfolio Transaction Costs Absent from UCITS KIIDs

The UCITS KII Regulation requires that where portfolio transaction costs are likely to have a **material impact on returns** (given the strategy of the product), this must be disclosed in the "objectives and investment policy" section of the KIID. The FCA found this almost universally ignored across the sample. In one extreme case, a **4% per annum transaction cost** was omitted from the KIID. Firms must make a product-specific assessment of what constitutes a material impact and disclose accordingly.

### Finding 2: PRIIPs KID Contradicted by Marketing Material

The PRIIPs Regulation prohibits marketing material from reducing the significance of information in the KID. The FCA found asset managers providing disclosures in factsheets and websites that showed **lower cost figures than the KID** — in some instances, factsheets omit costs entirely or show only the ongoing charge, excluding transaction costs, performance fees, and borrowing charges that are disclosed in the KID.

Named example: a fund's PRIIPs KID showed total charges of approximately **3% per annum**; the only cost disclosed on the factsheet was the **1.2% annual management charge (AMC)**. This factsheet figure not only understates costs but contradicts the KID by omitting components that the Regulation requires the KID to include.

### Finding 3: Disclosure Accessibility Failure

Even where all costs are technically available, the FCA found they are commonly disclosed in a way that requires **unreasonable effort** from customers to find and understand — typically scattered across separate pages or documents on a firm's website. This is treated as a regulatory concern in its own right, not just a presentation issue. Where additional charges have a material effect on investment returns, the difficulty of locating those charges is itself a potential breach of the obligation to communicate clearly and not misleadingly.

### Finding 4: AMC as Prohibited Terminology

Some UCITS product providers continue to prominently refer to the **Annual Management Charge (AMC)** as a headline cost figure. The FCA identified this practice as potentially misleading in TR 14-07 (2014 thematic review on clarity of fund charges) and reiterated this position in the 2019 review. Firms had been on notice since 2014. The AMC excludes other charges that investors bear; the figure that should be disclosed prominently is the **Ongoing Charges Figure (OCF)**, which is more comprehensive. Continued prominent use of AMC after five years of FCA guidance is treated as an aggravating factor.

## Regulatory Framework for Costs Disclosure

| Requirement | Instrument | Key obligation |
|-------------|------------|---------------|
| Transaction cost calculation | PRIIPs Regulation | Disclosed in KID; arrival price methodology for implicit costs |
| Aggregate cost disclosure | PRIIPs Regulation; MiFID II | All costs and charges; manufacturers provide data for distributor MiFID II compliance |
| UCITS KIID content | UCITS KII Regulation | Material transaction costs in "objectives and investment policy"; ongoing charges in charges section |
| Marketing material consistency | PRIIPs Regulation | Must not contradict or reduce significance of KID |
| General communications standard | PRIN 7; COBS | Fair, clear and not misleading |

## Key Points for Agents

- Portfolio transaction costs must be included in cost disclosures — omitting them (or reporting them as zero through UCITS-in-UCITS classification) is a named FCA failure. This was identified in 2019 and again in the 2021 product governance review.
- Four specific transaction cost calculation failures: arrival price misuse (negative cost from primary issues); anti-dilution levy misuse (negative net cost); outsourcing oversight failure; UCITS underlying costs misclassified as "other ongoing costs."
- The PRIIPs Regulation creates a specific legal prohibition: marketing material (factsheets, websites) must not contradict or reduce the significance of figures in the KID. Showing a lower cost figure on a factsheet than in the KID is a named breach.
- Material portfolio transaction costs must appear in the UCITS KIID "objectives and investment policy" section — not just be absent. This is a UCITS KII Regulation requirement, not merely good practice.
- "Annual Management Charge (AMC)" used as a headline figure is misleading per TR 14-07 (2014). Use of AMC rather than OCF five years after that guidance was treated as an aggravating compliance failure in 2019.
- Disclosure accessibility is a standalone regulatory concern: costs scattered across multiple documents requiring unreasonable effort to aggregate are a potential breach even if all figures are technically available somewhere.

# SYSC 6 — Compliance, Internal Audit and Financial Crime

## Overview

SYSC 6 has three sections:
- **SYSC 6.1** — Compliance: the baseline compliance policies obligation (all firms) and the enhanced compliance function requirements (common platform firms, management companies, P2P operators)
- **SYSC 6.2** — Internal audit: the internal audit function obligation for common platform firms, management companies, and P2P operators
- **SYSC 6.3** — Financial crime: the AML systems and controls obligation, the MLRO requirement, and the connection to JMLSG guidance

---

## SYSC 6.1 — Compliance

### Baseline Compliance Obligation (SYSC 6.1.1R — All Firms)

A firm must establish, implement and maintain adequate policies and procedures sufficient to ensure compliance of the firm including its managers, employees and appointed representatives (or where applicable, tied agents) with its obligations under the regulatory system — **and for countering the risk that the firm might be used to further financial crime (SYSC 6.1.1R)**.

Two distinct obligations arise from a single rule:
1. Regulatory compliance systems and controls
2. Financial crime prevention systems and controls

The financial crime element of SYSC 6.1.1R is the SYSC-level hook for the AML/CTF obligations elaborated in SYSC 6.3. The FCA provides supplementary guidance in FCG and FCTR **(SYSC 6.1.1AG; SYSC 6.3.11G)**.

### Compliance Policies for Management Companies and P2P Operators (SYSC 6.1.2R)

A management company or P2P operator must, taking into account the nature, scale and complexity of its business, establish, implement and maintain adequate policies and procedures designed to:
- Detect any risk of failure by the firm to comply with its obligations under the regulatory system
- Identify associated risks
- Put in place adequate measures and procedures to minimise such risks
- Enable the FCA to exercise its powers effectively **(SYSC 6.1.2R)**

### Compliance Policies for Common Platform Firms (SYSC 6.1.2-AR)

A common platform firm must establish, implement and maintain adequate policies and procedures designed to detect any risk of failure by the firm to comply with its obligations under the **UK law on markets in financial instruments** — and put in place adequate measures and procedures to minimise such risks and enable the FCA to exercise its powers effectively **(SYSC 6.1.2-AR)**.

---

### Compliance Function — Management Companies and P2P Operators (SYSC 6.1.3R)

A management company or P2P operator must maintain a **permanent and effective compliance function which operates independently** and has the following responsibilities **(SYSC 6.1.3R)**:
1. Monitor and assess the adequacy and effectiveness of the measures and procedures put in place in accordance with SYSC 6.1.2R, and the actions taken to address any deficiencies in the firm's compliance with its obligations
2. Advise and assist the relevant persons responsible for carrying out regulated activities to comply with the firm's obligations under the regulatory system

### Compliance Function — Common Platform Firms (SYSC 6.1.3-AR)

A common platform firm must establish and maintain a permanent and effective compliance function which operates independently and has four responsibilities **(SYSC 6.1.3-AR)**:
1. **Monitor** — on a permanent basis, monitor and assess on a regular basis the adequacy and effectiveness of the policies and procedures and actions taken to address compliance deficiencies
2. **Advise** — advise and assist relevant persons responsible for carrying out the firm's designated investment business to comply with obligations under UK law on markets in financial instruments
3. **Annual report to management body** — report to the management body, at least annually, on:
   - (a) the implementation and effectiveness of the overall control environment for the firm's designated investment business
   - (b) the risks that have been identified
   - (c) the complaints-handling reporting as well as remedies undertaken or to be undertaken
4. **Monitor complaints handling** — monitor the operations of the complaints-handling process and consider complaints as a source of relevant information in the context of its general monitoring responsibilities

### Risk-Based Monitoring Programme (SYSC 6.1.3-BR)

To comply with SYSC 6.1.3-AR(1) and (2), the firm's compliance function must conduct an assessment on the basis of which it must establish a **risk-based monitoring programme** that takes into consideration all areas of the firm's designated investment business and any relevant ancillary services, including relevant information gathered in relation to complaints-handling. The programme must establish priorities determined by the compliance risk assessment ensuring that compliance risk is comprehensively monitored **(SYSC 6.1.3-BR)**.

### Compliance Function Independence — Common Platform Firms (SYSC 6.1.3-CR)

To enable the compliance function to discharge its responsibilities properly and independently, a common platform firm must ensure that **(SYSC 6.1.3-CR)**:
1. The compliance function has the **necessary authority, resources, expertise and access to all relevant information**
2. A **compliance officer must be appointed** by the management body and is responsible for the compliance function and for any reporting as to compliance required by UK law on markets in financial instruments and by SYSC 4.3.2-AR(3)
3. The compliance function **reports on an ad-hoc basis directly to the management body** where it detects a significant risk of failure by the firm to comply with its obligations
4. The relevant persons involved in the compliance function **must not be involved in the performance of the services or activities they monitor**
5. The method of determining the remuneration of the relevant persons involved in the compliance function **must not compromise their objectivity** and must not be likely to do so

### Compliance Officer — Removal and Remuneration Independence (SYSC 6.1.4-ABG)

For SMCR firms required to appoint a compliance officer:
- The firm should have procedures to ensure that removal or other disciplinary sanctioning of the compliance officer **does not undermine the independence of the compliance function** **(SYSC 6.1.4-ABG(2))**
- In the FCA's view, it will be appropriate in many cases for the **removal or disciplinary sanctioning of the compliance officer to require the approval of a majority of the management body, including at least a majority of its members who do not perform any executive function** in the firm **(SYSC 6.1.4-ABG(3))**

This protection parallels the equivalent protection for the head of the internal audit function under SYSC 6.2.1B G.

### Compliance Independence for Management Companies and P2P Operators (SYSC 6.1.4R)

The same independence conditions apply — the compliance function must have authority, resources, expertise and access; a compliance officer must be appointed; the relevant persons must not be involved in the activities they monitor; and their remuneration must not compromise objectivity — with the exception that the compliance officer is appointed and responsible under SYSC 4.3.2R (not SYSC 4.3.2-AR) **(SYSC 6.1.4R)**.

### Compliance Oversight Function for Non-Common Platform Firms (SYSC 6.1.4AR)

A firm that is **not a common platform firm or management company** but which carries on **designated investment business** with or for retail clients or professional clients must allocate to a **director or senior manager** the function of **(SYSC 6.1.4AR)**:
- **(a)** having responsibility for oversight of the firm's compliance with the rules in:
  - COBS (Conduct of Business sourcebook)
  - COLL (Collective Investment Schemes sourcebook)
  - CASS (Client Assets sourcebook)
  - ICOBS (Insurance: Conduct of Business sourcebook)
- **(b)** reporting to the governing body in respect of that responsibility

This is distinct from the MLRO obligation in SYSC 6.3 — a single person may hold both roles, but the obligations are separate.

### Proportionality Carve-Out (SYSC 6.1.5R / 6.1.5AR)

A management company or P2P operator need not comply with SYSC 6.1.4R(3) or (4) (the non-involvement requirement and the remuneration non-compromise requirement) if it can demonstrate that in view of the nature, scale and complexity of its business, those requirements are not proportionate and the compliance function continues to be effective **(SYSC 6.1.5R)**.

A common platform firm has an equivalent proportionality carve-out for SYSC 6.1.3-CR(4) and (5), but must assess whether the effectiveness of the compliance function is compromised and review this assessment regularly **(SYSC 6.1.5AR)**.

## SYSC 6.2 — Internal Audit

### Internal Audit Obligation (SYSC 6.2.1R)

A firm that is a common platform firm, a management company, or a P2P operator must, where appropriate and proportionate in view of the nature, scale and complexity of its business, **establish and maintain an internal audit function** that is separate and independent from the other functions and activities of the firm **(SYSC 6.2.1R)**.

The internal audit function has four responsibilities:
1. Establish, implement and maintain an audit plan to examine and evaluate the adequacy and effectiveness of the firm's systems, internal control mechanisms and arrangements
2. Issue recommendations based on the result of work carried out
3. Verify compliance with those recommendations
4. Report in relation to internal audit matters (to senior management under SYSC 4.3.2R, or to the management body and senior management under SYSC 4.3.2-AR for common platform firms)

### Internal Audit as an SMF Function

For an SMCR firm that is a **PRA-authorised person**, the internal audit function is a **PRA controlled function (SMF5)**. For an **enhanced scope SMCR firm** that is not PRA-authorised, it is an **FCA controlled function (SMF5)** **(SYSC 6.2.2G(3))**.

### Independence Protection for Head of Internal Audit (SYSC 6.2.1BG)

The firm should have procedures to ensure that removal or disciplinary sanctioning of the **head of the internal audit function** does not undermine the independence of the function. In the FCA's view, removal or disciplinary sanctioning of the head of internal audit should require the approval of a majority of the management body, including at least a majority of its members who do not perform any executive function — paralleling the compliance officer protection **(SYSC 6.2.1BG)**.

## SYSC 6.3 — Financial Crime

### AML Systems and Controls Obligation (SYSC 6.3.1R)

A firm must ensure the policies and procedures established under SYSC 6.1.1R include **systems and controls that** **(SYSC 6.3.1R)**:
1. Enable it to identify, assess, monitor and manage money laundering risk
2. Are comprehensive and proportionate to the nature, scale and complexity of its activities

The firm must carry out a **regular assessment** of the adequacy of these systems and controls to ensure they continue to comply with SYSC 6.3.1R **(SYSC 6.3.3R)**.

### JMLSG Guidance as FCA Benchmark (SYSC 6.3.5G)

The FCA, when considering whether a breach of its rules on systems and controls against money laundering has occurred, **will have regard to whether a firm has followed relevant provisions in the guidance for the UK financial sector issued by the Joint Money Laundering Steering Group (JMLSG)** **(SYSC 6.3.5G)**. JMLSG guidance is therefore the operational benchmark against which SYSC 6.3 compliance is assessed in practice.

### Money Laundering Risk Factors (SYSC 6.3.6G)

In identifying its money laundering risk and in establishing systems and controls, a firm should consider a range of factors including:
1. Its customer, product and activity profiles
2. Its distribution channels
3. The complexity and volume of its transactions
4. Its processes and systems
5. Its operating environment

### Required System and Control Elements (SYSC 6.3.7G)

A firm's AML systems and controls should include:
1. **Training** — appropriate training for employees in relation to money laundering
2. **MLRO annual report** — appropriate provision of information to the governing body and senior management, including at least annually from the MLRO on the operation and effectiveness of those systems and controls
3. **Documentation** — appropriate documentation of risk management policies and risk profile in relation to money laundering
4. **Day-to-day measures** — appropriate measures to ensure that money laundering risk is taken into account in day-to-day operations (development of new products, taking on new customers, changes in business profile)
5. **Access** — appropriate measures to ensure that procedures for identification of new customers do not unreasonably deny access to potential customers who cannot reasonably be expected to produce detailed evidence of identity

### Director/Senior Manager Responsible for AML (SYSC 6.3.8R)

A firm must **allocate to a director or senior manager** (who may also be the MLRO) **overall responsibility within the firm for the establishment and maintenance of effective anti-money laundering systems and controls** **(SYSC 6.3.8R(1))**.

A firm may **not** allocate this overall responsibility to a person who is approved to perform the **other overall responsibility function** **(SYSC 6.3.8R(2))**. This prevents a catch-all SMF holder from absorbing AML accountability without specific expertise.

### MLRO Appointment (SYSC 6.3.9R)

A firm (with the exception of a sole trader who has no employees) must **(SYSC 6.3.9R)**:
1. **Appoint an individual as MLRO**, with responsibility for oversight of its compliance with the FCA's rules on systems and controls against money laundering
2. **Ensure its MLRO has a level of authority and independence** within the firm and access to resources and information sufficient to enable them to carry out that responsibility

The MLRO's role is to act as the **focal point for all activity within the firm relating to anti-money laundering** **(SYSC 6.3.10G)**. The FCA expects the MLRO to be **based in the United Kingdom** **(SYSC 6.3.10G)**.

## Key Points for Agents

- **SYSC 6.1.1R is a dual obligation — regulatory compliance AND financial crime prevention (SYSC 6.1.1R).** Both are mandatory for all FCA-authorised firms. The financial crime element is not optional and is not limited to AML-supervised firms — it applies to all FCA-authorised firms.
- **The compliance function for common platform firms must report to the management body at least annually on the control environment, identified risks, and complaints-handling remediation (SYSC 6.1.3-AR(3)).** Annual reporting to the board is a hard obligation, not best practice.
- **Compliance officer independence has two components: remuneration and removal (SYSC 6.1.3-CR(5); SYSC 6.1.4-ABG).** The remuneration method must not compromise objectivity. Removal or disciplinary action should require approval of a majority of the management body including a majority of NEDs. These protections parallel those for the head of internal audit.
- **For non-common platform firms carrying on designated investment business, SYSC 6.1.4AR requires a named director or senior manager responsible for compliance oversight (COBS/COLL/CASS/ICOBS) and reporting to the governing body.** This is distinct from the MLRO obligation and from the SM&CR compliance oversight function.
- **The MLRO must be UK-based and have a level of authority, independence, and resource access sufficient to fulfil the role (SYSC 6.3.9R/6.3.10G).** The director/senior manager with overall AML responsibility (SYSC 6.3.8R) and the MLRO may be the same person but hold two distinct obligations. Neither may be the person holding the other overall responsibility SMF.
- **The FCA's SYSC 6.3 compliance benchmark is JMLSG guidance (SYSC 6.3.5G).** Failure to follow JMLSG guidance does not automatically constitute a breach, but the FCA will have regard to it when assessing whether a breach of SYSC 6.3.1R has occurred.

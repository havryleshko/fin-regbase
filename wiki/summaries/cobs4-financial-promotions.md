# COBS 4 — Communicating with Clients, Including Financial Promotions

COBS 4 is the primary FCA conduct chapter governing how firms communicate with clients and communicate or approve financial promotions. It runs in parallel with the s.21 FSMA gateway (which prohibits unauthorised financial promotion) and sets the substantive standards that authorised firms and s.21 approvers must meet.

---

## COBS 4.1 — Application

COBS 4 applies to:
- **Communications** — any information a firm addresses to, or disseminates in a way likely to be received by, a client
- **Financial promotions** — invitations or inducements to engage in investment activity, communicated in the course of business

The chapter has a **dual-track structure**: some rules apply to all retail client communications (non-MiFID track, COBS 4.5) while others apply specifically to MiFID and insurance-based investment products (MiFID track, COBS 4.5A). Which track applies depends on the firm type and the product.

---

## COBS 4.2 — Fair, Clear and Not Misleading (FCNM)

### The Core Rule

**(COBS 4.2.1R)** A firm must ensure that every communication with a client, and every financial promotion, is fair, clear and not misleading.

This is the master standard for all firm communications and financial promotions — it applies regardless of which more specific rules also apply.

### Reasonable Steps Defence

**(COBS 4.2.6R)** Where a firm approves a financial promotion for communication by an unauthorised person (under s.21 FSMA), and that promotion is communicated in contravention of FSMA s.21, the firm has a defence to a s.138D civil liability claim if it took reasonable steps to ensure the promotion would be communicated only to persons to whom it could lawfully be communicated.

---

## COBS 4.3 — Identifiability

**(COBS 4.3.1R)** A financial promotion must be identifiable as a financial promotion. This is separate from the FCNM rule: a promotion may be clear and fair but still breach COBS 4.3 if it disguises its commercial nature.

---

## COBS 4.5 — Retail Client Communications (Non-MiFID)

### Seven-Part Standard

**(COBS 4.5.2R)** A communication addressed to a retail client must:

1. Have sufficient information having regard to the nature of the communication and the nature of the retail client
2. Be presented in a way that is likely to be understood by an average member of the group at whom it is directed
3. Not disguise, diminish or obscure important information, statements or warnings
4. Not use a prominent positive statement alongside a warning in a way likely to diminish the significance of the warning
5. Present performance comparisons in a way that is consistent and relevant
6. Include appropriate risk warnings where there is potential for loss
7. Not omit material information in a way likely to deceive the client

### Digital Approver FRN Link

**(COBS 4.5.2AR)** Where a direct offer financial promotion is communicated digitally, it must include a functioning hyperlink to the FCA Register page for the firm that approved or communicated it (using the FRN). This enables consumers to verify the approver's authorised status in real time.

### FIIA Verbatim Risk Warning

**(COBS 4.5.16R)** Where a communication relates to a forestry investment or similar illiquid alternative investment (FIIA), it must include the verbatim FCA risk warning in the prescribed form. Using a paraphrased or shortened version is not sufficient.

## COBS 4.5A — MiFID Communications

### Past Performance

**(COBS 4.5A.10R)** Where a MiFID communication includes past performance information:
- Must cover at least the last **5 complete calendar years** (or all complete years if fewer than 5)
- Must use **actual performance**, not simulated performance
- Must present performance on a **per-calendar-year basis**, with the current incomplete year shown separately if included
- Must not use **selective periods** that would present a misleadingly favourable picture
- Must give the performance's **reference period** and basis (e.g., gross or net of charges)

### Simulated Past Performance

**(COBS 4.5A.12R)** Where a MiFID communication includes simulated past performance:
- Must clearly **identify the performance as simulated**
- Must not use simulated performance as a **prominent feature** of the communication
- Must describe the **underlying financial instrument, index or strategy** on which the simulation is based

### Future Performance

**(COBS 4.5A.14R)** Where a MiFID communication includes future performance projections:
- Must not be based solely on past performance or simulated past performance
- Must be based on **reasonable assumptions supported by objective data**
- Must include a **prominent warning** that projections are not a reliable indicator of future performance

---

## COBS 4.7 — Specific Product Restrictions

**(COBS 4.7.6R)** Written communications about warrants and derivatives addressed to retail clients must meet additional requirements over and above COBS 4.5.2R, including specific language about risk of loss and margin requirements. Verbal communications are subject to equivalent oral disclosure.

---

## COBS 4.8 — Cold Calling

### The Restriction

**(COBS 4.8.2R)** A firm must not cold call a retail client unless **at least one** of the following grounds is satisfied:

1. **Existing client**: The person is an existing client of the firm (or has been a client within a reasonable period)
2. **Consent**: The person has clearly indicated willingness to receive unsolicited calls from the firm about investment business
3. **Generally marketed packaged product**: The call relates to a packaged product that is generally marketed to the public and the person has not indicated unwillingness to be called

Cold calling outside these three grounds is prohibited regardless of the method of introduction or lead source.

### Conduct During Calls

Regardless of whether the call is permitted, the firm must still comply with FCNM (COBS 4.2.1R), disclose the call's commercial purpose, and not use high-pressure sales techniques.

---

## COBS 4.9 — Overseas Financial Promotions

Where a firm communicates or approves a financial promotion for communication to persons in an overseas jurisdiction, it must include a disclosure explaining the extent to which any applicable UK regulatory protections (e.g., FSCS compensation, FOS access) do not apply. The disclosure must be clear, prominent and placed where the target audience is likely to see it before engaging with the substantive content.

---

## COBS 4.10 — The s.21 Approver Regime

The approver regime under COBS 4.10 governs firms that approve financial promotions for communication by **unauthorised persons** under s.21 FSMA. The regime has three distinct limbs.

### Pre-Approval Confirmation

**(COBS 4.10.2R)** Before approving a financial promotion, a firm must:
- Confirm that the promotion **complies with COBS 4** in all respects at the date of approval
- Have in place **ongoing monitoring arrangements** to identify if the promotion ceases to comply after approval and to withdraw approval promptly if it does

### Quarterly Attestation from Unauthorised Person

**(COBS 4.10.2R)** For financial promotions communicated by unauthorised persons, the approver must obtain a **quarterly written attestation** from the unauthorised person confirming that the promotion has not been materially changed since approval and that all conditions imposed by the approver continue to be met.

### Competence and Expertise

**(COBS 4.10.9AR)** A firm must not approve a financial promotion unless the individuals involved in the approval have the **competence and expertise** to assess whether the promotion meets the relevant requirements. For specialist products (e.g., cryptoassets, structured products, pension transfers), generic compliance competence is insufficient — sector-specific expertise is required.

*Note: The full treatment of the approver regime, including the FCA Gateway (requirement to apply for s.21 approval permission from January 2024) and crypto-specific approver rules, is at [financial-promotions-approver-regime.md](financial-promotions-approver-regime.md).*

## COBS 4.11 — Record Keeping

**(COBS 4.11.1R)** Retention periods for financial promotions records vary by product type:

| Communication type | Minimum retention period |
|---|---|
| Pension transfer / pension opt-out | Indefinitely |
| Life policy or personal pension | 5 years |
| MiFID business record | 5 years from date of communication |
| Other retail client financial promotion | 3 years |

The retention obligation applies to the promotion itself and to all approvals, sign-offs, ongoing monitoring records, and any attestations received under COBS 4.10.2R.

| Other regime | Interaction |
|---|---|
| CONC 3 | CONC has its own CFNM standard (CONC 3.3.1R) for consumer credit promotions; COBS 4 does not apply to those |
| PS23/6 (cryptoassets) | Qualifying cryptoasset promotions are subject to COBS 4 requirements AND crypto-specific consumer journey rules |
| COBS 9B | Ready-made suggestions delivered under targeted support will generally incorporate a financial promotion — COBS 4 obligations apply alongside COBS 9B.6.15G |
| SYSC 10 | Approvers must have conflicts of interest policies that address the approver role and third-party relationships |
| COBS 4.5A vs 4.5 | MiFID track (4.5A) takes precedence for MiFID communications; non-MiFID track (4.5) applies to all others |

## Key Points for Agents

- **FCNM is the master rule** (COBS 4.2.1R): every communication must be fair, clear and not misleading — this applies regardless of which more specific rules also apply.
- **Dual track**: Non-MiFID retail client comms are governed by the 7-part rule at COBS 4.5.2R; MiFID comms get the stricter COBS 4.5A rules including the 5-year past performance requirement and simulated performance identification rules.
- **Cold calling is restricted to 3 grounds** (COBS 4.8.2R): existing client, consent, or generally marketed packaged product. Any call outside these is prohibited.
- **Approvers have three obligations** (COBS 4.10.2R, 4.10.9AR): pre-approval COBS 4 confirmation, ongoing monitoring with withdrawal power, quarterly attestation from the unauthorised person — plus competence/expertise in the subject matter.
- **Record retention tiers** (COBS 4.11.1R): pension transfer/opt-out records indefinitely; life policy/personal pension 5 years; MiFID records 5 years; other retail 3 years.

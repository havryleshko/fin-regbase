# COBS 10A — Appropriateness (MiFID and insurance-based investment products provisions)

## Scope

COBS 10A applies to a firm which **(COBS 10A.1.1R)**:
1. Provides **investment services** in the course of **MiFID or equivalent third country business**; or
2. Carries on **insurance distribution** in relation to an **insurance-based investment product (IBIP)**;

other than when the firm makes a **personal recommendation**, is **providing targeted support**, or carries out **portfolio management**.

**Reliance:** COBS 10A also applies to a firm which assesses appropriateness **on behalf of a MiFID investment firm** so that the other firm may rely on the assessment under COBS 2.4.4R (Reliance on other investment firms: MiFID and equivalent business) **(COBS 10A.1.2R)**.

**IBIP scope:** For insurance distribution in relation to IBIPs, only those rules in COBS 10A which implemented the IDD, or are derived from the IDD Regulation, apply. IDD-derived rules are identified by a "**Note:**" following the relevant rule **(COBS 10A.1.5R–10A.1.6G)**.

---

## The Assessment Obligation

### Core Obligation (COBS 10A.2.1R)

A firm must ask the client to provide information regarding that client's **knowledge and experience** in the investment field relevant to the specific type of product or service offered or demanded so as to enable the firm to assess whether the service or product envisaged is **appropriate** for the client. **Note:** MiFID article 25(3); IDD article 30(2) first paragraph.

**IBIPs — additional demands and needs:** A firm carrying on insurance distribution is also required to comply with COBS 7.3 (additional insurance distribution obligations: demands and needs) **(COBS 10A.2.1AG)**. **Note:** IDD article 30(2) first paragraph.

### Bundled Packages (COBS 10A.2.2R)

Where a bundle of services or products is envisaged, the appropriateness assessment must consider whether the **overall bundled package is appropriate** **(COBS 10A.2.2R)**. **Note:** MiFID article 25(3); IDD article 30(2) first paragraph.

## Assessing Knowledge and Experience

### MiFID Business — Professional Client Assumption (COBS 10A.2.3R)

When assessing appropriateness, a firm:
1. Must determine whether the client has the necessary experience and knowledge to understand the risks; and
2. **May assume** that a **professional client** has the necessary experience and knowledge to understand the risks involved in relation to those particular investment services or transactions, or types of transaction or product, for which they are **classified as a professional client**.

This assumption applies in the appropriateness context for all professional clients (both per se and elective), in relation to the specific services/products for which they are classified. Unlike the suitability professional client assumptions under COBS 9A.2.5R, there is no further distinction between per se and elective professional clients for the knowledge and experience dimension in appropriateness.

### IBIPs (COBS 10A.2.3AR)

For IBIPs, the firm must determine whether the client has the necessary knowledge and experience to understand the risks involved in relation to the service or IBIP proposed or demanded when assessing whether the product distributed is appropriate. **Note:** IDD article 15.

### Information Required — MiFID Business (COBS 10A.2.4R)

The information regarding knowledge and experience must include, to the extent appropriate to the nature of the client, the service, and the type of product or transaction envisaged:
- **(1)** The types of service, transaction, and **financial instrument** with which the client is familiar;
- **(2)** The nature, volume, and frequency of the client's **transactions in financial instruments** and the period over which they have been carried out; and
- **(3)** The client's **level of education and profession or relevant former profession**.

### Information Required — IBIPs (COBS 10A.2.4AR)

Same three categories, adapted for the IBIP context: types of service/transaction/IBIP or financial instrument familiar with; nature, number, value, and frequency of past transactions in IBIPs or financial instruments; level of education and profession or relevant former profession.

Where information required for COBS 10A.2.1R and COBS 10A.2.2R has already been obtained for the purposes of COBS 7.3.4R (demands and needs), the firm **must not request it again** from the client **(COBS 10A.2.4AR(2))**. **Note:** IDD articles 17(1) and (3).

## No Discouraging Disclosure; Reliance; Existing Information

### MiFID Business (COBS 10A.2.5R)

A firm must **not encourage** a client not to provide information required for the purposes of COBS 10A.2.1R.

### IBIPs (COBS 10A.2.5AR)

Same prohibition for IBIPs. **Note:** IDD article 17(2).

### Reliance — MiFID Business (COBS 10A.2.6R)

A firm is entitled to rely on client-provided information unless it **is aware or ought to be aware** that the information is manifestly out of date, inaccurate, or incomplete. The "or ought to be aware" objective element applies — stricter than the COBS 10 non-MiFID standard.

### Reliance — IBIPs (COBS 10A.2.6AR)

Same standard with the "or ought to be aware" element. **Note:** IDD article 17(4).

### Use of Existing Information (COBS 10A.2.7G)

When assessing appropriateness, a firm may use information it **already has in its possession** — for both MiFID business and IBIPs.

### Knowledge Alone May Be Sufficient (COBS 10A.2.8G)

Depending on the circumstances, a firm may be satisfied that the client's **knowledge alone** is sufficient to understand the risks. Where reasonable, a firm **may infer knowledge from experience** — for both MiFID business and IBIPs.

### No Duty to Communicate Assessment (COBS 10A.2.10G)

If the firm is satisfied that the client has the necessary knowledge and experience, there is **no duty to communicate this to the client**. If the firm does so, it must not do so in a way that amounts to making a **personal recommendation** unless it complies with COBS 9A — communicating the assessment in a way that tips into advice moves the transaction into the suitability regime.

### Restricted Mass Market Investments (COBS 10A.2.11G)

When determining whether a client has the necessary knowledge to understand risks in relation to a restricted mass market investment, a firm should consider asking questions covering at least the matters in:
- **COBS 10 Annex 1G** for non-readily realisable securities; or
- **COBS 10 Annex 5G** for UK RIE cryptoasset exchange traded notes.

### Long-Term Asset Funds (COBS 10A.2.12G)

For units in a LTAF under COBS 4.12A (Promotion of restricted mass market investments), questions should cover at least the matters in **COBS 10 Annex 3G**.

## Warning Obligations

### Product Assessed as Inappropriate (COBS 10A.3.1R)

If a firm considers, on the basis of information received, that the product or service is **not appropriate** for the client, the firm must warn the client. The warning may be provided in a **standardised format**. **Note:** MiFID article 25(3); IDD article 30(2) second paragraph.

### Client Provides Insufficient Information (COBS 10A.3.2R)

If the client does not provide the information, or provides **insufficient information** regarding knowledge and experience, the firm must warn the client that the firm is **not in a position to determine** whether the product or service is appropriate. The warning may be provided in a **standardised format**. **Note:** MiFID article 25(3); IDD article 30(2) third paragraph.

### Client Insists on Proceeding After Warning (COBS 10A.3.3G)

If a client asks the firm to proceed despite a warning, it is for the **firm to consider whether to do so** having regard to the circumstances. The firm's decision must be recorded (see Record Keeping).

## When Appropriateness Need Not Be Assessed

### The Execution-Only / Non-Complex Exemption (COBS 10A.4.1R)

A firm is **not required** to assess appropriateness if either **(a)** or **(aa)**, and both **(b)** and **(c)**, are met:

**(a)** The service only consists of **execution or reception and transmission of client orders** (excluding ancillary service (2) in MiFID Annex I — granting of credits or loans where the relevant credits or loans do not comprise existing credit limits), relates to particular non-complex financial instruments (see below), and is provided **at the initiative of the client**; **or**

**(aa)** The **insurance distribution activity** relates to particular types of **non-complex IBIPs** (see below) and is carried out **at the initiative of the client**; and

**(b)** The client has been **clearly informed** (whether in a standardised format) that the firm is **not required to assess the appropriateness** of the financial instrument, service, or IBIP provided or offered, and therefore the client **does not benefit from the protection** of the appropriateness rules; and

**(c)** The firm complies with its obligations in relation to **conflicts of interest**.

**Note:** MiFID article 25(4); IDD article 30(3).

### Non-Complex Financial Instruments — MiFID (COBS 10A.4.1R(2))

The non-complex financial instruments for MiFID appropriateness exemption purposes are:
- **(a)** Shares in companies admitted to trading on a regulated market, EU regulated market, equivalent third country market, or MTF — **except** shares that embed a derivative and units in collective investment undertakings that are not UCITS;
- **(b)** Bonds or other forms of securitised debt admitted to trading on such markets — **except** those that embed a derivative or incorporate a structure which makes it difficult for the client to understand the risk involved;
- **(c)** Money market instruments — excluding those that embed a derivative or incorporate a structure making risk difficult to understand;
- **(d)** Shares or units in a UCITS — **excluding structured UCITS**;
- **(e)** Structured deposits — excluding those that incorporate a structure making it difficult for the client to understand the risk of return or the cost of exiting before term; and
- **(f)** Other non-complex financial instruments satisfying all **six criteria** under COBS 10A.4.2R.

### The Six-Criterion Non-Complex Test for MiFID (COBS 10A.4.2R)

A financial instrument is non-complex under COBS 10A.4.1R(2)(f) only if it satisfies **all six** of:
1. It is not a financial instrument covered by paragraphs 4–11 of Part 1 of Schedule 2 to the Regulated Activities Order, and is not a security giving the right to acquire or sell a transferable security or giving rise to a cash settlement by reference to transferable securities, currencies, interest rates, yields, commodities, or other indices or measures;
2. There are frequent opportunities to dispose of, redeem, or otherwise realise the instrument at **publicly available market prices** or prices made available or **validated by valuation systems independent of the issuer**;
3. It does not involve any **actual or potential liability exceeding the cost of acquiring** the instrument;
4. It does **not incorporate a clause, condition, or trigger that could fundamentally alter the nature, risk, or pay-out profile** — such as an instrument incorporating a right to convert into a different investment;
5. It does **not include any explicit or implicit exit charges that have the effect of making the instrument illiquid** even though there are technically frequent disposal opportunities; and
6. Adequately comprehensive information on its characteristics is publicly available and **readily understood by the average retail client**.

**Criteria 4 and 5 are additional compared to the COBS 10 non-MiFID test.** They catch structured products and instruments with surrender penalties that would otherwise appear non-complex.

**ESCC issuer call option carve-out (COBS 10A.4.1AR):** A plain vanilla listed bond issued by an ESCC issuer or ESCC subsidiary that includes a call option allowing early redemption at a price at or above par, where the option is exercisable otherwise than in response to fluctuations and the mechanism to calculate the cash repayment is made clear in the bond terms, does not mean the bond embeds a derivative or incorporates a structure making risk hard to understand.

### Non-Complex IBIPs (COBS 10A.4.1R(2A) and COBS 10A.4.3R)

An IBIP may be considered non-complex for the appropriateness exemption only if it satisfies **all five** of:
1. It includes a **contractually guaranteed minimum maturity value** which is at least the amount paid by the client after deduction of legitimate costs;
2. It does **not incorporate a clause, condition, or trigger that allows the insurance undertaking to materially alter the nature, risk, or pay-out profile**;
3. It provides options to **surrender or otherwise realise at a value that is available to the client**;
4. It does **not include any explicit or implicit charges which, even though there are technically options to surrender or realise, have the effect that doing so may cause unreasonable detriment** because the charges are disproportionate to the cost to the insurance undertaking; and
5. It does **not in any other way incorporate a structure making it difficult to understand the risks involved**.

**Note:** IDD article 16.

### Guidance on "At the Initiative of the Client" (COBS 10A.5.1G–10A.5.3G)

A service should be considered at the initiative of a client unless the client demands it in response to a **personalised communication** from or on behalf of the firm to that particular client which contains an invitation or is intended to influence the client in respect of a specific financial instrument, IBIP, or specific transaction. **Note:** MiFID recital 85.

A service can be considered at the client's initiative notwithstanding that the client demands it on the basis of a communication containing a **promotion or offer by any means that is general and addressed to the public** or a larger group. **Note:** MiFID recital 85.

A communication is **not personalised** solely because it contains the client's name and address, or because a mailing list has been filtered **(COBS 10A.5.3G(3))**.

### Suitability Already Assessed (COBS 10A.6.1G)

A firm need not assess appropriateness if it is receiving or transmitting an order, or carrying on insurance distribution, for which it has assessed **suitability under COBS 9A** or **suitability under COBS 9B** (Targeted support).

### Reliance on Another Firm's Assessment (COBS 10A.6.2G)

A firm **may not need to assess appropriateness** if it is able to rely on a recommendation made by an investment firm (under COBS 2.4.5G — Reliance on other investment firms: MiFID and equivalent business) or, for an IBIP, made by an insurance distributor (under COBS 2.4.5AR — Reliance on other insurance distributors).

## Record Keeping

### MiFID Business — Specific Record Content (COBS 10A.7.2R)

A firm must **maintain records** of the appropriateness assessments it undertakes. The records must include **all four** of:
- **(a)** The **result** of the appropriateness assessment;
- **(b)** Any **warning given to the client** that:
  - (i) an investment service or product had been assessed as potentially **inappropriate** for them; or
  - (ii) the client did **not provide sufficient information** to enable the firm to undertake an appropriateness assessment;
- **(c)** Whether the **client asked to proceed** with a transaction despite being given a warning under (b); and
- **(d)** Whether the **firm accepted** the client's request to proceed with a transaction despite being given a warning under (b).

**Item (d) is the critical obligation:** the firm must record its own decision on whether to execute after a warning. This creates a clear audit trail for every "traded against assessment" situation.

### IBIPs — Record Content (COBS 10A.7.2AR)

For IBIP appropriateness assessments, records must be maintained and must include **(COBS 10A.7.2AR)**:
- The information obtained from the client;
- Any documents agreed with the client (including documents setting out the rights of the parties and the terms of service);
- **(4)(a)** The result of the appropriateness assessment;
- **(4)(b)** Any warning given where the IBIP was assessed as potentially inappropriate, whether the client asked to proceed despite the warning, and whether the firm accepted the request to proceed;
- **(4)(c)** Any warning given where the client did not provide sufficient information, whether the client asked to proceed, and whether the firm accepted.

Records must be retained for **at least the duration of the relationship** between the firm and the client **(COBS 10A.7.2AR(3))**. **Note:** IDD articles 19(1) and (3).

### General Record Keeping Reference (COBS 10A.7.3G)

Records kept for the purposes of COBS 10A must be retained for a period of **at least five years** — firms should refer to SYSC 3.3 (for insurers and managing agents) and SYSC 9 (for other firms).

## Key Differences: COBS 10 vs COBS 10A

| Feature | COBS 10 (non-MiFID) | COBS 10A (MiFID / IBIP) |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Professional client assumption | None | May assume professional client has necessary K&E for relevant services/transactions |
| Reliance standard | "aware" information is manifestly wrong | "aware **or ought to be aware**" — objective element |
| Non-complex criteria | 4 criteria | 6 criteria (adds: no profile-altering clause/trigger; no implicit exit charges making instrument illiquid in practice) |
| Non-complex IBIP category | Not applicable | 5-criterion test: guaranteed minimum maturity value ≥ premium paid; no profile-altering trigger; surrender options at available value; no disproportionate exit charges; no structure making risks hard to understand |
| Record content | Assessment adequate to indicate what it was | 4 specific items (R): result; warnings given; whether client asked to proceed; whether firm accepted client's request |
| IBIP record retention | Not applicable | At least duration of relationship |
| Bundled packages | Not addressed | Hard Rule (R) — overall bundle must be assessed |
| Reliance on another firm | Not addressed | Firm may rely on appropriateness assessment made by another MiFID investment firm (COBS 2.4.5G) or insurance distributor (COBS 2.4.5AR) |

## Key Points for Agents

- **COBS 10A applies to MiFID investment services and IBIP insurance distribution (COBS 10A.1.1R)** — but not where the firm makes a personal recommendation, provides targeted support, or carries out portfolio management. Those trigger suitability under COBS 9A instead.
- **Professional client assumption (COBS 10A.2.3R):** For appropriateness purposes, a firm may assume a professional client has the necessary knowledge and experience for the services/transactions/products for which they are classified. No distinction between per se and elective professional clients for this purpose — both get the assumption.
- **Non-complex exemption has 6 criteria for MiFID instruments (COBS 10A.4.2R):** Criteria 4 (no profile-altering clause/trigger) and 5 (no implicit exit charges making instrument illiquid) are additional compared to the non-MiFID test. Platforms must apply the stricter MiFID definition for MiFID business.
- **Non-complex IBIP exemption: 5 criteria (COBS 10A.4.3R):** Anchored on a guaranteed minimum maturity value at least equal to premiums paid. Any IBIP that can materially alter its own risk/pay-out profile, or imposes disproportionate exit charges, cannot rely on the non-complex exemption.
- **Four-item mandatory record for MiFID appropriateness (COBS 10A.7.2R):** Must record: (a) result; (b) any warning (inappropriate or insufficient info); (c) whether client asked to proceed despite warning; (d) whether the firm accepted that request. Item (d) — the firm's own decision — is the critical compliance obligation. This creates an audit trail for every "traded against assessment" execution.
- **IBIP appropriateness records: at least duration of relationship (COBS 10A.7.2AR(3)).** Unlike the MiFID 5-year minimum, IBIP appropriateness records must be kept as long as the firm-client relationship continues.
- **Reliance on another firm (COBS 10A.6.2G):** A firm may not need to assess appropriateness if it can rely on an appropriateness or suitability assessment made by another investment firm or insurance distributor under the COBS 2.4 reliance provisions. This is only available for MiFID and IBIP business; no equivalent exists under COBS 10.

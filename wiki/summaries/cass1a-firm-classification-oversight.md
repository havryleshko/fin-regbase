# CASS 1A — CASS Firm Classification and Operational Oversight (Sourcebook Reference)

## What CASS 1A Does

CASS 1A establishes a **three-tier firm classification system** (small, medium, large) and calibrates the intensity of the CASS operational oversight obligation to firm type. It also governs the mechanism by which firms notify the FCA of their classification each year, and the personnel governance requirements for the individual allocated responsibility for CASS oversight.

CASS 1A sits at the apex of the CASS operational governance framework. It determines:
1. Which oversight obligation applies — the lighter small-firm regime (CASS 1A.3.1R) or the more intensive medium/large-firm regime (CASS 1A.3.1A R);
2. Whether the firm must submit a **Client Money and Assets Return (CMAR)** under SUP 16.14; and
3. How CASS oversight feeds into the **SM&CR certification regime** (SYSC 27.8.1R).

---

## Section 1A.1 — Application

CASS 1A applies to a firm when it is **(CASS 1A.1.1R)**:
- Subject to CASS 6 (safe custody assets); or
- Subject to CASS 7 (client money).

Classification rules are based on the **highest amount of client money or safe custody assets held during the previous calendar year** **(CASS 1A.2.1R)**. For firms in their first year of CASS activity, classification is based on projected amounts **(CASS 1A.2.3R)**.

CASS 1A does not apply to a firm after **failure** — the firm ceases to be subject to classification requirements from the point at which the circumstances described in CASS 6.6.56R or CASS 7.15.32G arise **(CASS 1A.1.5R)**.

---

## Section 1A.2 — Firm Classification

### The Three-Tier Table (CASS 1A.2.7R)

Classification is determined by reference to the **higher** of the firm's client money and safe custody assets figures:

| CASS Firm Type | Client Money Threshold | Safe Custody Assets Threshold |
|---------------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| Small CASS firm | Less than £1m | Less than £10m |
| Medium CASS firm | £1m or more but less than £1bn | £10m or more but less than £100bn |
| Large CASS firm | £1bn or more | £100bn or more |

A firm falls into the higher tier if **either** figure meets that tier's threshold. For example, a firm holding £500m client money but £120bn safe custody assets is a large CASS firm (custody assets exceed the £100bn threshold).

### Annual FCA Notification — 15th Business Day of January (CASS 1A.2.9R)

A firm must notify the FCA **by the 15th business day of January each year** of:
- The highest amount of client money held during the previous calendar year;
- The highest amount of safe custody assets held during the previous calendar year; and
- The resulting CASS firm type.

Classification takes effect from **1 February** in the year of notification **(CASS 1A.2.10R)**.

For medium and large CASS firms, submission of the **Client Money and Assets Return (CMAR)** under SUP 16.14 satisfies this annual notification obligation **(CASS 1A.2.11G)**. Small CASS firms do not submit the CMAR and must notify separately.

### Voluntary Opt-Up Election (CASS 1A.2.5R)

A firm may elect to be treated as a **higher firm type** than its actual threshold would require:
- A small CASS firm may elect to be treated as a medium CASS firm;
- A medium CASS firm may elect to be treated as a large CASS firm.

Requirements:
1. The firm must give the FCA **written notification** of the election;
2. Notification must be given **at least one week** before the intended effective date;
3. The FCA must **not have objected** to the election before it takes effect.

The election can subsequently be reversed. Opt-up is useful for firms approaching their upper threshold or operating group-wide governance structures calibrated to a higher tier.

## Section 1A.3 — Operational Oversight

### Small CASS Firms (CASS 1A.3.1R)

A small CASS firm must allocate to **a single director or senior manager** the responsibility for:
- CASS compliance oversight; and
- Reporting to the firm's governing body on CASS compliance.

The allocated individual does not need to be a separately approved person under the SMF regime for this function — the allocation is an internal governance requirement.

### Medium and Large CASS Firms (CASS 1A.3.1A R)

A medium or large CASS firm must maintain a **formal CASS operational oversight function**. This function carries all the obligations of the small-firm allocation, plus:
- The obligation to **complete and submit the CMAR** to the FCA under SUP 16.14 (annual submission).

The CASS operational oversight function is **not a Significant Influence Function (SIF) or Senior Manager Function (SMF)** — it does not require separate FCA approval under the SM&CR senior managers regime **(CASS 1A.3.1A R)**. However:

> If the CASS oversight function is allocated to a person who is **not** a senior manager or approved person, the function becomes the **CASS oversight FCA certification function** for that individual under **SYSC 27.8.1R**. The firm must then certify that person annually under the SM&CR certification regime.

This is the mechanism by which CASS oversight is brought within the SM&CR without being a separately approved function.

### Large AIFM / UCITS ManCo Alternative (CASS 1A.3.1C R)

Large AIFMs and UCITS management companies have an alternative allocation route available under CASS 1A.3.1C R, which takes account of the different governance structures applicable to those entity types.

### Dual Allocation Restriction (CASS 1A.3.2A R)

Once the CASS oversight function is allocated to an individual, **no additional responsibilities may be given to that person** unless the firm is satisfied on **reasonable grounds** that:
1. The individual can still discharge the CASS oversight function effectively with the additional responsibilities; and
2. Full CASS compliance will not be compromised.

This restriction applies to both new and ongoing allocations. The firm must document its assessment before adding responsibilities to the CASS oversight holder.

### Job-Sharing (CASS 1A.3.2B R)

Two persons may **jointly hold** the CASS oversight function. The dual allocation restriction in CASS 1A.3.2A R applies to each holder of the joint function.

### Record Retention (CASS 1A.3.3R)

Records of CASS oversight function allocations must be retained for **five years** from the date the allocation ceases to apply.

## Key Points for Agents

- **Three-tier classification; threshold is the HIGHER of client money and safe custody assets (CASS 1A.2.7R).** Small: <£1m AND <£10m. Medium: £1m–£1bn OR £10m–£100bn. Large: >£1bn OR >£100bn. A single figure breaching the higher tier threshold lifts the entire firm to that tier.
- **Annual FCA notification by 15th BD of January; classification takes effect 1 February (CASS 1A.2.9R; 1A.2.10R).** Medium/large firms satisfy this via CMAR under SUP 16.14. Small firms notify separately.
- **Voluntary opt-up available; written FCA notice at least one week before effective date (CASS 1A.2.5R).** FCA must not have objected. Useful where group governance is calibrated to a higher tier or a firm is near its upper threshold.
- **Small firms: single director/SM allocation (CASS 1A.3.1R). Medium/large: formal CASS operational oversight function + CMAR obligation (CASS 1A.3.1A R).** The function is not a separate approved person requirement. If held by a non-SMF, the holder must be certified annually as CASS oversight FCA certification function (SYSC 27.8.1R).
- **Dual allocation restriction: no additional responsibilities without documented reasonable grounds assessment (CASS 1A.3.2A R).** Job-sharing is permitted (CASS 1A.3.2B R). Allocation records retained 5 years (CASS 1A.3.3R).

# CONC 4 — Pre-Contractual Requirements

## What This Chapter Covers

CONC 4 sets out what firms must do and disclose before a regulated credit agreement, consumer hire agreement, or P2P agreement is entered into. It has eight sections:

| Section | Subject |
|---------|---------|
| **4.1** | Mandatory content of quotations for home-secured credit |
| **4.2** | Pre-contract disclosure and adequate explanations (lenders and brokers) |
| **4.3** | Pre-contractual requirements for P2P agreements |
| **4.4** | Pre-contractual requirements for credit brokers (fee disclosure and payment-detail gate) |
| **4.5** | Commission disclosure and discretionary commission arrangement prohibition |
| **4.6** | Pre-contract disclosure of CPA terms |
| **4.7** | Information required on entering current account agreements |
| **4.8** | Unfair pre-contract business practices |

CONC 4.2 is the substantive core: the adequate explanation obligation applies to all regulated credit lending and to credit brokers that take responsibility for providing the explanation.

---

## CONC 4.1 — Content of Quotations

When a firm provides a quotation for a credit agreement that would or might be secured on the customer's home, the quotation must contain mandatory risk warnings. The required warning depends on the type of agreement:

| Agreement type | Mandatory statement (capital letters, clear, legible, prominent) |
|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Standard home-secured credit** | "YOUR HOME IS AT RISK IF YOU DO NOT KEEP UP REPAYMENTS ON A MORTGAGE OR OTHER LOAN SECURED ON IT." **(CONC 4.1.3R(3))** |
| **Interest-only home mortgage** (no regular repayments of capital due, and lender cannot enforce by possession while customer occupies as main residence) | "CHECK THAT THIS MORTGAGE WILL MEET YOUR NEEDS IF YOU WANT TO MOVE OR SELL YOUR HOME OR YOU WANT YOUR FAMILY TO INHERIT IT. IF YOU ARE IN DOUBT, SEEK INDEPENDENT ADVICE." **(CONC 4.1.3R(2))** |
| **Foreign currency home mortgage** | "THE STERLING EQUIVALENT OF YOUR LIABILITY UNDER A FOREIGN CURRENCY MORTGAGE MAY BE INCREASED BY EXCHANGE RATE MOVEMENT." **(CONC 4.1.3R(4))** |
| **Home-secured consumer hire** | "YOUR HOME IS AT RISK IF YOU DO NOT KEEP UP PAYMENTS ON A HIRE AGREEMENT SECURED BY A MORTGAGE OR OTHER SECURITY ON YOUR HOME." **(CONC 4.1.3R(6))** |

Equivalent obligations apply to credit brokers providing quotations (CONC 4.1.4R). Statements must be in capital letters, clear, legible and prominent — prominence requires that the average customer's attention would be drawn to it in relation to the other content of the quotation. **(CONC 4.1.5R–4.1.6G)**

"Quotation" means any document by which a person gives a customer information about the terms on which a lender or owner is prepared to do business — it excludes financial promotions, section 58 CCA documents, documents sent for signature, and copies of unexecuted agreements. **(CONC 4.1.5R(2))**

## CONC 4.2 — Pre-Contract Disclosure and Adequate Explanations

### Scope

CONC 4.2 applies to consumer credit lenders and to credit brokers that take responsibility for providing the required explanations. It does **not** apply to: **(CONC 4.2.1R)**
- Credit agreements exceeding £60,260 (unless a residential renovation agreement)
- Agreements secured on land
- Authorised non-business overdraft agreements repayable on demand or within three months

### CONC 4.2.5R — The Adequate Explanation Obligation

Before making a regulated credit agreement, the firm must: **(CONC 4.2.5R(1))**

**(a)** Provide the customer with an **adequate explanation** of the matters in (2) to place the customer in a position to assess whether the agreement is adapted to their needs and financial situation.

**(b)** Advise the customer:
- (i) to consider the information required to be disclosed under CCA s.55; and
- (ii) where disclosed in person, that the customer is able to take it away.

**(c)** Provide the customer with an **opportunity to ask questions** about the agreement.

**(d)** Advise the customer how to ask the firm for **further information and explanation**.

**The five matters that must be adequately explained (CONC 4.2.5R(2)):**

| Matter | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **(a) Features making credit unsuitable** | Features that may make the credit unsuitable for particular types of use |
| **(b) Cost** | How much the customer will pay periodically and, where determinable, in total |
| **(c) Adverse features unlikely to be foreseen** | Features which may operate in a manner likely to have a significant adverse effect on the customer in a way the customer is unlikely to foresee |
| **(d) Consequences of payment failure** | The principal consequences of failing to make payments, including where applicable: total cost of debt growing; default charges; impaired credit rating and its effect on future access to credit; legal proceedings including charging orders (or inhibitions in Scotland); repossession of home or other property; where an article is taken in pawn, that it might be sold if not redeemed |
| **(e) Withdrawal rights** | The effect of the exercise of any right to withdraw from the agreement and how and when it may be exercised |

**Key constraints on the obligation:**

- The explanation should enable the customer to make a reasonable assessment of whether they can afford the credit and understand the key associated risks. **(CONC 4.2.6G)**
- The level and extent of explanation required depends on: type of credit; amount and duration; actual and potential costs; risk to the customer (higher the higher cost is relative to financial situation); purpose (if known); customer's apparent level of understanding; channel or medium. **(CONC 4.2.7G)**
- Even where the customer states they do not need an explanation, the firm must continue to comply. **(CONC 4.2.9R)**
- The firm must **not** encourage or induce the customer to waive their rights to an explanation. **(CONC 4.2.10R)**
- The firm must **not** require the customer to acknowledge that the explanation it has provided is adequate to satisfy CONC 4.2.5R. It may require acknowledgement that an explanation was given and written information received, but not acknowledgement of adequacy. **(CONC 4.2.13R–4.2.14G)**
- The firm must enable customers to obtain further information and explanation without undue cost or delay. **(CONC 4.2.12R)**

**Channel-specific guidance:**
- Online/electronic: providing a link to where information can be found is insufficient if the agreement can be concluded without accessing that link. The customer should pass through screens containing the required information. **(CONC 4.2.19G)**
- Telephone/face-to-face: solely providing a written explanation or reading from a script is unlikely to comply — interaction is important. **(CONC 4.2.20G)**

### CONC 4.2.5R(4A) — Durable Medium Requirement

Where the adequate explanation covers the matters in CONC 4.2.5R(2)(c) or (d) (adverse features and payment consequences) and these are given orally or in person, the matters in (c) and (d) and the advice required in (1)(b) must additionally be given **in a durable medium**. **(CONC 4.2.5R(4)–(4A))**

### CONC 4.2.8R — HCSTC-Specific Explanation

Where the agreement is high-cost short-term credit, the firm must explain as part of the CONC 4.2.5R(1)(a) obligation that entering into the agreement **would be unsuitable to support sustained borrowing over long periods and would be expensive as a means of longer-term borrowing**. **(CONC 4.2.8R)**

### CONC 4.2.15R — Product-Specific Additional Explanations

In addition to the CONC 4.2.5R general obligation, the following product types require specific additional mandatory content:

| Product | Additional mandatory explanation |
|---------|----------------------------------|
| **Credit token agreements** (credit cards, store cards) | (a) Different interest rates apply to different credit elements (e.g. cash withdrawal costs more); (b) implications of only making minimum repayments; (c) interest rates or charges may be increased; (d) rates may be increased based on the individual customer's risk; (e) limitations on any introductory rate offer (except for retail revolving credit and BNPL — see below); (f) conditions on balance transfers including fees **(CONC 4.2.15R(1))** |
| **Credit card cheques** | Higher associated costs relative to payment by credit card **(CONC 4.2.15R(2))** |
| **Home credit loans and HCSTC** | Effect of refinancing or extending the duration of the credit **(CONC 4.2.15R(3))** |
| **Bill of sale loan agreements** | Risk of losing the asset; repossession can occur without a court order; repossession may not clear the debt; unlike HP/conditional sale, no one-third protection **(CONC 4.2.15R(4))** |
| **HP and conditional sale agreements** | Customer does not own goods until all payments made; goods can be repossessed without court order until one-third of total amount payable has been paid **(CONC 4.2.15R(5))** |
| **Debt consolidation credit** | Where known: higher rate or longer repayment period resulting from consolidation; and whether the agreement is secured on the customer's property **(CONC 4.2.15R(6))** |
| **Agreements requiring a guarantor** | The requirement to provide security in the form of a guarantee **(CONC 4.2.15R(7))** |
| **Retail revolving credit and BNPL agreements** | All limitations that apply to any zero percentage or low interest promotional offer — including: circumstances in which interest or charges could become payable; how they would be calculated; date from which they would accrue; rate of interest; amount of principal on which interest would be charged. If failing to meet conditions means interest accrues at a higher rate or from the purchase date, this must be included **(CONC 4.2.15R(8))** |

### CONC 4.2.22R — Guarantors

Where an individual (other than the borrower) is to provide a guarantee or indemnity, the firm must before the agreement is made provide the **guarantor** with an adequate explanation of: the circumstances in which the guarantee might be called on; and the implications for the guarantor if it is. The same rules on channel, durable medium, and acknowledgement prohibition apply to the guarantor explanation. **(CONC 4.2.22R)**

## CONC 4.3 — P2P Agreements

CONC 4.3 mirrors the CONC 4.2 adequate explanation obligations for P2P platform operators in relation to prospective borrowers. The operator must apply CONC 4.2 as if it were the lender. **(CONC 4.3.5R)**

**Additional P2P-specific obligation (CONC 4.3.3AR):** Any fee payable by the borrower to the operator must be agreed between the borrower and the operator in writing or other durable medium before the P2P agreement is entered into. The operator must also disclose the fee to the lender to enable the lender to calculate the annual percentage rate of charge for the agreement.

Where a P2P agreement is secured on the borrower's home, the firm must give a prominent warning: "YOUR HOME MAY BE REPOSSESSED IF YOU DO NOT KEEP UP REPAYMENTS ON A MORTGAGE OR ANY OTHER DEBT SECURED ON IT." **(CONC 4.3.6R)**

## CONC 4.4 — Credit Brokers: Fee Disclosure and Payment-Detail Gate

### Fee Disclosure (CONC 4.4.2R)

A credit broker must: **(CONC 4.4.2R)**
1. Disclose to the customer the fee, if any, payable by the customer to the broker.
2. Record any such fee agreement in writing or other durable medium before a regulated credit agreement is entered into.
3. Disclose to the lender the fee payable by the customer, to enable the lender to calculate the APR.
4. Disclose to the customer how and when the fee is payable and the circumstances in which a refund may be available under CCA s.155.

### CONC 4.4.3R — Information Notice and Customer Confirmation Gate

A credit broker **must not** request, claim, demand, or take payment of a charge from a customer — or collect payment card details — unless it has first satisfied **two conditions**: **(CONC 4.4.3R)**

**Condition 1 — Information notice:** The firm must have sent the customer, in a durable medium, a notice containing:
- (a) The firm's legal name as it appears on the Financial Services Register
- (b) Whether the firm is a credit broker and not a lender (or, if also a lender, that it is acting as a credit broker)
- (c) A statement that the customer will be required to pay a charge in connection with the firm's services
- (d) The amount of the charge, or where not ascertainable, the basis on which it will be calculated
- (e) The method by which the firm will initiate or take payment

**Condition 2 — Customer confirmation:** The firm must have received from the customer, in a durable medium, an acknowledgement of receipt of the information notice confirming the customer is aware of its contents.

**Operational implications (CONC 4.4.5G):**
- The information notice must be sent to the customer in a durable medium — email or email attachment is acceptable; making the notice available on a website or emailing a link to a webpage is **not** sufficient.
- The firm must not ask for or access payment details until after the customer confirmation is received. Websites must be constructed so customers cannot access any payment input page until they have confirmed receipt.
- The rule applies to each firm in a broker chain separately — if firm A introduces the customer to firm B (a credit broker), firm A's notice cannot cover firm B's charges. Firm B must issue its own notice and receive its own customer confirmation.
- The rule cannot be avoided by labelling the charge as a "membership fee", "web registration fee", or any other description.
- The rule applies irrespective of whether the charge is payable to the firm or to a third party.

## CONC 4.5 — Commissions

### Disclosure Obligations

**CONC 4.5.3R:** A credit broker must **prominently disclose** to the customer, in good time before a credit or consumer hire agreement is entered into, the existence and nature of any commission or fee or other remuneration payable to the broker by the lender, owner, or third party, where the existence or amount of the commission could **actually or potentially**: (1) affect the impartiality of the broker in recommending the agreement; or (2) if made known to the customer, have a material impact on the customer's transactional decision to enter into the agreement.

**CONC 4.5.3AR:** Where disclosure under CONC 4.5.3R is required, the firm must simultaneously and with equal prominence also disclose **how the existence and nature of the commission may affect the amounts payable by the customer** under the agreement.

**CONC 4.5.4R:** At the customer's **request**, the broker must disclose the amount (or if the precise amount is not known, the likely amount) of any commission or fee payable.

### CONC 4.5.6R — Discretionary Commission Arrangement Prohibition

A lender or credit broker **must not** enter into, or have rights or obligations under, a **discretionary commission arrangement**. **(CONC 4.5.6R)**

See [[conc-discretionary-commission-arrangements]] for the full DCA framework including the three named arrangement types, the accrued commissions rule, and the motor finance context.

## CONC 4.6 — Pre-Contract CPA Disclosure

Before entering into any regulated credit agreement, consumer hire agreement, or P2P agreement under which the customer may grant a **continuous payment authority**, the firm must provide the customer with an adequate explanation of 11 specified matters: **(CONC 4.6.2R)**

1. What a CPA is and how it works
2. How the CPA will be applied by the firm — including, for HCSTC, that it may only be used twice to collect the whole sum due (or twice per instalment where the agreement provides for repayment in instalments)
3. How the customer can cancel the CPA
4. Whether alternative repayment options are available
5. The choice of an appropriate due date for payment
6. The choice of an alternative payment date (if applicable)
7. The consequences if sufficient funds are not available on the due date
8. Whether further attempts may be made and if so, on what basis, over what period, and at what frequency
9. For non-HCSTC: whether part payment may be sought and if so, on what basis and whether subject to a minimum
10. For HCSTC: that the firm will not seek part payment unless the firm is willing to accept that lesser sum and, after the customer is notified of the sum and the payment date, the customer gives express consent
11. Whether default fees and other charges may be added, the circumstances in which they may be incurred, and their amount or basis

**CONC 4.6.3R:** The terms of the CPA must be included in the credit agreement, consumer hire agreement, or P2P agreement in plain and intelligible language.

Where a guarantor is to grant a CPA, the firm must provide the guarantor with the same pre-contract CPA explanation before the guarantee is provided. **(CONC 4.6.5R)**

## CONC 4.7 — Current Account Agreement Information

When a firm enters into a current account agreement where there is a possibility of the account-holder overdrowing or exceeding a pre-arranged limit (which would constitute a regulated credit agreement), the current account agreement must contain: **(CONC 4.7.2R)**

- The interest rate on any excess above the agreed limit
- Conditions applicable to that rate
- Any reference rate on which the rate is based
- Information on rate changes (periods, conditions, and procedures)
- Other charges payable and conditions under which they may be varied

---

## CONC 4.8 — Unfair Pre-Contract Business Practices

Three hard-rule prohibitions apply to consumer credit lenders at the pre-contract stage:

**CONC 4.8.2R:** A firm must not **unfairly encourage, incentivise or induce** a customer to enter into a regulated credit agreement quickly **without allowing the customer time to consider** the pre-contract information under CCA s.55 and the explanations required under CONC 4.2.5R. (Note: stating an end date for a promotion does not alone constitute this behaviour.)

**CONC 4.8.4R:** A firm must not **unfairly encourage, incentivise or induce** a customer to enter into a regulated credit agreement **for an amount higher than the customer requests**. (Note: offering more credit than requested is not prohibited if based on a proper creditworthiness assessment and the customer is not pressurised.)

**CONC 4.8.6R:** A firm must not **lead a customer to believe** that their current debt repayments can be reduced under a regulated credit agreement **over the same term when this is not the case**.

## Position in the CONC Framework

CONC 4 sits between CONC 3 (financial promotions) and CONC 5 (creditworthiness assessment):

| Chapter | Stage | Core obligation |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| **CONC 3** | Marketing | Financial promotions must be fair, clear and not misleading |
| **CONC 4** | Pre-contract | Adequate explanation; quotation warnings; fee disclosure; CPA disclosure; no unfair pressure |
| **CONC 5** | Decision | Creditworthiness and affordability assessment before agreement |
| **CONC 6** | Post-contract | Monitoring; product-level protections; refinancing rules |
| **CONC 7** | Arrears/default | Arrears policies; forbearance; CPA two-strike; collection treatment |

The CONC 4.2.5R adequate explanation obligation is operationally linked to CONC 5: the explanation must address matters that include consequences of payment failure (connecting to the CONC 1.3.1G financial difficulty indicators) and withdrawal rights (enabling the customer to step back from an unsuitable agreement after receiving the explanation).

## Key Points for Agents

- **CONC 4.2.5R (adequate explanation):** 5-element obligation before any regulated credit agreement. Cannot be waived by the customer (4.2.9R). Firm cannot require the customer to acknowledge adequacy (4.2.13R). Online: link-only disclosure is insufficient if the agreement can be concluded without accessing it (4.2.19G).
- **CONC 4.2.8R (HCSTC):** Must specifically explain that HCSTC is unsuitable for sustained borrowing over long periods and is expensive as a means of longer-term borrowing.
- **CONC 4.2.15R(8) (BNPL/retail revolving):** Must explain in full the conditions of any promotional rate and the circumstances in which interest would become payable — including rate, principal, and accrual date. This is additional to the general CONC 4.2.5R explanation.
- **CONC 4.4.3R (broker payment-detail gate):** A credit broker cannot access customer payment details until it has sent a 6-item durable-medium information notice AND received a customer confirmation. A link to a website is insufficient. Each broker in a chain must issue its own notice.
- **CONC 4.5.6R (DCA prohibition):** Hard ban on discretionary commission arrangements. Three named types. See [[conc-discretionary-commission-arrangements]].
- **CONC 4.6.2R (pre-contract CPA explanation):** 11 mandatory items must be explained before any agreement under which a CPA may be granted. CPA terms must be incorporated into the agreement in plain and intelligible language (4.6.3R).
- **CONC 4.8.2R and 4.8.4R (unfair practices):** Hard prohibitions on pressuring customers to decide quickly or to borrow more than requested.

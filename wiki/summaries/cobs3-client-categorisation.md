# COBS 3 — Client Categorisation

## Application

**COBS 3.1.1R:** The scope of this chapter mirrors the scope of the Handbook rules to which it relates — i.e., it applies wherever another part of the Handbook turns on whether a person is a client, a retail client, a professional client, or an eligible counterparty.

**Mixed business trump rule (COBS 3.1.4R):** Where a firm conducts business for a client involving both MiFID or equivalent third country business and other regulated activities subject to COBS 3, it must categorise that client in accordance with the MiFID provisions for all such business. MiFID categorisation requirements prevail across the entire client relationship when MiFID business is present.

**Targeted support carve-out (COBS 3.1.2R(3)):** The client categorisation rules in COBS 3 do not differentiate between client categories for the provision of targeted support services (COBS 9B.3.4R). COBS 3 is not therefore relevant to a firm in relation to its targeted support activity.

---

## Client Definition (COBS 3.2)

**COBS 3.2.1R:** A "client" is a person to whom a firm provides, intends to provide or has provided: (a) a service in the course of a regulated activity; or (b) for MiFID or equivalent third country business, an ancillary service. A client includes a potential client **(COBS 3.2.1R(2))** and a person to whom a financial promotion is or is likely to be communicated **(COBS 3.2.1R(3))**.

A client of an appointed representative or tied agent is a client of the firm for which the AR/tied agent acts **(COBS 3.2.1R(4))**.

**Who is the client (COBS 3.2.3R):**
- Where a firm provides services to a person acting as agent: identity determined by the rule on agents as clients (COBS 2.4.3R)
- Personal/stakeholder pension scheme: a member or beneficiary is the client
- Trust (except those covered by the pension rule): the trustee is the client; underlying beneficiaries are not
- Non-MiFID fund without separate legal personality: the fund is the client
- Child Trust Fund: the registered contact or, in certain cases, the person under CTF Regulations

---

## General Notifications (COBS 3.3)

**COBS 3.3.1AR:** A firm must notify a new client, and any existing client it has newly categorised, of their categorisation as a retail client, professional client, or eligible counterparty. The notification must be in a **durable medium** and must be provided **prior to the provision of services** **(COBS 3.3.1BR)**. It must include:
- (a) any right the client has to request a different categorisation; and
- (b) any limitations to the level of client protection that a different categorisation would entail

**COBS 3.3.2G:** A firm must notify a professional client or ECP of its right to request re-categorisation to a higher level of protection — even if the firm would not agree to such requests. A firm need only notify a client of the right to request a lower level of protection if it is prepared to consider such requests.

---

## Retail Clients (COBS 3.4)

**COBS 3.4.1R:** A retail client is a client who is not a professional client or an eligible counterparty. It is the **residual, default category**. [Note: MiFID article 4(1)(11)]

**CTF override (COBS 3.4.2R):** Where a firm provides services relating to a Child Trust Fund (except for a personal recommendation relating to a contribution), its client is a retail client even if it would otherwise be categorised as professional or ECP.

**Targeted support override (COBS 3.4.3G):** A firm providing a targeted support service must treat its client as a retail client for that service, even if the client is categorised as professional or ECP for other purposes (COBS 9B.3.4R).

---

## Professional Clients (COBS 3.5)

A professional client is either a **per se professional client** or an **elective professional client** **(COBS 3.5.1R)**.

### Per Se Professional Clients (COBS 3.5.2R)

The following are per se professional clients unless and to the extent they are given a different categorisation:

**(1) Entities required to be authorised or regulated to operate in financial markets** — including credit institutions, investment firms, other authorised/regulated financial institutions, insurance companies, collective investment schemes and their management companies, pension funds and their management companies, commodity or commodity derivatives dealers, locals, and any other institutional investor.

**(2) Large undertakings — MiFID or equivalent third country business:** Meeting two of the following on a **company basis**:
- Balance sheet total: EUR 20,000,000
- Net turnover: EUR 40,000,000
- Own funds: EUR 2,000,000

**(3) Large undertakings — non-MiFID business:** Meeting **any** of:
- (a) Body corporate (incl. LLP) with called-up share capital or net assets of at least **£5 million** (or group equivalent at any time in previous 2 years)
- (b) Undertaking meeting **two of**: EUR 12,500,000 balance sheet / EUR 25,000,000 net turnover / 250 average employees
- (c) Partnership with net assets of at least **£5 million** (excluding loans to partners; calculated for limited partnerships without deducting such loans)
- (d) Trustee of trust (other than occupational pension/SSAS/personal pension/stakeholder) with assets of at least **£10 million** (aggregating cash and designated investments, before deducting liabilities)
- (e) Trustee of occupational pension/SSAS or personal pension/stakeholder scheme with: (i) at least **50 members**; and (ii) assets under management of at least **£10 million**

**(4)** National or regional governments, public bodies managing national or regional public debt, central banks, international or supranational institutions (World Bank, IMF, ECB, EIB, etc.)

**(5)** Another institutional investor whose main activity is to invest in financial instruments or designated investments (including securitisation vehicles).

**Local public authorities (COBS 3.5.2BR):** A firm must categorise a local public authority or municipality that does not manage public debt as a **retail client**, unless it is permitted to treat such a person as an elective professional client under COBS 3.5.3BR–3.5.3ER.

### Elective Professional Clients (COBS 3.5.3R)

A firm may treat a client (other than a local public authority or municipality) as an elective professional client if it complies with **(1)** and **(3)**, and where applicable **(2)**:

**(1) Qualitative test:** The firm undertakes an adequate assessment of the expertise, experience and knowledge of the client that gives reasonable assurance, in light of the nature of the transactions or services envisaged, that the client is capable of making its own investment decisions and understanding the risks involved.

**(2) Quantitative test** (for MiFID or equivalent third country business — at least two of three criteria must be satisfied):
- (a) The client has carried out transactions, in significant size, on the relevant market at an average frequency of **10 per quarter** over the previous four quarters
- (b) The size of the client's financial instrument portfolio, including cash deposits and financial instruments, exceeds **EUR 500,000**
- (c) The client works or has worked in the financial sector for at least **one year** in a professional position requiring knowledge of the transactions or services envisaged

**(3) Written procedure — three steps, all mandatory:**
- (a) The client must **state in writing** to the firm that it wishes to be treated as a professional client, either generally or in respect of a particular service, transaction, type of transaction, or product
- (b) The firm must give the client a **clear written warning** of the protections and investor compensation rights the client may lose
- (c) The client must **state in writing, in a separate document** from the contract, that it is aware of the consequences of losing such protections

**Important caveats:**
- An elective professional client should not be presumed to possess market knowledge and experience comparable to a per se professional client **(COBS 3.5.7G)**
- The qualitative test must be performed in relation to the person authorised to carry out transactions on behalf of the client where the client is an entity **(COBS 3.5.4R)**
- Before accepting a re-categorisation request, a firm must take all reasonable steps to ensure the client satisfies the qualitative test and, where applicable, the quantitative test **(COBS 3.5.6R)**

### Local Public Authorities — Elective Professional

A UK local public authority must be assessed against the quantitative test under COBS 3.5.3BR (portfolio > £10,000,000 AND one of: 10 transactions/quarter, 1 year relevant professional experience, or LGPS administering authority status). The qualitative and quantitative tests must be applied separately for pension scheme business and other business **(COBS 3.5.3CR)**.

### Ongoing Obligations (COBS 3.5.8G–3.5.9R)

**COBS 3.5.8G:** Professional clients are responsible for keeping the firm informed about any change that could affect their current categorisation.

**COBS 3.5.9R:** If a firm becomes aware that a client no longer fulfils the initial conditions that made it eligible for categorisation as an elective professional client, the firm must take the appropriate action. Where that action involves re-categorising the client as a retail client, the firm must notify the client of its new categorisation.

## Eligible Counterparties (COBS 3.6)

**COBS 3.6.1R:** An eligible counterparty is a client that is either a per se eligible counterparty or an elective eligible counterparty. A client can only be an ECP in relation to **eligible counterparty business** (PRIN 1 Annex 1 R provides an exception to this restriction).

### Per Se Eligible Counterparties (COBS 3.6.2R)

The following are per se ECPs (including non-UK equivalents) unless given a different categorisation:
1. An investment firm
2. A credit institution
3. An insurance company
4. A CIS authorised under UK UCITS provisions, or its management company
5. A pension fund or its management company
6. Another financial institution authorised or regulated under UK law
7. [deleted]
8. A national government or its corresponding office, including a public body managing national debt
9. A central bank
10. A supranational organisation

### Elective Eligible Counterparties

**For non-MiFID business (COBS 3.6.4R):** A firm may treat a client as an elective ECP if: (1) the client is an undertaking; and (1)(a) is a per se professional client (except institutional investor under COBS 3.5.2R(5)) and (1)(a)(i) is a body corporate with £10m called-up share capital or meets COBS 3.5.2R(3)(b) two-quantitative-test criteria, or (1)(a)(ii) meets the non-MiFID large undertaking criteria; and (b) requests ECP status; and (2) the firm follows the COBS 3.6.4B R procedure.

**For MiFID or equivalent third country business (COBS 3.6.4AR):** A firm may treat a client as an elective ECP if the client: (1) is an undertaking; (2) is a per se professional client (except institutional investor); and (3) requests ECP status; and the firm follows the COBS 3.6.4B R procedure.

**ECP procedure (COBS 3.6.4BR):** The firm shall provide the client with a clear written warning of the consequences for the client of such a request, including the protections they may lose. The client must confirm in writing: (a) the request to be treated as an ECP, either generally or for specific services/transactions/products; and (b) that they are aware of the consequences.

**COBS 3.6.6R:** A firm may obtain confirmation that a prospective counterparty agrees to ECP treatment either as a general agreement or for each individual transaction.

## Providing Clients with a Higher Level of Protection (COBS 3.7)

**COBS 3.7.1R:** A firm must allow a professional client or ECP to request re-categorisation as a client that benefits from a higher degree of protection.

**Firm's discretion (COBS 3.7.3AR):** A firm may, either on its own initiative or at the request of the client, treat a client as a professional or retail client where it might otherwise be an ECP, or as a retail client where it is per se professional under COBS 3.5.2R.

**ECP requesting higher protection (COBS 3.7.3BR):**
- (1) If an ECP requests re-categorisation to a higher level of investor protection, the firm must require the request to be made in writing and to indicate whether it refers to retail or professional treatment, and whether it covers one or more services, transactions, or products.
- **(2) If the ECP does not expressly request retail treatment, the firm must treat them as a professional client.**
- (3) If the ECP expressly requests retail treatment, the firm must treat them as a retail client.

**Per se professional requesting retail for MiFID business (COBS 3.7.5R):** The client will be classified as a retail client if it enters into a written agreement with the firm to the effect that it will not be treated as professional/ECP for the applicable conduct of business regime. The agreement must specify the scope of the re-categorisation.

**Re-categorisation ≠ DISP eligible complainant (COBS 3.7.8G):** Re-categorising a client as a retail client under COBS 3.7 does not necessarily mean it will become an eligible complainant under DISP. The eligible complainant criteria in DISP 2.7 apply separately.

**Granularity of re-categorisation (COBS 3.7.7G):** Additional protections may be provided on a general basis, trade by trade, in respect of one or more specified rules, for particular services/transactions, or for specific product types.

---

## Policies, Procedures and Records (COBS 3.8)

**COBS 3.8.1R:** A firm must implement appropriate written internal policies and procedures to categorise its clients. [Note: MiFID Annex II, fourth paragraph of section II.2]

**Records required (COBS 3.8.2R):**
- A record of the form of each notice and each agreement used under COBS 3 (made at time of first use; retained for the relevant period after the firm ceases business with clients provided with that form)
- Per-client records of: (a) the categorisation established, with sufficient information to support it; (b) evidence of despatch of required notices; and (c) a copy of any agreement entered into with the client

**Retention periods (COBS 3.8.2R(3)):**
| Business type | Retention period |
|---|---|
| Pension transfer, pension conversion, pension opt-out, FSAVC | Indefinitely |
| Life policy or pension contract | At least 5 years |
| MiFID or equivalent third country business | 5 years |
| Any other case | 3 years |

[Note: MiFID article 16(6)]

## Key Points for Agents

- **Retail is the residual default.** A client who is not a per se professional, elective professional, or eligible counterparty is a retail client by definition (COBS 3.4.1R). There is no positive test for retail status.
- **MiFID prevails in mixed business (COBS 3.1.4R).** Where a firm conducts both MiFID and non-MiFID business for the same client, MiFID categorisation requirements govern the entire client relationship. This is a trump rule, not a choice.
- **Elective professional requires all three steps (COBS 3.5.3R).** The qualitative test, the quantitative test (for MiFID business), and the written procedure are all mandatory. Omitting any step means the client cannot be validly treated as elective professional. The opt-up is initiated by the client, not the firm.
- **Large undertaking thresholds differ for MiFID vs non-MiFID.** MiFID: two of EUR 20m balance sheet / EUR 40m turnover / EUR 2m own funds. Non-MiFID: £5m called-up capital or two of EUR 12.5m balance sheet / EUR 25m turnover / 250 employees (plus separate partnership, trust, and pension scheme tests). **(COBS 3.5.2R)**
- **Ongoing monitoring is a hard Rule.** If a firm becomes aware an elective professional client no longer meets the conditions, it must act — potentially re-categorising back to retail with notification (COBS 3.5.9R). The client has a corresponding self-reporting obligation (COBS 3.5.8G).
- **ECP re-categorisation defaults to professional, not retail (COBS 3.7.3BR).** An ECP requesting higher protection without expressly requesting retail treatment must be treated as a professional client, not a retail client.
- **Re-categorisation as retail ≠ eligible DISP complainant (COBS 3.7.8G).** DISP 2.7 eligible complainant criteria apply separately — COBS 3.7 re-categorisation does not confer FOS access automatically.

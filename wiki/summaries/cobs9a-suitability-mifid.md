# COBS 9A — Suitability (MiFID and insurance-based investment products provisions)

## Scope

COBS 9A applies to a firm which **(COBS 9A.1.1R)**:
1. Provides **investment advice or portfolio management** in the course of **MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business**; or
2. Provides **investment advice** in relation to an **insurance-based investment product (IBIP)**.

**COBS 9B exclusion:** The rules in COBS 9A do not apply to a firm in relation to the provision of a targeted support service within the scope of COBS 9B **(COBS 9A.1.1AG)**.

**IBIPs — limited rule application:** For insurance-based investment products, only those rules which implemented the IDD, or are derived from the IDD Regulation, apply. A note ("**Note:**") following each such rule identifies it as IDD-derived **(COBS 9A.1.5R–9A.1.6G)**. This is significant: firms advising on IBIPs operate under a subset of COBS 9A rules, not the full MiFID framework.

**COBS 9 vs COBS 9A:** COBS 9 applies to non-MiFID, non-IBIP personal recommendations and discretionary management. COBS 9A applies to MiFID business and IBIPs. The regimes are parallel, not cumulative. A firm must determine which applies to each piece of business and apply the correct chapter.

---

## Core Suitability Obligation

### The Obligation (COBS 9A.2.1R)

When providing investment advice or portfolio management, a firm must:
1. Obtain the necessary information regarding the client's:
   - **(a) knowledge and experience** in the investment field relevant to the specific type of financial instrument, IBIP, or service;
   - **(b) financial situation**, including the client's **ability to bear losses**; and
   - **(c) investment objectives**, including the client's **risk tolerance**;
2. Only recommend investment services, financial instruments, or IBIPs, or take decisions to trade, which are **suitable for the client** — and in particular, in accordance with the client's risk tolerance and **ability to bear losses**.

**Note:** MiFID article 25(2); IDD article 30(1).

The explicit reference to "ability to bear losses" in COBS 9A.2.1R (alongside "risk tolerance" in the investment objectives dimension) is a harder formulation than COBS 9. Both dimensions must be assessed: risk tolerance (what the client says they are comfortable with) and ability to bear losses (what the client can financially afford to lose). Treating them as identical is a named FCA failure mode.

### Scope of the Suitability Assessment (COBS 9A.2.2G)

Firms should undertake a suitability assessment not only when making a personal recommendation to buy a financial instrument or IBIP, but **for all decisions whether to trade — including personal recommendations about whether to buy, hold, or sell an investment** **(COBS 9A.2.2G)**. Suitability is not only a pre-transaction obligation; it governs hold and sell decisions as well.

### Portfolio Management — Mandate Recommendations (COBS 9A.2.3G)

Where a firm providing portfolio management recommends or requests that a client give or alter a mandate defining the limits of the firm's discretion, that recommendation, request, or advice is treated as a recommendation for suitability purposes. A suitability assessment must be undertaken in relation to any such recommendation **(COBS 9A.2.3G)**.

### Insurance-Based Investment Products — Demands and Needs (COBS 9A.2.3AR)

When proposing an IBIP, a firm must ensure it is consistent with the **client's insurance demands and needs** **(COBS 9A.2.3AR)**. This is an additional requirement layered on top of the financial instrument suitability obligation. **Note:** IDD recital 44; article 20(1).

## Assessing the Extent of Information Required

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.4R)

A firm must:
1. Determine the extent of information to be obtained in light of **all the features of the service** to be provided; and
2. Obtain such information as is necessary to understand the essential facts about the client and to have a reasonable basis for determining that the specific transaction:
   - **(a)** meets the client's investment objectives, including their risk tolerance;
   - **(b)** is such that the client is financially able to bear any related investment risks consistent with their investment objectives; and
   - **(c)** is such that the client has the necessary experience and knowledge to understand the risks involved.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.4AR)

For an IBIP personal recommendation, the firm must determine the extent of information in light of all the features of the **advice** to be provided, and obtain information sufficient to have a reasonable basis for determining that the recommendation satisfies criteria (a), (b), and (c) above **(COBS 9A.2.4AR)**. Where information required under COBS 9A.2.1R and COBS 9A.2.16R has already been obtained (e.g. via the insurance demands and needs assessment), the firm must **not request it again** from the client **(COBS 9A.2.4AR(3))**. **Note:** IDD articles 9(1), (2), and 17(3).

---

## Professional Client Assumptions

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.5R)

If the client is a **professional client**, the firm may assume:
1. In relation to the products, transactions, and services for which it is so classified — the client has the **necessary level of experience and knowledge** for the purposes of COBS 9A.2.4R(2)(c) (the knowledge and experience dimension); and
2. In relation to investment advice to a **per se professional client only** — the client is **financially able to bear any related investment risks** consistent with their investment objectives (the financial situation dimension).

**The asymmetry is critical:** Elective professional clients get only assumption (1) — the firm still must assess their financial situation and ability to bear losses. Per se professional clients get both assumptions — the firm may skip both the knowledge/experience and financial situation assessments for investment advice. This relaxation does not apply to portfolio management financial situation assessments for even per se professional clients.

## Information to Be Collected

### Knowledge and Experience: MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.6R)

The information must include, to the extent appropriate to the nature of the client, the service, and the type of product or transaction envisaged, including their complexity and the risks involved:
- **(1)** The types of service, transaction, and financial instrument with which the client is familiar;
- **(2)** The nature, volume, and frequency of the client's transactions in financial instruments and the period over which they have been carried out; and
- **(3)** The level of education, and profession or relevant former profession of the client.

### Knowledge and Experience: IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.6AR)

The same three categories apply, adapted to the IBIP context — the client's familiarity with types of service, transaction, IBIP, or financial instrument; nature, number, value, and frequency of past transactions in IBIPs or financial instruments; and level of education and profession or relevant former profession. **Note:** IDD article 17(1).

### Financial Situation: MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.7R)

Must include, where relevant: source and extent of regular income; assets (liquid assets, investments, real property); and regular financial commitments.

### Financial Situation: IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.7AR)

Same as MiFID. The level of information gathered must be **appropriate to the specific type of IBIP or service being considered** **(COBS 9A.2.7AR(2))**. **Note:** IDD article 9(3).

### Investment Objectives: MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.8R)

Must include, where relevant: length of time the client wishes to hold the investment; preferences regarding risk taking; risk profile; and the purposes of the investment.

### Investment Objectives: IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.8AR)

Same as MiFID. The level of information must be **appropriate to the specific type of IBIP or service** **(COBS 9A.2.8AR(2))**. **Note:** IDD article 9(4).

## Reliability of Information

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.9R)

A firm must take **reasonable steps to ensure that the information collected about its clients is reliable**. Steps must include, but are not limited to:
- **(a)** Ensuring clients are aware of the importance of providing accurate and up-to-date information;
- **(b)** Ensuring all tools used (including risk profiling tools) are **fit-for-purpose** and **appropriately designed** for use with the firm's clients, with **any limitations identified and actively mitigated** through the suitability assessment process;
- **(c)** Ensuring questions are likely to be **understood by clients**, capture an accurate reflection of objectives and needs, and capture the information necessary for the assessment; and
- **(d)** Taking steps to ensure **consistency of client information**, including by considering whether there are obvious inaccuracies.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.9AR)

The same four obligations apply. **Note:** IDD article 10.

**The risk profiling tool obligation (COBS 9A.2.9R(b)) is a hard Rule (R) — not guidance.** A firm cannot use a risk profiling tool without identifying its limitations and actively mitigating them in each client's suitability assessment. Treating a tool output as a complete suitability assessment without separate risk capacity assessment is non-compliant.

---

## Maintaining Up-to-Date Information

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.10R)

If a firm has an **on-going relationship** with a client (such as by providing ongoing advice or portfolio management), it must have — and be able to **demonstrate** — appropriate policies and procedures to **maintain adequate and up-to-date information** about that client to the extent necessary to fulfil the suitability requirements in COBS 9A.2.4R **(COBS 9A.2.10R)**. This is a systems and controls obligation on top of the per-transaction assessment.

---

## Discouraging the Provision of Information

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.11R)

A firm **must not encourage** a client not to provide information required for the purposes of COBS 9A.2.1R.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.11AR)

Same prohibition. **Note:** IDD article 17(2).

---

## Reliance on Client Information

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.12R)

A firm is entitled to rely on client-provided information unless it **is aware or ought to be aware** that the information is **manifestly out of date, inaccurate or incomplete**. The formulation "or ought to be aware" is stricter than COBS 9's "aware" only — an objective element applies.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.12AR)

Same standard with "or ought to be aware". **Note:** IDD article 17(4).

---

## Insufficient Information — No Recommendation

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.13R)

If a firm does not obtain the information required by COBS 9A.2.1R, it **must not recommend** investment services or financial instruments to the client.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.13AR)

If a firm does not obtain the information required under COBS 9A.2.1R and COBS 9A.2.16R, it **must not provide a personal recommendation** on an insurance-based investment product. **Note:** IDD article 9(5).

### Client Proceeds Without Sufficient Information (COBS 9A.2.14G)

Although a firm may not be permitted to make a personal recommendation or take a decision to trade because it lacks the necessary information, its client may still ask the firm to provide another service — for example, to arrange a deal or to deal as agent. The firm should obtain written confirmation of the client's instructions. The firm should also bear in mind the client's best interests rule and any obligation it may have under the appropriateness rules (COBS 10A) when providing the different service **(COBS 9A.2.14G)**.

## Identifying the Subject of a Suitability Assessment

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.15R)

Where a client is a **legal person**, a **group of two or more natural persons**, or one or more natural persons are **represented by another natural person**, the firm must establish, record, and implement a **written policy** which sets out:
- **(a)** who should be subject to the suitability assessment; and
- **(b)** how the suitability assessment will be done in practice, including from whom information will be collected.

**How the three dimensions are allocated:**

| Scenario | Financial situation + investment objectives | Knowledge and experience |
|----------|---------------------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Natural person represented by another | The **underlying client** | The **representative** (or person authorised to transact) |
| Legal person / elective professional client | The **legal person itself** | The **person authorised to transact** |

### Group Insurance — IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.15AR)

For group insurance where an IBIP is concluded on behalf of a group of members and **each individual member cannot take an individual decision to join**, the firm must establish and implement a policy on who will be subject to the suitability assessment, including from whom information will be collected. The policy must be **recorded** **(COBS 9A.2.15AR(3))**. **Note:** IDD article 13.

---

## Bundled Packages

### MiFID Business and IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.16R)

Where a firm provides investment advice recommending a **bundled package** of services or products pursuant to COBS 6.1ZA.16R (MiFID) or COBS 6.1ZA.16AR–6.1ZA.16ER (IBIPs), the firm must ensure that the **overall bundled package is suitable** for the client **(COBS 9A.2.16R)**. **Note:** MiFID article 25(2); IDD article 30(1).

Where a financial instrument or IBIP is **linked to a loan, mortgage, or home reversion plan**, the firm should take account of the suitability of the **overall transaction** and have regard to applicable MCOB suitability rules **(COBS 9A.2.17G)**.

---

## Switching Investments — Cost-Benefit Analysis

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.18R)

When providing investment advice or portfolio management to **retail clients** that involves **switching investments** (either by selling an instrument and buying another, or by exercising a right to change in regard to an existing instrument), a firm must:
1. Collect the necessary information on the client's **existing investments** and the **recommended new investments**; and
2. Undertake an **analysis of the costs and benefits of the switch** such that it is reasonably able to **demonstrate that the benefits of switching are greater than the costs**.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.18AR)

The same obligation applies to a personal recommendation involving **switching between underlying investment assets** in an IBIP: collect information on existing and recommended new underlying investment assets, and undertake a cost-benefit analysis demonstrating benefits exceed costs. **Note:** IDD article 9(7).

**This is a hard Rule (R) in both contexts.** "Replacement business" analysis in the existing wiki concept page describes the supervisory failure mode. COBS 9A.2.18R is the underlying rule requiring quantification — the firm must be able to *demonstrate* the net benefit, not merely assert it.

---

## Adequate Policies and Procedures

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.19R)

A firm must have in place, and be able to demonstrate, **adequate policies and procedures** to ensure that:
1. It understands the **nature and features, including the costs and risks**, of the investment services and financial instruments selected for its clients; and
2. While taking into account cost and complexity, it assesses whether **equivalent investment services or financial instruments could meet the client's profile** **(COBS 9A.2.19R)**.

---

## Prohibition on Recommending Where Nothing Is Suitable

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.20R)

When providing investment advice or portfolio management, a firm **must not recommend or decide to trade** where none of the services or instruments are suitable for the client.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.20AR)

When providing a personal recommendation on an IBIP, the firm **must not make a recommendation** where none of the IBIPs are suitable for the client. **Note:** IDD article 9(6).

---

## Guidance on Assessing Suitability (COBS 9A.2.21G)

A transaction may be unsuitable due to:
- The risks of the associated financial instruments (or, for IBIPs, the risks of underlying investment assets);
- The type of transaction, or the type or characteristics of the product;
- The characteristics of the order; or
- The **frequency of trading / frequency of switching of underlying investment assets**.

A series of transactions, each suitable in isolation, may be **unsuitable if the recommendation or decisions to trade are made at a frequency not in the best interests of the client**. For portfolio management, a transaction might be unsuitable if it would result in an **unsuitable portfolio**. **Note:** IDD recital 9.

---

## Automated and Semi-Automated Systems

### MiFID Business (COBS 9A.2.23R)

A firm that provides investment advice or portfolio management **in whole or in part** through an **automated or semi-automated system** remains **fully responsible** for the suitability assessment required by COBS 9A.2. That obligation is **not affected by the use of an electronic system** in making the personal recommendation or decision to trade.

### IBIPs (COBS 9A.2.24G)

For the avoidance of doubt, a firm's responsibility to perform the suitability assessment in accordance with COBS 9A.2.1R and COBS 9A.2.16R is **not reduced** where a personal recommendation on an IBIP is provided in whole or in part through an automated or semi-automated system. **Note:** IDD article 12.

---

## Information to Be Provided to the Client

### Explaining Suitability Assessment Purpose (COBS 9A.3.1R / 9A.3.1AR)

When undertaking the suitability assessment, a firm:
- **Must not** create any ambiguity or confusion about its responsibilities in the suitability assessment process **(COBS 9A.3.1R(1) / 9A.3.1AR(1))**; and
- **Must inform the client**, clearly and simply, that the reason for assessing suitability is to enable the firm to **act in the client's best interest** **(COBS 9A.3.1R(2) / 9A.3.1AR(2))**.

**Note (IBIP):** IDD article 11.

### Suitability Reports — MiFID Business (COBS 9A.3.2R / 9A.3.3R)

When providing investment advice to a **retail client**, the firm must, **before the transaction is concluded**, provide a suitability report in a **durable medium** specifying:
- The advice given and how that advice meets the client's preferences, objectives, and other characteristics **(COBS 9A.3.2R(2)(a))**; and
- For IBIPs: the client's demands and needs (on the basis of information obtained) and a personalised recommendation explaining why a particular IBIP would best meet those demands and needs **(COBS 9A.3.2R(2)(b)(i)–(ii))**.

**Distance communication exception (COBS 9A.3.2R(3)):** Where the use of a means of distance communication prevents prior delivery of the suitability report, the firm may provide it in a durable medium **immediately after** the client is bound, provided: (a) the client has consented to receiving the report without undue delay after conclusion; and (b) the firm has given the client the option of **delaying the transaction** in order to receive the report first.

**MiFID retail suitability report content (COBS 9A.3.3R(1)):** Must include an outline of:
- **(a)** The advice given and how the recommendation is suitable for the client; and
- **(b)** How the recommendation meets their objectives and personal circumstances with reference to: (i) the investment term required; (ii) their knowledge and experience; and (iii) their **attitude to risk and capacity for loss**.

**Additional content — periodic review flag (COBS 9A.3.3R(2)):** The report must include information on whether the recommended services or instruments are **likely to require the retail client to seek a periodic review** of their arrangements, and draw the client's attention to this.

**Subsequent reports — changes only (COBS 9A.3.3R(3)–(4)):** For firms providing periodic suitability assessments, following the first suitability report, subsequent reports:
- Need only cover **changes** in the relevant services or instruments and the **circumstances of the client**; and
- **Need not repeat** all the information in the first report.

### Suitability Statement — IBIPs (COBS 9A.3.3AR)

When providing a personal recommendation on an IBIP, the firm must provide a **suitability statement** (not a suitability report) to the client that includes:
- **(a)** An outline of the personal recommendation given;
- **(b)** How the recommendation is suitable, in particular how it meets: (i) the client's investment objectives including risk tolerance; (ii) the client's financial situation including ability to bear losses; and (iii) the client's knowledge and experience;
- **(c)** The client's attention must be drawn to information on whether any recommended IBIP is **likely to require a periodic review**.

Where the firm provides periodic suitability assessment, subsequent statements may be limited to **changes** in services/underlying assets and/or client circumstances. **Note:** IDD article 14(1)–(3).

**IBIP suitability statement for professional clients (COBS 9A.3.2AR):** Where the recommendation is made to a **professional client**, the firm must provide the suitability statement information in accordance with COBS 7.4 prior to conclusion of the contract.

## Periodic Assessments of Suitability

### Disclosure Obligation Before Service Commencement (COBS 9A.3.6R)

A firm must, **in good time before providing investment advice** (or, for IBIPs, at least in good time prior to conclusion of the contract), inform the client whether it will provide a **periodic assessment of suitability** of the financial instruments or IBIPs recommended. The disclosure must cover **(COBS 9A.3.8R)**:
- **(a)** The **frequency and extent** of the periodic suitability assessment, and where relevant, the **conditions that trigger** that assessment;
- **(b)** The extent to which **information previously collected will be subject to reassessment**; and
- **(c)** The way in which an **updated recommendation** will be communicated to the client.

**Note:** MiFID article 24(4)(a)(iii); IDD article 29(1)(a).

### Minimum Annual Frequency — MiFID Business (COBS 9A.3.9R)

1. A firm providing a periodic suitability assessment **must review the suitability of the recommendations given at least annually**.
2. The **frequency must be increased** where appropriate by reference to: (i) the **risk profile of the client**; and (ii) the **type of financial instruments** recommended.

### Minimum Annual Frequency — IBIPs (COBS 9A.3.10R)

1. A firm providing a periodic assessment of suitability on IBIPs **must review, in accordance with the best interests of its client, the suitability of the recommended IBIPs at least annually**.
2. The **frequency must be increased** depending on the **characteristics of the client** (such as risk tolerance) and the **nature of the recommended IBIP**. **Note:** IDD article 14(4).

## Record Keeping

### General Obligations (COBS 9A.4.1G–9A.4.2G)

A firm to which SYSC 9 applies must keep orderly records of its business and internal organisation. Records must reflect the different requirements depending on whether the client is retail or professional **(COBS 9A.4.1G)**.

For record-keeping obligations: MiFID firms refer to SYSC 9; insurers and managing agents refer to SYSC 3.2 and SYSC 3.3.

### Record Keeping: MiFID Business (COBS 9A.4.2AR)

In complying with SYSC 9, a firm must keep a record of:
1. Its compliance with COBS 9A.2 and 9A.3 applicable to MiFID business in respect of **each assessment of suitability it undertakes**;
2. Its compliance with COBS 9A.3.6R in **informing each client** whether periodic suitability assessment will be provided; and
3. In respect of investment advice to a **retail client**: (a) the time and date on which the advice was provided; (b) the financial instrument recommended; and (c) the suitability report provided to the client.

### Retention of Records: IBIPs (COBS 9A.4.3R)

A firm must maintain records of the suitability assessment for IBIPs that include:
1. The information obtained from the client; and
2. Any documents agreed with the client, including **documents that set out the rights of the parties** and the other terms on which the firm will provide services.

Records must be retained for **at least the duration of the relationship** between the firm and the client. **Note:** IDD article 19(1).

### Required IBIP Record Content (COBS 9A.4.4R)

The record maintained under COBS 9A.4.3R must include:
1. The **result of the suitability assessment**;
2. The **recommendation made** and the suitability statement provided under COBS 9A.3.3AR;
3. **Any changes made by the firm** with regard to the suitability assessment, in particular any change to the **client's risk tolerance**; and
4. **Any changes to the underlying investment assets**. **Note:** IDD article 19(2).

## Key Differences: COBS 9A vs COBS 9

| Feature | COBS 9 (non-MiFID) | COBS 9A (MiFID / IBIP) |
|---------|-------------------|------------------------|
| Professional client assumptions | None | Knowledge/experience (all pro clients); financial situation (per se only, investment advice) |
| Reliance standard | "aware" information is manifestly wrong | "aware **or ought to be aware**" — objective element added |
| Tool reliability | No specific rule | Hard Rule (R) — tools must be fit-for-purpose, limitations identified and actively mitigated |
| Ongoing information obligation | No specific rule | Hard Rule (R) — policies/procedures to maintain up-to-date client information |
| Switching cost-benefit | Not specified | Hard Rule (R) — must demonstrate benefits > costs |
| Suitability report timing | Before conclusion (life policy); after (other) | **Before conclusion** (retail MiFID advice); distance communication exception available |
| Periodic suitability obligation | Not addressed | Hard Rule (R) — at least annually; increased frequency based on risk profile |
| Periodic disclosure | Not addressed | Hard Rule (R) — must disclose frequency, triggers, reassessment scope, and communication method |
| Automated systems | Not addressed | Hard Rule (R) — responsibility not reduced |
| IBIP record retention | Fixed tiers (5 years / 3 years / indefinitely) | **At least for the duration of the relationship** |
| Legal person / representative policy | Not addressed | Hard Rule (R) — must establish, record, and implement written policy |

## Key Points for Agents

- **COBS 9A scope (COBS 9A.1.1R):** Applies to MiFID/equivalent third country/optional exemption business involving investment advice or portfolio management, and to investment advice on IBIPs. COBS 9B targeted support exclusion applies. IBIPs: only IDD-derived rules in COBS 9A apply, identified by "Note" annotations.
- **Professional client assumptions (COBS 9A.2.5R):** For per se professional clients receiving investment advice, the firm may skip both the knowledge/experience assessment AND the financial situation/ability to bear losses assessment. For elective professional clients, only the knowledge/experience assumption is available — financial situation must still be assessed. These assumptions do not apply to portfolio management for the financial situation dimension.
- **Risk profiling tool reliability is a hard Rule (COBS 9A.2.9R):** Tools must be fit-for-purpose and appropriately designed for the firm's clients. Limitations must be identified and actively mitigated through the suitability assessment process. This is not guidance — it is a binding obligation applicable each time a tool is used.
- **Switching requires demonstrated cost-benefit analysis (COBS 9A.2.18R / 9A.2.18AR):** Where advice involves switching investments (MiFID) or switching underlying IBIP assets, the firm must collect information on existing and proposed new holdings and produce an analysis demonstrating benefits exceed costs. Assertion of net benefit is not sufficient — the firm must be able to demonstrate it.
- **Automated and semi-automated systems — full responsibility retained (COBS 9A.2.23R):** Using an automated system does not reduce or transfer the suitability obligation. The firm remains fully responsible. This applies to both MiFID (Rule) and IBIPs (Guidance, IDD article 12). Robo-advice models operate under this constraint.
- **Periodic suitability assessment — minimum annual frequency, plus mandatory pre-service disclosure (COBS 9A.3.6R; 9A.3.8R; 9A.3.9R–9A.3.10R):** Where a firm provides ongoing suitability assessment, it must review at least annually (increase frequency for higher-risk clients/instruments), and must disclose — before the service begins — the frequency, conditions that trigger reassessment, what information will be re-collected, and how updated recommendations will be communicated.
- **Legal person / representative suitability policy (COBS 9A.2.15R):** Where clients are legal persons, groups, or represented by another person, the firm must have a documented written policy. The financial situation and investment objectives of a represented natural person = the underlying client; knowledge and experience = the representative. For legal persons / elective professional clients: financial situation and objectives = the legal person; knowledge and experience = the authorised transactor.
- **IBIP record retention: at least for the duration of the relationship (COBS 9A.4.3R):** Unlike COBS 9's fixed-period tiers, IBIP suitability records must be kept for at least as long as the firm-client relationship continues.

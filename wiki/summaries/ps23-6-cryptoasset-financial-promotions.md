# PS23/6 — Financial Promotion Rules for Cryptoassets

PS23/6 is the FCA's policy statement setting out the final rules for financial promotions of cryptoassets. It took effect on **8 October 2023**. The statement implements a bespoke consumer journey framework for cryptoasset promotions, classifies qualifying cryptoassets as Restricted Mass Market Investments (RMMIs), and establishes the legal routes by which cryptoasset promotions may lawfully be communicated to UK consumers.

> **Regulatory basis:** The financial promotions regime for cryptoassets is founded on s.21 FSMA (the financial promotion restriction) and the Financial Promotions Order (FPO). The FCA's rule-making powers over registered cryptoasset businesses communicating financial promotions derive from legislation extending FSMA financial promotion rule-making powers to MLR-registered businesses.

---

## Chapter 1 — Summary and Scope

### Classification: Restricted Mass Market Investments

Qualifying cryptoassets are classified as **Restricted Mass Market Investments (RMMIs)** — the middle tier of a three-tier structure for high-risk investments. **(PS23/6 §1.1–1.4)**

| Tier | Label | Access |
|------|-------|--------|
| Lowest risk | Readily Realisable Securities / Standard investments | Mass marketing permitted |
| Middle | **Restricted Mass Market Investments (RMMI)** | Mass marketing permitted with full consumer journey protections |
| Highest risk | Non-Mass Market Investments (NMMI) | Mass marketing prohibited; targeted at professional/sophisticated investors only |

The RMMI classification means mass marketing of cryptoassets to UK consumers is **not prohibited** — but it requires compliance with a prescribed consumer journey before a Direct Offer Financial Promotion (DOFP) can be shown.

**Cryptoassets in scope** include stablecoins (regardless of backing), fan tokens, and most asset-backed tokens. The classification is broad and driven by the definition of "qualifying cryptoasset" in the FPO rather than by investment characteristics. **(PS23/6 §2.1–2.10)**

### Four Legal Routes to Communicate a Cryptoasset Promotion

A cryptoasset financial promotion may only be communicated to UK consumers via one of four routes: **(PS23/6 §1.13–1.16)**

1. An **FCA-authorised person** communicates it directly
2. An **unauthorised person** communicates it after it has been approved by an FCA-authorised s21 approver
3. A **MLR-registered cryptoasset business** communicates its own promotions under **FPO Article 73ZA**
4. The promotion falls within another **FPO exemption** not disapplied for cryptoassets

**Critical point:** Payment institutions and electronic money institutions authorised solely under the PSRs/EMRs (and not FSMA) are **not** "authorised persons" for s.21 purposes and cannot approve cryptoasset promotions. **(PS23/6 §1.16)**

### FPO Exemptions Disapplied

The following FPO exemptions **do not** apply to qualifying cryptoassets: **(PS23/6 §2.11–2.14)**

- **Article 48** — high net worth individuals
- **Article 50A** — self-certified sophisticated investors
- **Article 51** — members of investment networks
- **Article 61** — sale of goods and supply of services

## Chapter 3 — Consumer Journey Rules

See [[cryptoasset-financial-promotions-framework]] for the full operative framework including the DOFP sequence, client categorisation mechanics, appropriateness anti-gaming rules, and incentives ban.

### Risk Warning

The prescribed risk warning text for cryptoassets is: **(PS23/6 §3.1–3.13)**

> **Don't invest unless you're prepared to lose all the money you invest. This is a high-risk investment and you should not expect to be protected if something goes wrong. Take 2 mins to learn more.**

This wording was specifically amended (from the general RMMI risk warning) to inform consumers that FSCS protection is generally unavailable and FOS complaint routes may not apply to cryptoassets.

- **Digital media:** risk warning must appear as a pop-up (or equivalent), statically fixed in the middle of the screen, prominently brought to the consumer's attention, legible in its own border with right bold/underlined text, and without design features that reduce visibility.
- **Non-digital media:** risk warning must be prominent, legible in its own border, and without features reducing visibility.

### Risk Summary

Firms may tailor the risk summary template to the specifics of the cryptoasset being promoted where the template wording is not relevant or does not capture key risks of that specific cryptoasset. Any changes require a **documented rationale**. Amended summaries must remain in the spirit of the template — FAQ-style consumer-friendly language, not more than 2 minutes to read. The FCA will monitor and challenge risk summaries it believes do not accurately reflect the relevant risks. **(PS23/6 §3.9–3.13 Our response)**

### Incentives to Invest — Absolute Ban

The ban on financial incentives to invest (refer-a-friend bonuses, new-joiner bonuses, free token offers) applies to cryptoasset promotions. Unlike other RMMIs, **the "shareholder benefit" exemption does not apply to cryptoassets**. See [[cryptoasset-financial-promotions-framework]] for the intrinsic/extrinsic distinction. **(PS23/6 §§3.14–3.24)**

### Cooling-Off Period

A minimum **24-hour cooling-off period** applies before a DOFP can be shown. The 24-hour period starts from the moment the consumer **requests to view the DOFP**. The cooling-off period **only applies to first-time investors with the firm** — consumers who have never previously received a DOFP from that firm. Existing clients must still complete client categorisation and appropriateness before further DOFPs. **(PS23/6 §§3.19–3.28)**

### Personalised Risk Warning

Before a DOFP can be communicated, firms must show a personalised risk warning pop-up: **(PS23/6 §§3.29–3.35)**

> **[Client name], this is a high-risk investment. How would you feel if you lost the money you're about to invest? Take 2 min to learn more.**

The "Take 2 min to learn more" link must link to the same product-specific risk summary as the main risk warning. For non-digital media, the personalised risk warning and risk summary must be provided in a durable medium.

### Client Categorisation

Before a DOFP can be communicated for an RMMI, the consumer must declare they meet the criteria for one of: **(PS23/6 §§3.36–3.46 Our response)**

- **Restricted Investor** (investment limits apply — no more than 10% of net assets in RMMIs)
- **High Net Worth Investor**
- **Certified Sophisticated Investor**

**Self-certified sophisticated investor category does not apply to cryptoassets.** The existing self-certification criteria (e.g. syndicate membership, business angel networks) are not appropriate proxies for sophistication regarding cryptoassets.

Investor declarations are **only valid for 12 months**. Firms must re-categorise consumers after the 12-month period if they wish to make further DOFPs to them.

### Appropriateness Assessment

The RMMI must be assessed as appropriate for the consumer before a DOFP can be processed. See [[cryptoasset-financial-promotions-framework]] for the full anti-gaming rules. **(PS23/6 §§3.47–3.55)**

### Record Keeping

Firms must record, for each consumer: **(PS23/6 §§3.56–3.57)**

- Outcome of client categorisation (category declared; number not proceeding)
- Outcome of appropriateness assessments (final outcome per consumer per investment; number of assessments undertaken; number assessed appropriate vs inappropriate)

These records must be available to s21 approvers where the approver is not the communicating firm.

## Chapter 4 — Authorised Firms and the s21 Approver Gateway

### Requirements for Authorised Firms Communicating or Approving Promotions

Authorised firms communicating or approving cryptoasset financial promotions must: **(PS23/6 §§4.2–4.9)**

- Include the **approving firm's full name and date of approval** in the promotion; where space is limited (third-party platform), display "Approver FRN xxxxxx" as a clickable link to a page showing full name and date
- Have **competence and expertise (C&E)** in the specific investment product type being promoted (e.g. unlisted equities), not necessarily in the underlying commercial sector of the investee
- Monitor approved promotions for the **lifetime of the promotion** and withdraw approval as soon as reasonably practicable if the promotion is no longer compliant or fair, clear, and not misleading
- Obtain **quarterly attestations of "no material change"** from clients with approved promotions

### The s21 Regulatory Gateway

Once the gateway comes into effect, all authorised firms wishing to approve promotions for unauthorised persons must **apply to the FCA for permission**. **(PS23/6 §§4.12–4.17)**

- **Exemptions from the gateway:** approvals for group entities or appointed representatives; authorised persons approving their own promotions for communication by an unauthorised person
- **Post-approval notification:** within **7 days** of approving any promotion — notifying client name, product type, and size of issuance
- **Bi-annual reporting:** approval activity, revenue from approval activity, complaints received in connection with approval activity

### Consumer Duty Application to Authorised Firms

The Consumer Duty **applies to authorised firms** communicating or approving cryptoasset financial promotions. Key obligations: **(PS23/6 §§4.18–4.28)**

- Act in good faith; do not exploit or manipulate retail customers' behavioural biases to mis-lead or create demand
- Ensure communications support retail customers' understanding (Consumer Understanding outcome)
- Test, monitor and adapt communications to respond to feedback and support good outcomes
- Where harm results from a firm's acts or omissions in communicating/approving a promotion, the firm must take appropriate action to remedy this, including redress

**The Consumer Duty does not yet apply to MLR-registered businesses' own promotions.** **(PS23/6 §4.29)**

## Chapter 5 — MLR-Registered Businesses

### Article 73ZA — The MLR Exemption

FPO Article 73ZA permits MLR-registered cryptoasset businesses (cryptoasset exchange providers and custodian wallet providers registered under the Money Laundering Regulations) to communicate **their own** cryptoasset financial promotions without s21 approval, subject to complying with the FCA's financial promotion rules. **(PS23/6 §5.8)**

The exemption also covers **non-real-time promotions communicated on behalf of a registered business** where the registered business prepares the content of the promotion.

Cryptoasset businesses not registered under the MLRs cannot rely on Art. 73ZA. **(PS23/6 §5.9)**

### Rules Applicable to MLR-Registered Businesses

MLR-registered businesses communicating under Art. 73ZA must comply with the same rules as authorised persons communicating cryptoasset financial promotions, including: **(PS23/6 §§5.11–5.15)**

- **Principle 7** (Communications with clients)
- **Relevant parts of GEN** (statements about authorisation and regulation)
- **COBS 4** (Communicating with clients, including financial promotions)
- **COBS 10** (Assessing appropriateness)

This means all consumer journey rules (risk warning, personalised risk warning, cooling-off, client categorisation, appropriateness assessment, incentives ban, record keeping) apply equally to MLR-registered businesses communicating their own promotions.

### FCA Enforcement Powers over MLR-Registered Businesses

The FCA's supervisory and enforcement powers have been extended to cover MLR-registered businesses relying on Art. 73ZA: **(PS23/6 §§5.16–5.26)**

| Power | FSMA provision | Effect |
|-------|---------------|--------|
| Information request | s.165 | Require information, documents and data |
| Skilled persons review | s.166 | Commission third-party report at firm's cost |
| VREQ / OIREQ | s.55L | Impose voluntary or own-initiative requirements |
| Asset restriction | s.55P | Prevent disposal, withdrawal, or diminution of assets |
| Withdrawal / refrain direction | s.137S | Direct firm to withdraw a promotion or refrain from communicating it |

A registered cryptoasset business may not rely on Art. 73ZA where doing so would breach a requirement under s.55L or a direction under s.137S.

### Fit and Proper — Promotions at the MLR Gateway

As part of the MLR registration fitness and propriety assessment, the FCA will consider an applicant's previous and/or planned financial promotions and expect applicants to demonstrate how they will comply with the financial promotions regime. **(PS23/6 §5.7)**

## Key Points for Agents

- Qualifying cryptoassets are **RMMIs** — mass marketing is permitted but requires completion of a full consumer journey (risk warning, personalised risk warning, client categorisation, appropriateness, 24-hour cooling-off) before a DOFP can be shown.
- There are four lawful communication routes. PIs/EMIs authorised solely under PSRs/EMRs are **not** authorised persons and cannot approve cryptoasset promotions. MLR-registered businesses can communicate their own promotions under Art. 73ZA.
- The **24-hour cooling-off period applies only to first-time investors** with a specific firm. Client categorisation declarations expire after **12 months**.
- **Self-certified sophisticated investor category is not available for cryptoassets.** Only Restricted Investor, High Net Worth, and Certified Sophisticated categories apply.
- The **incentives ban is absolute for cryptoassets** — there is no shareholder benefit exemption. Refer-a-friend bonuses and free token offers are prohibited. Intrinsic governance/voting rights are not incentives.
- MLR-registered businesses must comply with **all the same consumer journey rules** as authorised persons when relying on Art. 73ZA. The FCA has extended FSMA enforcement powers to cover them.
- The **Consumer Duty applies to authorised firms** communicating or approving cryptoasset promotions. It does **not yet** apply to MLR-registered businesses' own promotions.
- FPO exemptions for **high net worth (Art. 48)**, **self-certified sophisticated (Art. 50A)**, **Art. 51**, and **Art. 61** are **disapplied** for cryptoassets.

# CONC 1 — Application, Purpose and Guidance on Financial Difficulties

## What This Document Is

CONC 1 is the opening chapter of the FCA's Consumer Credit sourcebook. It sets out three things: what CONC is and what it sits on top of (§1.1), who and what it applies to (§1.2), and how firms should recognise customers in financial difficulties (§1.3). It is the scoping foundation for all subsequent CONC chapters.

## CONC 1.1 — Application and Purpose

CONC is the specialist sourcebook for credit-related regulated activities **(CONC 1.1.1G)**. It adds to and builds on higher-level obligations in PRIN, GEN, SYSC and the Consumer Credit Act 1974 (CCA). Other Handbook sourcebooks also apply: complaints handling is in [[disp1-treating-complainants-fairly|DISP]], supervision and reporting in SUP, and client money rules in CASS **(CONC 1.1.3G)**.

CONC 1.1.4G explicitly reminds firms that all nine Principles for Businesses apply to credit-related activities. The list includes Principle 12 (act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers, including PRIN 2A — Consumer Duty). This is the CONC-internal confirmation that Consumer Duty applies to all CONC-regulated firms.

## CONC 1.2 — Who? What? Where?

### Who

CONC applies to any FCA-authorised firm carrying on credit-related regulated activities **(CONC 1.2.1R)**. Firms must ensure employees and agents comply, and take reasonable steps to ensure all other persons acting on their behalf comply **(CONC 1.2.2R)**.

Appointed representatives: CONC does not bind ARs directly, but under s.39(3)–(4) FSMA, the principal firm is fully responsible for an AR's acts and omissions. Anything an AR does is legally treated as done by the firm **(CONC 1.2.3G)**.

### What

The credit-related regulated activities in scope are **(CONC 1.2.4G)**:

1. Consumer credit lending
2. Credit broking
3. Debt counselling
4. Debt adjusting
5. Debt administration
6. Debt collecting
7. Providing credit information services
8. Providing credit references
9. Operating an electronic system in relation to lending (P2P — limited to borrower/prospective borrower context)
10. Consumer hiring

### Where

CONC applies (except CONC 3 on financial promotions) where **(CONC 1.2.5R)**:
- The customer's **habitual residence is in the UK**, whether the firm's establishment is in the UK or abroad.

This creates extraterritorial reach: a firm established outside the UK that serves UK-habitually-resident consumers must comply with CONC.

### Secured on Land — MCOB Boundary

CONC does **not** apply to credit agreements secured on land. Those go to MCOB **(CONC 1.2.7G)**. The narrow CONC exceptions are:
- Agreements where the borrower is a "relevant recipient of credit" but is not one or more individuals or trustees (e.g. a mixed-composition partnership)
- MCD article 3(1)(b) credit agreements secured on land where less than 40% is used as a dwelling **(CONC 1.2.7G(3))**

Credit broking in relation to these secured agreements may still constitute credit broking under RAO article 36A, even where the underlying agreement is exempt **(CONC 1.2.7G(4))**.

P2P platforms facilitating regulated mortgage contracts are excluded from CONC where MCOB applies **(CONC 1.2.12R)**.

### MCD Article 3(1)(b) Firms

Creditors and credit intermediaries under MCD article 3(1)(b) have a limited CONC application: only CONC 1.2, 1.3, 2.2, 2.7, 2.8, 2.9 apply to both, with additional sections applying to creditors (2.4, 2.10, 4.6, 6.4, 6.5, 6.7) and to intermediaries (2.5, 4.4.2R(4), 5.4, 6.8) **(CONC 1.2.8R)**. High-net-worth borrowers may waive regulated agreement protections subject to specific conditions **(CONC 1.2.10R)**.

## CONC 1.3 — Guidance on Financial Difficulties

CONC 1.3.1G sets out seven indicators that a firm **is aware or ought reasonably to be aware** of when a customer is in financial difficulties. These are triggers for obligations in CONC 6 and 7 (post-contractual and arrears handling):

1. Consecutively failing to meet minimum repayments on a credit card or store card
2. Adverse accurate entries on a credit file (not in dispute)
3. Outstanding county court judgments for non-payment of debt
4. Inability to meet repayments from disposable income — evidenced by non-payment of essential bills (e.g. utilities), borrowing further to repay existing debts, or repaying only by disposing of assets or security
5. Consecutively failing to meet repayments when due
6. Agreement to a debt management plan or other debt solution
7. Evidence of discussions with any firm (including a not-for-profit debt advice body) with a view to a DMP, debt solution, or debt counselling

These indicators are guidance (G), not rules. A firm cannot argue ignorance if these signals are present in data it holds or ought to hold.

## Key Points for Agents

- CONC is the specialist sourcebook for all credit-related regulated activities — 9 activity types plus consumer hiring. It is not limited to lending.
- Consumer Duty (Principle 12 / PRIN 2A) explicitly applies to CONC firms by virtue of CONC 1.1.4G. See [[consumer-duty-overview]].
- CONC applies whenever the customer's habitual residence is UK, regardless of where the firm is established — extraterritorial reach is a hard rule.
- Credit agreements secured on land go to MCOB, not CONC. Broking of those agreements may still be regulated under CONC.
- AR acts are the principal firm's acts by statute (s.39(4) FSMA) — full liability.
- The 7 financial difficulty indicators in CONC 1.3.1G are the operational trigger list for CONC 6 and 7 obligations.

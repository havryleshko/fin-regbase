# COBS 2.3A — Inducements (MiFID, ETT, OE and IBIP Business)

## Application — COBS 2.3A.1R

COBS 2.3A applies to a firm:
1. In relation to its MiFID, equivalent third country or optional exemption business; and
2. Carrying on insurance distribution activities in relation to an insurance-based investment product (IBIP).

**(COBS 2.3A.1R)**

### Relationship with Other Regimes

**Adviser charging (COBS 2.3A.2G):** A firm making a personal recommendation to a UK retail client in relation to a retail investment product (in the course of MiFID/ETT/OE business) or an IBIP is also required to comply with COBS 6.1A (adviser charging and remuneration). COBS 6.1A provides, among other things, that a firm must only be remunerated for a personal recommendation by adviser charges.

**Targeted support (COBS 2.3A.4AG):** A firm providing a targeted support service in the course of MiFID/ETT/OE business or in relation to an IBIP is also required to comply with COBS 9B.8 (charging and remuneration for targeted support). The requirements of COBS 2.3A remain potentially relevant to a targeted support firm to the extent it pays or accepts monetary or non-monetary benefits not prohibited by COBS 9B.8 or COBS 9B.9 — including, for example, payments from an affiliated company in relation to the provision of a targeted support service **(COBS 2.3A.4CG)**.

**Issuer and product provider obligations (COBS 2.3A.4BG):** Where a firm is an issuer or product provider and considers offering or paying benefits to another firm in connection with the provision of a targeted support service (or related services), it is also required to comply with COBS 9B.9.

---

## The Prohibition — COBS 2.3A.5R

Except where COBS 2.3A.6R applies, a firm must not:

- **(a)** pay to or accept from **any party other than the client or a person on behalf of the client** any fee or commission; or
- **(b)** provide to or receive from **any party other than the client or a person on behalf of the client** any non-monetary benefit

in connection with the provision of an investment service or ancillary service, or the distribution of an IBIP or an ancillary service.

**(COBS 2.3A.5R)** [Note: MiFID article 24(9); IDD articles 22(3), 29(2), 29(3)]

**Effect of breach (COBS 2.3A.7E):** A firm that fails to comply with COBS 2.3A.5R is to be regarded as not fulfilling its obligations in relation to: (1) conflicts of interest (SYSC 10); and (2) acting honestly, fairly and professionally in accordance with the best interests of its clients (COBS 2.1.1R).

---

## Exceptions — COBS 2.3A.6R

COBS 2.3A.5R does not apply to three categories of payment or benefit:

### (a) Quality-Enhancing Benefits

A fee, commission or non-monetary benefit which:
1. Is **designed to enhance the quality** of the relevant service to the client (see COBS 2.3A.8R; and, for IBIPs, also COBS 2.3A.9AR); and
2. Does **not impair** compliance with the firm's duty to act honestly, fairly and professionally in the best interests of the client.

Where a firm pays, provides, accepts or receives a quality-enhancing benefit, it must **clearly disclose** to the client, **prior to provision of the relevant service**, in a comprehensive, accurate and understandable manner:
- (a) the existence and nature of the payment or benefit; and
- (b) the amount, or — where the amount cannot be ascertained — the method for calculating it.

Where applicable, the firm must also inform the client of the mechanisms for transferring the fee, commission, monetary or non-monetary benefit received back to the client **(COBS 2.3A.6R(4))**.

**(COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(a), (2), (3), (4))**

### (b) Operational Payments

A payment or benefit which **enables or is necessary** for the provision of the investment service or distribution of the IBIP — such as custody costs, settlement and exchange fees, regulatory levies or legal fees — which **by its nature cannot give rise to conflicts** with the firm's duty to act honestly, fairly and professionally in the best interests of the client.

**(COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(b))**

### (c) Research Under COBS 2.3B

(In relation to MiFID, ETT or OE business only) Third party research received in accordance with COBS 2.3B (see COBS 2.3B.3R).

**(COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(c))**

---

## Quality Enhancement Test — COBS 2.3A.8R

For the purposes of the exception at COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(a), a fee, commission or non-monetary benefit is designed to enhance the quality of the relevant service to the client **only if** all four of the following conditions are met simultaneously:

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| **(a) Additional/higher service** | It is justified by the provision of an additional or higher level service to the client, proportional to the level of inducements received |
| **(b) No indirect self-benefit** | It does not directly benefit the recipient firm, its shareholders or employees without tangible benefit to the client |
| **(c) Ongoing justification** | It is justified by the provision of an ongoing benefit to the client in relation to any ongoing inducement |
| **(d) No bias or distortion** | The provision of the service by the firm to the client is not biased or distorted as a result of the fee, commission or non-monetary benefit |

**Ongoing monitoring obligation (COBS 2.3A.8R(2)):** A firm must fulfil these conditions on an ongoing basis for as long as it continues to pay or receive the fee, commission or non-monetary benefit.

**(COBS 2.3A.8R)** [Note: MiFID Delegated Directive article 11(2) and (3)]

### Examples of Quality-Enhancing Benefits — COBS 2.3A.9R

COBS 2.3A.9R gives three non-exhaustive examples where a fee, commission or NMB may be justified under COBS 2.3A.8R(1)(a):

1. **Broad restricted advice plus access:** Restricted advice on, and access to, a wide range of suitable financial instruments or IBIPs including from third-party product providers having no close links with the firm.

2. **Restricted advice combined with ongoing service:** Restricted advice combined with either:
   - (a) an offer to assess suitability of instruments/IBIPs at least annually; or
   - (b) another ongoing service likely to be of value (e.g. advice about the suggested optimal asset allocation).

3. **Access plus added-value tools:** Access at competitive price to a wide range of instruments/IBIPs meeting the client's needs (including from third-party providers with no close links with the firm), together with added-value tools such as objective information tools helping the client monitor, model and adjust the range of instruments/IBIPs, or providing periodic performance and charges reports.

**(COBS 2.3A.9R)** [Note: MiFID Delegated Directive article 11(2)]

## IBIP Additional Requirements — COBS 2.3A.9AR

For IBIPs, additional requirements apply on top of COBS 2.3A.8R. An inducement or inducement scheme **has a detrimental impact** on the quality of the relevant service to the client where it is of such a nature and scale that it provides an incentive to carry out insurance distribution activities in a way not in compliance with the obligation to act in the client's best interests **(COBS 2.3A.9AR(1))**.

An insurance intermediary or insurer must perform an **overall analysis** taking into account all relevant factors which may increase or decrease the risk of detrimental impact, and any organisational measures taken to prevent the risk **(COBS 2.3A.9AR(2))**.

The firm must in particular consider the following **six criteria** **(COBS 2.3A.9AR(3))**:

| Criterion | Issue identified |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **(a)** | Whether the inducement could provide incentive to offer/recommend a particular IBIP even though the firm could offer a different IBIP better meeting the client's needs |
| **(b)** | Whether the inducement is **solely or predominantly based on quantitative commercial criteria** or whether it takes into account qualitative criteria — compliance with applicable legal requirements, service quality, client satisfaction |
| **(c)** | The value of the inducement relative to the value of the IBIP and the services provided |
| **(d)** | Whether the inducement is **entirely or mainly paid at conclusion of the policy** or extends over the whole term of the contract |
| **(e)** | Whether there is an appropriate **reclaim mechanism** where the IBIP lapses or is surrendered at an early stage or the client's interests have been harmed |
| **(f)** | Whether there exists any form of **variable or contingent threshold or value accelerator** unlocked by attaining a target based on volume or value of sales |

**Criterion (f) — the volume/value accelerator test** — is the most significant. Any variable or contingent threshold that unlocks additional payments based on volume or value of product sales is presumptively detrimental to the client interest. The existence of such a mechanism is a strong indicator that the inducement is not quality-enhancing.

**(COBS 2.3A.9AR)** [Note: IDD Regulation articles 2(2), 2(3) and 8]

## Disclosure Obligations — COBS 2.3A.10R–2.3A.14R

### Pre-Service Disclosure — COBS 2.3A.10R

**Prior to** the provision of the relevant service, the firm must disclose to the client:
- The information set out in COBS 2.3A.6R(2) — existence/nature plus amount or calculation method; and
- Where applicable, COBS 2.3A.6R(4) — the transfer-back mechanism.

**Granularity of disclosure:** Minor non-monetary benefits may be described in a **generic** way. Other non-monetary benefits received or paid in connection with a service must be **individually priced and disclosed separately** **(COBS 2.3A.10R(2))**.

### Ex-Post Disclosure Where Amount Unknown — COBS 2.3A.11R

Where the firm is **unable to ascertain the amount** on an ex-ante basis and instead discloses the method of calculation, the firm must also inform the client of the exact amount received or paid on an **ex-post** basis **(COBS 2.3A.11R)**.

### Annual Disclosure for Ongoing Inducements — COBS 2.3A.12R

Where inducements are received on an **ongoing basis** in relation to an investment service or IBIP distribution, the firm must inform the client **at least annually** about the actual amounts of payments or benefits received. Minor non-monetary benefits may again be described generically **(COBS 2.3A.12R)**.

### Distribution Chain Obligation — COBS 2.3A.14R

**Each firm** in a distribution channel providing an investment service, ancillary service or distributing an IBIP must comply with its own disclosure obligations. The obligation cannot be discharged by a firm elsewhere in the chain **(COBS 2.3A.14R)**.

## UK Retail Ban — COBS 2.3A.15R

### Scope

This rule applies where a firm provides a **UK retail client** with:
- (a) independent advice;
- (b) restricted advice; or
- (c) portfolio management services.

### The Ban

The firm **must not accept** any fees, commission, monetary or non-monetary benefits paid or provided by any third party (or person acting on behalf of a third party) in relation to the provision of the relevant service to the client **(COBS 2.3A.15R(2))**.

For independent and restricted advice, the ban applies in connection with:
- (a) the firm's business of advising; or
- (b) any related service **(COBS 2.3A.15R(2A))**.

### Exceptions

The ban does **not** apply to:
- (a) **Acceptable minor non-monetary benefits** (COBS 2.3A.19R for investment services; COBS 6.1A.5AR for IBIP distribution); or
- (b) **Third party research** received in accordance with COBS 2.3B.

**(COBS 2.3A.15R(3))**

**The UK retail ban is an absolute prohibition.** It does not require assessment of quality enhancement or disclosure. There is no residual discretion for the firm to accept and retain third-party payments in connection with independent advice, restricted advice or portfolio management for UK retail clients, save for acceptable minor NMBs and COBS 2.3B research.

---

## Professional Clients and Overseas Retail — COBS 2.3A.16R

### Scope

This rule applies where a firm provides **independent advice or portfolio management** services to:
- (a) a retail client **outside the United Kingdom**; or
- (b) (for investment services) a **professional client**.

### The Obligation

The firm must not:
- **(a)** accept and **retain** any fees, commission or monetary benefits; or
- **(b)** accept any non-monetary benefits other than acceptable minor NMBs (COBS 2.3A.19R / COBS 6.1A.5AR) or COBS 2.3B research

where paid or provided by a third party or person on behalf of a third party.

### Transfer-Back Mechanism — COBS 2.3A.16R(3)

Unlike the absolute UK retail ban, the professional/overseas retail rule requires the firm to **transfer all received amounts back to the client**. The firm must:
- **(a)** return to the client as soon as reasonably possible any fees, commissions or monetary NMBs received;
- **(b)** transfer in full to the client all fees, commission or monetary benefits received from third parties;
- **(c)** establish and implement a policy to ensure such amounts are allocated and transferred to the client; and
- **(d)** inform the client about fees, commissions or monetary benefits transferred, such as through periodic reporting statements.

**(COBS 2.3A.16R)** [Note: MiFID articles 24(7)(b) and 24(8); MiFID Delegated Directive article 12(1) and (2)]

---

## Payments by Persons on Behalf of the Client — COBS 2.3A.18G

Fees, commissions or non-monetary benefits paid or provided by a person **on behalf of the client** are acceptable only if:
- That person is aware that such payments have been made on that client's behalf; and
- The amount and frequency of any payment is **agreed between the client and the firm** and is **not determined by a third party**.

This would typically be the case for accountants or lawyers acting under a clear payment instruction from the client, or where the client negotiates and pays a fee directly. **(COBS 2.3A.18G)** [Note: MiFID recital 75]

## Acceptable Minor Non-Monetary Benefits — COBS 2.3A.19R

An acceptable minor non-monetary benefit must satisfy **all four threshold criteria** and must **consist of one of 12 named types**:

### Four Threshold Criteria

1. Clearly disclosed prior to provision of the relevant service (may be in a generic description where applicable);
2. Capable of enhancing the quality of service provided to the client;
3. Of a scale and nature that it could not be judged to impair compliance with the duty to act honestly, fairly and professionally in the best interests of the client;
4. Reasonable, proportionate and of a scale unlikely to influence the firm's behaviour in any way detrimental to the interests of the relevant client.

### 12 Named Types (COBS 2.3A.19R(5))

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **(a)** | Information or documentation relating to a financial instrument or investment service — generic in nature or personalised to the individual client |
| **(b)** | Written material from a third party commissioned and paid for by a corporate issuer or potential issuer to promote a new issuance, where relationship is clearly disclosed and material made available simultaneously to any firms or to the general public |
| **(c)** | Participation in conferences, seminars and other training events on the benefits and features of a specific financial instrument or investment service |
| **(d)** | Hospitality of reasonable de minimis value — food and drink during a business meeting, or during conferences/seminars/training under (c) |
| **(e)** | Research relating to an issue of shares, debentures, warrants or certificates representing certain securities, which is produced (A) prior to issue completion, (B) by a person providing underwriting or placing services to the issuer, and made available to prospective investors in the issue |
| **(f)** | Research received for evaluating the research provider's research service — subject to: (i) trial period ≤ 3 months; (ii) no monetary or NMB consideration due at any time (including before or after trial); (iii) trial not commenced within 12 months of terminating any arrangement (including previous trial period) with the same research provider; (iv) firm records dates and conditions satisfied |
| **(g)** | [Deleted] |
| **(h)** | Third party research received by a firm providing investment services or ancillary services to clients where it relates to **fixed income, currency or commodity instruments** |
| **(i)** | Research received from a research provider that is **not engaged in execution services** and is not part of a financial services group that includes an investment firm offering execution or brokerage services |
| **(j)** | Written material **openly available** from a third party to any firm or the general public — "openly available" means no conditions or barriers to access other than those necessary to comply with regulatory obligations (e.g. requiring a log-in, sign-up or submission of user information) |
| **(k)** | **Corporate access services** relating to listed or unlisted companies with a **market capitalisation below £200m** |
| **(l)** | **Short-term trading commentary** that does not contain substantive analysis, and bespoke trade advisory services intrinsically linked to the execution of a transaction in financial instruments |

**Guidance notes:**
- A non-monetary benefit that involves a third party **allocating valuable resources to the firm** is not a minor non-monetary benefit and is considered to impair compliance with the duty to act in clients' best interests **(COBS 2.3A.21G)**.
- Non-substantive material — short-term market commentary on economic statistics/company results/upcoming events containing only a brief unsubstantiated summary of the third party's own opinion without substantive analysis — can be treated as type (a) information **(COBS 2.3A.22G)**.
- The particular features of fixed income, currency and commodity markets mean that pricing of transactions in those instruments typically does not take into account research services, making type (h) appropriate **(COBS 2.3A.22AG)**.

## Non-Advised Packaged Products — COBS 2.3A.23G–2.3A.25G

The FCA identifies arrangements that will breach the client's best interests rule where a firm sells or arranges the sale of a packaged product to a retail client.

**Volume overrides and indemnities — COBS 2.3A.24E:** Where a firm is required to disclose commission (COBS 6.4) to a client in relation to the sale of a packaged product (other than between firms in the same immediate group), the firm **should not** enter into:
- (a) volume overrides, where commission paid in respect of several transactions is more than a simple multiple of the commission for one transaction of the same kind; or
- (b) an agreement to indemnify the payment of commission on terms that would confer an additional financial benefit on the recipient in the event the commission becomes repayable.

Contravention may be relied upon as tending to establish contravention of COBS 2.3A.5R **(COBS 2.3A.24E(2))**.

**Increased commission — COBS 2.3A.25G:** If a firm enters into an arrangement under which commission is increased beyond the amount disclosed to the client, it is likely to have breached the disclosure rules in COBS 6.4 and the inducements rules in COBS 2.3A.6R(2) and (3), unless the increase is attributable to an increase in premiums or contributions.

## Credit to Adviser Firms — COBS 2.3A.27E

A retail investment product provider should not take steps that result in holding the capital or voting power of, or providing credit to, a firm that holds itself out as making personal recommendations to retail clients on retail investment products — unless all four conditions are met:
- (a) the holding/credit is acquired on commercial terms objectively comparable to those an independent person would require;
- (b) the firm has reliable written evidence of (a);
- (c) no arrangements channel business from the adviser firm to the product provider; and
- (d) the product provider is not able, and none of its associates is able, to exercise any influence over the personal recommendations made in relation to retail investment products.

Contravention may be relied upon as tending to establish contravention of COBS 2.3A.15R **(COBS 2.3A.27E(6))**.

## Record Keeping — COBS 2.3A.32R and 2.3A.34AR

### Evidence of Quality Enhancement — COBS 2.3A.32R

A firm must hold evidence that fees, commissions or NMBs paid or received are designed to enhance the quality of the relevant service to the client by:
1. Keeping an **internal list** of all fees, commissions and NMBs received from third parties in relation to the provision of services; and
2. **Recording how** those fees, commissions and NMBs paid or received (or that the firm intends to use) enhance the quality of services to clients, and the steps taken not to impair compliance with the duty to act honestly, fairly and professionally.

**(COBS 2.3A.32R)** [Note: MiFID Delegated Directive article 11(4)]

### MiFID Records of Disclosures Made — COBS 2.3A.34AR

In complying with SYSC 9, a firm to which MiFID/ETT/OE record-keeping rules apply must keep a record of the **information provided to clients** in compliance with the rules in this section **(COBS 2.3A.34AR)**.

The costs and charges coordination rules cross-reference back to COBS 2.3A.10R–2.3A.12R: firms must take into account COBS 6.1ZA.11R, 6.1ZA.12R, and 6.1ZA.14R (for MiFID business) and COBS 6.1ZA.11R–6.1ZA.13R and 6.1ZA.15AR (for IBIPs) when implementing disclosure requirements **(COBS 2.3A.13R)**.

## Key Points for Agents

- **COBS 2.3A applies to MiFID/ETT/OE business and IBIP distribution.** Non-MiFID non-advised commissions (e.g. non-IBIP packaged products to retail) are governed by different rules. Do not conflate the COBS 2.3A regime with non-MiFID commission rules.
- **The general prohibition (COBS 2.3A.5R) has three exceptions:** (a) quality-enhancing benefits meeting all four conditions in 2.3A.8R, with pre-service disclosure; (b) operational payments necessary for service delivery that cannot create conflicts; (c) COBS 2.3B research. These exceptions do not apply to the UK retail ban (COBS 2.3A.15R).
- **The UK retail ban (COBS 2.3A.15R) is absolute for independent advice, restricted advice and portfolio management to UK retail clients.** No assessment of quality enhancement, no discretion on whether to retain. The only permitted categories are acceptable minor NMBs (COBS 2.3A.19R) and COBS 2.3B research.
- **The professional/overseas retail rule (COBS 2.3A.16R) requires transfer-back, not just non-acceptance.** The firm must accept and then transfer all received fees/commissions/monetary benefits to the client as soon as reasonably possible, implement a policy to ensure this, and report transfers to the client through periodic statements.
- **The quality enhancement test (COBS 2.3A.8R) is four cumulative conditions and ongoing.** All four must be met and continue to be met for as long as the firm pays or receives the inducement. A one-time assessment at the point of arrangement is insufficient.
- **The IBIP volume/value accelerator test (COBS 2.3A.9AR(3)(f)) is a presumptive prohibition.** Any variable or contingent threshold unlocking additional payments based on sales volume or value is a strong indicator of detrimental impact. Relevant for all fintech insurance distribution platforms.
- **Record keeping (COBS 2.3A.32R) requires a documented internal list and a written explanation of how each item enhances quality.** A list alone is insufficient — the firm must record the link between the inducement and the service quality improvement, and the steps taken not to impair its duty to clients.
- **Breach of COBS 2.3A.5R is automatically also a breach of SYSC 10 (conflicts) and COBS 2.1.1R (best interests) — COBS 2.3A.7E.** The inducements rule is the interface between the conflicts of interest regime and the best execution/best interests regimes; a COBS 2.3A breach triggers cross-consequences.

# CONC 6 — Post-Contractual Requirements

## What This Chapter Covers

CONC 6 sets out obligations that apply after a regulated credit agreement or consumer hire agreement has been entered into — covering the period from conclusion of contract to repayment, including periods of difficulty or renegotiation. The chapter has seven active sections:

| Section | Subject |
|---------|---------|
| **6.1** | Application and scope |
| **6.3** | Disclosure obligations when a current account is overdrawn |
| **6.4** | Appropriation of payments across multiple agreements |
| **6.5** | Assignment of rights to third parties |
| **6.6** | Pawnbroking recordkeeping |
| **6.7** | Post-contract business practices (principal substantive section) |
| **6.8** | Credit broker post-contract disclosure obligations |

CONC 6.7 is the operationally significant section. Its obligations are concentrated in three product categories: **credit cards and store cards**, **retail revolving credit / BNPL**, and **high-cost short-term credit (HCSTC)**.

---

## CONC 6.1 — Application

CONC 6 applies to:
- Consumer credit lenders
- Consumer hiring firms
- Credit brokers (CONC 6.8 only)
- P2P platform operators (in relation to borrowers)

Individual CONC 6.7 rules specify their own narrower scope — most apply only to credit card and retail revolving credit, or only to HCSTC. **(CONC 6.1.1R)**

---

## CONC 6.3 — Disclosure on Overdrawing a Current Account

Where a firm has agreed to provide credit on a current account (i.e., a formal overdraft is in place), and the account balance exceeds the agreed limit or the customer draws on an unarranged facility:

- The firm must **inform the customer promptly** in writing of the fact of the excess, the interest rate applicable, and any charges applied. **(CONC 6.3.1R)**
- If the account remains in that state, the firm must provide this information **at least once a month**. **(CONC 6.3.3R)**

This is a hard disclosure obligation — not a best practice requirement.

---

## CONC 6.4 — Appropriation of Payments

Where a customer has **two or more regulated agreements** with the same firm and makes a payment that is insufficient to satisfy all sums due, the customer has the right under CCA 1974 s.81 to **elect which agreement** the payment should be attributed to. Where the customer does not elect, the firm must apply the payment in accordance with the CCA allocation rules and notify the customer of how the payment was appropriated. **(CONC 6.4.1G–6.4.2G)**

---

## CONC 6.5 — Assignment of Rights

Where a firm **assigns rights** under a regulated agreement to a third party, the customer must be notified. **(CONC 6.5.1R)** The assignee takes on the obligations that follow from those rights; the lender retains responsibility for conduct prior to assignment. See [[conc-debt-recovery-treatment]] for post-assignment lender obligations under CONC 7.12.

---

## CONC 6.6 — Pawnbroking Records

Firms holding goods as pawns must maintain the records required by CCA 1974 ss.114–122, including the pawn receipt and redemption records. **(CONC 6.6.1R–6.6.4R)** These are sector-specific procedural requirements without substantive policy content for general consumer credit.

---

## CONC 6.7 — Post-Contract Business Practices

### 6.7.2R — Pre-Arrears Monitoring Duty

A firm must **monitor customers' repayment records** and must **take appropriate action** where there are signs of actual or possible repayment difficulties. **(CONC 6.7.2R)**

This is a hard rule. It creates a proactive monitoring obligation that sits **before** the CONC 7 arrears framework — the duty to act on signs of difficulty does not begin only when a payment is missed. See [[conc-financial-difficulty-indicators]] for the seven indicators that establish constructive awareness.

For **credit cards and retail revolving credit** specifically, CONC 6.7.3AR imposes an equivalent product-specific monitoring obligation.

### 6.7.3CR — Pre-Arrears Policy Obligation

A firm must **establish, implement and maintain** a clear, effective and appropriate **written policy** for identifying and dealing with customers who show signs of actual or possible financial difficulty — even where no payment has been missed. **(CONC 6.7.3CR)**

This mirrors the arrears and vulnerability policy requirement in CONC 7.2.1R but operates at an earlier point in the customer lifecycle. The policy must be maintained, not just documented: see the policy/practice gap failure mode in [[vulnerable-customers]].

---

### 6.7.4R — Credit Card Repayment Allocation

For credit card and store card agreements, a firm must apply any repayment to **the balance attracting the highest rate of interest first**, before allocating to lower-interest balances. **(CONC 6.7.4R)**

This prevents firms from applying payments to low-interest promotional balances while high-interest debt accumulates.

### 6.7.4BR — Instalment Plan: Best Interests Condition

A firm must not offer a customer a **fixed instalment repayment plan** for a credit card or store card balance unless it has first **concluded that doing so is in the customer's best interests**. **(CONC 6.7.4BR)**

---

### 6.7.5R — Minimum Repayment Floor

For credit cards and store cards, the minimum repayment required from a customer must be an amount that covers, at minimum:

> **All interest + all fees + all charges applied to the account in the period, plus 1% of the amount outstanding.**

**(CONC 6.7.5R)**

The purpose is structural: minimum payment levels that cover only interest without touching principal extend debt indefinitely and increase the total cost of credit. This floor prevents that outcome.

---

### 6.7.7R and 6.7.10R — Credit Limit and Interest Rate Freeze

**Credit limit increases (CONC 6.7.7R):** A firm must not **increase a credit limit** on a credit card or retail revolving credit agreement where:
1. The customer has told the firm they do not wish to have their credit limit increased, **or**
2. The customer is **at risk of financial difficulties**.

**Interest rate increases (CONC 6.7.10R):** A firm must not **increase the interest rate** applicable to a credit card or retail revolving credit agreement where the customer is **at risk of financial difficulties**.

Both are hard rules.

### 6.7.11G — "At Risk of Financial Difficulties" — Defined

CONC 6.7.11G specifies the threshold for "at risk of financial difficulties" as used in CONC 6.7.7R and 6.7.10R:

A customer is at risk of financial difficulties where **one or more** of the following applies:

| Condition | Notes |
|-----------|-------|
| The customer is **in arrears of two or more consecutive payments** | Consecutive — not cumulative |
| The firm has agreed a **repayment arrangement** with the customer | Any formal arrangement, not limited to DMPs |
| The customer is in **serious discussion** about a debt management plan | Pre-DMP discussions count |

This is more precise than the general CONC 1.3.1G financial difficulty indicators — it is a binary threshold for specific product-level protections rather than a trigger list for broader awareness obligations.

### 6.7.8R and 6.7.9R — Automatic Credit Limit Increases

Where a firm proposes to automatically increase a customer's credit limit, it must:
1. Give the customer **advance notice** of the proposed increase. **(CONC 6.7.8R)**
2. Provide an opportunity to **opt out** before the increase takes effect.

The customer must be able to **cancel the increase** within a cooling-off period after notification. **(CONC 6.7.9R)**

---

### 6.7.13R — 60-Day Close-Account Right on Interest Rate Increase

Where a firm proposes to **increase the interest rate** on a credit card or retail revolving credit agreement pursuant to a contractual power to do so, the customer must be given the right to **close the account and repay the existing balance at the existing interest rate** within 60 days of receiving notice of the proposed increase. **(CONC 6.7.13R)**

### 6.7.14R — Valid Reason Requirement for Rate Increases

A firm **must not increase the interest rate** on a credit card or retail revolving credit agreement **unless there is a valid reason** for doing so. **(CONC 6.7.14R)**

Arbitrary or pretextual rate increases are prohibited — the firm must be able to identify and document a genuine commercial or risk-based reason.

---

### 6.7.16AR — BNPL/Promotional Rate Reminder Obligation

For **retail revolving credit and BNPL agreements** not subject to Part 6 of the Payment Services Regulations 2017, where a promotional rate (including a 0% rate) applies and is **conditional on the customer taking specified action** by a deadline:

The firm must **proactively remind** the customer:
- Of the **action the customer needs to take** to retain the promotional rate, and
- The **deadline by which they must act**,

in a medium **appropriate to the customer's communication preferences**, within a **reasonable period before** the deadline. **(CONC 6.7.16AR)**

This is a novel proactive obligation targeted at BNPL and deferred-interest products where customers routinely fail to act before the promotional period ends, resulting in large interest charges crystallising unexpectedly.

### 6.7.16BR — BNPL Partial Repayment Rule

For retail revolving credit and BNPL agreements, the agreement must be structured such that any **partial repayment made on or before a BNPL payment condition date reduces the customer's liability proportionally** — rather than being absorbed into a minimum payment that leaves the BNPL balance intact. **(CONC 6.7.16BR)**

---

### 6.7.17 — Definitions: Refinance and Exercise Forbearance

**Refinance** means to extend the period over which a customer is required to repay a debt under a credit agreement — whether by making a new agreement or by modifying the existing agreement. **(CONC 6.7.17)**

**Exercise forbearance** means to refinance in circumstances where **no additional interest accrues** during the extended period. **(CONC 6.7.17)**

The distinction between refinance and exercise of forbearance is operationally significant: the HCSTC two-refinancing cap (CONC 6.7.23R) does not apply to forbearance exercises; and the CPA two-strike rule count resets on forbearance (CONC 7.6.12R(3)(b)).

### 6.7.18R — No Encouragement of Unsustainable Refinancing

A firm **must not encourage** a customer to refinance if the result would be **commitments the customer cannot sustainably meet**. **(CONC 6.7.18R)**

### 6.7.19R — Refinancing Requires Customer Request or Consent

A firm **must not refinance** a customer's agreement **unless the customer has requested or consented to it**, or unless the firm reasonably believes the refinancing is **not against the customer's best interests**. **(CONC 6.7.19R)**

Unsolicited refinancing that benefits the firm (by extending the repayment period and thereby the interest income) without customer consent is prohibited.

### 6.7.20R — HCSTC Information Sheet Pre-Refinancing

Before refinancing a **high-cost short-term credit** agreement (other than by exercising forbearance), a firm must give the customer the **FCA information sheet** for HCSTC. **(CONC 6.7.20R)**

### 6.7.23R — HCSTC Two-Refinancing Cap

A firm **must not refinance** a high-cost short-term credit agreement (other than by exercising forbearance) on **more than two occasions**. **(CONC 6.7.23R)**

This is the structural counterpart to the CPA two-strike rule in CONC 7.6.12R. Together they close off both collection and rollover as routes to extended HCSTC debt:

| Route to extended HCSTC debt | Hard cap |
|------------------------------|---------|
| Repeat CPA collection attempts | 2 strikes (CONC 7.6.12R) |
| Refinancing | 2 refinances (CONC 6.7.23R) |

Both counts reset on a **forbearance exercise** (no-interest extension).

---

### 6.7.24R — CPA Amendment: Consent and Explanation Required

A firm **must not amend the terms of a CPA** without:
1. Providing the customer with an **adequate explanation** of the reason for and effect of the proposed amendment, and
2. Obtaining the customer's **consent** to the amendment. **(CONC 6.7.24R)**

This rule supplements the pre-conditions for CPA exercise in CONC 7.6.1R. See [[conc-cpa-rules]] for the full CPA framework.

### 6.7.26R — Category Code Requirement for Payment Requests

When presenting a payment request to a payment service provider, a firm must use the **correct category code** for the nature of the transaction. **(CONC 6.7.26R)**

This prevents misclassification of payment requests — for example, characterising a consumer credit repayment as a different transaction type to bypass category-level controls.

---

## CONC 6.8 — Credit Brokers: Post-Contract Disclosure

Credit brokers who have introduced a customer to a lender must make certain **post-contract disclosures** where:
- The broker received a fee or commission from the lender for the introduction, and
- That arrangement was not already disclosed in the broker's pre-contract information.

The broker must disclose: the existence of the fee, its amount or basis of calculation, and any material interest in the lender or credit product. **(CONC 6.8.2R–6.8.4R)**

Failure to disclose gives the customer a right to seek rescission of the credit agreement.

---

## Relationship to CONC 5 and CONC 7

CONC 6 sits between the pre-contract creditworthiness obligations of CONC 5 and the arrears/default framework of CONC 7:

| Chapter | Trigger | Core obligation |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **CONC 5** | Before agreement | Creditworthiness assessment; affordability test |
| **CONC 6** | After agreement, before/at first signs of difficulty | Monitor; pre-arrears policy; product-level protections |
| **CONC 7** | At or in arrears/default | Arrears policies; forbearance; CPA two-strike; interest freeze |

The pre-arrears monitoring duty in CONC 6.7.2R creates a continuous obligation — firms must not wait for a payment to be missed before activating their financial difficulty procedures. The CONC 1.3.1G indicators ([[conc-financial-difficulty-indicators]]) define the constructive knowledge standard that determines when CONC 6 obligations are live.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- **CONC 6.7.2R + 6.7.3CR** are hard rules: firms must actively monitor repayment records and have a written policy for identifying customers showing signs of difficulty — before arrears occur. The policy must be operationally embedded, not only documented.
- **CONC 6.7.5R (minimum repayment floor):** minimum repayments on credit cards and store cards must cover all interest, fees and charges for the period, plus 1% of the outstanding balance. This prevents minimum payment structures that service interest only.
- **CONC 6.7.7R and 6.7.10R:** a firm must not increase a credit limit or interest rate on a credit card/retail revolving credit agreement when the customer is "at risk of financial difficulties" — defined in 6.7.11G as: 2+ consecutive payments in arrears; a repayment arrangement agreed; or in serious DMP discussion.
- **CONC 6.7.16AR (BNPL/promotional rate reminder):** firms must proactively remind customers of actions needed to retain a promotional rate, in an appropriate medium, within a reasonable period before the deadline. This is a proactive obligation — silence is non-compliant.
- **CONC 6.7.23R (HCSTC two-refinancing cap):** HCSTC may not be refinanced (other than by forbearance) more than twice. Pairs with CONC 7.6.12R CPA two-strike rule to eliminate both rollover mechanisms.
- **Forbearance exercises are excluded** from both the refinancing cap and the CPA two-strike count. A no-interest extension (forbearance) resets both counters.

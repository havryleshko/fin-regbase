# SUP 3 — Auditors

## What SUP 3 Does

SUP 3 is the FCA Supervision sourcebook chapter that governs the relationship between a firm and its external auditor. Its purposes are to:

1. Ensure every in-scope firm maintains a qualified, independent auditor at all times.
2. Give the auditor the access and legal protection needed to discharge its statutory functions.
3. Create a direct reporting channel between the auditor and the regulator, independent of the firm.
4. For CASS 6/7 firms specifically, require an annual client assets report and empanel the auditor as a regulatory surveillance mechanism.

SUP 3 operates in conjunction with the firm's statutory audit obligations under the Companies Act 2006 (Part 16). The FCA requirements in SUP 3 are additional to, and in some respects stricter than, the Companies Act regime.

---

## Application

### SUP 3.1.2R — Scope Table

SUP 3 applies to a wide range of FCA-authorised firms. The obligation set varies by firm category:

- **SUP 3.2–3.9** (appointment, qualification, independence, cooperation, notification, rights and duties) apply broadly across all firm types listed in the SUP 3.1.2R table.
- **SUP 3.10** (client assets notification and report) and **SUP 3.11** (review of that report) apply specifically to firms that are subject to CASS 6 (custody rules) or CASS 7 (client money rules), including CASS 7 large firms.

The application table row at SUP 3.1.2R(5B) and rows (7)–(7D) and (10) cover the CASS 6 and CASS 7 firm types that must comply with SUP 3.10 and SUP 3.11.

## Appointment Obligation — The Five-Part Rule

### SUP 3.3.2R

A firm must:

1. **Appoint an eligible auditor** — an individual or firm that meets the qualification requirements in SUP 3.4 (registered statutory auditor with appropriate skills/experience for the firm's regulated activities).
2. **Notify the regulator without delay** of any vacancy, using the form at SUP 15 Annex 3.
3. **Fill the vacancy** — a vacancy must not be allowed to persist.
4. **Ensure the replacement auditor can take office** as soon as reasonably practicable after the vacancy arises.
5. **Notify the regulator of the new appointment**, providing the auditor's name, address, and the effective date of appointment.

### SUP 3.3.7R — Regulator's Backstop Power

If a firm fails to appoint a replacement within **28 days** of a vacancy arising, the appropriate regulator may itself appoint an auditor for the firm. This backstop power exists because audit continuity is a regulatory, not merely commercial, obligation. The regulator's appointment cannot be frustrated by inaction on the firm's part.

## Independence

### Dual-Track Structure

Independence obligations run on two separate tracks, each independently enforceable:

**Firm-side (SUP 3.5.2R / 3.5.3R):**
- The firm must take reasonable steps to ensure the auditor is and remains independent of the firm and able to form an objective view.
- If the firm cannot achieve auditor independence within a reasonable period, it must notify the FCA (and the PRA, if dual-regulated).

**Auditor-side (SUP 3.8.5R / 3.8.6R):**
- The auditor itself has an independent obligation to ensure it remains independent.
- Where a conflict of interest arises, the auditor must take appropriate action (which may include resignation or qualified reporting).

The two tracks are not alternatives. A firm that discovers an independence failure cannot cure it simply by notifying the FCA — the auditor must also take its own action under SUP 3.8.6R. Equally, an auditor that identifies an independence conflict cannot rely on the firm to resolve it.

## Cooperation with the Auditor — SUP 3.6.1R

### The Hard Rule

A firm must provide its auditor with all access and cooperation necessary for the auditor to discharge its functions. This includes:

- Access to the firm's books, accounts, and documents **at all times**.
- Access to any officer, employee, agent, or appointed representative of the firm.

### Outsourcing and Appointed Representatives — SUP 3.6.4R

Where a firm has outsourced activities to a third party, or has appointed representatives or tied agents, those arrangements must include an obligation for the third party / AR to give the auditor **the same access as the firm itself must give**. A firm cannot use outsourcing to insulate part of its operations from audit scrutiny.

### s.346 FSMA 2000 — Criminal Offence

**Knowingly or recklessly** providing false or misleading information to an auditor who is acting in discharge of their functions under FSMA is a **criminal offence** under s.346 FSMA 2000. This is one of the few criminal-offence provisions directly relevant to day-to-day regulatory compliance and applies to any officer or employee of the firm — not only to the firm itself.

## Principle 11 and Qualified Reports — SUP 3.7.2G

A firm should consider whether it is required to notify the FCA under **Principle 11** (be open and cooperative; disclose anything the FCA would reasonably expect to know) when:

- Its auditor qualifies the firm's annual financial statements, or
- Its auditor communicates concerns about the firm's internal controls to management.

A qualified audit opinion or an internal controls management letter is a material event. Firms that receive such communications should assess their Principle 11 and SUP 15.3 notification obligations immediately — the FCA's expectation is that a firm does not wait for the auditor's formal report before notifying if the underlying facts are already known.

## Auditor's Duties to the Regulator

### Cooperation with the FCA — SUP 3.8.2R

The auditor (not just the firm) must cooperate with the FCA in the discharge of the FCA's functions under FSMA. This is an obligation on the audit firm itself — it cannot be overridden by client confidentiality or the firm's instructions.

### Skilled Person Assistance — SUP 3.8.4R

Where the FCA appoints a skilled person under s.166 FSMA to conduct a review, the auditor must give the skilled person **all reasonable assistance**, including access to documents, records, and working papers **(FSMA s.166(5))**. This creates a direct collaboration channel between the statutory audit and the s.166 review process — the two instruments are designed to work in tandem.

### Direct Notification Right — SUP 3.8.10G / FSMA ss.342(5) and 343(5)

An auditor is **legally permitted to communicate directly with the FCA**, without the firm's consent, where the auditor:

- Becomes aware of matters during the course of the audit that are relevant to client assets protection, or
- Is satisfied that reporting to the firm would be ineffective or inappropriate.

This statutory right (FSMA ss.342(5) and 343(5)) means the auditor can go direct to the regulator and is protected from any breach of duty or defamation claim for doing so. The firm cannot contractually exclude or restrict this right.

## SUP 3.10 — Client Assets Notification and Report

### Scope

SUP 3.10 applies to every auditor appointed by a firm that is subject to CASS 6 or CASS 7. It creates obligations on the **auditor** (not solely on the firm) with respect to client assets.

### Annual Client Assets Report

The auditor must produce an **annual client assets report** to the firm. This is the report referenced in CASS 6.6.58G as required by firms holding client assets: the source rule is SUP 3.10, not CASS 6 itself. The report is a reasonable assurance engagement on whether the firm's systems and controls for client assets compliance were suitably designed and operating effectively during the period.

### Proactive Notification Obligation

The auditor must notify the FCA (and, where relevant, the PRA) **without delay** if, in the course of the audit:

- The auditor becomes aware that the firm has failed to comply with any rule relating to the protection of client assets, or
- The auditor reasonably concludes that the firm may be about to do so.

This obligation fires independently of whether the firm itself has notified the FCA. The auditor does not need to wait for the firm's annual report submission window — the obligation is triggered by discovery.

---

## SUP 3.11 — Review of the Client Assets Report

Once the firm receives the annual client assets report from its auditor under SUP 3.10, the firm must:

- **Review the report's findings** and consider whether any remedial action is required.
- **Address any identified deficiencies** in systems and controls.
- **Retain the report** as part of the CASS compliance records.

SUP 3.11 closes the loop: the auditor's report is not merely informational — it triggers a firm-side review obligation. Where the report identifies weaknesses, those weaknesses must be assessed in light of the firm's obligations under CASS 1A.3 (oversight function duties), CASS 6.6, and CASS 7.15.

## Relationship to Other Regimes

**CASS 6.6.58G:** References the auditor's client assets report required under SUP 3.10. CASS 6.6.58G is the client-asset rule that directs firms to the SUP 3 audit requirement; SUP 3.10 is the source of the obligation.

**s.166 FSMA Skilled Person Reviews:** The skilled person regime and the statutory audit are complementary. The FCA typically uses a s.166 skilled person review where it wants more granular assurance than the statutory audit provides, or where it has concerns about the auditor's independence. SUP 3.8.4R ensures the auditor must cooperate with any skilled person appointed.

**SM&CR:** The CASS oversight function (CASS 1A.3.1A R) is the internal governance counterpart to the external audit requirement. The CASS oversight officer is responsible for reviewing the annual client assets report and reporting to the governing body. See [[cass1a-firm-classification-oversight]] and [[smcr-certification-regime]].

**Principle 11 / SUP 15:** Qualified audit opinions and management letters about internal controls are Principle 11 notification triggers. See [[sysc4-governance-framework]].

## Key Points for Agents

- **Five-part appointment obligation; 28-day backstop (SUP 3.3.2R; 3.3.7R).** The firm must appoint, notify the regulator of any vacancy, fill the vacancy, enable the replacement to take office promptly, and notify of the new appointment. If the firm fails within 28 days, the regulator may appoint.
- **s.346 FSMA criminal offence for false information to auditor.** Knowingly or recklessly giving false or misleading information to the auditor in the course of its functions is a criminal offence — not merely a regulatory breach.
- **Cooperation is unconditional; outsourcing does not limit access (SUP 3.6.1R; 3.6.4R).** Full access to books, records, officers, and employees at all times. Outsourcing arrangements must contractually extend the same access to the auditor.
- **Independence is a dual-track obligation (SUP 3.5.2R/3.5.3R firm-side; SUP 3.8.5R/3.8.6R auditor-side).** Both the firm and the auditor independently must maintain and police independence. Firm notification to the FCA does not substitute for the auditor's own action.
- **Auditor can notify the FCA directly without the firm's consent (SUP 3.8.10G; FSMA ss.342(5)/343(5)).** The auditor has a statutory right — and in practice a duty — to go direct to the regulator where client assets or other regulatory concerns arise. The firm cannot block this.
- **Annual client assets report is a regulatory obligation, not a contractual extra (SUP 3.10).** For all CASS 6/7 firms, the auditor must produce an annual reasonable assurance report on client assets systems. This is the source of the CASS 6.6.58G audit requirement. Firms must review findings and address deficiencies (SUP 3.11).

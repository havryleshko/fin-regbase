# CONC 2 — Conduct of Business Standards: General

## What This Document Is

CONC 2 contains the general conduct of business standards that apply across consumer credit activity types. Unlike CONC 4–7, which focus on specific transactional stages, CONC 2 sets baseline obligations governing how firms treat customers and present themselves across all channels and all stages of the credit relationship. The April 2026 version covers: general principles (2.2), lenders' conduct (2.3), credit references (2.4), credit broking conduct (2.5), HCSTC price comparison websites (2.5A), debt counselling and adjusting (2.6), distance marketing (2.7), e-commerce (2.8), unsolicited credit tokens (2.9), mental capacity guidance (2.10), and remuneration/performance management (2.11).

---

## CONC 2.2 — General Principles

### Principle 6 and Misleading Names

Firms must have regard to Principle 6 — acting with due regard to the interests of customers and treating them fairly **(CONC 2.2.1R)**. A firm must not use a name, description, or title that is likely to mislead a customer into thinking that the firm is not involved in consumer credit activity **(CONC 2.2.3R)**.

### Active Election for Optional Products

A firm must not treat a customer as having actively elected to receive or take an optional additional product, service, or feature unless the customer has taken a positive, deliberate step to elect to receive or take that additional product, service, or feature **(CONC 2.2.6R)**.

Two things that do **not** constitute active election **(CONC 2.2.6R)**:
- A pre-ticked box (ticking a box on the customer's behalf)
- An omission — failing to untick a box or take a negative step

This rule applies to all optional add-ons including insurance products, debt protection features, and any other ancillary services offered alongside a credit agreement.

## CONC 2.3 — Lenders' Conduct

### Credit Card Cheques

A lender must not send credit card cheques to a customer except where the customer has made a prior request for them **(CONC 2.3.2R)**. For each customer request, the lender may send cheques only once in response to that request and may not send more than three cheques in response to any single request **(CONC 2.3.2R)**.

### Verification of Broker Authorisation

A lender must take reasonable steps to verify that a credit broker who introduces a customer to the lender is authorised to carry on that credit broking activity **(CONC 2.3.4R)**. A lender that receives an introduction from an unauthorised broker acts at regulatory risk.

## CONC 2.4 — Credit References

### Disclosure of Credit Reference Agency on Decline

Where a firm declines a customer's application for credit, in whole or in part, on the basis of information from a credit reference agency, the firm must promptly inform the customer that this is the case and give them the name of the credit reference agency that was consulted **(CONC 2.4.2R)**. This enables the customer to contact the CRA and, if necessary, challenge the accuracy of the data.

### Quotation Searches

A firm should ensure that quotation searches — carried out when considering whether to enter into a credit agreement without the customer having made a formal application — do not leave a footprint on the customer's credit file that could adversely affect their credit rating **(CONC 2.4.3G)**. Full application searches that leave a hard footprint should only be conducted when the customer has made a formal application.

## CONC 2.5 — Credit Broking Conduct

### Positive Obligations (CONC 2.5.3R)

Credit brokers must act honestly, fairly, and professionally in accordance with the best interests of their customers **(CONC 2.5.3R)**. This includes obligations to: not pressure customers; explain costs clearly; ensure introductions are appropriate; and have regard to the customer's financial circumstances and the suitability of the credit they are introducing.

### Unfair Business Practices — 22-Item Catalogue (CONC 2.5.8R)

CONC 2.5.8R prohibits a catalogue of specific unfair practices by credit brokers. Named prohibitions include (among others):

- Contacting customers who are registered with the Telephone Preference Service or via automated unsolicited calls
- Making calls or visits at inconvenient times or in a way that causes distress or embarrassment
- Using or directing customers to premium rate telephone numbers for complaints or queries, or conducting complaint calls exceeding 15 minutes on such lines
- Securing a higher credit amount, a worse rate of interest, or a preferred lender solely for the benefit of the broker's own financial interest rather than the customer's
- Pressurising customers into taking add-on insurance or other products they have not asked for
- Encouraging customers into consolidation loans that are against the customer's financial interests
- Arranging or facilitating unsustainable refinancing
- Extending credit to customers in a way that is harmful to them
- Charging a wrong-type introduction fee (e.g. a credit broker charging a fee when the arrangement is actually a debt-adjusting engagement) without the customer's informed consent
- Accessing a customer's payment account to take a fee without having obtained the customer's authority to do so
- Passing customer data to firms not authorised or entitled to receive it

See **[[conc-broker-unfair-practices]]** for the full treatment of the catalogue.

## CONC 2.5A — HCSTC Price Comparison Websites

### Application

CONC 2.5A applies to firms operating a price comparison website for high-cost short-term credit (HCSTC) — payday loan and similar short-term products.

### Ranking Prohibition

A firm operating an HCSTC price comparison website **must not** rank HCSTC results based on the commercial or financial interests of the operator or any third party **(CONC 2.5A.2R)**. Rankings must be based on the **total amount payable** by the customer.

### Search Functionality

The firm must enable customers to search and filter results by the amount of credit and the duration of the credit **(CONC 2.5A.3R)**. This ensures customers can compare like-for-like products for their specific borrowing need.

### Lender Coverage

A firm must list all lenders whose HCSTC products it covers (or make clear which lenders it covers and on what basis), so that customers are not misled about the scope of the comparison **(CONC 2.5A.4R)**.

### Separation of Results and Promotions

Search results and financial promotions must be presented in clearly distinguished, separate sections **(CONC 2.5A.5R)**. Lenders who pay for prominence cannot appear within the ranked results section.

## CONC 2.6 — Debt Counselling and Debt Adjusting

### Privacy Notice

A debt counsellor or debt adjuster must provide a privacy notice to the customer explaining how the firm will use the customer's data **(CONC 2.6 general)**. This must be provided before or at the start of the engagement.

### Unfair Practices

Firms carrying on debt counselling or debt adjusting must not **(CONC 2.6.6R)**:
- Pressure customers into purchasing products or services they have not asked for
- Exploit customer ignorance about their legal rights or financial situation
- Make visits to the customer's home or contact the customer at unreasonable times or in a manner causing distress

### Canvassing Off Trade Premises — Criminal Offence

A firm must not canvass debt counselling or debt adjusting services off trade premises **(CONC 2.6.5R)**. Canvassing debt counselling or debt adjusting off trade premises is a criminal offence under section 154 of the Consumer Credit Act 1974. This prohibition applies to all forms of unsolicited approaches to potential customers at their home or other non-trade premises.

### Call Duration Guidance

In relation to debt counselling or adjusting **(CONC 2.6.10G)**:
- It is unlikely to be reasonable for a call to exceed one hour
- Where a call longer than 15 minutes is necessary to provide the service, the firm should ensure it is not made on a premium rate number
- Status check calls should not exceed five minutes

## CONC 2.7 — Distance Marketing

### Application

CONC 2.7 applies to firms carrying on any distance marketing activity from an establishment in the UK with or for a consumer in the UK **(CONC 2.7.1R)**. It does not apply to consumer hire agreements or to authorised professional firms with respect to their non-mainstream regulated activities.

### Distance Marketing Disclosure — CONC 2 Annex 1

Before a consumer is bound by a distance contract or offer, the firm must provide the distance marketing information set out in CONC 2 Annex 1 in good time **(CONC 2.7.2R)**. The Annex 1 disclosure covers 20 items in four categories: information about the firm (identity, address, FSR number, regulatory status); information about the financial service (product description, total price, additional costs, limitations); information about the contract (cancellation rights and duration, early termination terms); and information about redress (FOS access, FSCS).

The disclosure must be **(CONC 2.7.3R)**:
- Clear and comprehensible
- Appropriate to the means of distance communication used
- Made in good faith and with due regard to the principles of good faith in commercial transactions

### Voice Telephony Exception — Abbreviated Disclosure

Where the firm makes a voice telephony call to the consumer, only the abbreviated distance marketing information set out in CONC 2 Annex 2 (6 items: firm identity, product description, total price, other costs, cancellation rights, and availability of further information) needs to be provided during that call, provided the consumer explicitly consents to receiving only that information **(CONC 2.7.11R)**. The firm must still provide the full Annex 1 disclosure in a durable medium accessible to the consumer in good time before they are bound.

### Durable Medium Requirement

Before the consumer is bound by any distance contract or offer, the firm must communicate all contractual terms and conditions and the Annex 1 disclosure information in a **durable medium** **(CONC 2.7.6R)**. Performance of the contract may only begin after the consumer has given approval **(CONC 2.7.8R)**.

### Consumer Rights — Paper Copies and Means of Communication

At any time during the contractual relationship, the consumer is entitled to receive the contractual terms and conditions on paper, at request **(CONC 2.7.14R)**. The consumer is also entitled to change the means of distance communication used, unless this is incompatible with the contract or the nature of the service.

### Unsolicited Services

A firm must not enforce, or seek to enforce, any obligations under a distance contract in the event of an unsolicited supply of services **(CONC 2.7.15R)**. The absence of a reply does not constitute consent.

### Mandatory Nature of Consumer Rights

If a consumer purports to waive any right created or implied by CONC 2.7, a firm must not accept that waiver, nor seek to rely on or enforce it **(CONC 2.7.16R)**. The distance marketing protections are non-waivable.

## CONC 2.8 — E-Commerce

### Firm Identification Obligations

A firm carrying on electronic commerce activity must make the following information easily, directly, and permanently accessible **(CONC 2.8.2R)**:
- Its name and geographic address
- Contact details enabling rapid, direct communication (including email address)
- Statutory status disclosure (GEN 4 Annex 1), including a statement that it is on the Financial Services Register with its firm reference number
- Where applicable: professional body, professional title, professional rules reference, and VAT number

### Price Clarity

If a firm refers to price, it must do so clearly and unambiguously, indicating whether the price is inclusive of tax and delivery costs **(CONC 2.8.3R)**.

### Commercial Communications

Commercial communications (including promotional offers and competitions) that are part of an information society service must **(CONC 2.8.4R)**:
- Be clearly identifiable as commercial communications
- Clearly identify the person on whose behalf they are made
- Have promotion conditions (qualification/participation) easily accessible and presented clearly and unambiguously

Unsolicited commercial communications sent by email by a UK-established firm must be identifiable as unsolicited as soon as they are received **(CONC 2.8.5R)**.

### Order Placement Requirements

Before an ECA recipient places an order, the firm must clearly, comprehensibly, and unambiguously provide **(CONC 2.8.6R)**:
- The technical steps required to conclude the contract
- Whether the concluded contract will be filed and accessible
- The means for identifying and correcting input errors
- The languages in which the contract can be concluded

The firm must also acknowledge receipt of the recipient's order without undue delay and by electronic means. Contractual terms and conditions must be provided in a form that allows the recipient to store and reproduce them **(CONC 2.8.8R)**.

## CONC 2.9 — Unsolicited Credit Tokens

A firm must not give a person a credit token (credit card, store card, or similar instrument) unless that person has asked for it **(CONC 2.9.2R)**. The request must be contained in a document signed by the person, unless the credit-token agreement is a small borrower-lender-supplier agreement **(CONC 2.9.2R(2))**.

**Exceptions** — the prohibition does not apply to giving a credit token **(CONC 2.9.2R(3))**:
- For use under a credit-token agreement already in existence; or
- In renewal or replacement of a previously accepted credit token under a credit-token agreement that continues in force (whether or not varied)

The renewal/replacement exception covers normal card renewals (replacing an expiring card). Sending an additional or different card, or giving a card to a new person, requires a signed request.

## CONC 2.10 — Mental Capacity Guidance

See **[[conc-mental-capacity-guidance]]** for the full treatment. Summary of core obligations:

- **Presumption of capacity (CONC 2.10.4G):** A firm must assume a customer has mental capacity unless the firm knows or reasonably suspects otherwise.
- **9 indicators of limitation (CONC 2.10.8G):** Specific behavioural, informational, and communication indicators that should trigger reasonable suspicion — including the customer not appearing to understand what they are applying for, inability to retain or weigh up information, behaviour out of character, third-party concerns raised, or inability to communicate a decision.
- **Duty to assist (CONC 2.10.5G):** Where suspicion arises, the firm must take reasonable steps to assist the customer to make a decision before treating them as lacking capacity. Exclusion is the last resort, not the first response.
- **High scrutiny on creditworthiness (CONC 2.10.18G–2.10.19G):** Where a mental capacity limitation is suspected, the firm must apply a high level of scrutiny to the creditworthiness assessment and should not place over-reliance on information provided by the customer.
- **Unsuitable credit prohibition (CONC 2.10.20G):** A firm must not provide credit it knows, or reasonably believes, to be unsuitable to the customer's needs — even where that credit would be affordable.

## CONC 2.11 — Remuneration and Performance Management

### Application

CONC 2.11 applies to firms with respect to credit-related regulated activities and unregulated activities financed by credit agreements where the firm is a consumer credit lender or credit broker **(CONC 2.11.1R)**. It does not apply to firms already subject to SYSC 19B (AIFM) or SYSC 19G (MIFIDPRU) remuneration codes, or to remuneration provisions under CRD, AIFMD, UCITS, or MiFID made by an EEA regulator **(CONC 2.11.2R)**.

### Purpose

CONC 2.11 amplifies Principle 3 (management and control) and SYSC 4.1.1R (systems and controls) **(CONC 2.11.3G)**. It addresses the risk that a firm's remuneration or performance management policies for employees, appointed representatives, and individual agents may drive behaviours that breach regulatory requirements — in particular where:
- Staff bonuses or commissions are determined in whole or in part by the volume or value of credit provided or debt collected
- Appraisals or day-to-day management focus on volume or value of credit or debt targets
- Incentives apply to unregulated activities (e.g. sale of goods) financed by credit agreements where the firm is also lender or broker

### Hard Rule — CONC 2.11.4R

A firm **must**, in relation to any risk of failure to comply with its regulatory obligations arising from its remuneration or performance management policies, procedures, and practices:
1. Establish, implement, and maintain adequate **policies and procedures designed to detect** that risk; and
2. Put in place adequate **measures and procedures designed to manage** that risk.

The depth of compliance required must take into account the nature, scale, and complexity of the firm's business and activities **(CONC 2.11.4R(2))**.

### Examples of Measures and Procedures (CONC 2.11.5G)

- Monitoring the nature of sales activities and debt collection
- Collecting management information to identify trends or patterns in employee/agent behaviour
- Establishing procedures for appropriate action if an employee or agent is found to have behaved inappropriately
- Maintaining governance oversight and regular review of remuneration arrangements by a senior committee or senior management

### Manager Conflicts of Interest (CONC 2.11.6G)

Where an employee's or agent's activities are monitored by a manager whose own remuneration is affected by the volume or value of the sales or debt collected by that team member, any potential conflicts of interest that arise should be adequately managed.

## Key Points for Agents

- Pre-ticked boxes and negative-option defaults do not constitute active election for optional products (CONC 2.2.6R). A positive deliberate action by the customer is required.
- Credit brokers face a 22-item unfair practices catalogue (CONC 2.5.8R). Key hard prohibitions: contacting TPS-registered customers; securing more credit or a worse rate for own benefit; accessing payment accounts without authorisation; passing data to unauthorised firms.
- HCSTC PCW operators must rank results by total amount payable — not by commercial interests — and must separate search results from promotions (CONC 2.5A).
- Canvassing debt counselling or debt adjusting off trade premises is a criminal offence under CCA s.154.
- For distance contracts: full Annex 1 disclosure must be in a durable medium before the consumer is bound. Voice telephony allows abbreviated Annex 2 during the call, but Annex 1 must still follow in durable medium. Consumer rights under CONC 2.7 cannot be waived.
- Firms must not send credit tokens unless the person has signed a request document. Normal card renewals are exempt.
- Mental capacity: presume capacity; assist before excluding; apply high scrutiny to creditworthiness; do not provide credit known to be unsuitable even if affordable.
- Volume-based remuneration and debt collection incentives create CONC 2.11.4R obligations. The firm must have policies to detect and manage this risk, with governance-level oversight.

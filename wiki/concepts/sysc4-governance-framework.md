# SYSC 4 Governance Framework — Overview

## The Two-Track Structure

SYSC 4 operates on two tracks depending on the type of firm:

| Firm type | Governance track |
|-----------|-----------------|
| **Common platform firms** (MiFID investment firms, management companies, full-scope UK AIFMs) | SYSC 4.1.1R baseline + SYSC 4.1.1-AR detailed requirements + SYSC 4.3A management body rules |
| **All other FCA-authorised firms** | SYSC 4.1.1R baseline + SYSC 4.4 apportionment rules |

Both tracks share the baseline governance Rule **(SYSC 4.1.1R)**. The tracks diverge at the management/oversight layer: common platform firms have the structured management body obligations; all other SMCR firms have the apportionment obligation.

---

## The Baseline Governance Rule (SYSC 4.1.1R — All Firms)

The baseline Rule requires every authorised firm to have **robust governance arrangements** comprising **(SYSC 4.1.1R)**:

1. **Clear organisational structure** — with well-defined, transparent and consistent lines of responsibility
2. **Effective risk processes** — to identify, manage, monitor and report the risks it is or might be exposed to
3. **Internal control mechanisms** — including sound administrative and accounting procedures
4. **Information processing safeguards** — effective control and safeguard arrangements for information processing systems

This is not a "should" provision — it is a hard Rule applicable to all firms regardless of size or regulatory classification. Proportionality applies to how the Rule is met, not to whether it applies.

---

## Common Platform Track: Management Body

### The Seven-Function Mandate (SYSC 4.3A.1R)

For common platform firms, the management body must:
1. Have overall responsibility for the firm
2. Approve/oversee strategic objectives, risk strategy and internal governance
3. Ensure accounting/financial reporting integrity
4. Oversee disclosure and communications
5. Provide effective oversight of senior management
6. Monitor and periodically assess strategic objective adequacy, governance effectiveness, and client service policy adequacy
7. Have adequate access to information for oversight and monitoring

### Chair/CEO Separation (SYSC 4.3A.2R)

The chair of the management body cannot simultaneously hold the CEO function (SMF1) or the chief executive function within the same firm. This is a hard Rule — waivable only by FCA application under s.138A FSMA. It is not a default "best practice" requirement.

### Two Persons + Two Independent Minds (SYSC 4.2.2R / 4.2.4G)

Management must be undertaken by at least two persons. The FCA's operational test: **at least two independent minds must be applied to formulation and implementation of all significant policies**. A single dominant individual in a two-person team raises doubts about compliance regardless of titles. Both individuals must play a part in all significant decisions and influence strategy and general direction.

## Non-Common Platform Track: Apportionment

### The Apportionment Obligation (SYSC 4.4.3R)

Every non-common platform SMCR firm must maintain a clear and appropriate apportionment of significant responsibilities among directors and senior managers so that it is clear who holds each responsibility and the business can be adequately monitored and controlled.

### Two Mandatory Allocated Functions (SYSC 4.4.5R)

Two specific functions must be allocated to named individuals:
- **Function 1**: Dealing with the apportionment of significant responsibilities
- **Function 2**: Overseeing the establishment and maintenance of systems and controls

For most firms, both functions must be allocated to the **chief executive** (or, for group companies, a group director/senior manager of equivalent or greater seniority). The FCA considers allocation to one or two individuals appropriate for most firms.

This is the non-Enhanced-SMCR equivalent of the SM&CR Responsibilities Map (SYSC 25). Enhanced firms have the full Responsibilities Map obligation; all other non-common platform SMCR firms have the SYSC 4.4 obligation.

---

## The Compliance and Financial Crime Layer (SYSC 6.1.1R)

Beneath the governance structure sits the compliance obligation: a firm must have adequate policies and procedures to ensure compliance with the regulatory system **and** to counter the risk of being used to further financial crime **(SYSC 6.1.1R)**. Both elements are mandatory for all FCA-authorised firms — not just AML-supervised or financially-regulated firms.

For common platform firms, this escalates to:
- A **permanent, independent compliance function** with four defined responsibilities (monitoring, advising, annual management body report, complaints oversight) **(SYSC 6.1.3-AR)*
- A **compliance officer** appointed by the management body, with independence protections for remuneration and removal
- An **annual report to the management body** on the control environment, identified risks, and complaints remediation

## How SYSC 4 Sits Within the Broader SYSC Architecture

| SYSC chapter | Subject | Relationship to SYSC 4 |
|-------------|---------|----------------------|
| SYSC 4 | Governance and organisational requirements | Foundation |
| SYSC 6 | Compliance function, internal audit, AML | Sits on top of SYSC 4; uses SYSC 4 governance as the vehicle for compliance and AML obligations |
| SYSC 9 | Record-keeping | Operated by the governance structure established under SYSC 4 |
| SYSC 10 | Conflicts of interest | Governed by the oversight mechanisms established under SYSC 4 |
| SYSC 15A | Operational resilience | Extends the BCP/systems elements of SYSC 4.1 into important business services |
| SYSC 25 | Responsibilities Map (Enhanced firms) | Replaces SYSC 4.4 apportionment for Enhanced SMCR firms |

## Key Points for Agents

- **SYSC 4.1.1R is a hard Rule for all firms** — not guidance, not proportionate to firm type. Every authorised firm must have clear lines of responsibility, effective risk processes, internal controls, and information system safeguards.
- **Common platform firms have the management body seven-function mandate (SYSC 4.3A.1R) and a hard chair/CEO separation Rule (SYSC 4.3A.2R).** The annual governance assessment by the management body — including assessment of governance effectiveness and client service policy adequacy — is mandatory.
- **For all other SMCR firms, the two mandatory allocated functions (apportionment + systems/controls oversight) must go to the chief executive or equivalent (SYSC 4.4.5R).** This is not optional and not satisfiable by general board-level oversight without a named individual.
- **The compliance obligation in SYSC 6.1.1R has two distinct limbs: regulatory compliance AND financial crime prevention.** Both apply to all FCA-authorised firms. For common platform firms, the compliance function must make an annual report to the management body, and removal of the compliance officer requires majority management body approval including a majority of NEDs.
- **SYSC 4 and SYSC 6 are the governance infrastructure beneath SM&CR.** SM&CR (SYSC 25 for Enhanced firms; SYSC 4.4 for others) allocates accountability. SYSC 4.1.1R and SYSC 6.1.1R establish the underlying systems and controls that those accountable individuals are responsible for maintaining.

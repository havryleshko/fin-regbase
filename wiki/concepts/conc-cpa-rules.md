# CONC — Continuous Payment Authority Rules

## What This Covers

A **continuous payment authority (CPA)** is an authority granted by a customer to a firm enabling the firm to instruct a payment service provider to take payments from the customer's payment account — typically a debit card or bank account — without the customer re-authorising each payment. CPAs are distinct from standing orders or direct debits: the firm, not the customer, controls the timing and amount of each request.

CONC 7.6 regulates the exercise of CPAs in the context of consumer credit and P2P agreements. The rules are hard obligations except where marked as guidance. Note: CONC 7.6 (and CONC 7.1.3A R) applies to regulated credit agreements **and** to credit agreements that are exempt agreements under article 60C(4A) RAO — widening its reach to certain buy-now-pay-later products **(CONC 7.1.3A R)**.

---

## Pre-Conditions for Exercise

A firm **must not** exercise its rights under a CPA (or purport to do so) unless **(CONC 7.6.1R)**:
1. It has been **explained to the customer** that the CPA would be used in the way in question, and
2. The exercise is **in accordance with the terms** specified in the credit or P2P agreement.

If the firm wishes to **change the terms** of a CPA, it must contact the customer, provide an adequate explanation of the reason and effect of the proposed change, and **obtain the customer's consent** before the change takes effect **(CONC 7.6.1R(2))**.

Where the agreement does not provide for a CPA but the firm proposes to grant one (e.g. to set up a forbearance arrangement), the firm must: explain why the CPA is proposed, provide an adequate explanation of its terms and operation, give that information in a durable medium, and give the customer a reasonable opportunity to consider before they consent **(CONC 7.6.2A R)**.

---

## Conduct Prohibitions — General CPA Exercise

A firm **should not** request a PSP to make a CPA payment **(CONC 7.6.2G)**:
1. Where the amount or basis is not specified in the credit or P2P agreement, unless the firm has complied with CONC 7.6.1R(2) (consent to change).
2. To recover default fees or other sums unless those amounts were specified in the agreement and referred to in the pre-contractual explanation.
3. For a partial amount (less than full sum due) before making reasonable attempts to collect the full amount on the due date — except under the HCSTC instalment rules (CONC 7.6.14R).
4. Before the due date, unless the firm has complied with CONC 7.6.1R(2).
5. After the due date at a date, period, or frequency other than specified in the agreement, unless consent obtained.
6. From a third party's payment account other than as specifically agreed by the third party.

---

## Core Conduct Obligation: Proportionality and Forbearance

**CONC 7.6.3R** is a hard rule with two elements:
1. A firm **must** exercise CPA rights in a manner that is **reasonable, proportionate and not excessive**.
2. A firm **must** exercise **appropriate forbearance** if it becomes aware that the customer **is or may be experiencing financial difficulties**.

Factors that determine whether exercise is reasonable and proportionate include **(CONC 7.6.4G)**:
- Whether the firm is aware or has reason to believe the customer is in actual or potential financial difficulties which the CPA may exacerbate.
- Whether the customer has been notified of the payment failure and responded to contact from the firm.

---

## Specific Conduct Failures (CONC 7.6.5G)

A firm is likely to contravene CONC 7.6.3R if it:
1. Requests a payment **before income or other funds may reasonably be expected to reach the account** — for example, where the firm knows the customer's salary payment date.
2. Requests payment where it has **reason to believe funds are insufficient** and taking the payment would leave the customer without funds for priority debts or essential living expenses (mortgage, rent, council tax, food, utilities).
3. Requests a **partial payment** before making reasonable attempts to collect the full amount on the due date.
4. Continues CPA exercise for an **unreasonable period after the payment due date** without taking steps to establish the reason for failure.

---

## Financial Difficulty — Mandatory Suspension

**CONC 7.6.7R** is a hard rule — absolute prohibition:

A firm **must not** exercise CPA rights:
1. If the customer provides **reasonable evidence of being in financial difficulties** and cannot afford to repay the debt, **or**
2. Where the firm **otherwise becomes aware** of the customer being in financial difficulties and not being able to afford to repay.

**CONC 7.6.9G** provides the working standard: inability to recover the whole amount due by the **end of the next working day** after the due date is an indicator that the customer may be in financial difficulties. In that case, the firm should suspend CPA exercise until it has made **reasonable efforts to contact the customer** to establish the reason for payment failure and whether the customer is in financial difficulties.

Where a customer informs the firm of financial difficulties, pending evidence, the firm should consider suspending CPA exercise **(CONC 7.6.8G(2))**.

See **[[conc-financial-difficulty-indicators]]** for the seven indicators that may establish financial difficulty.

---

## High-Cost Short-Term Credit — Two-Strike Rule

**CONC 7.6.12R** applies specifically to **high-cost short-term credit (HCSTC)** (payday loans and similar products). It does not apply where the agreement provides for repayment in instalments **(CONC 7.6.12R(6))** — for those, see CONC 7.6.13R.

The rule: a firm **must not** request a PSP to make a CPA payment to collect (in whole or in part) a sum due for HCSTC if it has already done so under the same agreement on **two previous occasions** and those two requests were refused **(CONC 7.6.12R(1))**.

**Counting rules:**
- Where HCSTC has been refinanced (other than as forbearance), the new and old agreements are treated as the **same agreement** for counting purposes **(CONC 7.6.12R(2)(a))**.
- Where the firm exercises forbearance, any refusal that took place before the forbearance was granted is **disregarded** — the count resets **(CONC 7.6.12R(3)(b))**.
- Exception: after two refused attempts, if the firm contacts the customer and the customer gives express consent to further CPA requests following a refinance, the count resets **(CONC 7.6.12R(5))**.

---

## HCSTC Instalment Credit — Two-Strike Rule per Instalment

For HCSTC that provides for repayment in instalments, **CONC 7.6.13R** applies:

After two refused CPA attempts to collect the same specific instalment, the firm must not:
1. Make a further request to collect that specific instalment via CPA.
2. Make a request to collect any other instalment under the agreement,

**unless** the firm contacts the customer, notifies them of the refusals, payment of the relevant instalment has been made by other means (and the customer is not in arrears), and the customer gives express consent to further CPA requests.

---

## No Partial Amounts for HCSTC

**CONC 7.6.14R:** For HCSTC, a firm **must not** request a PSP to collect a sum via CPA if that sum is **less than the full sum due** at the time of the request.

**Exception (CONC 7.6.14R(2)):** Where the firm has refinanced and set up a repayment plan (through a forbearance indulgence), notified the customer of the plan amounts and frequency, and the customer gives express consent, the firm may collect the reduced repayment amounts under the plan.

---

## Guarantors and HCSTC

For HCSTC where a guarantor has provided a guarantee or indemnity, the two-strike rules (CONC 7.6.12R and 7.6.13R) apply **separately** to the CPA granted by the borrower and the CPA granted by the guarantor. This means the firm may make up to two requests to the borrower's CPA and, if those fail, up to two requests to the guarantor's CPA **(CONC 7.6.15A G)**.

---

## Customer's Right to Cancel a CPA

**CONC 7.6.16R:** A firm must not, by any means, improperly or unfairly **inhibit or discourage** a customer from cancelling a CPA. Prohibited conduct includes:
1. Misleading the customer about the right to cancel or how it may be exercised.
2. Failing to respond promptly to requests to amend or cancel the CPA.
3. Intimidating a customer who wishes to cancel.
4. Requiring the customer to go through an unduly complicated process to cancel.

**CONC 7.6.17R:** A firm **must cease** to exercise its rights under a CPA **once it is notified that the CPA has been cancelled**.

---

## Record-Keeping

Firms must keep sufficient records to ascertain that they have complied with all CPA obligations, including: records of payment requests (including refusals), and written or other records of the consents required under CONC 7.6.1R, 7.6.12R, 7.6.13R and 7.6.14R **(CONC 7.6.15G(3))**. SYSC 9.1.1R and 9.1.1AR apply.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- CPA can only be exercised if it has been explained to the customer and is in accordance with the credit agreement terms (CONC 7.6.1R). CPA terms cannot be changed without customer consent.
- For all credit types: CPA must be exercised proportionately. The firm must show appropriate forbearance if it is aware the customer may be in financial difficulties (CONC 7.6.3R). This is a hard rule.
- For all credit types: CPA **must not** be exercised if the customer provides reasonable evidence of financial difficulty and inability to repay, or if the firm otherwise becomes aware (CONC 7.6.7R). Inability to recover the full amount by the next working day is a practical indicator.
- For HCSTC only: after two refused CPA attempts under the same agreement, no further CPA attempt may be made (CONC 7.6.12R). The count resets on forbearance but not on refinance.
- For HCSTC instalments: the two-strike rule applies per instalment — and a block on one instalment also blocks attempts for any other instalment (CONC 7.6.13R).
- Customers have an unconditional right to cancel a CPA. Any process that inhibits, discourages, or delays cancellation is a hard-rule breach (CONC 7.6.16R). Cancellation must be acted on immediately (CONC 7.6.17R).
- Record every CPA request, refusal, and customer consent. These are FCA audit targets (CONC 7.6.15G(3)).

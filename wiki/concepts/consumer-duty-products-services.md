# FCA Consumer Duty — Products and Services Outcome

## The Obligation

> All products and services for consumers must be **fit for purpose** — designed to meet the needs, characteristics and objectives of a defined target group of customers, and distributed appropriately.
> **(PS22/9 §6.1)**

This outcome governs the full lifecycle of product governance: design, approval, marketing, ongoing management and distribution.

## What Firms Must Do

### Manufacturers

Firms that create, develop, design, issue, operate or underwrite a product or service. **(PS22/9 §2.24)**

Must:
- Define a **target market** — the group of customers for whose needs, characteristics and objectives the product or service is designed. **(PS22/9 §6.1)**
- Ensure the product or service is fit for purpose for that target market throughout its lifecycle.
- Test and review the product to confirm it continues to meet target market needs.
- Share **relevant information** with distributors to enable them to comply with the Duty — including the results of value assessments and target market information. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**
- Notify distributors if a problem is identified that requires action.

If a manufacturer identifies a serious problem with a product, it should act promptly — but must consider the customer impact of withdrawal or suspension before acting unilaterally. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**

### Distributors

Firms that offer, sell, recommend, advise on, propose or provide a product or service. **(PS22/9 §2.24)**

Must:
- Distribute only to customers within the manufacturer's identified **target market**, or maintain a consistent distribution strategy. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**
- Understand the target market and the product's purpose, features and risks.
- Share sales and customer data back to manufacturers to support product reviews. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**

### Both Manufacturers and Distributors

- Must consider the needs of customers with **characteristics of vulnerability** within the target market. **(PS22/9 §6.9)**
- Must monitor whether the product or service continues to deliver good outcomes for customers.
- Where product complexity is necessary to deliver features, this is acceptable — but features visible to consumers must be **understandable to the target market**. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**

## Interaction with Existing Rules (PROD)

Firms that comply with the **Product Intervention and Product Governance sourcebook (PROD)** — specifically PROD 3 for investments, PROD 4 for non-investment insurance, PROD 7 for funeral plans — **satisfy this outcome**. They are not subject to both sets of rules simultaneously. **(PS22/9 §6.5, §6.7)**

Where PROD 3 applies as guidance (e.g. asset managers), firms may choose to follow PROD 3 or the Consumer Duty products and services rules — but not partly both. **(PS22/9 §6.7)**

## Application to Services

The outcome applies to **services** as well as products, including: distribution processes, investment platforms, model portfolio services, debt counselling services, arranging transactions, payment services. **(PS22/9 §6.10)**

## Application to Closed Products

The detailed rules on target market identification and distribution strategy do **not** apply to closed products (no longer on sale). However, high-level obligations under the cross-cutting rules — including reviewing whether aspects of the design could lead to foreseeable harm — still apply. **(PS22/9 §3.14)**

## Key Points for Agents

- Products and services must be designed for a defined target market and confirmed fit for purpose throughout the lifecycle — not only at launch.
- Manufacturers are responsible for target market definition; distributors must follow it and must not distribute outside it without justification.
- Manufacturers must share target market and value assessment results with distributors — without sharing commercially sensitive information such as margin breakdowns.
- Complexity in product design is acceptable if necessary for functionality, but consumer-facing features must be understandable to the target market.
- Existing PROD compliance satisfies this outcome — firms already meeting PROD do not face additional obligations on this outcome.

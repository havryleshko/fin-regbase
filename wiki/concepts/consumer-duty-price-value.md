# FCA Consumer Duty — Price and Value Outcome

## The Obligation

> All consumers must receive **fair value**: a reasonable relationship between the price paid for a product or service and the overall benefit they receive from it.
> **(PS22/9 §7.1)**

Value is assessed holistically — it is not purely about price. A product that meets the other elements of the Duty (fit for purpose, transparently sold, customers properly supported) is generally more likely to represent fair value. **(PS22/9 §7.8)**

## What "Fair Value" Means

The FCA defines fair value as a **reasonable relationship between price and benefit** — assessed at the level of the product or service, not for each individual customer. **(PS22/9 §3.18)**

Key principles:
- Value includes both **monetary and non-monetary** costs and benefits. Firms do not need to quantify non-monetary factors but must give qualitative consideration to them. **(PS22/9 §7.8)**
- Products where consumers pay in non-monetary terms (e.g. data for a free current account) are still subject to value assessment. **(PS22/9 §7.8)**
- Products with **no financial or non-financial cost to the consumer** (e.g. debt advice funded by other sources) are exempt from value assessment. **(PS22/9 §7.8)**

## What It Does Not Prohibit

The price and value outcome does **not** ban: **(PS22/9 §7.8)**
- **Differential pricing** between new and existing customers, where both groups receive fair value and discounts are transparent.
- **Cross-subsidies** between products.
- **Cost-plus pricing** is not required.
- Firms selling similar products under different brands at different prices — provided both offer fair value.

**Price walking** (progressively increasing prices at renewal without corresponding value improvement) would not meet the Duty in any sector, consistent with the FCA's existing general insurance pricing work. **(PS22/9 §7.7)**

## Manufacturer and Distributor Responsibilities

**Manufacturers** must:
- Conduct a value assessment for their product or service.
- Share **results** of the value assessment with distributors — but not commercially sensitive information such as margin breakdowns or risk-based pricing detail. A high-level summary of benefits, total prices, and confirmation of fair value is sufficient. **(PS22/9 §7.9)**

**Distributors** must:
- Understand the benefits of the product to the target market, the total price and fees.
- Ensure their own charges, and the cumulative charges across the distribution chain, do not cause the product to cease to provide fair value. **(PS22/9 §7.9)**
- Firms are **not required to duplicate** manufacturer value assessments — each firm is responsible only for the prices it controls. **(PS22/9 §7.9)**

Cumulative charges across complex chains (e.g. investment platforms with multiple service layers) require particular attention. Simple, flat chains (e.g. mortgages) require less. **(PS22/9 §7.9)**

## Application to Existing and Closed Products

The fair value rules apply **on a forward-looking basis** to existing open and closed products. **(PS22/9 §3.6)** This is not retrospective — the FCA is not asking firms to assess whether historical pricing breached past rules.

For closed products, firms can factor in the **original cost assumptions** when those products were written, including the cost of financing. **(PS22/9 §3.14)**

If a firm identifies a product that does not offer fair value, it must take action — but is **never required to amend vested contractual rights**. Options include: changing non-vested fees or charges; providing additional support or information; offering forbearance. **(PS22/9 §3.14)**

## Interaction with Existing Rules

Firms meeting the following existing rules automatically satisfy this outcome: **(PS22/9 §7.12)**
- **PROD 4** (non-investment insurance value assessment)
- **COLL 6.6, COLL 8.5 or COLL 15.7** (asset management value assessment)
- **PROD 7** (funeral plans)
- **COBS 19** IGC/GAA value assessments (pensions) — firms must use these assessments but must still meet the price and value outcome requirements

## Key Points for Agents

- Fair value means a reasonable relationship between price and benefit — assessed at product level, not individual customer level.
- Manufacturers must conduct value assessments and share results with distributors; distributors must ensure their charges don't create cumulative poor value across the chain.
- Differential pricing is permitted; price walking that creates unfair outcomes is not.
- For closed books: firms must assess fair value on a forward-looking basis but may factor in original pricing assumptions; they cannot be required to amend vested contractual rights.
- Existing PROD 4, COLL 6/8/15 and PROD 7 compliance satisfies this outcome.

# Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) — Third CDD Tier for Low-Risk Situations

## What SDD Is

Simplified Due Diligence (SDD) is a **third tier of CDD** that sits below standard CDD and above Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD). It permits firms to adjust — but not to eliminate — CDD obligations in situations where the ML/TF risk is assessed as genuinely low.

SDD is not an exemption from CDD. A firm applying SDD must still comply with all CDD measures; SDD allows adjustment of the **timing, quantity, quality, or monitoring frequency** of those measures. **(MLR Reg 37(1); JMLSG §5.4.1; Annex 5-III)**

The three-tier structure:

| Tier | When | What |
|------|------|------|
| **SDD** | Low ML/TF risk | Adjusted CDD — reduced timing, quantity, quality, or frequency |
| **Standard CDD** | Default | Identify/verify customer and BO; understand purpose |
| **EDD** | Higher risk or mandatory trigger | Enhanced obligations; in some cases mandatory senior management approval |

## When SDD May Be Applied

A firm may apply SDD in relation to a particular business relationship or transaction if it determines, taking into account its risk assessment, that the relationship or transaction presents a **low degree of risk of ML/TF**. **(MLR Reg 37(1); JMLSG §5.4.1)**

### Qualifying Risk Factors for SDD

When assessing whether the low degree of risk threshold is met, firms must consider at minimum the following factors **(MLR Reg 37(3); JMLSG §5.4.2)**:

**(i) Customer type:**
- A public administration or publicly owned enterprise
- An individual resident in a geographical area of low risk
- A credit institution or financial institution subject to requirements equivalent to the Fourth Money Laundering Directive
- A company listed on a regulated market

**(ii) Product/scheme type:**
- Certain life assurance and e-money products (sector-specific criteria in JMLSG Part II)
- Pension, superannuation or similar schemes providing retirement benefits where contributions are made by an employer or by deduction from an employee's wages, and scheme rules do not permit assignment of a member's interest **(MLR Reg 37(3)(b)(iii); JMLSG §5.4.4)**
- Child Trust Funds and Junior ISAs **(MLR Reg 37(3)(b)(vi)–(vii); JMLSG §5.4.5)**

Annex 5-III to JMLSG Part I Chapter 5 sets out fuller risk factor guidelines for SDD.

### Junior ISA — Special Timing Rule

SDD may be applied to Junior ISAs during the account's life, but identity must be verified when the child reaches 18 and becomes entitled to the funds (or at the next trigger event thereafter), unless identity has already been verified for another relationship. Where the child can manage the account from age 16, firms may choose to undertake CDD at that stage to avoid delay when the account converts to a full ISA at 18. **(JMLSG §5.4.6–5.4.7)**

## What SDD Permits

SDD allows the firm to adjust (not eliminate) its CDD measures in any of the following ways **(JMLSG Annex 5-III)**:

### Adjusting Timing
- Verifying identity during establishment of the relationship rather than before
- Verifying identity once transactions exceed a defined threshold or once a reasonable time limit has elapsed

Constraints: this must not create a de facto exemption from CDD; the threshold or time limit must be set at a reasonably low level; the firm must have systems to detect when the threshold is reached.

### Adjusting Quantity
- Verifying identity on the basis of one document only (rather than multiple)
- Assuming the nature and purpose of the business relationship because the product has only one possible use (e.g. a company pension scheme, a shopping centre gift card)

### Adjusting Quality or Source
- Accepting customer-provided information (rather than independent source) to verify the beneficial owner's identity (but **not** the customer's own identity — independent source still required for customer verification)
- Where all aspects of the relationship carry very low risk, relying on source of funds as evidence (e.g. state benefit payments or transfer from an account in the customer's name at an EEA firm)

### Adjusting Monitoring Frequency
- Updating CDD only when trigger events occur (new product, transaction threshold reached)
- Monitoring transactions above a certain threshold only — but the threshold must be reasonable and the firm must be able to identify linked transactions which together would exceed it

## When SDD Must Not Be Applied

SDD must stop, and must not be (re-)applied, in any of the following situations **(JMLSG §5.4.8)**:

- The firm's risk assessment changes so it no longer considers the risk to be low
- The firm suspects money laundering or terrorist financing
- There are doubts about the veracity or accuracy of documents or information previously obtained for identification or verification

SDD does not exempt the firm from reporting suspicious transactions. **(JMLSG Annex 5-III)**

## Obligations That SDD Does Not Remove

Even where SDD applies: **(JMLSG §5.4.9)**

- The obligation to conduct **ongoing monitoring** remains — though its extent may be adjusted to reflect the low-risk assessment
- The obligation to **report knowledge or suspicion** of ML/TF to the nominated officer is unaffected
- The information obtained under SDD must still be sufficient to allow the firm to identify unusual or suspicious transactions

## Documentation Requirement

Firms must **document the rationale** for the decision to apply SDD. **(JMLSG §5.4.10)**

## Relationship to Third-Party Reliance

A firm relying on another firm's CDD under MLR Reg 39 cannot rely on SDD carried out by that firm. Reliance is only permissible on standard-level or above CDD. **(JMLSG §5.6.10)**

## Key Points for Agents

- SDD is a third CDD tier, not an exemption. All CDD measures still apply; SDD permits adjustment of timing, quantity, quality, or monitoring frequency only.
- Six specified qualifying categories: public administrations; low-risk geography individuals; regulated firms subject to equivalent MLR requirements; listed companies; employer-contribution pension schemes (no member assignment); Child Trust Funds and Junior ISAs.
- SDD must be immediately suspended if risk changes, suspicion arises, or CDD data integrity is doubted.
- Ongoing monitoring and SAR reporting obligations survive SDD — they cannot be removed, only adjusted in extent.
- The firm must document its rationale for applying SDD.
- SDD-verified identity cannot be passed to another firm under the third-party reliance regime. Reliance requires standard CDD or above.

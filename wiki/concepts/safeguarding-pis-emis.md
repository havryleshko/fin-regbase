# Safeguarding — Payment Institutions and E-Money Institutions

## Purpose of Safeguarding

Safeguarding protects customer funds so that, if a PI or EMI enters insolvency, the funds are returned quickly and in full. Without adequate safeguarding, the return of customer funds may be delayed and customers may not recover all their money. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023, Outcome 1, Priority 1)**

Safeguarding obligations arise under:
- **PSRs 2017, Regs 22–27** — for payment institutions
- **EMRs 2011, Regs 20–25** — for e-money institutions

Detailed operational guidance is in the **FCA Approach Document, Chapter 10**.

---

## When the Obligation Arises

The safeguarding obligation arises **on receipt of relevant funds** — not at the end of the business day and not when the transaction is completed. Funds must be placed in a safeguarded account or covered by an insurance/guarantee arrangement as soon as they are received. **(FCA Approach Document Ch. 10)**

This is operationally significant: a firm that batches its safeguarding at end of day is non-compliant for the period during which received funds sit in an unsafeguarded account.

---

## What Must Be Safeguarded

Firms must safeguard **"relevant funds"** — a defined term in the PSRs/EMRs. For PIs this means funds received from payment service users in connection with payment transactions (received funds). For EMIs, it includes both funds received in exchange for e-money issued and funds received in connection with any unrelated payment services the EMI provides.

**Named FCA finding:** Many firms do not have documented processes for consistently identifying which funds are "relevant funds." This is a prerequisite to all other safeguarding obligations — if a firm cannot identify what must be protected, it cannot reliably protect it. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

---

## Safeguarding Methods

Firms may choose between two approaches:

### Segregation Method

Relevant funds must be placed in a **segregated account** with:
- A credit institution (bank)
- A central bank
- An authorised e-money institution

The segregated account must:
- Be **named or described** so that it is clearly identifiable as a safeguarding account (not the firm's own current or operating account)
- Hold **only relevant funds** — no other interests of any person (including the firm itself) may rank ahead of or equal to the relevant fund holders' interests in that account
- **Not be shared** with another firm's safeguarding arrangement — each firm must use its own dedicated safeguarding account
- **Not be used for any other purpose** — the account's sole function is to hold relevant funds

The firm must obtain a **written acknowledgement of segregation** from the credit institution confirming: (i) the institution understands the account holds relevant funds belonging to customers, (ii) the funds are separate from the firm's own funds, and (iii) the institution will not set off claims it holds against the firm against balances in the account. Failure to obtain this acknowledgement — or failure to perform appropriate due diligence on the institution holding the account — is a named FCA compliance failure. **(Approach Document Ch. 10; Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

**Secure liquid assets:** Where a firm earns profits or returns on relevant funds (e.g. interest), those amounts can also be safeguarded using **secure, liquid assets** — investments that can be readily converted to cash to cover the relevant funds balance. **(Approach Document Ch. 10)**

### Insurance/Comparable Guarantee Method

As an alternative, funds may be covered by an insurance policy or comparable guarantee from an insurance undertaking or credit institution that:
- Is not the same as the firm
- Has not been approved to provide this service to more than a certain proportion of the firm's total relevant funds

Where this method is used, the firm must ensure the policy or guarantee documents are current and provide adequate cover.

---

## Reconciliation — Daily Requirement

Firms must undertake both **internal and external reconciliations at least once a day** to ensure that safeguarded funds are adequate and not excessive. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

- **Internal reconciliation:** Cross-referencing the firm's own records of relevant funds against the balance held in the safeguarded account or covered by the insurance policy.
- **External reconciliation:** Confirming with the account-holding institution (or insurer) that the balance reflects what the firm's records show.

**Named FCA finding:** Inadequate reconciliation procedures are among the most commonly observed safeguarding failures. A reconciliation performed less frequently than daily, or that does not identify discrepancies in real time, does not satisfy this requirement.

---

## Annual Safeguarding Audit

Since July 2020 (incorporated into the Approach Document), firms that are required to undertake a **statutory audit** must also arrange an **annual audit of their safeguarding arrangements**. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

**Obligations arising from the audit:**
- The firm's auditor is required to notify the FCA if, in its capacity as auditor, it becomes aware of a breach (or potential breach) of any PSR/EMR requirement of **material significance** — including safeguarding requirements and the organisational arrangements requirement.
- For EMIs: this notification obligation applies whether the finding relates to e-money issuance, unrelated payment services, or both.
- Firms must **notify the FCA in writing without delay** if, in any material respect, they have not complied with or are unable to comply with safeguarding requirements — regardless of whether this is identified via audit or otherwise.
- Where deficiencies are identified, firms must be prepared to explain the remedial actions being taken.

**Named FCA finding:** Some firms have not yet appointed auditors, and the FCA is not being consistently informed of adverse audit findings or the actions taken to address them. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

## Insolvency Protection — Asset Pool and PESAR

### The Asset Pool

In the insolvency of a PI or EMI, safeguarded relevant funds form a **protected asset pool**. Customers of the failed firm have a **first-priority claim** against this asset pool — they rank ahead of the general creditors of the firm. **(PSRs 2017 Reg 24(3); EMRs 2011 Reg 24(3); Approach Document Ch. 10)**

This insolvency priority is the mechanism that delivers the core purpose of safeguarding: in a failure, customers recover their funds from the asset pool before any creditor is paid from those funds.

### PESAR — Payment and Electronic Money Special Administration Regime

The **Payment and Electronic Money Special Administration Regime (PESAR)** is the insolvency framework specifically designed for failed PIs and EMIs. When a PI or EMI enters insolvency, the special administrator has specific statutory duties:

1. **Return client assets** — ensure relevant funds in the asset pool are returned to customers as quickly as possible
2. **Rescue or wind down** — either rescue the firm as a going concern, achieve a better result for creditors than in liquidation, or wind down in an orderly way

PESAR differs from general corporate insolvency because the special administrator's primary duty is to customers, not creditors. Firms must maintain records sufficient for a PESAR administrator to identify which customer funds correspond to which amount held in the asset pool.

---

## Record Keeping for Insolvency Return

Firms must maintain records that enable the firm itself, or a third party such as an insolvency practitioner or PESAR special administrator, to identify the customer to which any held funds relate. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

Records required:
1. The segregated account(s) or insurance policy/guarantee documentation
2. Customer-level records matching each relevant fund balance to the customer who provided it
3. Daily reconciliation records confirming the safeguarded balance is adequate

---

## Relationship to Wind-Down Planning

Safeguarding and wind-down planning are linked: the purpose of safeguarding is to ensure that funds can be returned to customers in an insolvency scenario, but this depends on the firm also maintaining records sufficient for a practitioner to identify and distribute those funds. See [[wind-down-planning]] for the operational planning obligations.

## Common Safeguarding Failures (FCA-Named)

| Failure | Consequence |
|---------|------------|
| No documented process to identify relevant funds | Cannot reliably determine what must be safeguarded |
| Reconciliation performed less than daily | Safeguarded amount may be inadequate or excessive at any given time |
| No written acknowledgement of segregation from credit institution | Segregation claim is not evidenced against the account-holder |
| Lack of due diligence on safeguarding account provider | Risk that the provider is itself unsuitable |
| No annual safeguarding audit (where statutory audit required) | Absence of independent verification; FCA regulatory notification obligation breached |
| Inadequate customer-level records | Insolvency practitioner cannot return funds to correct customers |

---

## FCA Notification Obligations

Firms must notify the FCA **without delay** (using Form **NOT002**) if, in any material respect, they:
- Have not complied with safeguarding requirements, or
- Are unable to comply with safeguarding requirements

This obligation applies whether or not the failure is identified via the annual audit. The FCA expects firms to be proactive — self-identification and prompt notification are taken into account in supervisory response. **(Approach Document Ch. 10; PSRs 2017 Reg 27)**

See [[psr-regulatory-reporting]] for the NOT002 notification mechanics.

| Failure | Consequence |
|---------|------------|
| No documented process to identify relevant funds | Cannot reliably determine what must be safeguarded |

## Key Points for Agents

- **Obligation on receipt:** Safeguarding applies from the moment relevant funds are received — not end of day.
- "Relevant funds" is a defined term — firms must have documented processes to identify which funds are in scope.
- **Segregation account requirements:** Must be named, hold only relevant funds, no competing interests, not shared with any other firm, acknowledgement letter in place with full terms.
- Reconciliation must be performed at least daily (both internal and external). This is a minimum, not a target.
- Where a statutory audit is required, an annual safeguarding audit is also required. Adverse findings must be reported to the FCA without delay via NOT002.
- **PESAR and the asset pool:** In insolvency, safeguarded funds form a protected asset pool giving customers first-priority claims — this only works if records exist to match each fund to each customer.
- **Capital and safeguarding are separate:** Safeguarded customer funds are not regulatory capital and cannot be counted toward own funds.

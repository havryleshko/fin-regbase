# Client Order Handling, Limit Orders, and Personal Account Dealing — COBS 11

## COBS 11.3 Client Order Handling

### The Overarching Obligation

**COBS 11.3.1R:** A firm (other than a UCITS management company providing collective portfolio management services) that is authorised to execute orders on behalf of clients must implement procedures and arrangements which provide for the **prompt, fair and expeditious execution of client orders**, relative to other orders or the trading interests of the firm. These procedures must allow for the execution of otherwise comparable orders in accordance with the **time of their reception**. [Note: MiFID article 28(1), paras 1–2]

A UCITS management company providing collective portfolio management services must establish and implement procedures providing for the prompt, fair and expeditious execution of portfolio transactions on behalf of the UCITS scheme it manages. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 27(1)]

**COBS 11.3.3G:** Orders should not be treated as otherwise comparable if they are received by different media and it would not be practicable for them to be treated sequentially.

### Three Carrying-Out Obligations

**COBS 11.3.2AR:** A firm must, when carrying out client orders:

1. **Accurate recording and allocation:** Ensure that orders executed on behalf of clients are **promptly and accurately recorded and allocated**
2. **Sequential execution:** Carry out otherwise comparable client orders **sequentially and promptly** unless the characteristics of the order or prevailing market conditions make this impracticable, or the interests of the client require otherwise
3. **Retail client notification:** Inform a retail client about any **material difficulty relevant to the proper carrying out of orders** promptly upon becoming aware of the difficulty

### Settlement of Executed Orders

**COBS 11.3.4AR:** Where a firm is responsible for overseeing or arranging the settlement of an executed order, it must take all reasonable steps to ensure that any client financial instruments or client money received in settlement are **promptly and correctly delivered** to the account of the appropriate client.

**COBS 11.3.4R:** Where a management company executes the order itself in the course of providing collective portfolio management services, it must take all reasonable steps to ensure that client financial instruments or client funds received in settlement are **promptly and correctly delivered** to the account of the appropriate UCITS scheme. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 27(1)]

### No Misuse of Pending Client Order Information

**COBS 11.3.5AR:** A firm must **not misuse information relating to pending client orders**, and must take all reasonable steps to prevent the misuse of such information by any of its relevant persons.

**COBS 11.3.6G:** Any use by a firm of information relating to a pending client order in order to deal on own account in the financial instruments to which the client order relates, or in **related** financial instruments, should be considered a misuse of that information. However, market makers or bodies authorised to act as counterparties confining themselves to their legitimate business of buying and selling financial instruments, or persons authorised to execute orders on behalf of third parties confining themselves to carrying out an order dutifully, should not in itself be deemed a misuse.

The prohibition operates **without prejudice to the Market Abuse Regulation** — it is an additional conduct obligation on top of UK MAR requirements.

### Aggregation of Orders

**COBS 11.3.7AR:** A firm must **not carry out a client order or a transaction for own account in aggregation with another client order** unless all three of the following conditions are met:

1. **Unlikely to disadvantage:** It is unlikely that the aggregation of orders will work overall to the **disadvantage of any client** whose orders are to be aggregated
2. **Client disclosure:** It is disclosed to each client whose order is to be aggregated that the **effect of aggregation may work to their disadvantage** in relation to a particular order
3. **Order allocation policy:** The firm has established and effectively implemented an **order allocation policy** which provides for the fair allocation of aggregated orders, including how volume and price determine allocations and the treatment of partial executions

**COBS 11.3.7BR:** A management company must ensure that the order allocation policy is in **sufficiently precise terms**. [Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 28(1)]

### Partial Execution of Aggregated Client Orders

**COBS 11.3.8AR:** Where a firm aggregates an order with one or more other client orders and the aggregated order is partially executed, it must allocate the related trades **in accordance with its order allocation policy**. [Note: MiFID article 28(2)]

### Aggregation with Own Account Transactions

**COBS 11.3.9AR:** A firm which has aggregated transactions for own account with one or more client orders must **not allocate the related trades in a way that is detrimental to a client**. [Note: MiFID article 28(3)]

**COBS 11.3.10AR:** Where a firm aggregates a client order with a transaction for own account and the aggregated order is **partially executed**, it must allocate the related trades to the **client in priority to the firm**. [Note: MiFID article 28(4)]

**Exception:** Where the firm is able to demonstrate on reasonable grounds that without the combination it would not have been able to carry out the order on such advantageous terms, or at all, it may then allocate the own account transaction **proportionally**, in accordance with the order allocation policy.

**COBS 11.3.11AR:** The order allocation policy must include procedures designed to **prevent the reallocation** of own account transactions, in a way detrimental to the client, of transactions executed in combination with client orders.

**COBS 11.3.12G:** The reallocation of transactions should be considered detrimental to a client if, as an effect of that reallocation, **unfair precedence is given to the firm** or to any particular person.

### Scope of "Carrying Out Client Orders"

**COBS 11.3.13G:** For the purposes of this section, carrying out client orders includes:
1. The execution of orders on behalf of clients
2. The placing of orders with other entities for execution that result from decisions to deal in financial instruments on behalf of clients when providing portfolio management or collective portfolio management services
3. The transmission of client orders to other entities for execution when providing the service of reception and transmission of orders

### Record Retention

**COBS 11.3.15R:** A firm subject to COBS 11.3.2AR to COBS 11.3.13G must **retain the records** it is required to make under those rules.

## COBS 11.4 Client Limit Orders

### Obligation to Make Unexecuted Limit Orders Public

**COBS 11.4.1R:** Unless a client expressly instructs otherwise, a firm must, in the case of a **client limit order** in respect of shares admitted to trading on a regulated market or traded on a trading venue which is **not immediately executed** under prevailing market conditions, take measures to facilitate the **earliest possible execution of that order by making the limit order public immediately** in a manner which is easily accessible to other market participants. [Note: MiFID article 28(2)]

**COBS 11.4.2G:** In respect of transactions executed between **eligible counterparties**, the obligation to disclose client limit orders should only apply where the counterparty is explicitly sending a limit order to a firm for its execution.

### How Limit Orders May Be Made Public

**COBS 11.4.3AR:** A client limit order is considered made available to the public when:
- The investment firm has submitted the order for execution to a regulated market or an MTF; or
- The order has been published by a person authorised to provide data reporting services under the DRS Regulations

— and the order **can be easily executed as soon as market conditions allow**.

**COBS 11.4.4AG:** Firms may comply with COBS 11.4.1R by **transmitting the client limit order to a trading venue**.

### Exemption for Large-Scale Orders

**COBS 11.4.5R:** The obligation in COBS 11.4.1R to make a limit order public is **disapplied** in respect of transactions that are large in scale compared with normal market size, as determined under article 4 of MiFIR.

## COBS 11.5A Record Keeping: Client Orders and Transactions

COBS 11.5 (old record keeping) is deleted. COBS 11.5A is the live provision.

**Scope (COBS 11.5A.1R):** Does not apply to corporate finance business carried on by a firm which is not a MiFID investment firm.

### Initial Order Records — 16 Minimum Fields

**COBS 11.5A.2R:** A firm must, in relation to every initial order received from a client and every initial decision to deal taken, **immediately record and keep** at the disposal of the FCA at least the details set out in COBS 11.5A.4R. Where those details are also prescribed by EU Regulation 600/2014 (Articles 25 and 26), they must be maintained consistently with those standards.

**COBS 11.5A.4R — 16 minimum fields for initial orders:**
1. Name and designation of the client
2. Name and designation of any relevant person acting on behalf of the client
3. Trader ID responsible within the firm for the investment decision
4. Algo ID responsible within the firm for the investment decision
5. B/S indicator
6. Instrument identification
7. Unit price and price notation
8. Price
9. Price multiplier
10. Currency 1
11. Currency 2
12. Initial quantity and quantity notation
13. Validity period
14. Type of the order
15. Any other details, conditions and particular instructions from the client
16. Date and exact time of receipt of the order or when the decision to deal was made (measured per **MiFID RTS 25** methodology)

### Transaction and Order Processing Records — 40 Minimum Fields

**COBS 11.5A.3R:** A firm must, immediately after receiving a client order or making a decision to deal, record and keep at the disposal of the FCA at least the details set out in COBS 11.5A.5R.

**COBS 11.5A.5R — 40 minimum fields for transaction processing** (in addition to the initial order fields, adds):
5. Transaction reference number
6. Order ID (firm's designation)
7. Order ID assigned by the trading venue on receipt
8. Unique identification for aggregated orders: format "aggregated_X" where X = number of clients whose orders are aggregated
9. Segment MIC code of the trading venue to which the order has been submitted
10. Name and other designation of the person to whom the order was transmitted
11. Seller and Buyer designation
12. Trading capacity
13. Trader ID responsible for execution
14. Algo ID responsible for execution
(Plus instruments detail fields:)
17. Ultimate underlying
18. Put/Call identifier
19. Strike price
20. Upfront payment
21. Delivery type
22. Option style
23. Maturity date
(Plus execution tracking fields:)
29. Remaining quantity
30. Modified quantity
31. Executed quantity
32. Date and exact time of submission of order or decision to deal (MiFID RTS 25)
33. Date and exact time of any message transmitted to/received from trading venue in relation to events affecting an order (RTS 25 clock synchronisation)
34. Date and exact time of any message transmitted to/received from another investment firm in relation to events affecting an order (RTS 25)
35. Any message transmitted to/received from a trading venue in relation to orders placed by the firm
36. Any other details and conditions submitted to/received from another firm in relation to the order
37. Each placed order's sequence to reflect the chronology of every event affecting it (modifications, cancellations, execution)
38. Short selling flag
39. SSR exemption flag
40. Waiver flag

## COBS 11.7 Personal Account Dealing (Non-MiFID Business)

**Application (COBS 11.7.-1R):** This section does **not** apply to a firm in relation to MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business — which is governed by COBS 11.7A.

**COBS 11.1.4R:** The personal account dealing sections apply to the designated investment business of a firm in relation to activities carried on from an establishment in the United Kingdom.

### Core Prohibition

**COBS 11.7.1R:** A firm that conducts **designated investment business** must establish, implement and maintain adequate arrangements preventing relevant persons who are involved in activities that may give rise to a conflict of interest, or who have access to inside information or other confidential information relating to clients or transactions, from:

1. Entering into a **personal transaction** meeting at least one of the following criteria:
   - (a) That person is prohibited from entering into it under the Market Abuse Regulation
   - (b) It involves the misuse or improper disclosure of confidential information
   - (c) It conflicts or is likely to conflict with an obligation of the firm to a customer under the regulatory system, MiFID, or the UCITS Directive
2. **Advising or procuring** any other person to enter into such a transaction (other than in the proper course of employment or contract for services)
3. **Disclosing** any information or opinion where the relevant person knows or reasonably ought to know that as a result of the disclosure, the recipient will or would be likely to: (a) enter into a transaction covered by (1) or a relevant provision; or (b) advise or procure another person to enter into such a transaction

[Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 13(1)]

### Four Implementation Requirements

**COBS 11.7.4R:** The arrangements must in particular ensure:
1. Each relevant person is **aware of the restrictions** on personal transactions and the measures established by the firm in connection with personal transactions and disclosure
2. The firm is **informed promptly** of any personal transaction entered into by a relevant person (either by notification or by other procedures enabling the firm to identify such transactions); or in the case of outsourcing, the service provider maintains a record of personal transactions by relevant persons and provides that information to the firm promptly on request
3. A **record is kept** of personal transactions notified to or identified by the firm, including any authorisation or prohibition in connection with that transaction
4. (The outsourcing arm of requirement 2 above applies as a separate strand)

[Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 13(2)]

### Three Disapplications

**COBS 11.7.5R:** The section does not apply to:
1. Personal transactions under a **discretionary portfolio management service** where there is no prior communication in connection with the transaction between the portfolio manager and the relevant person (or other person for whose account the transaction is executed)
2. Personal transactions in **units or shares in collective undertakings** that comply with the conditions necessary to enjoy UCITS Directive rights or are subject to EEA-State equivalent supervision, where the relevant person and any other person for whose account transactions are effected are not involved in the management of that undertaking
3. Personal transactions in **life policies**

[Note: UCITS implementing Directive, article 13(3)]

### Who is a "Relevant Person"

**COBS 11.7.6R:** A person who is not: (a) a director, partner or equivalent, manager, or appointed representative (or tied agent) of the firm; or (b) a director, partner or equivalent, or manager of any appointed representative (or tied agent) of the firm — will only be a relevant person to the extent that they are involved in the **provision of designated investment business** or collective portfolio management services.

### Successive Transactions Under Prior Instructions

**COBS 11.7.7R:** Where successive personal transactions are carried out in accordance with prior instructions given by a person, the obligations of this section do not apply: (a) separately to each successive transaction if those instructions remain in force and unchanged; or (b) to the termination or withdrawal of such instructions (provided financial instruments previously acquired pursuant to the instructions are not disposed of at the same time as the instructions terminate or are withdrawn). The obligations do apply when the instructions are changed or new instructions are issued.

## COBS 11.7A Personal Account Dealing — MiFID Business

**Application (COBS 11.7A.1R):** This chapter applies to a firm in relation to its **MiFID, equivalent third country, or optional exemption business**.

### Obligation to Establish PA Dealing Rules

**COBS 11.7A.3R:** A firm that conducts designated investment business must establish **appropriate rules governing personal transactions** undertaken by managers, employees, and tied agents. [Note: MiFID article 16(2)]

### Core Requirements

**COBS 11.7A.5R(1):** A firm must establish, implement and maintain adequate arrangements aimed at preventing relevant persons from:

**(para 2)** Entering into a personal transaction meeting at least one of three criteria:
- (a) Prohibited under the Market Abuse Regulation
- (b) Involves misuse or improper disclosure of confidential information
- (c) Conflicts or is likely to conflict with an obligation of the firm under UK law on markets in financial instruments

**(para 3)** Advising or recommending (other than in the proper course of employment) any other person to enter into a transaction in financial instruments which, if it were a personal transaction of the relevant person, would be covered by para (2) or a relevant provision

**(para 4)** Subject to MAR Article 10(1), disclosing any information or opinion to any other person where the relevant person knows or reasonably ought to know that as a result the recipient will or would be likely to: (a) enter into a transaction covered by para (2) or a relevant provision; or (b) advise or procure another person to enter into such a transaction

### Five Design Requirements

**COBS 11.7A.5R(5):** The arrangements must ensure:
- (a) Each relevant person is aware of restrictions on personal transactions and measures established in connection with personal transactions and disclosure under this rule
- (b) The firm is informed promptly of any personal transaction entered into by a relevant person, either by notification or by other procedures enabling the firm to identify such transactions
- (c) A record is kept of the personal transaction notified to or identified by the firm, including any authorisation or prohibition in connection with it

**COBS 11.7A.5R(5A):** In the case of **outsourcing arrangements**, the firm must ensure the service provider maintains a record of personal transactions by relevant persons and provides that information to the firm promptly on request.

### Two Disapplications

**COBS 11.7A.2R(3):** The requirements in COBS 11.7A.5R do not apply to:
- (a) Personal transactions under a **discretionary portfolio management service** where there is no prior communication between the portfolio manager and the relevant person (or other person for whose account the transaction is executed)
- (b) Personal transactions in **units or shares in UCITS or AIFs** subject to supervision under UK law requiring equivalent risk spreading, where the relevant person and any other person for whose account the transactions are effected are not involved in the management of that undertaking

### Successive Transactions

**COBS 11.7A.6R:** Same successive transaction carve-out as COBS 11.7.7R applies to the MiFID track: where successive personal transactions are carried out in accordance with unchanged prior instructions, the obligations do not apply separately to each successive transaction or to the termination/withdrawal of instructions. Obligations do apply when instructions change or new instructions are issued.

## Key Points for Agents

- **Sequential execution for comparable client orders is mandatory** (COBS 11.3.2AR): comparable orders must be executed in order of receipt unless the order's characteristics or market conditions make this impracticable, or client interests require otherwise. Orders received by different media that cannot practicably be treated sequentially are not "otherwise comparable" (COBS 11.3.3G).
- **Aggregation with own account requires three conditions and client priority:** Aggregation with own account is only permissible where disadvantage to clients is unlikely, the client is informed it may work to their disadvantage, and a written order allocation policy is in place. On partial execution, the client always takes priority over the firm's own account — unless the firm can demonstrate the combination was necessary to obtain favourable terms at all (COBS 11.3.7AR; COBS 11.3.10AR).
- **Unexecuted limit orders in shares must be made public immediately** (unless the client instructs otherwise or the order is large in scale under MiFIR article 4). Firms may comply by transmitting to a trading venue (COBS 11.4.1R; COBS 11.4.4AG).
- **Pending client order information must not be misused** — any use of that information to deal on own account in the relevant or related financial instruments is a misuse (COBS 11.3.5AR; 11.3.6G). This obligation operates in addition to, not instead of, UK MAR prohibitions.
- **Two parallel personal account dealing regimes — different firm scope:** COBS 11.7 covers non-MiFID designated investment business from UK establishments; COBS 11.7A covers MiFID/equivalent third country/optional exemption business. Both prohibit MAR-prohibited transactions, misuse of confidential information, and conflicts — but COBS 11.7A is the MiFID article 16(2) implementing provision and the standard formulation refers to "UK law on markets in financial instruments" rather than the regulatory system.
- **Record keeping — immediate obligation, 16 fields for orders, 40 for transactions:** MiFID RTS 25 timestamps are required for exact time fields. The obligation arises immediately on receipt of the order or decision to deal, not at a later stage (COBS 11.5A.2R–11.5A.5R).

# SM&CR — Senior Managers Regime

## Scope and the PI/EMI Exclusion

SM&CR applies to **FSMA-authorised firms** supervised by the FCA under FSMA. Firms that hold only payment services authorisation or e-money authorisation — and are not otherwise FSMA-authorised — are **entirely outside the scope of SM&CR**. Those firms have no SMF obligations, no Certification Regime obligations, and no Conduct Rule obligations under this framework.

Three tiers exist for FSMA-authorised solo-regulated firms:

| Tier | Default population | PRs |
|------|-------------------|-----|
| **Limited Scope** | Specific categories: limited permission consumer credit, CMCs not carrying on other regulated activity, oil/energy market participants (principal purpose non-regulated), authorised professional firms (non-mainstream only), insurance intermediaries (non-investment only) | None |
| **Core** | All other solo-regulated FSMA-authorised firms | 5 |
| **Enhanced** | Most complex firms (SYSC 23 Annex 1: asset managers above AUM threshold, investment banks, corporate finance, platform operators, etc.) | 12 |

Firms can opt up to Enhanced voluntarily via Form O (3-month compliance deadline). Moving to Enhanced because criteria are met carries a **1-year transition period**. Firms cannot cherry-pick rules from different tiers.

## What a Senior Management Function Is

A Senior Management Function (SMF) is an FCA-controlled function that a person must be individually approved to perform before they may carry it out. *(FSMA s.59)* There are three categories:

- **Governing Functions** — leadership roles (CEO, Executive Director, Chair, Partner)
- **Required Functions** — roles the Handbook may require firms to have (Compliance Oversight, MLRO)
- **Systems & Controls Functions** — Enhanced-only oversight roles (CFO, CRO, COO, Head of Internal Audit)

SMFs apply only if someone is actually performing the relevant role, or the SMF is a Required Function mandated by Handbook rules. The SM&CR does not require firms to change their governance structure or create new roles.

An individual can hold multiple SMFs simultaneously. Separate FCA approval is needed for each function but only **one SoR** is required, covering all functions held.

## Senior Management Functions by Tier

### Core SMFs (6)

| SMF | Role |
|-----|------|
| SMF1 – Chief Executive | Person(s) responsible, under immediate authority of the governing body, for conduct of the whole business. The CEO's seniority does not permit the governing body to avoid allocating specific responsibilities to other SMs. |
| SMF3 – Executive Director | A director of the firm, other than a NED |
| SMF27 – Partner | A partner in the firm (not a limited partner under the Limited Partnership Act 1907) |
| SMF9 – Chair | Person responsible for chairing and overseeing performance of the governing body. **The only SMF a Non-Executive Director can hold** under the Core tier. |
| SMF16 – Compliance Oversight | Person responsible for the compliance function and reporting to the governing body. Required Function only. |
| SMF17 – MLRO | Person responsible for overseeing the firm's compliance with FCA AML systems-and-controls rules. Required Function only. |

Non-Executive Directors (other than the Chair) are **not** FCA-approved under Core. They are, however, subject to Conduct Rules, FIT requirements, and regulatory reference obligations.

### Limited Scope SMFs (3)

SMF29 (Limited Scope Function), SMF16 (Compliance Oversight), SMF17 (MLRO). No PRs. SoRs required for all SMF holders.

### Enhanced SMFs (17)

All Core SMFs plus:

**Additional Governing Functions:** SMF7 (Group Entity Senior Manager), SMF10 (Chair of Risk Committee), SMF11 (Chair of Audit Committee), SMF12 (Chair of Remuneration Committee), SMF13 (Chair of Nominations Committee), SMF14 (Senior Independent Director), SMF18 (Other Overall Responsibility — senior executive responsible for a business area who holds no other SMF).

**Systems & Controls Functions:** SMF2 (Chief Finance Function), SMF4 (Chief Risk Function), SMF5 (Head of Internal Audit), SMF24 (Chief Operations Function — HR, systems and technology).

NED committee chairs (SMF10–13) and the SID (SMF14) are FCA-approved for Enhanced firms. Other NEDs remain unapproved but subject to Conduct Rules, FIT, and regulatory references.

**Head of Legal exclusion:** The Head of Legal is expressly excluded from SM approval across all tiers (legal privilege grounds). The Head of Legal is included in the Certification Regime and subject to Conduct Rules. **(FCA Guide for Solo-Regulated Firms)**

## Statements of Responsibilities (SoRs)

Every Senior Manager must have a SoR filed with the FCA that sets out:
- Their SMF(s)
- Areas of the business for which they are responsible
- Any PRs allocated to them

SoRs must be kept current. PR reallocations require updated SoRs to be submitted using Form J. Reallocating a PR to a different SM does not require re-approval of that SM.

Where responsibilities are shared between two SMs, both SoRs must clearly describe the shared nature of the responsibility. The SoR is the primary document the FCA uses to identify who was accountable for what area and when.

## Duty of Responsibility

Under FSMA s.66A, the FCA may take disciplinary action against a Senior Manager where:

1. The firm has contravened a relevant requirement; and
2. The contravention occurred in an area of business for which the Senior Manager was responsible at the relevant time

**Statutory defence:** A Senior Manager is not liable if they took such steps as it was reasonable to expect them to take to prevent the contravention occurring (or continuing). The burden of establishing the defence lies on the Senior Manager.

The Duty applies per-SM to their own area of responsibility — not to the firm as a whole. This means that a contravention in Area X does not expose the Senior Manager responsible for Area Y, unless Area Y's SM took actions (or failed to act) that contributed to the Area X contravention.

The Duty requires Senior Managers to maintain evidence of the steps they took to prevent problems — not merely to assert that they did so.

## Prescribed Responsibilities (PRs)

PRs are specific regulatory obligations defined in SYSC 24 that firms must formally allocate to named Senior Managers. They are **in addition to** the inherent responsibilities that come with an SMF role. Their purpose is to ensure that key conduct and prudential risk areas always have a named, individually accountable SM.

PRs do not apply to Limited Scope firms or EEA branches.

### Core PRs (5)

| PR Ref. | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| (a) | Performance by the firm of its obligations under the SMR, including implementation and oversight |
| (b) | Performance by the firm of its obligations under the Certification Regime |
| (b-1) | Performance by the firm of its obligations in respect of notifications and training of the Conduct Rules |
| (d) | Responsibility for the firm's policies and procedures for countering the risk that the firm might be used to further financial crime |
| (z) | Responsibility for the firm's compliance with CASS *(if applicable)* |

AFMs additionally carry PR(za): assessments of value, independent director representation, acting in investors' best interests.

### Enhanced PRs (12)

All Core PRs plus:

| PR Ref. | Obligation |
|---------|-----------|
| (c) | Compliance with the rules relating to the firm's Responsibilities Map |
| (j) | Safeguarding and overseeing independence and performance of the internal audit function (SYSC 6.2) |
| (j-3) | Where internal audit is outsourced: ensuring independence from external audit, including supervision of outsourced internal auditors |
| (k) | Safeguarding and overseeing independence and performance of the compliance function (SYSC 6.1) |
| (l) | Safeguarding and overseeing independence and performance of the risk function (SYSC 7.1.21R/22R) |
| (s) | Managing the firm's internal stress-tests and ensuring accuracy and timeliness of stress-testing information provided to the FCA |
| (t) | Developing and maintaining the firm's business model |

**Allocation rules:**
- Each PR should normally be held by **one person**; sharing requires justification and must be explained in the relevant SoRs
- PRs (j), (k), and (l) should be allocated to a NED or partner without management responsibilities where possible, to preserve the independence of the functions being overseen
- PRs **cannot** be allocated to the SMF18 (Other Overall Responsibility) holder — except PR(z) for CASS compliance
- PR allocation should follow the **most senior person responsible for that issue in practice**, not necessarily the person holding the related SMF role

## Enhanced-Only Requirements

### Overall Responsibility

Every activity, business area, and management function of the firm — including those carried out from overseas branches — must have a Senior Manager with overall responsibility for it. *(SYSC 25)* This is to prevent gaps where no named SM is accountable and to prevent issues falling between the cracks.

Having Overall Responsibility means the SM: (i) has ultimate responsibility for managing or supervising that function; (ii) briefs and reports to the governing body about it; and (iii) puts matters for decision about it to the governing body.

Key points:
- Overall Responsibility **cannot be divided** but **can be shared** (two SMs jointly accountable)
- Day-to-day management of the function is not required — the SM must be the most senior person ultimately responsible
- Scope equals the scope of the Conduct Rules — applies to regulated and unregulated financial services activities

### Responsibilities Map

Enhanced firms must prepare and maintain a single document under SYSC 25 setting out:
- How PRs have been allocated among Senior Managers
- Which SM has Overall Responsibility for each activity, business area, and management function
- Individuals' and committees' reporting lines
- How any responsibilities are shared or divided

The Responsibilities Map gives the FCA a collective view of governance, allows it to verify there are no gaps in SoRs, and identifies who to approach about any specific area. SYSC 25 Annex 1 provides a template (non-mandatory).

### Handover Procedures

Enhanced firms must take all reasonable steps to ensure that a person taking on an SMF role has all the information and materials they could reasonably need to do the job effectively. *(SYSC 25.9)* One mechanism is a handover note from the predecessor. Firms must:
- Have a documented handover policy
- Maintain adequate records of steps taken when handovers occur

## Operational Rules

### 12-Week Rule

A person can cover for a Senior Manager without FCA approval where the absence is temporary or reasonably unforeseen and the appointment is for less than **12 consecutive weeks**. *(SUP 10C.3.13R)*

### Outsourcing

Outsourcing an operational function does not outsource accountability for it. The Senior Manager responsible for that function retains full regulatory accountability regardless of who operationally delivers it. You may outsource the function — you cannot outsource the accountability.

### Overseas Senior Managers

No territorial limitation. An individual performing an SMF is subject to SM&CR obligations whether based in the UK or overseas.

## Key Points for Agents

- Under **FSMA 2000** and the **FCA Guide for Solo-Regulated Firms**: SM&CR applies only to FSMA-authorised firms. PIs and EMIs not otherwise FSMA-authorised are **entirely outside scope** — no SMFs, no Certification Regime, no Conduct Rule obligations under SM&CR. **(FSMA 2000; FCA Guide for Solo-Regulated Firms)**
- **Head of Legal exclusion:** The Head of Legal is **expressly excluded from SM approval across all tiers** on legal privilege grounds — they cannot hold an SMF designation regardless of the breadth of their responsibilities. They are not outside SM&CR entirely: the Head of Legal is included in the **Certification Regime** and subject to **Conduct Rules**. The exclusion is from the Senior Managers Regime (SMF approval) only. **(FCA Guide for Solo-Regulated Firms)**
- **Overall Responsibility is Enhanced-only — does not exist in Core firms (SYSC 25):** The Overall Responsibility requirement (that every activity, business area, and management function has a named SM accountable for it) **is an Enhanced-tier obligation only under SYSC 25**. It does not exist in Core firms or Limited Scope firms at all. Even if the Head of Legal were eligible for SMF designation, they could only hold Overall Responsibility in an Enhanced firm — a Core firm has no such requirement. Additionally, Overall Responsibility must be allocated **per activity/area** — it cannot be assigned as a blanket designation to one person covering all regulated activities simultaneously. **(SYSC 25)**
- **PIs and EMIs are not without conduct obligations** — they are instead subject to: the FCA's Principles for Businesses (PRIN), including Principle 2A (Consumer Duty where applicable); the conduct standards in the PSRs 2017 and EMRs 2011; and the FCA Approach Document. The Individual Conduct Rule 6 (act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers) applies to FSMA firms via SM&CR — for PI/EMI staff, equivalent obligations arise through the Consumer Duty framework, not SM&CR directly.
- Three tiers: Limited Scope (no PRs) / Core (5 PRs) / Enhanced (12 PRs). Core is the default.
- Senior Managers must be FCA-approved before performing their SMF (FSMA s.59). The only way to cover without approval is the 12-week temporary absence rule.
- The Duty of Responsibility (FSMA s.66A) creates personal liability for contraventions in an SM's area unless they took reasonable steps to prevent it. The burden of proof of the defence is on the SM.
- PRs are firm-level obligations that must be named to a specific SM in their SoR. Moving a PR requires Form J submission, not re-approval.
- PR(d) (financial crime) and PR(b-1) (conduct rule training) are Core PRs — every Core firm must have a named SM accountable for financial crime.
- Enhanced-only: Overall Responsibility (every activity/area/function must have a named SM); Responsibilities Map (SYSC 25 mandatory document); Handover Procedures (documented policy + records).
- Outsourcing an operational function does not outsource accountability for it.

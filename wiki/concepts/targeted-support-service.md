# Targeted Support Service (COBS 9B)

## What Targeted Support Is

Targeted support is a regulated activity under **Article 55A of the Regulated Activities Order (RAO)**, introduced in April 2026. It sits between execution-only services and personalised investment advice:

| Service type | Basis | Regulatory standard |
|---|---|---|
| Execution-only | Client-initiated, no recommendation | Appropriateness (COBS 10/10A) |
| **Targeted support** | **Firm or client-initiated; segment-level recommendation** | **COBS 9B (suitability at segment level)** |
| Investment advice | Personalised recommendation | Suitability (COBS 9/9A) |

A firm that provides targeted support is making a **"ready-made suggestion"** — a recommendation designed for a pre-defined group of consumers (a "consumer segment"), not for the individual client. The firm assesses suitability at the level of the segment; the client receives a suggestion tailored to the segment's characteristics, not to their own specific circumstances.

---

## The Consumer Segment Architecture

### Definition (COBS 9B.4.4R)

A consumer segment is a group of retail customers defined by:
- **Including characteristics** — characteristics a client must possess to be in the segment
- **Excluding characteristics** — characteristics that disqualify a client from the segment

Both types must be explicitly defined. The combination of including and excluding characteristics is the segment's identity.

### Pre-Definition (COBS 9B.4.5R)

All consumer segments and their associated ready-made suggestions must be **defined before targeted support is provided**. A firm cannot create a segment on the fly for a specific client interaction. Each segment has exactly one ready-made suggestion.

### Granularity (COBS 9B.4.9R)

Segments must be sufficiently granular that the associated ready-made suggestion is **genuinely suitable for all individuals** in the segment. An overly broad segment (such as "all retail clients with a moderate risk appetite") would not satisfy the requirement because no single product recommendation could be suitable for such a heterogeneous group.

### Evidenced Assumptions (COBS 9B.4.11R)

Assumptions about the characteristics of segment members must be **evidenced** — the firm must be able to demonstrate, on request, that the segment definition reflects a coherent and well-founded group.

### One Segment Per Client (COBS 9B.4.7R)

A client may only be aligned with **one consumer segment** at any time.

---

## The Ready-Made Suggestion

### Suitability Standard (COBS 9B.4.20R)

The ready-made suggestion must be **suitable for the consumer segment as a whole**. This is a suitability test at the segment level — not individual suitability (as in COBS 9/9A), but materially higher than appropriateness (COBS 10/10A which only assesses knowledge and experience). The firm must assess whether the suggestion would be suitable for any client who satisfies the segment characteristics.

---

## Delivery Mechanics

### The Alignment Gate (COBS 9B.5.5R)

Before delivering a ready-made suggestion, the firm must confirm that the client satisfies **all including characteristics** and none of the **excluding characteristics** of the relevant consumer segment. If the client does not align with any segment, no ready-made suggestion may be delivered.

### The Known-Unsuitability Override (COBS 9B.5.11R)

Even where a client technically aligns with a segment, the firm must **not deliver the ready-made suggestion** if it is aware — or ought reasonably to be aware — of individual circumstances that make the suggestion unsuitable for that specific client. This creates a mandatory override at the individual level on top of the segment-level design.

### Client Opt-Out (COBS 9B.5.14R)

A firm must provide the means for a client to elect **at any time** not to receive targeted support. A client who has opted out must not receive firm-initiated targeted support interactions unless they subsequently opt back in.

---

## Hard Prohibitions

These are categorical rules — they cannot be waived by the firm, the client, or by firm policy.

### 1. Safeguarded Benefits (COBS 9B.2.3R)
Where a client has **safeguarded benefits** (DB pension rights, guaranteed annuity rates, or other protected pension benefits), targeted support cannot be provided until the client has received **regulated financial advice** from an appropriately authorised firm.

### 2. Pension Consolidation (COBS 9B.4.38R)
A ready-made suggestion **must not** involve the consolidation of pension rights into a new scheme. Pension consolidation requires full personalised suitability advice and cannot be delivered via targeted support.

### 3. Restricted Products (COBS 9B.4.42R)
A ready-made suggestion **must not** involve:
- **Restricted mass market investments (RMMIs)** — qualifying cryptoassets and equivalent high-risk investments
- **Non-mass market investments** — speculative illiquid securities (mini-bonds, etc.)
- **COBS 22-restricted products** — products subject to distribution restrictions

These products remain within the appropriateness or full advice regimes.

### 4. Annuities (COBS 9B.4.28R)
Targeted support can suggest that an annuity may be appropriate for a segment, but **cannot recommend a specific annuity product**, obtain or share quotes, or go further than directing the client to MoneyHelper or a whole-of-market comparison service.

---

## The Commission Ban

**(COBS 9B.8.2R)** A firm providing targeted support must not solicit or accept — and must ensure its associates do not solicit or accept — any **fees, commissions, or monetary or non-monetary benefits from third parties** in connection with:
- Providing the targeted support service itself
- Arranging or executing a transaction that was the subject of a ready-made suggestion
- Managing a client relationship with a discretionary investment manager (DIM)
- Recommending a DIM or IFA to the client

**The ban applies regardless of whether the firm intends to pass the benefit on to the client.**

The only meaningful exception is the narrow **whole-of-market annuity brokerage referral** route (COBS 9B.8.4R): commissions are permitted where the targeted support relates exclusively to annuities, follows the 9B.4.28R–9B.4.37G special rules, and involves a referral to a whole-of-market brokerage.

**Note:** COBS 6.1A adviser charging rules do **not** apply to targeted support. Targeted support has its own remuneration framework.

---

## The Mandatory Disclosure Package

At the point of delivery of a ready-made suggestion, the firm must provide (in a durable medium — COBS 9B.6.17R):

1. **"Targeted Support" label** (COBS 9B.6.1R): The service must be described as "Targeted Support" at the point the suggestion is provided.
2. **Nature and limitations** (COBS 9B.6.3R): The suggestion is not comprehensive, individualised investment advice.
3. **Segment characteristics** (COBS 9B.6.5R): The including and excluding characteristics of the consumer segment; that the suggestion was designed for the segment, not the individual; and any scope limitations (e.g., if only the firm's own products were considered).
4. **Article 55A RAO statutory statement** (COBS 9B.6.6G): Three-part statement that — (a) the recommendation is not based on comprehensive consideration of the client's individual circumstances; (b) it is not specific to the client; (c) it is based on the characteristics of the group.

A firm that satisfies the Art. 55A(4)–(5) RAO statement obligation simultaneously satisfies elements of 9B.6.3R and 9B.6.5R (COBS 9B.6.7G).

## Relationship to Other Regimes

| Regime | Interaction |
|---|---|
| COBS 9/9A (suitability) | Targeted support displaces suitability for the suggestion — no individual suitability assessment required. Periodic individual suitability reviews (COBS 9A.3.6R) are not required. |
| COBS 10/10A (appropriateness) | Targeted support displaces appropriateness — firm is making a suggestion, not responding to a client-initiated transaction. |
| COBS 4 (financial promotions) | Ready-made suggestions will generally incorporate a financial promotion — COBS 4 obligations apply alongside COBS 9B (COBS 9B.6.15G). |
| PROD 3 (product governance) | Where the firm is also the manufacturer, PROD 3 obligations continue to apply. Target market assessments are relevant to consumer segment design. |
| Consumer Duty (PRIN 2A) | Applies in full — in particular the communications rules (PRIN 2A.5.3R) and the obligation to give clients sufficient opportunity to understand their options (PRIN 2A.6.2R(3)). |

---

## Systems and Controls Obligations

**(COBS 9B.10.1R)** Firms must maintain systems and controls covering the design and delivery of the targeted support service. Specific obligations:
- Employees must be appropriately trained to identify client alignment and to respond appropriately to information beyond what is needed for alignment (COBS 9B.10.3G)
- Ongoing monitoring of outcomes at the segment and product level — not individual periodic reassessments (COBS 9B.10.4G/10.5G)
- Regular review of consumer segments and ready-made suggestions for continued appropriateness (COBS 9B.10.9R)

---

## Key Points for Agents

- **Three absolute prohibitions**: safeguarded benefits (advice first — COBS 9B.2.3R); pension consolidation (never via targeted support — COBS 9B.4.38R); RMMIs/non-mass market/COBS 22-restricted products (always excluded — COBS 9B.4.42R).
- **The commission ban** (9B.8.2R) covers not just the suggestion itself but also the resulting transaction, DIM relationship management, and IFA referrals. The only exception is the narrow whole-of-market annuity brokerage referral route.
- **The Art. 55A RAO disclosure package** is mandatory in a durable medium at point of delivery: not comprehensive, not specific to the client, based on group characteristics. Missing any element is a breach of COBS 9B.6.
- **Known-unsuitability override** (9B.5.11R): segment-level suitability is a floor, not a ceiling. If the firm has individual-level information indicating unsuitability, it must not deliver the suggestion regardless of segment alignment.
- **No individual periodic review** required — unlike COBS 9A advisory relationships, targeted support monitoring is at segment and product level only.

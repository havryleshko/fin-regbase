# Agent and Distributor Oversight — Payment Institutions and E-Money Institutions

## Overview

PIs and EMIs can extend their activities through agents and (for EMIs) distributors. The PI or EMI bears full regulatory responsibility for the conduct of those third parties. The FCA identified inadequate due diligence and monitoring of agents and distributors as a named root cause of governance failures across the payments portfolio. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023, Cross-Cutting Priority 1)**

---

## Agents — The Framework

### What Agents Do

Agents provide **payment services** on behalf of a PI or EMI. They act as an extension of the authorised firm's regulated activities and are therefore treated as part of the firm for regulatory purposes.

### Registration Requirement

**It is the responsibility of the PI or EMI to ensure that its agents are registered with the FCA.** Agents cannot provide payment services on behalf of a PI or EMI without being on the FCA Register. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

The registration process requires the PI or EMI to submit agent details to the FCA. Unregistered agents providing payment services create a direct regulatory breach by the PI or EMI.

### Fitness and Propriety

The PI/EMI must satisfy itself that:
- Directors and individuals responsible for providing payment services (including agents) are **fit and proper**
- Those individuals have **appropriate knowledge and experience** for the activities they carry out

**(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

### Ongoing Compliance Responsibility

The PI or EMI must take **all reasonable steps** to ensure agents comply with the PSRs/EMRs at onboarding and on an ongoing basis. This requires:
- Appropriate due diligence before onboarding an agent
- Ongoing monitoring of the agent's activities
- Systems and controls sufficient to oversee agent conduct effectively

**(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

**Named FCA failure:** Inadequate meaningful due diligence before onboarding agents and a lack of ongoing monitoring. The FCA treats this as a governance failure at the PI/EMI level, not just a deficiency of the agent. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

---

## Distributors — The Framework (EMIs Only)

### What Distributors Do

EMI distributors **distribute and redeem e-money** on behalf of an EMI. This is a narrower function than agent activity.

**Critical distinction: distributors are NOT permitted to provide payment services.** Only agents can provide payment services. A third party that is used as a distributor but also provides payment services is acting in excess of its permitted scope — and the EMI that permitted this arrangement is in breach. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

### Notification Requirement (Not Registration)

Unlike agents, **individual distributors do not need to be registered with the FCA**. However, EMIs must **identify their proposed use of distributors to the FCA** — notification rather than registration. **(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

### Responsibility for Distributor Conduct

An EMI is responsible for **anything done or omitted by a distributor** in carrying out activities on behalf of the EMI. The same logic applies as for agents: the EMI cannot delegate regulatory responsibility to the distributor.

---

## Agent vs. Distributor — Key Distinctions

| Feature | Agent (PI or EMI) | Distributor (EMI only) |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Permitted to provide payment services | Yes | **No** |
| Permitted to distribute/redeem e-money | Yes (if EMI appoints them as both) | Yes |
| FCA registration required | Yes — PI/EMI must register them | No — FCA notification only |
| PI/EMI responsible for conduct | Yes | Yes |
| Individual due diligence required | Yes | Yes |
| Ongoing monitoring required | Yes | Yes |

## Communications Through Agents and Distributors

Where agents or distributors **advertise or promote** the regulated activities of the PI or EMI, those communications must:
- Be **fair, clear and not misleading**
- Make clear **for which PI or EMI** the agent or distributor is acting

**(Portfolio letter, March 2023)**

This means the PI or EMI must have oversight of promotional materials produced by agents and distributors. A communications failure by an agent or distributor in this context is a communications failure by the PI or EMI.

---

## Governance Framework for Agent/Distributor Management

Firms should operate a governance structure that includes, at minimum:

1. **Due diligence at onboarding** — covering fitness and propriety of individuals, business model assessment, compliance capability
2. **Written agreements** — setting out the scope of permitted activities, compliance obligations, and reporting requirements
3. **Ongoing monitoring** — transaction monitoring, complaint analysis, periodic reviews
4. **Escalation procedures** — clear routes for addressing non-compliance by agents/distributors, up to and including termination
5. **Board-level oversight** — the principal firm's board must receive MI covering agent/distributor conduct as part of its governance responsibilities

---

## Relationship to Consumer Duty

Where agents or distributors interact with retail customers, those interactions fall within the Consumer Duty scope as interactions carried out on behalf of the PI or EMI. The PI or EMI cannot rely on a third-party relationship to reduce its Consumer Duty obligations. The Products & Services outcome requires firms to consider how the distribution chain affects retail customer outcomes — agent and distributor oversight is part of this. *(PS22/9 §6.1; FG22/5)*

---

## Key Points for Agents

- Agents provide payment services on behalf of a PI/EMI and must be registered with the FCA — it is the PI/EMI's responsibility to ensure registration, not the agent's.
- Distributors distribute and redeem e-money only — they cannot provide payment services. EMIs must notify the FCA of proposed use of distributors; individual registration is not required.
- The PI/EMI is responsible for anything done or omitted by its agents and distributors — there is no pass-through of regulatory liability.
- Due diligence at onboarding and ongoing monitoring are both mandatory. Inadequate monitoring is a named FCA governance failure.
- All agent/distributor communications promoting regulated activities must be fair, clear, not misleading, and identify the authorised firm.

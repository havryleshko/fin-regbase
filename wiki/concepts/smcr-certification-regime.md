# SM&CR — Certification Regime, Fitness and Propriety, and Regulatory References

## What the Certification Regime Is

The Certification Regime covers functions that are not Senior Management Functions (SMFs) but which can have a **significant impact on customers, the firm, and/or market integrity**. The design reflects a deliberate policy choice: firms are better placed than the regulator to assess whether the specific people doing these roles are fit and proper, given the volume and diversity of individuals involved.

The key distinctions from the Senior Managers Regime:

| Feature | Senior Managers Regime | Certification Regime |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|
| Who approves the individual | FCA (before performing the function) | **The firm** |
| FCA Register | Yes | Yes (PS19/7) |
| Frequency | One-off approval (kept under review) | At least **annually** |
| Standard | FIT test | FIT test |

The Certification Regime is a requirement under FSMA, set out in SYSC 27 of the FCA Handbook.

---

## Who the Certification Regime Applies To

The Certification Regime applies to **employees only** (including contractors and secondees who personally provide services under an arrangement where they are subject to the firm's supervision, direction or control). It does not apply to Non-Executive Directors.

In very small firms where all relevant roles are held by Senior Managers supported only by ancillary staff, there may be **no one in the Certification Regime at all**. If a sole trader has no employees, the Certification Regime does not apply.

The regime applies on a **legal entity basis**: the whole SM&CR, including the Certification Regime, applies to each legal entity in a group separately.

## Certification Functions (8)

| Certification Function | Key Detail |
|------------------------|-----------|
| **Significant Management Function (CF29 equivalent)** | Individuals with significant responsibility for a business unit — detail in SYSC 27.7 |
| **Proprietary traders** | All proprietary traders, without restriction to revenue-generating areas |
| **CASS Operational Oversight (CF10a equivalent)** | Oversight of the operational effectiveness of a firm's CASS systems and controls for client money and assets |
| **Functions subject to qualification requirements** | Mortgage advisers, retail investment advisers, pension transfer specialists, and others listed in the Training and Competence Sourcebook. Includes for example: mortgage advisers, retail investment advisers |
| **Client dealing functions** | Individuals who deal with clients in ways that can significantly affect the client relationship. Expanded from APR CF30. |
| **Supervisors of Certification Function staff** | Those who manage or supervise any person performing one of the above Certification Functions |
| **Material Risk Takers** | As defined under the applicable remuneration code (relevant to larger and more complex IFPRU firms) |
| **Algorithmic trading** | Those involved in the creation or deployment of algorithmic trading systems in ways that can significantly affect the firm or the market |

## Annual Certification Obligation

Firms must check and confirm (certify) at least once a year that each Certification Function holder is fit and proper to perform their function. The certificate issued by the firm must:

- State that the authorised firm is satisfied the person is a fit and proper person to perform the relevant Certification Function
- Set out the aspects of the firm's business in which the individual will be involved

The certification assessment must take into account whether the individual:
- Has obtained any relevant qualification
- Has undergone, or is undergoing, relevant training
- Possesses a relevant level of competence

If a firm concludes a Certification Function holder is no longer fit and proper, it must not certify them and must notify the FCA accordingly.

## Fitness and Propriety (FIT Test)

The FCA's FIT sourcebook applies to all SM&CR populations — Senior Managers, Certification Function holders, and non-approved NEDs. Three elements must all be satisfied:

### Element 1: Honesty, Integrity and Reputation

Includes consideration of:
- Criminal convictions (including spent convictions in some circumstances)
- Civil judgements and regulatory findings in the UK or overseas
- Adverse findings in any regulatory investigation or disciplinary proceedings
- Whether the person has been involved in any business association with persons of known bad reputation

### Element 2: Competence and Capability

- Relevant qualifications and training
- Knowledge of the regulatory environment
- Sufficient skills and experience for the role being performed
- Previous conduct in similar roles

### Element 3: Financial Soundness

Financial soundness is not assessed based solely on wealth. The focus is on:
- History of financial defaults, county court judgements, or insolvency
- Whether any issues arose from dishonesty, recklessness, or other character concerns (a single adverse event in difficult personal circumstances carries less weight than a pattern)

## Criminal Records Checks

Criminal records checks are required as part of the F&P assessment, but the requirement is not uniform across populations:

| Population | Criminal Records Check |
|------------|----------------------|
| Senior Managers (all SMF holders) | **Mandatory** |
| Non-Executive Directors (including non-approved NEDs) | **Mandatory** |
| Certification Function staff | **Firm's discretion** — not required by the Handbook |
| Other conduct rule staff | Not required |

This is a meaningful operational distinction: firms cannot simply apply the same onboarding process to all SM&CR populations and assume they are compliant.

## Regulatory References

### When Required

Firms must obtain a regulatory reference from previous employers when hiring:
- Senior Managers (SMF holders)
- Certification Function staff
- Non-Executive Directors (including non-approved NEDs)

The lookback period is **6 years**.

### What Must Be Disclosed

The regulatory reference regime imposes **mandatory disclosure obligations** on the issuing firm. The reference must include:

- Any breaches of Conduct Rules that occurred in the lookback period
- Any findings that the person was not fit and proper
- Disciplinary history relating to honesty, integrity, or professional competence
- Any relevant ongoing or concluded investigations by the FCA or PRA

### NDAs and Serious Misconduct

**Non-disclosure agreements cannot suppress information that must be disclosed under the regulatory reference rules.** An NDA that purports to prevent disclosure of conduct rule breaches or F&P findings is ineffective to that extent.

**No time limit for serious misconduct:** Where a person left a firm before an investigation concluded, but the firm would have concluded there was serious misconduct, the **6-year lookback does not apply**. The firm must disclose the information regardless of when the events occurred.

### Requesting Updated References

A firm that has already hired an individual can request an updated regulatory reference from the person's previous employer if new relevant information may have come to light since the original reference.

## Key Points for Agents

- The Certification Regime requires **firm-issued** annual certificates — the FCA does not approve Certification Function holders. The firm bears responsibility for ensuring these individuals are fit and proper.
- Eight Certification Functions: Significant Management (CF29 equivalent), proprietary traders, CASS Operational Oversight, qualification-required functions, client dealing, supervisors of certified staff, Material Risk Takers, algorithmic trading.
- FIT test — three elements: honesty/integrity/reputation; competence/capability; financial soundness. All three must be satisfied.
- **Criminal records checks are mandatory for SMs and NEDs only** — discretionary for Certification staff. Applying a one-size-fits-all check policy is fine but is not a Handbook requirement for Certification staff.
- Regulatory references: 6-year lookback. NDAs cannot prevent required disclosures. Where the person left before an investigation concluded but serious misconduct would have been found, **there is no time limit** — the 6-year rule does not apply.
- The Certification Regime applies on a legal entity basis. It applies to employees (including contractors/secondees) but **not to Non-Executive Directors** as such.

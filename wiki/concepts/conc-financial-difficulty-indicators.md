# CONC — Financial Difficulty Indicators

## What This Is

CONC 1.3.1G provides a non-exhaustive list of circumstances that may indicate a customer is in financial difficulties — and that a firm **is aware or ought reasonably to be aware of**. These are the operational signals that activate obligations in later CONC chapters, principally CONC 6 (post-contractual requirements) and CONC 7 (arrears, default and recovery).

This is guidance (G), not a rule. But the "ought reasonably to be aware" standard means a firm cannot claim ignorance if these signals appear in data it holds or has access to.

## The Seven Indicators

**(CONC 1.3.1G)**

| # | Indicator | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Consecutively failing to meet **minimum repayments** on a credit card or store card | Pattern of consecutive misses — not a single missed payment |
| 2 | **Adverse accurate entries on a credit file**, not in dispute | Disputed entries do not trigger this indicator; accurate ones do |
| 3 | Outstanding **county court judgments** for non-payment of debt | CCJs are publicly accessible — firms should screen for these |
| 4 | **Inability to meet repayments from disposable income** | Evidenced by: non-payment of essential bills (utilities); borrowing further to repay existing debts; repaying only by disposing of assets or security |
| 5 | Consecutively failing to meet **repayments when due** (not limited to minimums) | Broader than indicator 1 — applies to all credit repayments |
| 6 | **Agreement to a debt management plan** or other debt solution | Formal engagement with a debt solution is unambiguous |
| 7 | Evidence of **discussions with any firm** (including a not-for-profit debt advice body) with a view to a DMP, debt solution, or debt counselling | Pre-DMP discussions count — does not require a formal agreement |

## How These Indicators Interact with CONC 6 and 7

These indicators are definitional building blocks. When they are present, firms have specific obligations under:

- **CONC 6.7**: Post-contract business practices — including requirements to treat customers in or approaching difficulties appropriately
- **CONC 7.2**: Obligation to have clear, effective and appropriate policies for customers in or approaching arrears
- **CONC 7.3**: Specific treatment standards for customers in or approaching arrears — lenders, owners and debt collectors

The indicators are forward-looking: a firm that identifies any of these signals in its data should treat the customer as potentially in financial difficulty, not wait for formal default.

## Relationship to Vulnerability

Financial difficulty is distinct from but overlapping with vulnerability. A customer in financial difficulty is not automatically a vulnerable customer under FG21/1 **(see [[vulnerable-customers]])**. However:

- Financial difficulty is one of the four HLRC vulnerability drivers (Life Events — job loss, debt, relationship breakdown)
- A customer experiencing indicators 4, 6 or 7 is likely to be showing signs of low financial resilience — one of the four vulnerability characteristics
- Firms should consider whether financial difficulty indicators warrant a vulnerability assessment, not just arrears treatment

The CONC 1.3.1G indicators are CONC-specific operational triggers. The FG21/1 vulnerability framework is broader and applies across all FCA-regulated activities.

## The "Ought Reasonably to Be Aware" Standard

This phrase sets a constructive knowledge standard. A firm cannot ignore signals in its own data. Relevant sources firms are expected to monitor include:

- Internal account behaviour (payment history, minimum payment patterns)
- Credit bureau data (adverse entries, CCJs) where obtained
- Customer communications (calls, messages mentioning debt advice or DMP discussions)
- Open banking transaction data (where consented)

This standard will be assessed by the FCA in the context of what data the firm held and what a reasonable firm of that type would have done with it.

## Key Points for Agents

- CONC 1.3.1G lists 7 indicators of financial difficulty — non-exhaustive, but the primary operational trigger list for CONC 6 and 7 obligations.
- The standard is "aware or ought reasonably to be aware" — constructive knowledge applies; firms cannot ignore their own data.
- Consecutive missed minimum repayments on cards and consecutive missed repayments on any credit are distinct indicators (1 and 5).
- Pre-DMP discussions (indicator 7) count as a financial difficulty signal — firms do not need to wait for a formal debt solution to be in place.
- Financial difficulty and vulnerability overlap but are not the same. Financial difficulty indicators should prompt a vulnerability assessment, not just arrears treatment procedures.

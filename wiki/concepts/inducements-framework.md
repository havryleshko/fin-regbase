# Inducements Framework — COBS 2.3A

## What This Document Covers

COBS 2.3A sets out the FCA's inducements regime for MiFID, equivalent third country or optional exemption (ETT/OE) business and for IBIP distribution. It governs when a firm may accept or pay fees, commissions, and non-monetary benefits in connection with the provision of investment services to clients. The chapter is structured around three overlapping obligations: a general prohibition (with exceptions), a hard UK retail ban with no residual discretion, and a transfer-back obligation for professional clients and overseas retail clients.

This framework applies entirely separately from — but in parallel with — the conflicts of interest regime in SYSC 10, the best execution obligation in COBS 11.2A, and the PFOF prohibition in COBS 11.2A.19R. Breach of COBS 2.3A.5R automatically triggers breach of SYSC 10 and COBS 2.1.1R **(COBS 2.3A.7E)**.

---

## The Three-Track Structure

The inducements regime operates on three tracks that depend on the type of service and the client category.

| Track | Applies to | Obligation |
|-------|-----------|------------|
| **UK retail ban** | Independent advice, restricted advice, portfolio management → UK retail clients | Absolute ban on accepting third-party fees/commissions/NMBs. Exceptions only: acceptable minor NMBs (COBS 2.3A.19R) and COBS 2.3B research |
| **Transfer-back** | Independent advice, portfolio management → overseas retail or professional clients | May not accept and retain third-party fees/commissions/monetary benefits — must transfer all received amounts back to client as soon as reasonably possible |
| **General prohibition + exceptions** | All other MiFID/IBIP business | Third-party payments prohibited unless: (a) quality-enhancing under 2.3A.8R + disclosed; (b) operational payments necessary for service with no conflict potential; or (c) COBS 2.3B research |

**The UK retail ban (Track 1) displaces Tracks 2 and 3 for UK retail clients receiving independent/restricted advice or PM.** The quality enhancement test is irrelevant in Track 1 — there is no residual discretion, and no amount of enhanced service justifies accepting and retaining third-party payments. The transfer-back obligation (Track 2) applies only to overseas retail and professional clients receiving independent advice or PM — not to restricted advice recipients **(COBS 2.3A.16R(1))**.

---

## Track 1 — UK Retail Ban (COBS 2.3A.15R)

Where a firm provides **UK retail clients** with independent advice, restricted advice or portfolio management, it must not accept any fees, commission, monetary or non-monetary benefits paid or provided by any third party.

**The ban is absolute.** It applies to the firm's entire advisory business and related services **(COBS 2.3A.15R(2A))**. It does not require consideration of quality enhancement, client knowledge, or the amount involved.

**Only two permitted categories:**
1. Acceptable minor non-monetary benefits satisfying all four criteria and within the 12 named types in COBS 2.3A.19R; and
2. Third party research received in accordance with COBS 2.3B.

**Practical implication:** Any product provider paying trail commission or other remuneration to a UK financial adviser in relation to independent or restricted advice is in breach regardless of whether the adviser has obtained informed consent or made disclosure to the client. The ban is structural, not disclosure-based.

---

## Track 2 — Transfer-Back for Professional and Overseas Retail (COBS 2.3A.16R)

Where a firm provides **independent advice or portfolio management** to overseas retail clients or professional clients, it may not accept and retain third-party fees/commissions/monetary benefits.

Unlike the UK retail ban, the firm **receives** the amounts but must **transfer them back** to the client:
- As soon as reasonably possible after receipt;
- In full (no netting or set-off);
- Under a documented policy for allocation and transfer; and
- With notification to the client via periodic reporting statements.

The permitted non-monetary exceptions are the same as Track 1: acceptable minor NMBs (COBS 2.3A.19R) and COBS 2.3B research.

**Critical distinction from Track 1:** Restricted advice is not in scope of Track 2. A firm providing restricted advice (not independent advice or PM) to a professional client falls under Track 3 only — the general prohibition with exceptions. The Track 2 transfer-back obligation does not apply.

---

## Track 3 — General Prohibition With Exceptions (COBS 2.3A.5R / 2.3A.6R)

For all other MiFID/IBIP business not caught by Tracks 1 or 2, the general prohibition applies: no third-party fees, commissions or non-monetary benefits may be accepted, paid, received or provided.

Three exceptions carve out permitted payments.

### Exception (a): Quality-Enhancing Benefits

The most significant exception. The fee, commission or NMB must:
1. Be designed to enhance the quality of the relevant service to the client; AND
2. Not impair the firm's duty to act honestly, fairly and professionally in clients' best interests.

The quality enhancement test in **COBS 2.3A.8R** operationalises these two conditions via **four cumulative requirements**:
- Additional or higher-level service proportional to the inducement;
- No direct benefit to the firm/shareholders/employees without tangible client benefit;
- Justified by ongoing benefit to client for any ongoing inducement;
- No bias or distortion of service as result of the inducement.

These four conditions must be satisfied **on an ongoing basis** for as long as the firm continues to pay or receive the inducement — not just at inception.

**Disclosure is mandatory** (not optional) for quality-enhancing benefits: prior to service, the firm must disclose the existence, nature, and amount (or calculation method) of the inducement. Where amounts are unknown in advance, an ex-post disclosure of actual amounts is also required **(COBS 2.3A.11R)**. For ongoing inducements, an annual disclosure of actual amounts received is required **(COBS 2.3A.12R)**.

**Quality-enhancing examples (COBS 2.3A.9R):** Restricted advice plus broad instrument access from unlinked providers; restricted advice combined with annual suitability review or other ongoing service; competitive access to broad range of instruments with decision-support tools.

### Exception (b): Operational Payments

Payments that **enable or are necessary** for the service and **by their nature cannot give rise to conflicts** — custody costs, settlement fees, exchange fees, regulatory levies, legal fees. These are excluded from the prohibition entirely and require no disclosure beyond the normal costs and charges framework.

### Exception (c): COBS 2.3B Research

Third-party research received in accordance with COBS 2.3B is expressly carved out from the prohibition for MiFID/ETT/OE business **(COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(c))**. COBS 2.3B governs research payment accounts (RPAs) and the unbundling of research costs from execution commissions. This is the regulatory interface between the inducements regime and the PFOF framework.

---

## The IBIP Overlay — COBS 2.3A.9AR

For insurance-based investment products, additional requirements apply on top of the general quality enhancement test. An inducement **has a detrimental impact** where it is of such a nature and scale that it provides an incentive to carry out insurance distribution in a way not in clients' best interests.

Firms distributing IBIPs must conduct an **overall analysis** of all factors that may increase or decrease the risk of detrimental impact, considering six specific criteria:

1. Incentive to recommend an IBIP over a better-fitting alternative;
2. Whether criteria are predominantly quantitative (sales volume) vs qualitative (compliance, service quality, client satisfaction);
3. Value of inducement relative to IBIP value and services provided;
4. Whether inducement is front-loaded (paid at conclusion) or distributed over policy term;
5. Existence of a reclaim mechanism if IBIP lapses or is surrendered early or client interests harmed;
6. **Volume/value accelerator test:** existence of any variable or contingent threshold or value accelerator unlocked by attaining a volume or value target.

**The volume/value accelerator (criterion 6) is the sharpest test.** Any inducement structure where the rate or amount increases once sales targets are hit — tiered commissions, bonus tranches, performance uplifts — is presumptively detrimental. The existence of such a mechanism does not automatically make the arrangement unlawful, but it triggers an elevated burden on the firm to demonstrate that the structure does not in practice incentivise biased recommendations.

## Acceptable Minor Non-Monetary Benefits — COBS 2.3A.19R

Acceptable minor NMBs are a distinct category relevant in all three tracks. In Tracks 1 and 2 they are the only permitted non-monetary benefit (alongside COBS 2.3B research). In Track 3 they qualify as a subset of exception (a) quality-enhancing benefits.

### Four Threshold Criteria

All four must be met:
1. Clearly disclosed prior to service (generic description where applicable);
2. Capable of enhancing service quality;
3. Cannot be judged to impair compliance with the best interests duty;
4. Reasonable, proportionate and unlikely to influence firm behaviour detrimentally.

### Key Types From the 12-Type List (COBS 2.3A.19R(5))

**Types (h) and (i) — research carve-outs:**
- Type (h): Third-party research on **fixed income, currency or commodity instruments** — receivable by firms providing investment services or ancillary services. The rationale is that pricing in these markets does not typically factor in research costs, so research on FI/FX/commodities cannot realistically influence order routing.
- Type (i): Research from providers **not engaged in execution services** and not in a financial services group including an investment firm offering execution or brokerage. The independence from execution is the qualifying condition — it eliminates the PFOF-adjacent conflict.

**Type (f) — research trial periods:**
- Maximum 3-month trial;
- No consideration at any time (before, during or after trial);
- 12-month gap required before the same provider can offer another trial (including any previous trial period);
- Firm must record dates and how conditions were satisfied.

This type is critical for investment managers considering research relationships before committing to a paid arrangement — the 12-month gap rule prevents serial trial periods from becoming a back-door free research arrangement.

**Type (k) — corporate access services:**
- Only for companies with market cap **below £200m**;
- For larger companies, corporate access services are not acceptable minor NMBs and would need to qualify under the general quality-enhancing exception or as COBS 2.3B research.

**Type (l) — short-term trading commentary:**
- Does not contain substantive analysis;
- Includes bespoke trade advisory services intrinsically linked to execution of a transaction.
- Material that reiterates a view based on existing recommendations or substantive research is **not** type (l) **(COBS 2.3A.22G)**.

## Interaction With Other Regulatory Frameworks

### COBS 11.2A.19R (PFOF Prohibition)

The PFOF prohibition in COBS 11.2A.19R prohibits a firm from receiving any remuneration for routing client orders to a particular venue in a way that infringes SYSC 10 or COBS 2.3A. COBS 2.3A is therefore a **constituent element** of the PFOF prohibition — not a separate parallel regime. A PFOF arrangement that constitutes an inducement under COBS 2.3A.5R cannot be rehabilitated by disclosure; it must be restructured or eliminated.

See [[payment-for-order-flow]] for the operative analysis of PFOF structures under this combined framework.

### SYSC 10 (Conflicts of Interest)

COBS 2.3A.7E makes explicit what MiFID article 24(9) presupposes: a breach of COBS 2.3A.5R automatically constitutes a failure to comply with the SYSC 10 conflicts framework (and with COBS 2.1.1R best interests). The inducements prohibition is the specific conflicts management obligation for third-party payments. SYSC 10 Type 5 conflict (inducements received from third parties) **(SYSC 10.1.4R)** is the structural basis for COBS 2.3A's requirements.

See [[sysc10-conflicts-of-interest]] for the conflicts identification and management framework.

### COBS 9A / COBS 9 (Suitability)

For firms providing personal recommendations, COBS 2.3A intersects suitability in two ways:
- A firm providing UK retail clients with independent or restricted advice under COBS 9A that accepts third-party payments breaches both COBS 2.3A.15R (inducements) and, if those payments bias its recommendations, COBS 9A (suitability).
- PROD 3.3.18R requires distributors to check compliance with, among other rules, COBS 2.3A as part of their product governance arrangements.

### COBS 9B (Targeted Support)

A firm providing a targeted support service under COBS 9B is additionally subject to COBS 9B.8 (commission ban on third-party payments in connection with the service). COBS 2.3A remains potentially relevant where payments are made that are not prohibited by COBS 9B.8 — for example, payments from affiliated companies in relation to the targeted support service **(COBS 2.3A.4CG)**.

### COBS 2.3B (Research Unbundling)

COBS 2.3B governs research payment accounts (RPAs) and joint payments for research and execution services — the two structured routes by which a firm can receive third-party research without it constituting an inducement. The COBS 2.3A.6R(1)(c) carve-out directly incorporates COBS 2.3B — research received under COBS 2.3B is permitted for MiFID/ETT/OE business without any quality enhancement assessment.

**Three permissible routes (COBS 2.3B.3R):** Direct payment from own resources; RPA meeting COBS 2.3B.4R; joint payments meeting COBS 2.3B.25R. Research received outside these routes is an inducement under COBS 2.3A.

**Core RPA condition — volume/value de-linkage (COBS 2.3B.4R(1)(b)):** The RPA research charge to clients must not be linked to the volume or value of transactions executed. This is the definitional requirement that separates an RPA from a bundled commission. RPA surpluses must be rebated or carried forward, never retained **(COBS 2.3B.8R(3))**.

**Research is not an execution factor — hard Rule (COBS 2.3B.29R):** Research services must not be treated as an execution factor under COBS 11.2A.2R. This prevents re-bundling research into best execution analysis.

**Negative list (COBS 2.3B.23G):** 12 categories cannot be paid from an RPA or joint payments, including post-trade analytics, price feeds, portfolio valuation, seminar fees, corporate access services, OMS, and the administration costs of the RPA itself.

See [[research-unbundling]] and [[cobs2-3b-inducements-research]] for the full treatment.

## Record-Keeping Obligations

**COBS 2.3A.32R** requires firms to hold two types of evidence:
1. An **internal list** of all fees, commissions and NMBs received from third parties; and
2. A **written record** of how each item enhances service quality and the steps taken to ensure it does not impair the duty to clients.

The list alone is insufficient. The firm must document the quality enhancement link for each item. A generic statement that "we believe all payments on the list enhance service quality" does not satisfy condition (2) — the record must be specific to each payment type.

**COBS 2.3A.34AR** adds the requirement to record the information provided to clients in compliance with the disclosure obligations, as part of the firm's SYSC 9 record-keeping framework for MiFID business.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- **Three tracks, not one.** UK retail + independent/restricted advice/PM = absolute ban. Professional/overseas retail + independent advice/PM = accept but transfer back in full. All other MiFID/IBIP = prohibited unless quality-enhancing (with mandatory disclosure), operational, or COBS 2.3B research. The track determines the obligation; never apply the quality enhancement test to a UK retail advised client.
- **Quality enhancement is four cumulative conditions, ongoing (COBS 2.3A.8R(2)).** A one-time assessment at the point of arranging an inducement is not enough. The firm must continue to satisfy all four conditions for as long as it pays or receives the inducement. Periodic review of inducement arrangements is mandatory as a matter of compliance — not guidance.
- **The IBIP volume/value accelerator (COBS 2.3A.9AR(3)(f)) is the sharpest IBIP test.** Tiered commissions, bonus tranches, or any payment that increases on hitting a sales target are presumptively detrimental. Fintech insurance distribution platforms that receive variable commissions from insurers must perform an overall analysis and document it.
- **Types (h) and (i) of acceptable minor NMBs are the primary research routes for investment managers not using COBS 2.3B RPAs.** Type (h) is for FI/FX/commodity research; type (i) is for independent research providers not in an execution group. Both are available under Track 3 and as the only NMB route in Tracks 1 and 2.
- **Research trial periods (type (f)) carry a 12-month gap requirement.** A firm cannot run successive free trials with the same provider — there must be a 12-month gap after any termination of a research arrangement (including previous trials) before starting a new trial. Record-keeping is mandatory.
- **Breach of COBS 2.3A.5R = automatic breach of SYSC 10 and COBS 2.1.1R (COBS 2.3A.7E).** The FCA does not need to make separate findings on conflicts or best interests — the inducements breach is self-proving for both. This has enforcement and supervisory significance: a single COBS 2.3A.5R finding generates two additional regulatory breaches without further analysis.
- **Record-keeping (COBS 2.3A.32R) requires both the list AND the quality enhancement explanation for each item.** Firms that maintain a register of inducements without documenting the quality link are in breach of COBS 2.3A.32R(2).

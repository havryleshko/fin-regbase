# FCA Consumer Duty — Consumer Principle (Principle 12)

## The Rule

> **A firm must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers.**
> *(PRIN 2A — Consumer Principle, Principle 12)*

This is the apex obligation of the Consumer Duty. All other Duty rules — the cross-cutting rules and the four outcome rules — elaborate what "acting to deliver good outcomes" means in practice. **(PS22/9 §4.6)**

## Why "Good Outcomes" Not "Best Interests"

The FCA explicitly chose "good outcomes" over "best interests" and "fair treatment." **(PS22/9 §4.6–§4.7)** The reasoning:

- "Best interests" was rejected as less clear in the context of outcomes-based regulation and already carried specific meaning in advice contexts.
- "Fair outcomes" was rejected because the Duty sets a **higher** standard than Principle 6 (treat customers fairly) — using different language signals that distinction.
- "Good outcomes" is defined objectively by the cross-cutting rules and four outcome rules combined. It is not subjective. **(PS22/9 §4.6)**

An agent reasoning about whether a firm's conduct is "treating customers fairly" is applying the pre-Duty standard. The operative question under the Duty is whether the firm is **acting to deliver good outcomes**.

## Disapplication of Principles 6 and 7

Where Principle 12 applies, **Principles 6 and 7 are disapplied**. **(PS22/9 §4.12)**

- Principle 6 (a firm must pay due regard to the interests of its customers and treat them fairly) — disapplied in scope.
- Principle 7 (a firm must pay due regard to the information needs of its clients, and communicate information to them in a way which is clear, fair and not misleading) — disapplied in scope.

Principles 6 and 7 **continue to apply** to: wholesale business; SMEs outside the Duty's scope; other out-of-scope activities. **(PS22/9 §4.12)**

Existing Handbook and non-Handbook material linked to Principles 6 and 7 **remains relevant** — but firms should recognise that Principle 12 imposes a higher standard. Failing to meet existing Principles 6 or 7 guidance will likely also breach Principle 12. **(PS22/9 §4.15)**

## Proportionality

The Duty only requires what can reasonably be expected of a **prudent firm** carrying on the same activity in relation to the same product or service. This is an objective standard — not one firms can self-define. **(PS22/9 §4.8)**

Factors shaping what is "reasonable": **(PS22/9 §4.11)**
- The nature and risk level of the product or service
- The characteristics of the retail customers (e.g. financial capability, vulnerability)
- The firm's role in the distribution chain

A smaller firm has the same obligations as a larger one — but what it takes to meet those obligations will differ in scale. **(PS22/9 §4.11)**

## The "Prudent Firm" Test in Practice

The FCA's self-test question for firms: **(PS22/9 §4.11)**

> "Are we applying the same standards and capabilities to delivering good consumer outcomes as we apply to generating sales and revenue?"

This is a useful framing for compliance assessments and audit purposes.

## SM&CR Individual Conduct Rule 6

Under SM&CR, a new **Individual Conduct Rule 6** requires individuals at all levels to act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers where they carry out activities within scope of the Duty. **(PS22/9 §13.16–§13.22)** Senior managers are accountable for good consumer outcomes within their areas of responsibility.

## Key Points for Agents

- Principle 12 states: firms must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers. This is the single apex rule.
- "Good outcomes" supersedes "treat customers fairly" — TCF compliance does not automatically satisfy Principle 12.
- Principles 6 and 7 are disapplied wherever Principle 12 applies; they remain live for wholesale and out-of-scope business.
- The Duty applies proportionately based on firm role, product risk and customer characteristics — but proportionality does not reduce the obligation, only the steps required to meet it.
- Under SM&CR, Individual Conduct Rule 6 makes the Duty a personal obligation on individuals, not just firms.

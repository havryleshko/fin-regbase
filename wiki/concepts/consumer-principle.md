# FCA Consumer Duty — Consumer Principle (Principle 12)

## The Rule

> **A firm must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers.**
> *(PRIN 2A — Consumer Principle, Principle 12)*

This is the apex obligation of the Consumer Duty. All other Duty rules — the cross-cutting rules and the four outcome rules — elaborate what "acting to deliver good outcomes" means in practice. **(PS22/9 §4.6)**

## Why "Good Outcomes" Not "Best Interests"

The FCA explicitly chose "good outcomes" over "best interests" and "fair treatment." **(PS22/9 §4.6–§4.7)** The reasoning:

- "Best interests" was rejected as less clear in the context of outcomes-based regulation and already carried specific meaning in advice contexts.
- "Fair outcomes" was rejected because the Duty sets a **higher** standard than Principle 6 (treat customers fairly) — using different language signals that distinction.
- "Good outcomes" is defined objectively by the cross-cutting rules and four outcome rules combined. It is not subjective. **(PS22/9 §4.6)**

An agent reasoning about whether a firm's conduct is "treating customers fairly" is applying the pre-Duty standard. The operative question under the Duty is whether the firm is **acting to deliver good outcomes**.

## Disapplication of Principles 6 and 7

Where Principle 12 applies, **Principles 6 and 7 are disapplied**. **(PS22/9 §4.12; PRIN 3.2.10R)**

The binding rule is **PRIN 3.2.10R** (hard Rule): where Principle 12 and PRIN 2A apply to a firm in connection with an activity, Principles 6 and 7 do not apply to that firm in connection with that activity. This is a clean displacement — P6 and P7 are inapplicable, not merely superseded.

- Principle 6 (a firm must pay due regard to the interests of its customers and treat them fairly) — disapplied in scope.
- Principle 7 (a firm must pay due regard to the information needs of its clients, and communicate information to them in a way which is clear, fair and not misleading) — disapplied in scope.

Principles 6 and 7 **continue to apply** to: wholesale business; SMEs outside the Duty's scope; other out-of-scope activities. **(PS22/9 §4.12)**

Existing Handbook and non-Handbook material linked to Principles 6 and 7 **remains relevant** — but firms should recognise that Principle 12 imposes a higher standard. Failing to meet existing Principles 6 or 7 guidance will likely also breach Principle 12. **(PS22/9 §4.15)**

## Proportionality

The Duty only requires what can reasonably be expected of a **prudent firm** carrying on the same activity in relation to the same product or service. This is an objective standard — not one firms can self-define. **(PS22/9 §4.8)**

Factors shaping what is "reasonable": **(PS22/9 §4.11)**
- The nature and risk level of the product or service
- The characteristics of the retail customers (e.g. financial capability, vulnerability)
- The firm's role in the distribution chain

A smaller firm has the same obligations as a larger one — but what it takes to meet those obligations will differ in scale. **(PS22/9 §4.11)**

## The "Prudent Firm" Test in Practice

The FCA's self-test question for firms: **(PS22/9 §4.11)**

> "Are we applying the same standards and capabilities to delivering good consumer outcomes as we apply to generating sales and revenue?"

This is a useful framing for compliance assessments and audit purposes.

## SM&CR Individual Conduct Rule 6

Under SM&CR, a new **Individual Conduct Rule 6** requires individuals at all levels to act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers where they carry out activities within scope of the Duty. **(PS22/9 §13.16–§13.22)** Senior managers are accountable for good consumer outcomes within their areas of responsibility.

**Scope note:** SM&CR applies only to FSMA-authorised firms. Payment institutions and e-money institutions that are not otherwise FSMA-authorised are outside SM&CR scope — Conduct Rule 6 does not apply to their employees as a personal obligation. Those firms' Consumer Duty accountability operates at the firm and board level only. See [[smcr-conduct-rules]] and [[smcr-senior-managers-regime]].

## Activity Scope — Outer Boundary of Principle 12

Principle 12 does not apply to all retail customer activity. Its scope is bounded by **PRIN 3.2.6R** and **PRIN 3.2.8R** (both hard Rules):

- P12 applies only where the regulated activity is addressed by a rule in: COBS, ICOBS, MCOB, BCOBS, CMCOB, FPCOB, PROD, or CONC **(PRIN 3.2.6R)**.
- P12 does **not** apply merely because an activity is mentioned in one of those sourcebooks — it must be addressed by a rule in that sourcebook **(PRIN 3.2.8R)**.

A regulated activity falling outside those sourcebooks is outside P12's scope, even where retail customers are involved. Firms must map each activity to a specific sourcebook rule to determine P12 applicability.

## Cross-Cutting Rules Exhaust Principle 12

**PRIN 2A.2.26R** (hard Rule): A firm that complies with the three cross-cutting rules (act in good faith; avoid foreseeable harm; enable financial objectives) and the four outcome rules is taken to have complied with Principle 12.

There is no residual P12 obligation beyond the specific PRIN 2A rules. Compliance assessment must engage with PRIN 2A.2–2A.6 directly — not with Principle 12 text in the abstract.

## Guarantors as Retail Customers

**PRIN 3.4.3AR** (hard Rule): A person who provides a guarantee or indemnity under a regulated credit agreement, regulated consumer hire agreement, or P2P agreement — and who is not the borrower or hirer under that agreement — is treated as a **retail customer** for the purposes of Principle 12 and PRIN 2A.

This extends the Consumer Duty to guarantor relationships. A firm must deliver good outcomes for guarantors as well as borrowers. There is no equivalent extension in CONC's jurisdictional scope rule (CONC 1.2.5R) — this is P12-specific.

## No Private Right of Action

**PRIN 3.4.4R** (hard Rule): A contravention of a rule in PRIN — including any rule in PRIN 2A — does **not** give rise to a right of action under FSMA s.138D.

Retail customers cannot sue a firm for damages based on a Principle 12 breach under FSMA. Consumer remedies for P12 breaches are: FOS complaint, FCA enforcement (which may produce restitution or redress), and separate private law claims on other causes of action (contract, tort). This is a deliberate structural choice — the Principles are supervision and enforcement tools, not a litigation gateway.

## FCA Notification Duty in Distribution Chains

**PRIN 2A.9.17R** (hard Rule): A firm in a distribution chain must **notify the FCA** if it becomes aware, or has reasonable grounds to believe, that another firm in the chain is or may not be complying with Principle 12 or PRIN 2A.

This applies regardless of whether the non-compliant firm is a manufacturer, distributor, or intermediary. There is no materiality threshold. Awareness triggers an immediate reporting obligation — it cannot be deferred pending internal investigation or commercial negotiation.

## Key Points for Agents

- Principle 12 states: firms must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers. This is the single apex rule.
- "Good outcomes" supersedes "treat customers fairly" — TCF compliance does not automatically satisfy Principle 12.
- Principles 6 and 7 are disapplied wherever Principle 12 applies (PRIN 3.2.10R); they remain live for wholesale and out-of-scope business.
- P12 activity scope is bounded: it only applies where the activity is addressed by a specific rule in COBS, ICOBS, MCOB, BCOBS, CMCOB, FPCOB, PROD, or CONC (PRIN 3.2.6R; PRIN 3.2.8R).
- Compliance with PRIN 2A.2–2A.6 exhausts P12 — there is no residual Principle 12 obligation beyond those rules (PRIN 2A.2.26R).
- Guarantors and indemnifiers under regulated credit, consumer hire, or P2P agreements are retail customers for P12 purposes (PRIN 3.4.3AR).
- P12 breaches do not give retail customers a right to sue for damages under FSMA s.138D (PRIN 3.4.4R). Remedies are FOS, FCA enforcement, and separate private law claims.
- Any firm in a distribution chain must notify the FCA if it becomes aware another firm in the chain may be breaching P12/PRIN 2A (PRIN 2A.9.17R).
- The Duty applies proportionately based on firm role, product risk and customer characteristics — but proportionality does not reduce the obligation, only the steps required to meet it.
- Under SM&CR, Individual Conduct Rule 6 makes the Duty a personal obligation on individuals, not just firms.

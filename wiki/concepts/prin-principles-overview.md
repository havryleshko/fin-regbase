# FCA Principles for Businesses — Overview

## What the Principles Are

The Principles for Businesses are twelve hard Rules that express the **fundamental obligations of every authorised firm** under the FCA's regulatory framework. They derive authority from the FCA's rule-making powers under the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 and reflect the statutory objectives **(PRIN 1.1.2G)**. They are not guidance — they are enforceable obligations, and breach gives rise to disciplinary sanctions.

The Principles are general statements: they do not replace specific rulebook obligations in COBS, CONC, MCOB, and other sourcebooks, but operate alongside them. A firm can breach a Principle even if it has complied with every specific rule, and vice versa.

---

## The Twelve Principles

All 12 Principles are hard Rules **(PRIN 2.1.1R)**:

| No. | Name | Core Obligation |
|-----|------|----------------|
| 1 | Integrity | Conduct business with integrity. |
| 2 | Skill, care and diligence | Conduct business with due skill, care and diligence. |
| 3 | Management and control | Take reasonable care to organise and control affairs responsibly and effectively, with adequate risk management systems. |
| 4 | Financial prudence | Maintain adequate financial resources. |
| 5 | Market conduct | Observe proper standards of market conduct. |
| 6 | Customers' interests | Pay due regard to the interests of customers and treat them fairly. |
| 7 | Communications with clients | Pay due regard to clients' information needs; communicate clearly, fairly and without misleading. |
| 8 | Conflicts of interest | Manage conflicts of interest fairly — between firm and customer, and between customers. |
| 9 | Customers: relationships of trust | Take reasonable care to ensure the suitability of advice and discretionary decisions for any customer entitled to rely on the firm's judgment. |
| 10 | Clients' assets | Arrange adequate protection for clients' assets when responsible for them. |
| 11 | Relations with regulators | Deal with regulators openly and cooperatively; disclose to the FCA anything it would reasonably expect notice of. |
| 12 | Consumer Duty | Act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers. |

---

## Territorial Reach

| Principle | Territorial Reach |
|---|---|
| P1, P2, P3 | UK activities + worldwide in a prudential context |
| P4 | Worldwide |
| P5 | UK activities + worldwide where activity could harm UK financial system confidence |
| P6–P10 | UK activities |
| P11 | Worldwide |
| P12 | Where retail customers are **located in the UK** |

P12's trigger — customers "located" in the UK — is wider than CONC's "habitually resident" test (CONC 1.2.5R). It may catch firms serving customers temporarily present in the UK.

---

## Interaction Between the Principles

### P6 and P7 Displaced by P12

**PRIN 3.2.10R** (hard rule): Where Principle 12 and PRIN 2A apply, Principles 6 and 7 do **not** apply. P12 sets a higher standard than P6 or P7 for retail customers; P6 and P7 remain live for wholesale and out-of-scope business.

### P3, P4, and P11 — Group Activities

Principles 3, 4, and 11 take into account the activities of other members of the firm's group **(PRIN 1.1.5G)**. A parent firm cannot disregard a subsidiary's deficient risk management when assessing its own Principle 3 compliance. A subsidiary's conduct in relation to Principle 11 can be affected by what other group members do.

### P12 — Distribution Chain Limitation

In a distribution chain, each firm is only subject to Principle 12 to the extent it **determines or materially influences** retail customer outcomes **(PRIN 3.2.7R)**. This is not a relief from Principle 12 — it scopes each firm's individual obligation to its actual contribution to outcomes.

---

## Activity Scope of Principle 12

Principle 12 and PRIN 2A apply only where the relevant regulated activity is addressed by a rule in: COBS, ICOBS, MCOB, BCOBS, CMCOB, FPCOB, PROD, or CONC **(PRIN 3.2.6R)**. Regulated activities not addressed by any rule in those sourcebooks are outside P12's scope even if retail customers are involved.

---

## Eligible Counterparty (ECP) Business

For non-MiFID business, a firm may classify certain clients as eligible counterparties under PRIN 1 Annex 1. The effect **(PRIN 3.4.1R)**:
- The only P7 obligation in ECP business is that communications must not be misleading.
- Principles 6, 8, 9, and 10 are effectively disapplied for ECP business.

**ECP classification is not available** for consumer credit, payment services, or e-money activities **(PRIN 3.4.-1R)**.

---

## Consequences of Breach

- **Disciplinary sanctions**: FCA must show the firm was at fault — what constitutes fault varies by Principle **(PRIN 1.1.7G)**.
- **Fit and proper**: Breaching the Principles may call into question whether the firm is still fit and proper for its Part 4A permission **(PRIN 1.1.4G)**.
- **Powers**: Principle breaches trigger FCA's powers to vary permission, investigate, intervene, seek injunctions, or require restitution.
- **No private right of action**: Breach of a PRIN rule does not give any person a right to damages under FSMA s.138D **(PRIN 3.4.4R)**. Consumer remedies are FOS complaints and FCA enforcement.

---

## Extension to Non-Firms

The Principles (and PRIN 2A) apply to payment institutions, e-money institutions, and RAISPs in relation to their regulated payment services and e-money activities **(PRIN 3.1.1AR)**. These entities are not FSMA "firms" but are expressly brought within PRIN's scope. References to "firm" in PRIN 2 and PRIN 2A include these persons.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- All 12 Principles are hard Rules — not guidance. Breach gives rise to disciplinary sanctions, not private damages claims (PRIN 3.4.4R).
- Principle 12 (Consumer Duty) is the apex retail customer obligation. Where P12 applies, P6 and P7 are displaced (PRIN 3.2.10R). P12 is detailed out in PRIN 2A.
- Principles 3, 4, and 11 have group scope — the FCA can assess a firm's compliance in light of what its group members do.
- P4 and P11 apply worldwide. P12 applies where retail customers are *located* in the UK — a wider trigger than habitual residence (PRIN 3.3.1R).
- ECP classification that disapplies P6/P7/P8/P9/P10 is unavailable for credit, payment services, and e-money (PRIN 3.4.-1R).
- Payment institutions, e-money institutions, and RAISPs are expressly caught by PRIN — not merely by sector-specific regulations (PRIN 3.1.1AR).

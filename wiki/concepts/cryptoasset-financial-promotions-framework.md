# Cryptoasset Financial Promotions Framework — Consumer Journey Rules

This page sets out the operative consumer journey rules that apply whenever a firm communicates a Direct Offer Financial Promotion (DOFP) for a qualifying cryptoasset (a Restricted Mass Market Investment, RMMI) to a UK retail consumer. These rules derive from PS23/6 (effective 8 October 2023) and apply equally to FCA-authorised firms and MLR-registered cryptoasset businesses relying on FPO Art. 73ZA.

For the legal communication routes, classification, and FPO exemption disapplications, see [[ps23-6-cryptoasset-financial-promotions]].

---

## The DOFP Sequence — Mandatory Order

A Direct Offer Financial Promotion (DOFP) is a financial promotion that contains either (a) an offer by the firm or another person to enter into a controlled agreement with any responding person, or (b) an invitation to any person responding to the communication to make an offer, **and** which specifies the manner of response or includes a form by which any response may be made. Any promotion with a "buy now" button, an application form, or a mechanism enabling the consumer to place money into an investment is likely to constitute a DOFP. **(PS23/6 §§3.21–3.22)**

The following steps must be completed in order before a DOFP may be shown:

| Step | What the firm must do | Notes |
|------|-----------------------|-------|
| 1 | Show the **risk warning** (mass-market communication) | Risk warning must meet prominence requirements for the medium |
| 2 | Consumer **requests to view the DOFP** — 24-hour cooling-off clock starts here | Only for first-time investors with the firm |
| 3 | Firm begins **on-boarding** — conducts KYC/AML checks, obtains consumer's name | May be done during the cooling-off period |
| 4 | Show the **personalised risk warning** pop-up (using consumer's name) | Must be done before categorisation |
| 5 | Consumer completes **client categorisation** declaration | Declaration valid for 12 months |
| 6 | Firm conducts **appropriateness assessment** | Time taken counts toward the 24-hour cooling-off |
| 7 | **24 hours** have elapsed since step 2 | If steps 3–6 take longer than 24 hours, no additional wait required; consumer must still give active consent to proceed |
| 8 | Firm shows the **DOFP** — consumer may place money in the investment | — |

**(PS23/6 §3.19; §§3.23–3.26, Figure 2)**

The 24-hour cooling-off rule **does not apply to each individual transaction** in a cryptoasset — it applies only when a consumer has never previously received a DOFP from that specific firm. For existing clients, the firm must still ensure client categorisation is current and appropriateness has been assessed for the specific investment. **(PS23/6 §3.28 Our response)**

## Risk Warning

### Standard Risk Warning Text

The prescribed standard risk warning for cryptoasset financial promotions is: **(PS23/6 §3.13 Our response)**

> **Don't invest unless you're prepared to lose all the money you invest. This is a high-risk investment and you should not expect to be protected if something goes wrong. Take 2 mins to learn more.**

The "Take 2 mins to learn more" text must link to the product-specific risk summary.

### Prominence Requirements by Medium

| Requirement | Digital (website, app) | Non-digital (TV/radio, phone, postal) |
|-------------|------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Format | Pop-up or equivalent | Risk warning prominence applies to the summary |
| Placement | Statically fixed in the middle of the screen; the focus of the screen | Prominent |
| Border | Legible, contained in its own border with right bold/underlined text | Legible, contained in its own border with right bold/underlined text |
| Design | Without any feature that reduces visibility or prominence | Without any feature that reduces visibility or prominence |
| "Take 2 mins" link | Must be included | Does not need to be included |

**(PS23/6 §3.8, Table 2)**

### Risk Summary

Firms may tailor the risk summary template where template wording is not relevant to, or does not capture key risks of, the specific cryptoasset being promoted. Requirements: **(PS23/6 §3.13 Our response)**

- Changes must have a **documented rationale** (valid reason must be recorded)
- Amended summaries must remain in the spirit of the FCA template
- FAQ-style consumer-friendly language
- Must not take more than **2 minutes to read**
- There is no requirement for the risk summary to be the same for a particular firm throughout the whole consumer journey

The FCA will monitor and challenge risk summaries it believes do not accurately reflect the relevant risks of the specific cryptoasset being promoted.

## Incentives to Invest — Absolute Ban

### General Rule

Firms must not communicate financial promotions for cryptoassets that offer any monetary or non-monetary benefit that incentivises investment activity — for example, refer-a-friend bonuses or new-joiner bonuses. **(PS23/6 §§3.14–3.24)**

### No Shareholder Benefit Exemption

For RMMIs generally, a "shareholder benefit" carve-out exists — benefits intrinsic to the investment and exclusively bound up with its function or business model are not treated as incentives (e.g. a brewery offering investors a discount on its products). This exemption **does not apply to cryptoassets**. The programmable nature of cryptoassets creates an unacceptable risk that firms would use this exemption to promote benefits that distort consumer investment decisions. **(PS23/6 §3.24 Our response)**

### Intrinsic vs Extrinsic Benefits

Although the shareholder benefit exemption is disapplied, the FCA distinguishes between intrinsic and extrinsic benefits:

- **Not an incentive:** Benefits that are intrinsic to the cryptoasset or exclusively bound up with its function and/or business model — e.g. governance or voting rights inherent in a token used for establishing governance arrangements on a platform or project.
- **Likely an incentive:** Benefits that are not intrinsic to the cryptoasset and are used to motivate purchase — e.g. offering additional "free" cryptoassets. A benefit available only for a **limited time period** is also likely to be an incentive regardless of its nature. **(PS23/6 §3.24 Our response)**

## Personalised Risk Warning

Before the DOFP can be communicated, firms must show a **personalised risk warning** pop-up using the consumer's name: **(PS23/6 §§3.29–3.35)**

> **[Client name], this is a high-risk investment. How would you feel if you lost the money you're about to invest? Take 2 min to learn more.**

### Prominence Requirements (Table 3)

| Requirement | Digital | Non-digital |
|-------------|---------|-------------|
| How delivered | Pop-up (or equivalent), clearly brought to client's attention | Must be communicated |
| Prominence | Pop-up prominence requirements — same as main risk warning | No specific requirements |
| "Take 2 mins" text | Must be included; links to risk summary | Does not need to be included |
| Next step | Consumer must be invited to specify whether they wish to leave or continue with the investment journey | Consumer must be invited to specify whether they wish to leave or continue |
| Accompanying risk summary — how delivered | Must be linked from "Take 2 mins to learn more" | Must be provided in a durable medium |
| Accompanying risk summary — prominence | Same pop-up prominence requirements | Prominent; legible in its own border; without features reducing visibility |

## Client Categorisation

Before a DOFP can be communicated, consumers must declare they meet the criteria for one of the following categories: **(PS23/6 §§3.36–3.46 Our response)**

| Category | Criteria | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Restricted Investor** | No more than 10% of net assets invested in RMMIs | Most protective; consumer self-declares compliance with investment limit |
| **High Net Worth Investor** | Annual income ≥ £100,000 or net assets ≥ £250,000 (stated to nearest £10,000/£100,000) | Declaration valid 12 months |
| **Certified Sophisticated Investor** | Certified by an authorised firm as having relevant investment sophistication | The certifying firm cannot subsequently promote to the same individual |
| ~~Self-certified sophisticated investor~~ | ~~Not applicable to cryptoassets~~ | **Disapplied** for cryptoassets — existing criteria are not appropriate proxies for cryptoasset sophistication |

### 12-Month Validity

Investor declarations are valid for **12 months only**. This reflects the possibility of changes in life circumstances (e.g. employment losses) that may affect eligibility. Firms must re-categorise consumers after expiry if they wish to make further DOFPs. **(PS23/6 §3.46 Our response; §3.60c)**

### Category Interaction

Where a consumer is categorised as certified or self-certified sophisticated for some RMMIs (e.g. equity crowdfunding), that categorisation does not count toward the 10% Restricted Investor limit for other RMMIs (e.g. cryptoassets). These are assessed separately. **(PS23/6 §3.46 Our response)**

## Appropriateness Assessment

### Requirement

The specific RMMI must be assessed as appropriate for the consumer **before** an application or order in response to a DOFP can be processed. The assessment must determine that the consumer has the necessary experience and knowledge to understand the risks of the specific product or service. **This obligation applies to all three permitted client categories — Restricted Investor, High Net Worth, and Certified Sophisticated — without exception.** **(PS23/6 §§3.47–3.49; COBS 4.12A; COBS 10.2.1)**

Firms may gather information and conduct the assessment before the DOFP is shown (as part of the on-boarding process). The time taken for the assessment **counts toward the 24-hour cooling-off period**. **(PS23/6 §3.49)*

### Anti-Gaming Rules

The assessment must be designed to resist gaming. Key requirements: **(PS23/6 §§3.50–3.53)**

1. **Different questions each time:** Questions must differ on each assessment attempt — different formulations, a different number of correct answers required, or questions drawn from a question bank in rotation. Firms may use a question bank, alternate sequences, or other technology to achieve this.

2. **24-hour lock-out after failure:** Where an investment is assessed as inappropriate for a consumer, the firm **cannot re-assess the appropriateness of that same investment for the same client for at least 24 hours**. This lock-out applies **from the consumer's second failed attempt onwards** — it is a minimum standard from the second attempt and firms may implement it from the first attempt if they judge this appropriate. **(PS23/6 §3.50)**

3. **No disclosure of specific failed answers:** Firms must **not inform consumers of the specific answers** that caused the assessment to be inappropriate. They may only tell the consumer the **broad topic areas** that caused the failure — not the individual questions answered incorrectly. This prevents consumers from switching only the flagged answer on the next attempt.

4. **No encouragement to retake after failure:** Firms must **not encourage** consumers to retake the assessment after the investment has been assessed as inappropriate, unless the consumer has already indicated they want to on their own initiative. Firms may inform consumers of factual options (e.g. that a lock-out period has ended, or the option to retake). They must not suggest what the consumer should do or apply time pressure. **(PS23/6 §3.53)**

5. **Multiple assessments for diverse product ranges:** Firms offering a wide range of cryptoasset types must ensure their appropriateness assessment covers the risks of the **specific type** of cryptoasset being promoted. Products with distinct risk profiles — e.g. lending, borrowing, staking, or complex yield models — may require separate assessments. **(PS23/6 §3.55 Our response; §3.60d)**

### Position in Consumer Journey

The appropriateness assessment may be conducted before the DOFP is shown, as part of client on-boarding. The rules require the assessment to be completed before the DOFP is processed, not before the DOFP is shown. However, most firms will conduct it before showing the DOFP to integrate it cleanly into the on-boarding flow. **(PS23/6 §3.49)**

## Record Keeping

Firms must keep records of the following metrics for each consumer: **(PS23/6 §§3.56–3.57)**

**Client categorisation:**
- Number of consumers that did not proceed with the consumer journey at client categorisation (i.e. could not be categorised)
- Outcome of client categorisation — the category the consumer was placed into and the reason they believe they meet those criteria

**Appropriateness assessment:**
- Final outcome for each consumer per investment
- Number of times each consumer was subject to the assessment for the same investment
- Number of assessments determining the investment to be appropriate vs inappropriate
- Total number of assessments undertaken

Where the firm communicating the DOFP is not the s21 approver, these records must be available to the approving firm. Approvers should use this data as part of their ongoing monitoring of approved promotions. **(PS23/6 §3.57)**

## Existing vs New Customers — When Each Rule Applies

Figure 3 of PS23/6 sets out a decision tree: **(PS23/6 §§3.58–3.60, Figure 3)**

| Question | If No | If Yes |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Has the consumer previously received a DOFP from the firm? | Comply with all consumer journey rules (including 24-hour cooling-off and personalised risk warning) | → Next question |
| Has the consumer signed a valid investor declaration with the firm within the last 12 months? | Comply with client categorisation rules | → Next question |
| Has the firm assessed the specific investment as appropriate for the consumer? | Comply with appropriateness assessment rules | Can communicate DOFP |

**Key implications:**
- Positive frictions (personalised risk warning + 24-hour cooling-off) apply only to first-time investors with the firm
- All existing clients must still have current client categorisation (within 12 months) before further DOFPs
- All existing clients must still have the **specific investment** assessed as appropriate before each new investment type is offered as a DOFP
- These rules do not restrict consumers from selling their existing cryptoasset investments — the purpose is to protect consumers when investing, not to trap them in investments

## Key Points for Agents

- The DOFP sequence is mandatory and must be followed in order: risk warning → consumer requests DOFP (24-hour clock starts) → KYC/AML + name → personalised risk warning → client categorisation → appropriateness → 24 hours elapsed → DOFP shown.
- **24-hour cooling-off applies only to first-time investors** with the firm. **(PS23/6 §3.19)** Existing investors (those who have previously received a DOFP from that firm) skip this step but must still have current categorisation and appropriateness assessed.
- **Multiple violations in an immediate-retake scenario (e.g., customer fails then passes 5 minutes later):** (a) The 24-hour cooling-off for first-time investors **(PS23/6 §3.19)** has not elapsed — the entire consumer journey cannot complete in minutes; (b) questions must differ between attempts, making a 5-minute pass strongly indicative of question reuse or coaching — a named breach **(PS23/6 §3.51)**; (c) even though the 24-hour lock-out formally applies from the second failed attempt, the firm must use different questions from the first attempt onwards; (d) the firm must not encourage retakes or disclose specific wrong answers — only broad topic areas; (e) processing the investment before the cooling-off period has elapsed and before a valid appropriateness assessment is complete violates both PS23/6 §3.19 and §§3.50–3.53. **(PS23/6 §3.19; §§3.50–3.53)**
- **Self-certified sophisticated investor category does not apply to cryptoassets.** Only Restricted Investor, High Net Worth, and Certified Sophisticated categories are available. **(PS23/6 §3.46; COBS 4.12A)**
- **The appropriateness assessment is mandatory for all three permitted categories** — it cannot be skipped even for High Net Worth or Certified Sophisticated investors. **(PS23/6 §§3.47–3.49; COBS 4.12A)**
- **Client categorisation declarations expire after 12 months** — re-categorisation is required before further DOFPs. **(PS23/6 §3.46)**
- **Incentives ban — absolute, no shareholder benefit exemption, applies to MLR-registered businesses (PS23/6 §3.24; COBS 4.12A):** Cash bonuses, refer-a-friend bonuses, new-joiner bonuses, and free token offers are all prohibited under the absolute incentives ban. Unlike other RMMIs, there is **no shareholder benefit exemption** for cryptoassets. A **time-limited bonus does not create an exemption** — benefits available only for a limited period are likely to be incentives regardless of form. The prohibition applies equally to **FCA-authorised firms and MLR-registered cryptoasset businesses relying on FPO Art. 73ZA** — the same rules apply to both. **(PS23/6 §3.24; COBS 4.12A)**
- The **incentives ban is absolute — no shareholder benefit exemption**. Free token offers and limited-time benefit offers are prohibited. Governance/voting rights intrinsic to a token are not incentives.
- **Appropriateness anti-gaming:** questions must differ each time; only broad topic failure areas may be disclosed (not specific answers); 24-hour lock-out applies from second failed attempt; firms must not encourage retakes. Diverse product ranges may need multiple separate assessments. **(PS23/6 §§3.50–3.53)**
- The **time taken for the appropriateness assessment counts toward** the 24-hour cooling-off period.

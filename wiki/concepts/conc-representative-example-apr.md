# CONC — Representative Example and APR Disclosure Rules

## What This Covers

CONC 3.5 sets out the specific cost-disclosure rules for financial promotions for unsecured consumer credit. These rules determine when a representative example is required, what it must contain, when an APR alone is required, how APRs must be expressed, and which expressions are restricted. CONC 3.6.8R adds additional restricted expressions for secured promotions.

---

## When a Representative Example Is Required

A firm must include a **representative example** (and the firm's postal address) in any financial promotion that **(CONC 3.5.3R)**:

- **Indicates a rate of interest**, or
- **Indicates any cost of credit** — including fees, charges, or total cost references

This trigger is broad. Any mention of cost — even indirectly by referencing a product's affordability — is likely to trigger the representative example requirement. Image advertising (name/logo only, no cost reference) is excluded **(CONC 3.1.12G)**.

---

## Eight Mandatory Elements of the Representative Example (CONC 3.5.5R)

The representative example must include all of the following:

| # | Element | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Rate of interest** | Must state whether fixed or variable |
| 2 | **Other charges** in the total charge for credit | Any fees, arrangement fees, or other charges included in the TCC |
| 3 | **Total amount of credit** | The credit amount advanced to the customer |
| 4 | **Representative APR** | Presented as "%APR"; "representative" if applicable |
| 5 | **Cash price and advance payment** | Required for purchase promotions; omit for straightforward loans |
| 6 | **Duration** | Period of the agreement |
| 7 | **Total amount payable** | Sum of all payments (interest + capital + charges) |
| 8 | **Amount of each repayment** | Must be given where determinable |

**Presentation requirements:**
- All eight elements must be given **equal prominence** — no single element may be visually subordinated to others within the example
- The example must be clearly labelled **"representative example"**
- The example must be given **no less prominence** than any other cost information in the promotion
- The example must be given **no less prominence** than any incentive to apply (gifts, discounts, offers, ease/speed of application)

---

## The "Representative" Threshold

The representative APR must reflect the rate at which the firm **reasonably expects** to enter into at least **51% of regulated credit agreements** concluded from that promotion. If different APRs apply to different customer segments, the representative APR must be the one applicable to the 51% majority **(Consumer Credit (Advertisements) Regulations 2010)**.

A firm cannot cherry-pick its best-rate APR to headline the promotion unless that rate genuinely applies to the majority of customers who respond to it.

---

## Three APR Triggers — No Full Example Required (CONC 3.5.7R)

Even where a full representative example is not triggered (e.g. no cost reference in the promotion), a **representative APR must be stated** where the promotion:

| Trigger | Example |
|---|---|
| **(a) Implies credit is restricted** to certain applicants or only available on certain conditions | "Exclusive to existing customers", "only for those with excellent credit" |
| **(b) Makes a favourable comparison** with another product or provider | "Our rate beats [competitor]", "lower than the market average" |
| **(c) Offers an incentive to apply** | Cashback, gifts, discounts, loyalty points; OR promoting the **speed or ease** of the application process |

**The speed/ease trigger (trigger (c))** is operationally significant: a promotion that says "apply in 3 minutes" or "instant decision" — with no cost information at all — still triggers the requirement to include the representative APR because it offers an incentive (convenience) to apply.

---

## APR Formatting (CONC 3.5.9R)

| Requirement | Format |
|---|---|
| APR notation | Must be shown as **"%APR"** |
| Variable rate | Must include the word **"variable"** |
| Representative figure | Must include the word **"representative"** |
| Overdraft promotions (written) | Must include the statement **"How does our overdraft compare?"** followed by the prescribed comparison methodology |

---

## Compulsory Ancillary Services (CONC 3.5.10R)

Where the credit product is only available with a compulsory ancillary service — for example, a mandatory payment protection insurance or mandatory account fee — the financial promotion must include:

- A **clear, concise and prominent** statement that the ancillary service is required
- This statement must be **presented alongside the representative APR** — not relegated to footnotes or terms

---

## Security Requirement Statement (CONC 3.5.11R)

Where credit is or may be secured (other than land-secured), the promotion must:

- **State that security is or may be required**
- **Specify the nature of the security** (e.g. "secured on your vehicle", "secured on assets")

---

## Restricted Expressions — Unsecured (CONC 3.5.12R)

The following expressions may only be used where the stated literal condition is met:

| Expression | Permitted only when |
|---|---|
| **"overdraft"** | The agreement is a genuine overdraft facility |
| **"interest free"** | Total amount payable ≤ cash price (i.e. no cost to the customer) |
| **"no deposit"** | No advance payment of any kind is required |
| **"gift" / "present"** | The item or benefit has no repayment condition attached whatsoever |
| **"weekly equivalent"** | Repayments are actually made on a weekly basis under the agreement |

A firm that advertises "interest free" where there is an arrangement fee that is included in the total charge for credit — making the total amount payable higher than the cash price — breaches CONC 3.5.12R.

---

## Additional Restricted Expressions — Secured (CONC 3.6.8R)

Promotions for credit secured on land are subject to all the unsecured restricted expressions above, plus:

| Expression | Permitted only when |
|---|---|
| **"loan guaranteed" / "pre-approved"** | The lending is truly unconditional — no creditworthiness, income, or property checks will affect the outcome |
| **"no credit checks"** | No credit check of any kind is in fact carried out |

These expressions are prohibited even as softened variants: "subject to status" does not cure a "pre-approved" headline claim.

---

## Key Points for Agents

- Representative example required whenever any rate of interest or cost of credit is indicated — not just when the full APR is stated. Any cost reference triggers the obligation — including a daily rate ("£2 per day"), weekly cost, or total charge figure.
- Eight elements, all equal prominence, labelled "representative example", given no less prominence than any incentive to apply.
- Representative APR = rate at which at least 51% of customers from that promotion are expected to receive credit. Cannot cherry-pick the best available rate unless it genuinely applies to the majority.
- Three APR-only triggers: restricted audience implication; favourable comparison; incentive to apply — including speed or ease of application. Phrases like "apply in 60 seconds", "instant decision", or "3-minute application" are incentives to apply and trigger the APR requirement even with no cost reference in the promotion.
- APR must be shown as "%APR"; variable products = "variable"; representative figure = "representative".
- Five restricted expressions (unsecured): "overdraft", "interest free", "no deposit", "gift/present", "weekly equivalent" — each strictly literal. Secured adds "loan guaranteed/pre-approved" and "no credit checks".
- Compulsory ancillary services must be clearly stated alongside the representative APR — not in footnotes.

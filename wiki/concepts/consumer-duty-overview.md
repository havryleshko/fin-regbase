# FCA Consumer Duty — Overview

## What It Is

The Consumer Duty is a package of rules introduced by the FCA through PS22/9 (July 2022) that sets a higher standard of care firms must provide to retail customers. It is the primary regulatory framework governing retail financial services conduct in the UK. The overarching obligation is:

> **Firms must act to deliver good outcomes for retail customers.** (Principle 12, PS22/9 §4.6)

This replaced Principles 6 (treat customers fairly) and 7 (clear, fair and not misleading communications) for in-scope activity. "Good outcomes" is an intentionally higher standard than "fair treatment." **(PS22/9 §4.7)**

## The Structure: Three Layers

The Duty operates as three interlocking layers. **(PS22/9 §1.15)**

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  Consumer Principle (Principle 12)               │
│  "Act to deliver good outcomes for retail        │
│   customers"                                     │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Cross-Cutting Rules (apply across all outcomes) │
│  1. Act in good faith                            │
│  2. Avoid causing foreseeable harm               │
│  3. Enable customers to pursue financial         │
│     objectives                                   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Four Outcome Rules                              │
│  Products & Services │ Price & Value             │
│  Consumer Understanding │ Consumer Support       │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Scope: Who It Applies To

The Duty applies to all FCA-authorised firms that **determine or materially influence** retail customer outcomes — across the entire distribution chain. **(PS22/9 §2.10)** This includes:

- Manufacturers (firms that create, develop, design, issue, operate or underwrite a product or service)
- Distributors (firms that offer, sell, recommend, advise on, propose or provide a product or service)
- Firms with no direct customer relationship, if they have material influence over product design, pricing, communications or support

Each firm is responsible only for **its own activities** — not for the actions of other firms in the chain. **(PS22/9 §2.16)**

**Out of scope:** Purely wholesale products not designed for retail distribution; activities excluded by Handbook or legislation; non-regulated activities that are not ancillary to a regulated activity. **(PS22/9 §2.20, §2.28)**

### Sector-Specific Retail Customer Scope

FG22/5 maps the "retail customer" definition to the specific sourcebook test in each sector: **(FG22/5 §2.3)**

| Sector | Sourcebook | Retail customer scope |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Consumer credit | CONC | All regulated credit-related activities |
| Deposit-taking | BCOBS | Consumers, micro-enterprises, charities <£1m, natural persons acting as trustees outside trade/business |
| Insurance | ICOBS | Excludes reinsurance, large commercial risks, group insurance extension |
| Investments | COBS | Customers who are not professional clients (elected under COBS) |
| Mortgages | MCOB | All regulated mortgage contracts; excludes unregulated buy-to-let, commercial lending |
| Payments/e-money | PSRs/EMRs | Consumers, micro-enterprises, small charities |

**Critical addition:** A firm that encourages a customer to opt up to professional client status specifically to avoid Consumer Duty protections is itself in breach of the Duty. The Duty applies to the process of determining client status. **(FG22/5 §2.10)**

## The Outcomes Standard

Firms must not only intend good outcomes — they must **monitor, evidence and act on** whether outcomes are actually being delivered. **(PS22/9 §1.16)** This evidence requirement is what distinguishes the Duty from TCF. Annual board reports must assess whether the firm is delivering good outcomes. **(PS22/9 §13.12)**

The Duty applies at both **target market level** (product design, communications design) and **individual customer level** (one-to-one interactions, bespoke services). **(PS22/9 §5.15)**

## Implementation Dates

| Scope | Date |
|-------|------|
| New products + existing open products/services | 31 July 2023 |
| Closed products/services | 31 July 2024 |

**(PS22/9 §1.57)**

The Duty applies to closed books on a **forward-looking** basis only — past actions are judged against the rules that applied at the time. **(PS22/9 §3.1)**

## Relationship to Previous Standards

| Standard | Status under Consumer Duty |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Principle 6 (TCF) | Disapplied in scope; TCF outcomes still relevant but subsumed |
| Principle 7 (clear, fair, not misleading) | Disapplied in scope |
| PROD 3/4 product governance rules | Meeting these satisfies the products/services outcome |
| COLL 6/8/15 value rules | Meeting these satisfies the price/value outcome |

**(PS22/9 §4.12, §4.15, §6.5, §7.12)**

## Redress and Enforcement

No **private right of action** attaches to the Duty. Consumer redress routes are:
- [[disp1-treating-complainants-fairly|Financial Ombudsman Service (FOS)]] — for individual complaints (see [[disp2-fos-jurisdiction]] for FOS jurisdiction scope)
- FCA enforcement powers including fines and restitution under FSMA s.384 **(PS22/9 §11.1, §11.10)**

## Key Points for Agents

- The Consumer Duty has three layers: Principle 12 (apex), three cross-cutting rules (conduct standards), four outcomes (specific areas). All must be met simultaneously.
- "Good outcomes" is a higher standard than "treat customers fairly" — TCF compliance does not automatically mean Consumer Duty compliance.
- The Duty runs through the distribution chain: every firm that can materially influence retail customer outcomes is in scope.
- Firms must evidence outcomes are being delivered, not merely intend them — monitoring, MI and annual board review are mandatory.
- Implementation: open products from 31 July 2023; closed books from 31 July 2024.
- The retail customer definition varies by sector sourcebook — check CONC, BCOBS, ICOBS, COBS, MCOB or PSRs/EMRs as applicable.
- Encouraging a customer to opt up to professional client status to escape the Duty is itself a Duty breach.

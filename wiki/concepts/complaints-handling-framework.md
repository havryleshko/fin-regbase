# Complaints Handling Framework — DISP 1 Operational Rules

## Overview

DISP 1 sets out the complaints handling framework applicable to FCA-regulated firms, payment service providers, and e-money institutions. This page covers the five operationally critical features of DISP 1 that are most frequently misunderstood or overlooked: the dual timeline system, the 3-business-day track, the root cause analysis obligation, the named senior individual requirement, and the PSP/EMI separate reporting regime.

For the full sourcebook structure, see [[disp1-treating-complainants-fairly]].

---

## 1. The Dual Timeline System

DISP 1 operates two distinct response timelines depending on the nature of the complaint.

### Standard Complaints — 8 Weeks

For most complaints, a respondent must by the end of **8 weeks** from receipt either:
- Send a **final response** (accepting, offering redress without accepting, or rejecting with reasons), which must enclose the FOS standard explanatory leaflet, provide the FOS website, and tell the complainant they may now refer to the FOS; or
- Send a **written response** explaining why a final response cannot yet be provided, indicating when it can be, and informing the complainant they may now refer to the FOS.

*(DISP 1.6.2R)*

### EMD and PSD Complaints — 15 / 35 Business Days

Where a complaint is an **EMD complaint** (relating to electronic money) or a **PSD complaint** (relating to payment services), a materially shorter timeline applies:

| Scenario | Deadline |
|----------|----------|
| Standard | Final response within **15 business days** of receipt |
| Exceptional circumstances beyond respondent's control | Holding response by day 15 + final response by day **35 business days** |

*(DISP 1.6.2AR)*

**Key operational points:**
- Where only **part** of a complaint is an EMD/PSD complaint, that part must be handled on the 15/35-day timeline. *(DISP 1.6.2BR)*
- A respondent may choose to treat the **whole** complaint on the shorter timeline (DISP 1.6.2AR), simplifying case management. *(DISP 1.6.2CR)*
- EMD/PSD final responses must be **on paper or another durable medium** agreed with the complainant.
- The 15/35-day timeline is a hard rule derived from article 101 of the Payment Services Directive — it cannot be waived by procedure or internal policy.

### How the Two Timelines Interact

A complaint about a PI's or EMI's payment services that also raises a Consumer Duty concern (e.g. failure to support a customer in financial difficulty alongside failure to execute a payment) will have its payment services element subject to the 15-business-day deadline and any non-payment-services element subject to the 8-week deadline. Respondents should handle such mixed complaints in parts if needed *(DISP 1.6.1AG)*.

## 2. The 3-Business-Day Track

### What It Is

Complaints resolved by **close of business on the third business day** following the day of receipt are exempt from:
- The complaints time limit rules (DISP 1.6) — no final response, no 8-week or 15/35-day clock
- The complaints forwarding rules (DISP 1.7)

*(DISP 1.5.1R)*

The complaints **resolution** rules (DISP 1.4 — investigate competently, assess fairly, offer redress, explain assessment) continue to apply in full. *(DISP 1.5.2G)*

### When a Complaint Is Resolved Under DISP 1.5

A complaint is resolved under this section where the complainant has **indicated acceptance** of the respondent's response. Neither the response nor the acceptance needs to be in writing. *(DISP 1.5.2AR)*

This means an oral response to an oral complaint, accepted orally by the complainant, can be a 3-day resolution — provided the summary resolution communication is then sent in writing.

### The Summary Resolution Communication

Where the respondent considers a complaint to be resolved under DISP 1.5, it must **promptly** send the complainant a summary resolution communication — a written communication that:

1. Acknowledges the complaint and states the respondent considers it resolved
2. Tells the complainant that if dissatisfied they may refer to the FOS
3. Indicates whether the respondent consents to waive the DISP 2 FOS referral time limits (prescribed wording in DISP 1 Annex 3R)
4. Provides the FOS website address
5. Refers to further information on the FOS website

*(DISP 1.5.4R)*

The summary resolution communication is **not** the same as a final response under DISP 1.6.2R. It does not require the respondent to explain its assessment or set out reasons. It functions as a notice of resolution plus FOS signposting.

### Operational Significance

The 3-day track is the mechanism most firms use for the bulk of their complaints volume. Complaints reported as "closed within 3 days" appear as a distinct category in the DISP 1 Annex 1R return (reporting Table 2, row 22) and are counted as upheld or not upheld for reporting purposes even if resolved informally. Where the respondent has no enough information to make a decision but chooses to make a goodwill payment, the complaint is treated as upheld for reporting purposes. *(DISP 1.10.3G)*

## 3. Root Cause Analysis — A Hard Mandatory Obligation

### The Rule

A respondent **must** put in place appropriate management controls and take reasonable steps to identify and remedy recurring or systemic problems in handling complaints. *(DISP 1.3.3R)* The obligation has three limbs:

1. **Analyse** individual complaints to identify root causes common to types of complaint
2. **Consider** whether such root causes may affect **other processes or products, including those not directly complained of**
3. **Correct**, where reasonable, those root causes

Limb (2) is the most operationally demanding. The obligation does not stop at the product or service that generated the complaints — it extends to everything else in the firm that could be affected by the same root cause, even if no customer has yet complained about it.

### Management Information Requirements

The guidance at DISP 1.3.3BG sets out what processes should exist to support compliance with DISP 1.3.3R, scaled to the nature, scale, and complexity of the firm's business:

- Collection of MI on complaint causes and the products/services they relate to, including 3-day complaints
- A process to identify root causes
- A process to prioritise root cause remediation
- A process to assess whether root causes affect other products or processes
- A process for deciding how and whether to correct root causes
- **Regular reporting to senior personnel** on recurring or systemic problems — so that senior management can identify, measure, manage and control risks
- Records of analysis and decisions taken by senior personnel in response to root cause MI

### Proactive Redress for Non-Complainants

Where a respondent identifies recurring or systemic problems — whether through complaints or otherwise — it **should** consider whether customers who have **not complained** may have suffered detriment, and whether it is fair and reasonable to proactively contact those customers and offer redress or remediation. *(DISP 1.3.6G)*

This is guidance (G), not a rule. However, failure to follow it is treated as a potential breach of Principle 6 (Customers' interests). The FCA uses this provision as the basis for requiring firms to run reactive redress exercises after supervisory intervention — firms that proactively identify and remediate systemic problems are treated more favourably than firms that wait for regulatory action.

## 4. Named Senior Individual — DISP 1 Governance

### The Obligation

A firm **must** appoint an individual to have responsibility for oversight of the firm's compliance with DISP 1. *(DISP 1.3.7R)*

The individual appointed must be:
- Carrying out an **FCA governing function** at the firm or in the same group *(DISP 1.3.7R(2))*, or
- If no such individual exists at the firm or group: a person of **appropriate seniority** — and a person approved to perform the **limited scope function** (SMF29) for the firm or a firm in the same group satisfies this condition *(DISP 1.3.7R(3)–(4))*

### Relationship to SM&CR

This rule creates a named personal accountability line for DISP 1 compliance that runs to an FCA-approved individual. For firms within SM&CR scope, this means the DISP 1 compliance owner must be an SMF holder (or limited scope function holder). This is a distinct obligation from the SM&CR Prescribed Responsibility framework — PR(b) covers performance of obligations under the Certification Regime, not complaints handling — and is separate from the Consumer Duty board champion requirement.

Firms need not proactively notify the FCA or the FOS of who the individual is, but must provide the name promptly on request. *(DISP 1.3.8G)*

### PI/EMI Implication

PIs and EMIs that are not FSMA-authorised are not within SM&CR scope *(see [[smcr-senior-managers-regime]])*. DISP 1.3.7R applies to "firms" and is therefore primarily directed at FSMA-authorised respondents. However, PIs and EMIs are still subject to DISP 1 handling and time limit rules (DISP 1.1.10ACR), and the FCA expects equivalent governance standards — the FCG 7 model of a named senior individual with complaints authority applies by analogy.

## 5. PSP/EMI Complaints Reporting — DISP 1.10B Separate Regime

### What DISP 1.10B Is

DISP 1.10B creates a **separate annual complaints reporting obligation** for:
- Credit institutions that provide payment services or issue electronic money
- Electronic money institutions (EMIs)
- Payment institutions (PIs)
- Registered account information service providers (RAISPs)

These respondents must provide the FCA with a **complete annual report** concerning complaints received about payment services and electronic money, in the format specified in **DISP 1 Annex 1AD**. *(DISP 1.10B.1)*

### Broader Scope Than Standard Reporting

The most important distinction from DISP 1.10 standard reporting is scope. DISP 1.10B.2G states explicitly:

> "In contrast to the other provisions in DISP 1 which generally apply only to complaints from eligible complainants, the complaints reporting directions apply **in addition to complaints from payment service users that are not eligible complainants**."

This means PIs and EMIs must report ALL complaints about payment services and electronic money — including complaints from business customers, corporate users, and payment service users who fall outside the eligible complainant definition. The Annex 1AD return must cover the full scope of payment services and e-money complaints, not the eligible-complainant subset.

### Reporting Periods and Submission

The DISP 1.10B reporting period is annual:
- Where the respondent has an accounting reference date: the year immediately following that date
- Where the respondent has no accounting reference date: the year ending 31 December

Reports are due within **30 business days** of the end of the relevant period. *(DISP 1.10B.9–1.10B.10D)*

### Relationship to Standard DISP 1.10 Reporting

A PI or EMI that is also an FSMA-authorised firm is subject to **both** DISP 1.10 (standard bi-annual reporting in Annex 1R for all eligible complainant complaints) **and** DISP 1.10B (annual Annex 1AD reporting for all payment services/e-money complaints including non-eligible-complainants). These are separate obligations with different formats, scopes, and submission deadlines.

The DISP 1.10B return also includes a separate table (Table 4) for complaints relating to alleged **authorised push payment fraud** — a distinct reporting category not included in the standard Annex 1R return.

## Record Keeping

Complaint records must be retained for:

- **5 years** for complaints relating to UCITS collective portfolio management
- **3 years** for all other complaints

from the date the complaint was received. *(DISP 1.9.1R)*

Records of the measures taken for resolution can and should be used to support the root cause analysis MI requirements under DISP 1.3.3BG. *(DISP 1.9.2G)*

## Key Points for Agents

- There are **two mandatory response timelines**: 8 weeks (standard) and 15 business days / 35 business days (EMD/PSD). The shorter timeline is derived from the Payment Services Directive and cannot be waived. Mixed complaints must be handled in parts if needed.
- **The 3-day track** exempts firms from time limit and forwarding rules but still requires the complaints resolution rules to be met. A summary resolution communication (lighter than a final response) must be sent in writing even where the acceptance was oral.
- **Root cause analysis (DISP 1.3.3R) is a hard rule**, not guidance. Firms must have MI systems, senior management reporting, and processes to assess whether root causes affect other products and processes — including those not complained about. Failure to identify and remediate systemic problems proactively is a Principle 6 breach risk.
- **Every FSMA-authorised firm must name an SMF holder** (or limited scope function holder) as DISP 1 compliance owner (DISP 1.3.7R). This is a distinct accountability mechanism from SM&CR PRs and the Consumer Duty board champion.
- **DISP 1.10B creates a separate annual complaints reporting regime for PIs/EMIs** in Annex 1AD format. It covers all payment services/e-money complaints — including those from non-eligible-complainant business users — which is a broader scope than the standard DISP 1.10 regime.
